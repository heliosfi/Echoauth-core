ENGINEER OBJECTION & FAQ SHEET


MCG · NI-AI · EchoAuth


Purpose


This document pre-empts common engineering, security, and product objections so the team:

• Does not overbuild

• Does not reinterpret intent

• Does not weaken safety

• Does not drift from the handoff


⸻


OBJECTION 1


“Why not let NI-AI decide when to execute? It already understands context.”


Answer:

Because understanding is not authority.


NI-AI is intentionally limited to explanation and clarity. Granting execution authority to an interpretive system introduces ambiguity, bias, and audit risk.


Design Rule:


NI-AI may explain decisions but may never make them.


⸻


OBJECTION 2


“Why can’t humans override the system if they know better?”


Answer:

Humans can always stop the system.

Humans cannot force unsafe execution.


Allowing forced overrides defeats the purpose of a safety architecture and creates liability.


Design Rule:


Human authority includes revocation and shutdown, not escalation.


⸻


OBJECTION 3


“Why not skip CONFIRM if the signal is strong?”


Answer:

Because strength without repetition is noise.


CONFIRM exists to prevent single-event overreaction. Skipping it reintroduces the very instability the system is designed to eliminate.


Design Rule:


All execution paths must pass through CONFIRM.


⸻


OBJECTION 4


“This feels too restrictive. Aren’t we missing opportunities?”


Answer:

No. The system is designed to miss opportunities rather than create risk.


Non-action is a successful outcome when structure is incomplete.


Design Rule:


Restraint is not failure; it is the goal.


⸻


OBJECTION 5


“Why not add fallback automation if execution fails?”


Answer:

Fallback automation creates hidden execution paths.


Failures must reduce capability, not redirect it.


Design Rule:


There are no automatic retries that bypass state.


⸻


OBJECTION 6


“Why is execution denied by default?”


Answer:

Because default allow creates silent risk.


Execution must be earned through explicit alignment of state, permission, and authorization.


Design Rule:


DENY is the only safe default.


⸻


OBJECTION 7


“Can we cache permissions to improve performance?”


Answer:

No. Cached permissions create stale authority and race conditions.


Authorization is evaluated on every execution attempt.


Design Rule:


Permission is ephemeral, not persistent.


⸻


OBJECTION 8


“Why log non-actions? That seems noisy.”


Answer:

Because non-action is a decision outcome.


Logs prove the system is behaving correctly under restraint.


Design Rule:


If nothing happens, it still happened correctly.


⸻


OBJECTION 9


“Why not infer user intent or urgency?”


Answer:

Intent inference introduces subjectivity and risk.


The system operates on structure, not psychology.


Design Rule:


No emotional, motivational, or intent modeling.


⸻


OBJECTION 10


“Can we add more intelligence later?”


Answer:

Only through explicit architectural review.


This system is intentionally minimal and bounded.


Design Rule:


Capability expansion requires re-approval, not iteration drift.


⸻


RED FLAGS (STOP WORK IF HEARD)


If any engineer says:

• “It would be smarter if…”

• “We can just auto-approve when…”

• “Users won’t notice if…”

• “We can shortcut this state…”


→ Stop and review.

Those statements indicate drift.
