EchoAuth — Parent Resolution Guide


What to Do When the School or Clinic Calls


First, the most important thing to understand


If the system stopped, nothing went wrong.

The system is asking you to lead.


EchoAuth halts when it detects conflicting instructions, unclear authority, or uncertainty that could affect your child’s safety. This is intentional.


A halt means:


“We need a clear yes or no from you before anything continues.”


⸻


When You Get the Call


You may hear:

• “The system locked us out”

• “EchoAuth stopped responding”

• “We can’t proceed with the task”


Translate that mentally to:


“The system is protecting my child and needs my decision.”


⸻


What You Do (Step-by-Step)


1. Stay Grounded


You do not need to fix the system.

You do not need to rush.


The system is paused on purpose.


⸻


2. Open the Parent / Caregiver Portal


Go to the Governance Hub.


You will see:

• Which task or activity is paused

• Why it paused (conflict, timing, or authority)

• Which modules detected the issue


This is information, not an error report.


⸻


3. Decide the Outcome You Want


Ask yourself:

• Do I want the task to proceed as planned?

• Do I want it adjusted?

• Do I want it stopped for now?


There is no “right” answer — only your answer.


⸻


4. Issue a Unified Instruction


Select the desired action and re-confirm your authority (biometric or secure PIN).


This does two things:

• Re-establishes you as the single decision authority

• Gives the system a clear, safe direction


⸻


5. Confirm Execution Resumes


Once confirmed:

• The system unlocks

• Execution resumes only according to your instruction

• Delegates can continue their role safely


⸻


What You Do Not Need to Do

• You do not need to justify your decision to the system

• You do not need to resolve staff disagreements

• You do not need to override safeguards

• You do not need to act immediately under pressure


The halt gives you time.


⸻


How to Respond to Staff (One Sentence)


If you want a simple script, use this:


“Thanks for pausing. I’ll review it now and send a clear instruction through the parent portal.”


That reinforces:

• You are in control

• The system worked

• No one is at fault


⸻


Remember Why This Exists


Your child may not always be able to say:

• “This is too much”

• “I need a break”

• “This change isn’t okay”


EchoAuth pauses so those decisions never get made around your child without you.


⸻


Bottom Line (Read This When It Feels Inconvenient)


The system does not stop because something broke.

It stops because your authority matters.


⸻


Parent Principle


If the system asks you to decide, it’s doing its job.
