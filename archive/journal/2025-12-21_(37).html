Universal Statement (Room-Safe, Grant-Safe, Government-Safe)


EchoAuth is a child-first care coordination and access system designed for autistic and non-verbal children.

It enables shared visibility across parents, caregivers, schools, and clinicians while enforcing parental authority, non-verbal access, and strict safety controls.


The system does not diagnose, prescribe, or make medical decisions.

It records observations, stores clinician-provided documents, tracks caregiver-entered schedules, issues time-based reminders, and pauses on uncertainty.


All professional input is advisory and documented.

All actions that affect the child require parental approval.

All system behavior is logged, auditable, and non-interpretive.


EchoAuth is built to reduce caregiver burden, improve coordination, and increase safety and continuity for children who cannot reliably authenticate, communicate, or self-advocate — while remaining scalable, ethical, and institution-ready.
