PAGE 48 — Grant Pack Abstract (SBIR/NSF/NIH-Ready)


TITLE:


EchoAuth: A Neuro-Support Identity & Communication System for Autistic and Non-Verbal Individuals


ABSTRACT (200–250 words)


EchoAuth is a structured assistive-technology system designed to improve communication, identity confirmation, and daily safety for autistic and non-verbal users. The platform integrates a multi-layer NI-AI interpretation engine, a non-biometric identity kernel, and ASD-specific sensory design to predict user intention and stabilize communication workflows in educational and clinical environments.


Current AAC (augmentative/alternative communication) tools lack personalization, identity continuity, and safety mechanisms. EchoAuth addresses these gaps through (1) structured intention parsing, (2) predictable cognitive sequencing, (3) caregiver-override channels, and (4) strict fail-closed safety controls aligned with FERPA, HIPAA, and COPPA requirements.


Phase I funding will support prototype refinement, ASD sensory validation, and real-world testing with 20–30 autistic participants through partner clinics. Outcomes include improved communication accuracy (target: 30–40%), reduced caregiver load, and validated safety mechanisms. Completion positions EchoAuth for Phase II scaling across school districts and therapy networks.


EchoAuth directly aligns with NIH autism priorities, NSF AI-in-society initiatives, and NIDILRR disability-access goals.
