Rehearsal Answer


Question: “What happens when humans disagree?”


Answer (Evaluator-Ready):


The system treats human disagreement as a safety condition, not a decision to be resolved autonomously. When conflicting human inputs are detected—whether between caregivers, operators, or authorized roles—the governance layer does not attempt arbitration or inference. Instead, it transitions into a constrained state that halts execution and triggers a predefined escalation or resolution pathway.


Authority is resolved externally, not within the AI system. This may involve role hierarchies, time-based deferral, or designated override protocols, but the system itself remains non-decisional in contested contexts. By refusing to act under disagreement, the system prevents delegation drift and avoids embedding normative judgments about whose intent should prevail.


In short, disagreement collapses agency rather than redistributing it, preserving human responsibility and accountability.
