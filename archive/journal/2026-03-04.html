Deterministic Execution Gating for AI Systems in Vulnerable Care Environments


Overview


Artificial intelligence assistants are increasingly being introduced into educational, therapeutic, and caregiving environments. In these settings, AI systems may generate recommendations, instructions, or automated actions that affect vulnerable individuals. Current AI safety approaches focus primarily on improving model outputs; however, they do not reliably prevent unauthorized or unsafe execution of AI-generated actions.


This project introduces a deterministic execution-gating layer that ensures AI systems cannot act without explicit, verified human authorization.


The system is designed for high-trust care environments, particularly those supporting neurodivergent individuals, where incorrect or unauthorized AI actions can create safety, behavioral, or clinical risks.


---


Problem


AI systems used in assistive and caregiving contexts present three primary risks:


• AI-generated instructions may be incorrect or inappropriate  

• Systems may autonomously modify recommendations or behavioral plans  

• Automated workflows may execute without caregiver approval


In environments supporting autistic or non-verbal individuals, these risks are amplified because the user may not be able to question or refuse system actions.


---


Proposed Solution


The project develops a deterministic execution gate that sits between AI outputs and real-world actions.


Instead of trusting the AI system to behave correctly, the gate enforces a simple rule:


No action occurs unless an authorized human has explicitly approved it.


The gate evaluates every AI-triggered action against a caregiver-authorized rule set before execution.


If authorization is missing or ambiguous, the system halts execution by default.


---


Core Safety Mechanisms


1. Identity Verification  

   Confirms the identity of the person requesting or approving an action.


2. Intent Binding  

   Links AI-generated recommendations to a specific human decision.


3. Authorization Validation  

   Confirms that the action falls within caregiver-defined constraints.


4. Deterministic Execution Gate  

   Prevents system actions unless authorization conditions are satisfied.


5. Audit Logging  

   Records all approvals, denials, and halted actions for transparency and oversight.


---


Pilot Objective


The initial pilot will evaluate whether deterministic execution gating can reduce unauthorized or unsafe AI actions in assistive technology environments serving neurodivergent users.


Evaluation metrics include:


• frequency of blocked unauthorized actions  

• caregiver oversight effectiveness  

• system reliability under uncertainty  

• usability in real caregiving workflows


---


Expected Impact


This approach shifts AI safety from attempting to control model behavior to controlling system execution.


By ensuring that AI systems cannot act without explicit human authorization, the technology provides a practical safety layer for AI deployment in:


• autism support tools  

• educational assistants  

• behavioral therapy systems  

• clinical decision support environments


The result is a mechanical safety guardrail that protects vulnerable users while still allowing beneficial AI assistance.
