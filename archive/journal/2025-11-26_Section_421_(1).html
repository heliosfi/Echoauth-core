421.1 — Title of Invention


NI-AI™ (Natural Intellect – Applied Intelligence): A Human-Dominant, Multi-Signal, Direction-Bound Cognitive Support System for Neurodivergent and Non-Verbal Children.


421.2 — Field of the Invention


This invention relates to child-safety applied intelligence, non-verbal expression transcription, sensory-overload risk projection, caregiver-override governance, and hardware-minimal AI deployment frameworks.


421.3 — Purpose


A funding appendix that:

• positions the NI-AI system for grant, pilot-budget, and investor evaluation,

• communicates system novelty and safety-corridor governance,

• asserts that all reasoning operations remain narrow and caregiver-controlled,

• prohibits AGI autonomy, behavioral surveillance creep, medical diagnosis, or biometric identity storage,

• and enables multi-environment applicability (school, clinic observation, community hubs, public spaces) under human authority.


421.4 — Problem Addressed


Current systems fail neurodivergent and non-verbal children because they lack:

• clear boundary checks,

• caregiver-only override channels,

• environmental-risk prediction without emotional inference,

• and hardware-minimal adoptability for institutional pilots.


This appendix exists to remove confusion before release, map value for funding reviewers, and maintain defensibility without changing filed claims.
