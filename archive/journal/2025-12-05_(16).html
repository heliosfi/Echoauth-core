Revenue Model & Sustainability Plan (EchoAuth™)


EchoAuth™ is built to be financially sustainable, socially responsible, and accessible to low-income families — while generating stable recurring revenue from institutions.

This mixed-tier system ensures no family is ever financially burdened, while institutional partners (schools, clinics, daycares) cover the operational weight through deductible subscription fees.


⸻


1. Revenue Streams


A. Institutional Subscription Model (Primary Revenue Driver)


Institutions that serve autistic and neurodivergent children pay a subscription because they directly benefit from EchoAuth’s compliance, safety logs, authentication, and liability protection.


Examples of paying institutions:

• Daycare centers

• Public & private schools

• ASD therapy centers

• Clinics & behavioral programs

• After-school & community centers


Subscription model (monthly or annual):

• Small institutions: $99–$199/mo

• Mid-size centers: $299–$499/mo

• Large districts / networks: Custom enterprise contracts


Tax advantage:

Institutions can write off EchoAuth’s subscription fee as a business or operational expense.

→ This keeps institutions willing to pay.

→ This protects low-income families from costs.


⸻


B. One-Time Onboarding Fee for Individual Caregivers


For caregivers who are not institutions (e.g., babysitters, home aides, relatives, respite workers):

• One-time onboarding/verification fee: $49–$79

• No monthly subscription

• No recurring financial burden

• This covers:

• Identity verification

• Training module

• Safe-care guidelines

• EchoAuth badge for parent trust


This keeps EchoAuth inclusive and financially accessible.


⸻


C. Affordable Subscription for Parents Who Can Afford It


Parents who want EchoAuth for home use can choose:

• $10–$15/mo “Family Tier”

• Sliding scale discount for low-income households

• Proof-of-income reduces fee to $0–$5/mo


This ensures no child is denied protection because of financial struggle.


⸻


D. Wearable Contribution Channel (Optional / Supplemental)


EchoAuth’s future wearable line will include:

• Kinetic-powered bracelets

• Clothing with micro-energy harvesting

• Certified charity badges for athletes & creators


A small percentage of every wearable sold goes into the EchoAuth Family Assistance Fund, covering subscriptions for struggling families.


This fund does not affect CDFI loan repayment, but enhances long-term sustainability and social good impact.
