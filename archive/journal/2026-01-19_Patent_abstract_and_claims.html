1) Patent Abstract (Examiner-Safe, Locked)


Abstract


EchoAuth is a caregiver-anchored authorization system for protected individuals, including autistic children who cannot reliably self-authorize. The system enforces presence-based and continuity-based authorization by validating authorized human roles—such as parents, caregivers, school staff, and clinical staff—prior to permitting any system action affecting the protected individual.

Unlike conventional authentication systems that rely on individual credentials or inferred intent, EchoAuth refuses execution when authorization is absent, ambiguous, or broken, and escalates control to verified human authorities. The system explicitly excludes diagnostic inference and does not assess medical, psychological, or educational status, operating solely as a human-safe authorization and coordination mechanism across home, educational, and clinical environments.


This abstract is deliberately narrow, defensible, and aligned with 101/112 survivability.

2) Core System Claim (Claim Group 1)


Independent Claim (System)


A human-safe authorization system for a protected individual, comprising:

1. a presence verification module configured to detect the real-time presence of at least one authorized human actor associated with the protected individual;

2. an authority validation module configured to confirm that the authorized human actor holds a permitted role selected from a predefined set including parent, legal guardian, caregiver, educational staff, or clinical support staff;

3. a continuity assessment module configured to determine whether authorization remains valid across time and context;

4. an execution control gate configured to permit system actions affecting the protected individual only when presence, authority, and continuity are simultaneously satisfied; and

5. a refusal mechanism configured to deny execution and trigger escalation when any authorization condition is unmet or ambiguous,


wherein the system does not infer intent or diagnostic status of the protected individual and does not autonomously resolve conflicts between authorized human actors.


⸻


3) Core Method Claim (Claim Group 2)


Independent Claim (Method)


A method of enforcing human-anchored authorization for a protected individual, comprising:

1. verifying the presence of an authorized human actor;

2. validating the role-based authority of the authorized human actor relative to the protected individual;

3. assessing continuity of authorization across a temporal or environmental change;

4. permitting an action affecting the protected individual only when all authorization conditions are satisfied; and

5. refusing execution and escalating to human resolution when authorization is absent, broken, or ambiguous,


wherein the method excludes diagnostic determination and prohibits autonomous decision-making under contested authority.
