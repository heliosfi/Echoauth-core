HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP INTERACTION MODEL


MCG · NI-AI · EchoAuth


This block defines how humans interact with the system without weakening safety, authority boundaries, or control logic.


The guiding principle is:


Humans remain the ultimate authority; the system remains the ultimate enforcer of safety.


⸻


1. ROLE OF THE HUMAN


Humans are:

• Observers

• Reviewers

• Authorizers


Humans are not:

• Automated decision engines

• Real-time overrides

• Hidden execution paths


Human interaction is explicit, logged, and bounded.


⸻


2. HUMAN INTERACTION MODES


There are three allowed interaction modes.

No others exist.


⸻


2.1 OBSERVATION MODE


(Default Human State)


Purpose

Allow humans to understand system status without influencing it.


Behavior

• Humans receive explanations from NI-AI

• Humans can view:

• Current MCG state

• Authorization status from EchoAuth

• Lifecycle phase


Restrictions

• No execution requests allowed

• No state changes allowed

• No priority escalation


Observation never implies consent or readiness.


⸻


2.2 REVIEW MODE


(Human Evaluation Without Control)


Purpose

Allow humans to assess system behavior and outcomes.


Behavior

• NI-AI provides:

• Context summaries

• State transitions

• Reasons for non-action or de-escalation


Restrictions

• Humans cannot force MCG state changes

• Humans cannot bypass EchoAuth

• Humans cannot “approve” execution retroactively


Review is informational, not operational.


⸻


2.3 AUTHORIZATION MODE


(Explicit Human Permission)


Purpose

Allow humans to grant permission within predefined bounds.


Entry Conditions

• MCG must already permit Execute

• EchoAuth must validate identity and role

• Authorization must be intentional and explicit


Behavior

• Humans may approve or deny bounded execution

• Approval is:

• Time-limited

• Scope-limited

• Revocable


Human authorization enables action; it does not force it.


⸻


3. HOW HUMAN INPUT IS HANDLED BY EACH SYSTEM


⸻


3.1 NI-AI


(Explanation & Clarity)

• Translates system state into human-readable form

• Explains:

• Why execution is allowed or denied

• Why the system returned to Observe

• Does not advocate for action


NI-AI never pressures humans.


⸻


3.2 MCG


(Decision Discipline)

• Treats human input as context, not command

• Does not change state due to human preference

• Only structural conditions affect state transitions


Human intent does not override structure.


⸻


3.3 EchoAuth


(Permission Enforcement)

• Verifies human identity and role

• Enforces authorization limits

• Blocks any attempt to exceed granted scope


EchoAuth does not interpret intent — it enforces rules.


⸻


4. HUMAN OVERRIDE POLICY


(Intentionally Limited)


There is no unrestricted override.


Allowed:

• Human revocation of permission

• Human shutdown of execution

• Human denial of authorization


Not allowed:

• Forcing Execute

• Skipping MCG states

• Bypassing EchoAuth checks


Humans can always stop the system.

Humans cannot force unsafe action.


⸻


5. ERROR PREVENTION & MISUSE PROTECTION


The model prevents:

• Accidental execution

• Emotional decision pressure

• Authority confusion

• Silent overrides


All human actions are:

• Explicit

• Logged

• Auditable


⸻


6. AUDIT & TRACEABILITY


Every human interaction records:

• Identity (via EchoAuth)

• Interaction mode

• Timestamp

• Outcome (approved, denied, observed)


There are no invisible interactions.


⸻


7. SAFETY GUARANTEES

• Human presence increases transparency, not risk

• Human authority is respected but bounded

• The system never assumes human intent

• Execution remains reversible at all times


⸻


SINGLE-SENTENCE SUMMARY (ENGINEER ROOM)


Humans observe and authorize within bounds; the system enforces discipline, safety, and final execution control.
