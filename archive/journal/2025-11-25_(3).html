⭐ SECTION 323 — ADAPTIVE ESCALATION GOVERNANCE ENGINE (AEGE)


(NI-AI Patent Module — Safety-First Decision Framework)

────────────────────────────────────────


The AEGE regulates how NI-AI decides when to intervene, escalate, or notify a caregiver, especially for neurodivergent children or users who cannot self-report issues reliably.


This engine ensures NI-AI NEVER:

• ignores risk,

• over-escalates and causes panic,

• escalates wrongly,

• or fails to escalate when necessary.


It creates a controlled, predictable, parent-aligned escalation system.


────────────────────────────────────────


323.1 — Technical Field


The AEGE relates to artificial intelligence systems configured for safety-first decision-making, prioritizing escalation only when multi-signal validation indicates risk, and preventing unauthorized, unnecessary, or unsafe escalation events.


────────────────────────────────────────


323.2 — Purpose


This engine ensures that NI-AI:

• knows when to step in,

• knows when NOT to step in,

• knows when to lightly notify,

• knows when to alert caregivers,

• knows when risk requires urgent action,

without hallucinating danger or missing danger.


It gives NI-AI regulation-grade safety logic.


────────────────────────────────────────


323.3 — Functional Definition


AEGE is a tiered escalation controller that assigns a “risk level” to every detected event:


Level 0 — Normal State


Everything is stable; no action needed.


Level 1 — Soft Attention


NI-AI tracks something unusual but does not interrupt.

Examples:

• Slight quietness

• Minor behavioral change

• Irregular message pacing


NI-AI internally notes and monitors.


Level 2 — Soft Notification


NI-AI gently notifies caregiver:

“Hey, something changed. Not urgent, but worth watching.”


Level 3 — Strong Recommendation


NI-AI strongly suggests action:

• Hydration reminder

• Check on the child

• Environmental adjustment


Not panic — actionable guidance.


Level 4 — Alert Mode


NI-AI directly signals high concern:

• Possible dehydration

• Possible meltdown onset

• Medical-risk indication

• Sudden behavioral shutdown

• Dangerous silence in nonverbal child


Level 5 — Emergency Escalation


Triggered only if:

• multiple risk signals align, or

• caregiver pre-configures emergency conditions.


NI-AI NEVER alerts emergency services on its own.

It only alerts the caregiver unless explicitly authorized in the settings.

323.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Multi-Factor Risk Intersector (MFRI)


Cross-checks signals from at least 3 different NI-AI engines before escalating.


(b) Escalation Threshold Matrix (ETM)


Each child/user has a custom threshold — prevents false alarms.


(c) Contextual Override (CO)


NI-AI recognizes when risk is contextual:

• late-night calm ≠ danger

• sensory shutdown vs resting state

• stimming vs distress


(d) Drift Bias Correction (DBC)


Ensures escalation is based on real patterns, not anomalies or hallucinations.


(e) Parent-Centric Priority Layer (PCPL)


All escalation logic is aligned with caregiver instructions and sensitivity preferences.


(f) Prior Escalation Memory (PEM)


NI-AI remembers previous patterns and avoids repeating unnecessary alerts.


────────────────────────────────────────


⭐ 323.7 — Safety Principles


AEGE is built on principles that protect families:

• NI-AI never overrides caregivers.

• NI-AI never triggers emergency services without explicit permission.

• NI-AI must validate risk through multiple independent channels.

• NI-AI must provide calm, structured, non-traumatic notifications.

• NI-AI defaults to caution, not panic.

• NI-AI learns each child’s unique behavior pattern.
