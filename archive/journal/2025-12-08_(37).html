⭐ PAGE 13 — PILOT AGREEMENTS & INSTITUTIONAL CONTRACTS


(Template for schools, ASD clinics, hospitals, and research partners)


EchoAuth pilot engagements are governed by a standardized institutional agreement to ensure safety, clarity, compliance, and mutual understanding. The agreement covers operational, technical, and legal responsibilities for both HeliosFi LLC (the provider) and the participating institution (the pilot site).


⸻


13.1 Purpose of the Pilot Agreement


The EchoAuth Pilot Agreement establishes:

• scope of the pilot (duration, participants, environment)

• roles and responsibilities

• data and privacy boundaries

• safety procedures and stop conditions

• training requirements

• reporting and feedback expectations

• no-cost / low-cost access model for Phase I


This document ensures an ASD-safe testing and evaluation process.


⸻


13.2 Definitions


“EchoAuth System”

The assistive NI-AI platform used for communication and identity stabilization for autistic and non-verbal individuals.


“Participant”

A minor or adult with autism (or related neurodivergence) who engages with EchoAuth under guardian or clinician supervision.


“Pilot Site”

The school, ASD clinic, therapy center, or research institution that agrees to participate in a structured, supervised EchoAuth evaluation.


“Data”

Non-identifiable logs of system performance metrics (communication accuracy, time-to-expression, stability), unless otherwise approved by an IRB or institution.


“Guardian”

Parent or legal representative responsible for consent and oversight.


⸻


13.3 Scope of Pilot Deployment


Each pilot operates under the following standardized structure:


Duration


4–8 weeks, depending on institution scheduling.


Participants


5–20 participants, selected by the institution according to inclusion criteria defined in Page 12.


Environment

• ASD therapy rooms

• Special education classrooms

• Controlled clinical spaces

• In-home testing ONLY under prior approval


Session Frequency


1–2 sessions per participant weekly (10–15 minutes each).


Supervision Requirement


Every interaction must have:

• a clinician, therapist, teacher, OR guardian present

• no unsupervised use at any time


⸻


13.4 Responsibilities of HeliosFi LLC (Provider)


HeliosFi LLC agrees to:

1. Provide Pilot-Ready Hardware/Software

• pre-configured EchoAuth prototype

• user manuals and digital guides

• clean deployment kits

2. Conduct Onboarding Training

• 45-minute virtual or in-person training session

• covers safety, calm-mode, data boundaries, and intervention protocol

3. Ensure System Stability

• updated safety wrapper

• identity kernel calibration

• predictable communication templates

4. Protect Participant Data

• no personal identifiers collected

• no biometrics

• no emotional/behavioral inference

5. Provide Technical Support

• 24/7 support during pilot

• issue resolution within 24–48 hours

6. Deliver Final Pilot Report

• aggregated metrics

• system performance

• recommendations

• no individual private data


⸻


13.5 Responsibilities of the Pilot Site (Institution)


The institution agrees to:

1. Select Eligible Participants

• according to safety criteria defined in Page 12

• obtain guardian consent for each participant

2. Provide Trained Supervisors

• teachers, clinicians, therapists, or aides

• responsible for monitoring and guiding sessions

3. Ensure Safe Testing Environment

• minimize sensory overload

• maintain quiet and predictable environment

• uphold stop conditions

4. Adhere to Data Privacy Protocols

• no unauthorized recording

• no external analytics

• no off-label/system modifications

5. Submit Session Notes (If Agreed)

• high-level feedback

• no personally identifiable information

• optional qualitative observations

6. Participate in Post-Pilot Evaluation

• short survey

• 30-minute review meeting


⸻


13.6 Guardian Consent Requirements


Guardians must receive and sign a standardized consent form that includes:

• description of EchoAuth

• purpose of testing

• voluntary participation

• right to withdraw at any time

• no clinical or medical risk

• what data is collected

• how data is protected

• how the system is used safely


Consent is required BEFORE any EchoAuth interaction.


⸻


13.7 Institutional Data Protection Terms


The following protections must be upheld:

• EchoAuth does not store personal identifiers.

• No biometric data is processed.

• No emotional inference is performed.

• All logs are stored securely and expire automatically after the retention period.

• Pilot site may request immediate deletion of all logs at any time.


This meets FERPA, HIPAA-aligned, and COPPA-safe guidelines.


⸻


13.8 Financial Terms (Phase I Pilot)


Phase I pilots are typically:

• no-cost for selected ASD clinics or

• $0–$3,000 for school districts depending on scope and training load


Full-scale pilots after Phase I range:

• $5,000–$15,000 per site


Grants, SBIR funding, or institutional matching may reduce or fully cover costs.


⸻


13.9 Deliverables Provided to the Institution


At the end of the pilot, HeliosFi LLC delivers:


1. Pilot Summary Report

• communication accuracy improvements

• identity stability performance

• user engagement metrics

• sensory tolerance observations


2. Safety Review

• any flagged sessions

• system behavior logs

• evidence of fail-closed consistency


3. Recommendations

• readiness for full deployment

• required refinements

• proposed next-phase activities
