Document 1 — System Description


EchoAuth with NI-AI Subsystem

(Federal / Institutional Submission Version)


⸻


System Identity Statement (Anti-Drift Anchor)


EchoAuth is the parent system. NI-AI is a bounded subsystem within EchoAuth that provides interpretation and contextual risk modeling only. NI-AI does not execute actions, authorize access, or assert authority. Execution is gated by a separate sequencing mechanism (CEG), and identity and authorization are enforced by EchoAuth. Human authority is final. The system is refusal-first and halts under uncertainty.


⸻


1. Purpose & Intended Use


EchoAuth is a safety-first, identity-anchored governance system designed to support secure, predictable, and auditable human–AI interaction in sensitive environments.


The NI-AI subsystem operates solely as an interpretation and modeling layer, providing contextual understanding, stability assessment, and risk signals to downstream governance components. It is intended to reduce operational risk, not to replace human judgment or authority.


Primary intended uses include:

• Stabilizing AI interactions under uncertainty

• Preventing unauthorized or unsafe execution

• Supporting identity-bound, auditable decision workflows

• Enabling refusal-first behavior in high-risk or ambiguous conditions


EchoAuth with NI-AI is suitable for pilots, research deployments, and operational support roles where human authority must remain intact.


⸻


2. System Boundary


The system boundary is explicitly defined and enforced.


Included within scope:

• Identity and authorization enforcement (EchoAuth)

• Context interpretation and risk modeling (NI-AI)

• Policy enforcement and safety arbitration (Governance Halo)

• Execution sequencing and gating (CEG)

• Audit logging and integrity monitoring


Explicitly out of scope:

• Autonomous decision-making

• Independent execution authority

• Diagnostic or medical classification

• Psychological or intent inference as truth

• Self-modifying policy or identity rules


No component may act outside its defined role.


⸻


3. Core Components (Separation of Powers)


EchoAuth — Identity & Authorization

• Enforces identity assurance and access control

• Maintains immutable authority relationships

• Provides continuous verification without transferring authority

• Serves as the root of trust for all sessions


NI-AI — Interpretation & Modeling (Subsystem)

• Models context, stability, and risk

• Produces probabilistic signals only

• Does not execute, authorize, or decide

• Cannot override constraints or policy


Governance Halo — Policy Enforcement

• Evaluates proposed actions against constraints

• Enforces refusal, halt, or escalation

• Preserves human authority

• Prevents unsafe or non-compliant behavior


CEG — Execution Sequencing

• Issues single-use execution tokens

• Enforces ordering and pause/hold/deny states

• Logs all grant, hold, and refusal events

• Cannot interpret meaning or intent


⸻


4. Non-Goals & Explicit Exclusions


The system is not designed to:

• Operate autonomously

• Replace human decision-makers

• Optimize outcomes independently

• Infer user intent as authoritative truth

• Diagnose medical, psychological, or behavioral conditions

• Continue operation when constraints are unresolved


These exclusions are intentional and enforced by design.


⸻


5. Human Authority & Oversight


Human authority is always final.

• The system cannot self-authorize actions

• Disagreement collapses agency rather than redistributing it

• Escalation paths are predefined

• Manual override and shutdown are supported at all times


The system supports human operators by preventing unsafe execution, not by making decisions on their behalf.


⸻


6. Failure Behavior & Safety Posture


The system is designed to fail safely.

• If identity assurance fails → no execution

• If context is unstable → refusal or halt

• If biological overload indicators exceed thresholds → non-essential operations stop

• If governance constraints conflict → execution is denied

• If sequencing control is unavailable → no action proceeds


The system does not degrade into autonomous behavior under failure conditions.


⸻


7. Auditability & Traceability


All critical events are logged and reviewable, including:

• Authentication attempts

• Policy enforcement decisions

• Execution grants, holds, and denials

• Drift and instability signals

• Halt and escalation events


Logs are designed to support:

• Security review

• Incident response

• Post-hoc analysis

• Institutional accountability
