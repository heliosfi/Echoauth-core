⸻


EchoAuth — Features by Stage (1–25)


(Canonical System Reference)


⸻


STAGE 1 — Portal Architecture & Role Separation

• Separate portals for Parent, Child, School/Clinic, Caregiver

• Shared child record underneath

• Role-based access enforced at system level (not UI)


Purpose: Prevent role confusion and authority overlap.


⸻


STAGE 2 — Parental Authority Lock

• Parent is sole authority over:

• access

• approvals

• activations

• schedules

• No auto-activation by professionals

• No override paths


Purpose: Structural consent enforcement.


⸻


STAGE 3 — Child Access Interface (Flame Login)

• Non-verbal entry

• No credentials

• Calm, familiar cues

• No failure states


Purpose: Safe access for non-verbal ASD children.


⸻


STAGE 4 — Confidence Gating & Pause-on-Uncertainty

• Access ladder: Deny → Pause → Limited → Trusted → Verified

• System pauses instead of guessing

• Caregiver confirmation when needed


Purpose: Safety without stress or inference.


⸻


STAGE 5 — Shared Child Development Record

• One timeline across home, school, clinic

• Role-stamped entries

• Time-stamped events


Purpose: Single source of truth with preserved perspective.


⸻


STAGE 6 — Professional Contribution (Scoped)

• Schools/clinics can:

• observe

• document

• propose supports

• Cannot activate or decide


Purpose: Enable contribution without authority leakage.


⸻


STAGE 7 — Records Vault

• Upload doctor notes, prescriptions (documents), IEPs

• Reference-only storage

• Parent controls visibility


Purpose: Continuity without medical execution.


⸻


STAGE 8 — Medication & Care Schedule Tracking

• Parent enters schedules

• Time-based reminders

• Dose logging

• Missed-window flags


Purpose: Care logistics, not treatment.


⸻


STAGE 9 — Task Execution & Outcome Loop

• Propose → Approve → Execute → Log

• Tasks only run if parent-approved

• Outcomes logged factually


Purpose: Coordinated action with accountability.


⸻


STAGE 10 — Audit Logging (Global)

• Logs for:

• access

• actions

• uploads

• approvals

• Append-only


Purpose: Trust, traceability, accountability.


⸻


STAGE 11 — System Logging Framework

• Event-based logs

• Records what happened, not why

• No interpretation layer


Purpose: Evidence without narrative bias.


⸻


STAGE 12 — Access & Authentication Logs

• Session start/end

• Confidence band reached

• Pauses and denials logged


Purpose: Prove safe non-verbal access behavior.


⸻


STAGE 13 — Development & Observation Logs

• Categories (routine, engagement, regulation, etc.)

• Role- and org-stamped

• No scoring or labels


Purpose: Longitudinal development tracking (non-clinical).


⸻


STAGE 14 — Recommendation & Decision Logs

• Two separate streams:

• professional recommendations

• parent decisions

• No execution without both


Purpose: Verifiable authority preservation.


⸻


STAGE 15 — Care Task & Outcome Logs

• Who executed

• When

• Outcome notes

• Completion state


Purpose: Real-world usage evidence.


⸻


STAGE 16 — Medication & Reminder Logs

• Schedule creation

• Reminder sent

• Dose logged

• Missed-dose check


Purpose: Safety assurance.


⸻


STAGE 17 — Data Access & Audit Logs

• Who viewed what

• When

• For how long

• Under which role


Purpose: Transparency and compliance.


⸻


STAGE 18 — De-Identified Reporting

• Aggregated usage metrics

• Engagement trends

• Adherence patterns

• No PII


Purpose: Evaluation and scale readiness.


⸻


STAGE 19 — Role-Based Onboarding

• Parents onboard first

• Others invited by parent

• Scope acknowledged per role


Purpose: Clean entry, no confusion.


⸻


STAGE 20 — Role-Specific Training

• Embedded, short training

• Different per role

• Focus on what helps the child


Purpose: Adoption without burden.


⸻


STAGE 21 — Role-to-Feature Mapping

• Each role sees only what they need

• No hidden capabilities

• Clear boundaries


Purpose: Prevent misuse and mistakes.


⸻


STAGE 22 — Live Day-to-Day Operation Flow

• Real-life sequence:

• child engages

• adults observe

• parent approves

• tasks execute

• outcomes logged


Purpose: Reduce meetings, reduce repetition.


⸻


STAGE 23 — Safety Reinforcement in Daily Use

• Automatic pause on uncertainty

• Block on scope violations

• Parent confirmation on sensitive changes


Purpose: Safety stays active, not theoretical.


⸻


STAGE 24 — Scaling More Hands

• Delegated caregivers

• Task-scoped access

• Shared visibility

• No authority dilution


Purpose: More help without chaos.


⸻


STAGE 25 — Continuous Learning Loop (Non-Clinical)

• Shows what’s used

• Highlights gaps

• Supports adjustment with consent


Purpose: Improve support without labeling.


⸻


One-Line System Truth (Study Anchor)


EchoAuth coordinates people, not decisions.

Parents decide.

Professionals contribute.

The system records, reminds, and pauses.
