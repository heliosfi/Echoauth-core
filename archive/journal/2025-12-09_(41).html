⭐ 37.1 — OVERVIEW


EchoAuth operates across three regulated domains:

1. Education Data → FERPA

2. Health & Clinical Data → HIPAA

3. Child Online Safety → COPPA


EchoAuth’s architecture was designed from Day One to comply with all three without requiring biometric data, facial scans, or externally stored identifiers.


⸻


⭐ 37.2 — FERPA Compliance (Schools, IEP Programs, District Deployments)


FERPA governs how student education records must be handled.


EchoAuth FERPA Principles


1. Minimal Data Collection

EchoAuth stores no personally identifiable information (PII) unless explicitly provided by a parent or school admin.


2. Local Device First

All communication logs can remain entirely on-device or within a district-managed server.


3. Parent / Guardian Rights

Parents can:

✔ review logs

✔ request deletion

✔ restrict data access

✔ disable cloud syncing


4. No Third-Party Sharing

EchoAuth does not share data with external AI services, advertisers, or cloud analytics unless institution-approved.


5. IEP Integration Without Exposure

IEP-related communication events (needs, stress indicators, requests for help) are tagged internally without exporting sensitive learning data.


⸻


⭐ 37.3 — HIPAA Compliance (Clinics, Therapy Centers, Telehealth)


HIPAA regulates how health-related information must be protected.


EchoAuth HIPAA Principles


1. No External Transmission of PHI by Default

All potentially sensitive communication remains stored locally unless clinicians explicitly export it.


2. Encryption Everywhere

✔ In transit (TLS 1.2+)

✔ At rest (AES-256)

✔ Session-level encryption keys tied to ORI identity


3. No Biometric Storage

EchoAuth never stores:

✘ facial recognition

✘ fingerprints

✘ voiceprints

✘ iris scans

This removes a massive HIPAA liability surface.


4. Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Clinician access requires:

✔ device trust

✔ clinician PIN

✔ audit logging


5. Auditable Logs

Every clinician access event is logged, signed, and exportable for compliance review.


⸻


⭐ 37.4 — COPPA Compliance (Children Under 13)


COPPA protects children using digital platforms.


EchoAuth COPPA Principles


1. Zero Account Creation for Minors

Children do NOT create “accounts.”

All identity is handled through the ORI kernel, not personal data.


2. No Online Tracking or Profiling

EchoAuth does NOT:

✘ track online activity

✘ create behavioral profiles for marketing

✘ share data with external networks


3. Parent/Guardian Required for Activation

EchoAuth cannot operate in a child-centered mode without:

✔ parent approval

✔ or institution-level activation (schools, therapy centers)


4. No Open Internet Content

EchoAuth never pulls unfiltered web content, satisfying COPPA’s content safety rules.


5. Strict Data Minimization

Only essential communication and safety-intent data is stored.
