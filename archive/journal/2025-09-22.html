EchoAuth directly aligns with NIH’s precision medicine goals in autism research. Breakthrough studies show autism is not one condition but a spectrum with biologically distinct subgroups — for example, children positive for folate receptor autoantibodies who benefit from folinic acid. Meanwhile, large population studies clarify environmental debates, and the FDA has recently approved leucovorin for cerebral folate deficiency. These shifts highlight the urgent need for adaptive, family-centered technologies that can integrate with emerging biomedical pathways.


Our Phase I aims focus on:

1. Developing a sensory-adaptive login prototype (visuals, audio, elemental resonance, personal-object recognition).

2. Building a parent/clinician dashboard for precision tracking and therapeutic alignment.

3. Conducting a feasibility pilot with 30 families to evaluate usability, engagement, and clinical integration.


By grounding our work in both scientific evidence and lived family experience, EchoAuth offers a safe, non-invasive, and adaptable platform to enhance quality of life for children on the spectrum.


We are deeply grateful for NIH’s leadership in advancing autism research and family support. Thank you for considering our application. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss alignment with your institute’s priorities.
