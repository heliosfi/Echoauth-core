🔥 EchoAuth Filing Scaffold (Final, No Links, No Boxes)


1. Invention Title


EchoAuth™: Biometric, Coherence-Enforced Caregiver–Child Authentication Chamber


2. Background — Problem Scaffold


Typical authentication systems rely on passwords, SMS codes, or broad biometric templates. These systems fail when used for non-verbal autistic children and caregivers managing high sensory or cognitive load. They also do not enforce identity continuity between caregiver and child at runtime, which can lead to ambiguous inference and increased caregiver burden.


Caregivers for non-verbal ASD children oversee digital and physical access simultaneously, including apps, cameras, IoT sensors, and public environments. Generic AI used in these systems can drift in tone, hallucinate interpretations, or fail to handle ambiguous behavioral input safely. Existing security layers lack coherence enforcement and reliable human-fallback routing for ambiguity.


EchoAuth solves this by introducing a constrained authentication chamber that verifies caregiver–child pairs using biometric and behavioral coherence under immutable safety rules. The system disables unsafe autonomy, preserves identity anchoring, logs provenance, and routes ambiguous cases to humans only rather than assuming.


3. Safety-Examiner Abstract (~149 words)


EchoAuth™ is a biometric, coherence-enforced caregiver–child authentication chamber governed by a tightly constrained Neuro-Invariant AI runtime (NI-AI). The system authenticates caregivers and non-verbal autistic children using behavioral and biometric signals while enforcing immutable safety constraints that disable model drift, hallucinated inference, or unverified autonomy. If coherence or identity conditions cannot be verified, the chamber blocks the interaction and routes it to a human-only fallback path, preserving caregiver consent and identity governance. Interaction threads are preserved by a Thread-Linking Engine (TLE) to reduce cognitive load for caregivers operating in high sensory environments. All decisions are logged in a time-stamped audit trail for examiner review. The chamber integrates with digital and physical downstream authentication endpoints including applications, IoT devices, camera monitoring, caregiver hubs, and autism-centered supervised community deployments. The system intentionally avoids speculative inference, favors safety over assumption, and protects identity anchoring for neurodivergent users.


4. Core Safety Invariant


“Sacrifice everything that is not required for safety, but protect everything required for identity and re-anchoring — enforced at runtime.”
