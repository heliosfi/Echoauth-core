1️⃣ Common Mistakes Micro-Card


“What Staff Will Still Try Anyway”


(Pocket-sized / lanyard / back-of-badge)


Why This Exists


Even trained staff will try to “help.”

This card names the shortcuts before they happen.


⸻


❌ Common Mistakes (Do Not Do These)


“Just This Once”

• ❌ “The child is calm — I’ll extend it a bit”

• ❌ “We’ve done this before, it’s fine”


Why it fails:

Past approval ≠ current authorization.


⸻


Retrying After a Halt

• ❌ Re-tapping the same command

• ❌ Closing and reopening the app

• ❌ Switching devices or accounts


Why it fails:

The halt is intentional. Retrying increases conflict, not clarity.


⸻


Guessing Caregiver Intent

• ❌ “They would want this”

• ❌ “This is usually allowed”

• ❌ “It’s clinically appropriate”


Why it fails:

The system does not infer intent. Neither should you.


⸻


Acting First, Documenting Later

• ❌ Making the change, then writing it up

• ❌ “We’ll tell them after”


Why it fails:

Documentation does not retroactively authorize action.


⸻


Treating the System as Broken

• ❌ “The app froze”

• ❌ “The system is glitching”


Why it fails:

A halt is not an error. It’s a safety state.


⸻


✅ The Only Correct Response


STOP → Document → Notify Caregiver


⸻


Micro-Rule (Bottom of Card)


If you feel rushed, slow down.

If the system stops, you stop.

3️⃣ Threshold Tuning Worksheet


Adjusting S7 / S22 Without Authority Drift


(Internal use only — not field-facing)


Purpose


This worksheet exists to tune sensitivity, not authority.


You are allowed to adjust when the system asks for help,

not who decides.


⸻


Section A — S7 (Ambiguity Detection)


Question to Ask


Are we halting because intent is unclear — or because tolerance is too narrow?


Adjustable (Safe)

• Time-window overlap tolerance

• Pre-authorized micro-variation ranges

• Confidence thresholds for proposal vs halt


Never Adjustable

• Who resolves ambiguity

• Whether ambiguity allows execution


⸻


Example Tuning


Scenario Current Behavior Possible Adjustment

2-min delay triggers halt Too sensitive Allow ±3 min buffer

Clinician switches prompt phrasing Flagged as variance Allow phrasing variants

New activity suggested Halt Correct — keep



⸻


Section B — S22 (Execution Flow)


Question to Ask


Is execution rigid — or just correctly bounded?


Adjustable (Safe)

• Allowed pivots within a task

• Pre-approved escalation paths

• Execution pacing limits


Never Adjustable

• Delegate authority scope

• Ability to bypass caregiver review

• Execution during unresolved conflict


⸻


Example Tuning


Scenario Current Behavior Correct Response

Therapist wants softer prompt Halt Allow within-range pivot

Therapist wants new method Halt Correct — caregiver decision

Teacher skips transition Halt Correct — keep



⸻


Section C — Red Line (Do Not Cross)


If a proposed tuning allows:

• Delegates to decide instead of caregivers

• Execution under unresolved disagreement

• “Temporary” bypasses


It is not tuning — it is drift.


⸻


Worksheet Rule


We tune thresholds to reduce noise, not to increase autonomy.
