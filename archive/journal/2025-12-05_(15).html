📌 Narrative Summary


EchoAuth is entering its first operational year with a sharply focused goal: complete development, secure licensing, and launch our adaptive authentication platform for autistic children, parents, and institutions. Revenue begins in Month 4 as early adopters (parents & small programs) onboard. Institutional subscription revenues begin in Month 6 as daycare centers, clinical programs, and schools integrate EchoAuth to strengthen safety, compliance, and caregiver transparency.


Our cost structure is intentionally lean, using contract engineers, cloud-optimized compute, and staged deployment. The CDFI microloan enables us to finalize the EchoAuth kernel, secure the first licensing assets, launch the FastAPI gateway, produce the prototype wearables, and begin outreach to institutions.


Break-even is projected late in the first year (Month 11–12), with sustainability supported by three revenue streams:


Revenue Streams

1. Parent Subscriptions (affordable, sliding-scale for low-income families)

2. Institutional Subscriptions (daycares, schools, clinical programs; tax-deductible business expense)

3. One-Time Onboarding for Individual Caregivers (nannies, babysitters, respite workers)


No family is financially burdened — low-income households receive reduced-rate access, and future wearables generate kinetic micro-donations to support families who cannot afford subscriptions.
