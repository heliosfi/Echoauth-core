EchoAuth™ — One-Page System Overview


Engineer- and Funder-Safe


Founder: Nicholas B. Carty

Focus: Caregiver-assisted access, communication, and safety workflows (including non-verbal and neurodivergent contexts)


⸻


1. The Problem


Caregivers and families supporting non-verbal or neurodivergent individuals often rely on fragmented tools that are:

• Not designed for shared caregiver oversight

• Difficult to audit or explain

• Unsafe when AI systems act without clear permission boundaries

• Non-compliant with expectations around consent, control, and accountability


Most AI systems prioritize automation over governance, creating risk in sensitive human-centered environments.


⸻


2. The Solution


EchoAuth™ is a governance-first human–AI support system.


It is designed to ensure that:

• Human intent always comes first

• AI assists but never self-authorizes

• Every action is checked for safety, coherence, and permission

• System behavior is visible, auditable, and pause-able


EchoAuth does not diagnose, manipulate behavior, or replace human judgment.

It supports caregiver-guided decision flows with built-in safety controls.


⸻


3. How the System Works (Simple)


EchoAuth separates roles clearly:

• Human (NI – Natural Intellect):

Initiates intent, requests, and confirmations.

• AI (Artificial Intelligence):

Performs bounded computation (e.g., signal framing, assistance, summarization).

Cannot act independently.

• MCG (Governance Layer):

Final authority that approves, pauses, or blocks actions based on safety, consent, and coherence rules.

• Mediator Hub:

Routes requests and responses while enforcing governance checks.

• User Interface (Display):

Shows system status and accepts requests only (no overrides).


Nothing happens without governance approval.


⸻


4. Safety & Accountability (Core Differentiator)


EchoAuth is built around explicit safeguards:

• No autonomous AI action

• No bypass of governance checks

• No hidden decision logic

• Full visibility into system state and pauses

• Audit-ready decision flow


This makes the system suitable for regulated, caregiver-dependent, and trust-sensitive environments.


⸻


5. Why This Matters to Funders


EchoAuth prioritizes:

• Risk reduction over novelty

• Responsible deployment over scale-at-all-costs

• Transparency over black-box behavior


This approach aligns with:

• CDFI and mission-driven lending goals

• Kiva’s emphasis on responsible entrepreneurship

• Institutional expectations around safety, explainability, and social impact


⸻


6. Use of Funds (High Level)


Initial funding supports:

• Prototype refinement and testing

• Compliance-aligned documentation

• Caregiver-focused pilot deployment

• Engineering validation and audit readiness


The goal is measured, accountable progress, not speculative expansion.


⸻


7. Bottom Line


EchoAuth is not trying to make AI more powerful.

It is designed to make AI safe, accountable, and useful where human care and responsibility matter most.
