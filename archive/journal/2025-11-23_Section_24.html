⭐ 24.2 Hardware Inputs (Toy-Based Sensors)


STM includes tiny, safe sensors that detect:


A. Pressure Sensor


Detects:

• squeezing

• hugging

• pressing

• clutching

• “comfort grip”

• “pain squeeze”


B. Accelerometer / Motion Sensor


Detects:

• shaking

• swinging

• pacing while holding toy

• rocking rhythm

• freeze while clutching


C. Temperature Micro-Sensor


Detects:

• hot flashes (fever onset)

• cold-stress

• warm grip (calming)

• lack of touch (withdrawal)


D. Micro-Haptic Receiver


Receives wearable or hub signals to:

• vibrate gently

• regulate emotions

• support calming


E. Low-Level Sound Exposure Sensor


(Not a microphone; detects sound pressure only.)

Detects:

• noise spikes

• high sensory load


F. Proximity Chip


Detects when the child:

• moves away from safe areas

• leaves home boundaries

• approaches risk zones

⭐ 24.3 Hardware Outputs


The Smart Toy Module can output:


A. Calming Haptic Vibrations


Soft pulses to soothe sensory overload.


B. Warmth or Cool Touch Patch (Optional)


Temperature modulation for:

• comfort

• sensory grounding

C. Color-Glow Indicator (Tiny LED)


Displays:

• blue = calm

• green = focused

• yellow = rising stress

• orange = overload

• red = danger/medical alert


D. Comfort Tone Output


Low-frequency, toy-safe tones:

• heartbeat tone

• ocean rumble

• soft hum

• sensory reset tone


E. Message Relay to Caregiver


The toy can “speak” to parents via the app:

• “He is gripping tightly — stress rising.”

• “Temperature suggests fever.”

• “Movement shows shutdown coming.”

STM connects to all major engines:


✔ NEDE — nonverbal decoding
