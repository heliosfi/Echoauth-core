Independent Claim Format — “7-Stage Multimodal Cognitive Pipeline”


A system comprising:

1. An input acquisition module configured to collect multimodal sensory data representing at least one of a child state, caregiver state, and environmental condition.

2. A biological state extraction module configured to transform the multimodal sensory data into a structured biological state vector comprising features associated with emotional load, stress probability, movement intensity, voice stress markers, and melanin-based resonance attributes.

3. A state stabilization engine configured to classify the biological state vector into one of a plurality of stability categories including stable, unstable, needs regulation, and unsafe, and further configured to halt system decision-making upon detection of unstable or unsafe states.

4. A cognitive interpretation engine configured to generate a cognitive frame by mapping the biological state vector to semantic constructs including emotional interpretation, intent estimation, ASD-specific behavior pattern recognition, and non-verbal communication decoding.

5. A governance module comprising a navigator component, a guardian component, and an architect component, the governance module configured to synthesize a governed intent from the cognitive frame.

6. A safety invariant engine configured to evaluate the governed intent against a plurality of non-violable safety invariants including no harm, no drift, no hallucination, no unsafe unlock, no uncertainty-based guessing, emotional neutrality, and decision traceability, and to block any governed intent that violates an invariant.

7. A chamber decision engine configured to output a final decision selected from a set comprising: unlock granted, unlock denied, hold, regulate, and reset, and further configured to generate caregiver guidance signals and chamber control signals based on the final decision.
