EchoAuth — One-Page System Overview


Parent-Governed, Cognition-First Authentication


EchoAuth is a parent-governed authentication and access-control system designed to ensure safe, consistent access for children across environments involving multiple caregivers, staff, or institutions. It solves a coordination problem—not a medical or instructional one—by enforcing explicit, parent-defined rules through a calm, predictable interface that minimizes cognitive load.


EchoAuth does not diagnose, treat, monitor, or analyze behavior or cognition. It functions solely as an assistive authentication and policy-enforcement interface.


⸻


System Architecture (At a Glance)


EchoAuth operates through three distinct portals, each with a single responsibility. Roles do not overlap.


Child Portal — Experience

• Minimal, predictable interface

• Non-verbal identity recognition

• One task at a time; no choice overload

• Simple confirmation action to proceed


Purpose: Allow calm, intuitive access without language, passwords, or decision pressure.


⸻


Caregiver Portal — Execution

• Role-restricted access

• Supports approved routines and activities

• No policy creation or overrides

• Actions are guided and logged


Purpose: Enable consistent support without requiring interpretation or authority.


⸻


Parent Portal — Governance

• Defines all rules, permissions, and boundaries

• Approves caregivers and roles

• Sets identity recognition elements

• Reviews audit logs and outcomes


Purpose: Centralize authority and prevent rule drift across environments.


⸻


Authority & Governance Model

• Parents / Legal Guardians are the sole policy authority

• Caregivers / Staff execute within assigned roles only

• EchoAuth enforces rules deterministically and consistently


Authority never shifts based on setting, staff, or circumstance.


⸻


Safety, Accountability, and Audit

• Every access attempt is logged with time, role, and outcome

• No hidden overrides or silent changes

• If a request falls outside policy, access pauses automatically

• Audit logs are immutable and reviewable by authorized parties


⸻


Non-Clinical Boundary (Explicit)


EchoAuth is not:

• Diagnostic

• Therapeutic

• Behavioral

• Instructional


It does not provide recommendations or modify behavior.

Its role is limited to authentication and access consistency.


⸻


Core System Principle (Lock This)


Parents define the rules.

Caregivers support execution.

EchoAuth enforces consistency.
