⭐ SECTION 315 — REAL-TIME SOCIAL–ENVIRONMENTAL INTERACTION REGULATION ENGINE (SEIRE)


(NI-AI Patent Module)

────────────────────────────────────────


315.1 — Technical Field


The SEIRE relates to systems and methods for detecting, interpreting, and regulating social and environmental interaction pressure in real time—particularly for autistic children and neurodivergent users.


This engine governs NI-AI’s ability to:

• sense when social interaction becomes overwhelming,

• monitor environmental social density,

• evaluate proximity and behavior of others,

• prevent social overload,

• provide guidance or calming intervention.


It is one of the most clinically valuable modules for ASD support.


⸻


315.2 — Purpose


In real environments—classrooms, parks, trains, stores, waiting rooms—autistic and sensory-sensitive users face:

• unpredictable social approaches

• accidental crowding

• fast-moving groups

• conversations layered on top of noise

• unwanted touching

• chaotic or pushy interactions


These social-environmental pressure spikes often cause:

• emotional shutdown

• panic behavior

• communication freeze

• sensory meltdown


SEIRE exists to prevent this by providing:


✔ Social Density detection

✔ Social Pressure scoring

✔ Proximity-threat assessment

✔ Motion vector tracking

✔ Social overload prediction

✔ Real-time calming cues

✔ Environmental adjustment recommendations

✔ Caregiver micro-alerts


⸻


315.3 — Functional Definition


SEIRE is a dual-channel regulation system operating across:

1. Environmental Social Channel

• number of people

• motion direction

• movement proximity

• crowd density

• noise from conversation

2. Direct Interaction Channel

• who is approaching

• how close they are

• what emotional energy they bring

• sudden gestures

• unexpected physical contact


Combined, these channels are translated into the:


Social Overload Index (SOI) — a 0–100 score indicating risk.


⸻


315.4 — Inputs

• Microphone (conversation density, voice energy)

• Camera/device sensors (if enabled)

• Motion patterns of surrounding individuals

• Ambient crowd noise profile

• NI-AI Emotional State Engine (ESE) readings

• Thread-Linking Engine (for context)

• Personal Social Baseline mapping (from Section 84+)

• Historical overload triggers


Optional advanced inputs (future hardware):

• wearable proximity sensors

• child’s micro-movement patterns

• temperature shifts (stress indicator)


⸻


315.5 — Outputs

• Social Overload Index (SOI)

• Social-Pressure Predictions (5–30 seconds ahead)

• Caregiver alerts

• UI simplification

• Social Density meter (visual indicator)

• Behavior guidance (simple, calming language)

• “Safe direction” recommendations

• Auto-switch to “Low-Social Mode”


⸻


315.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Social Density Mapping Layer (SDML)


Measures:

• number of people around

• motion vectors

• crowd flow direction

• noise from overlapping voices


Output: Social Density Score (SDS)


⸻


(b) Proximity Risk Engine (PRE)


Tracks:

• sudden closeness

• unexpected touches

• fast-approaching individuals

• directional threat vectors


Output: Proximity Risk Score (PRS)


⸻


(c) Interaction Pressure Classifier (IPC)


Classifies interactions:

• friendly

• neutral

• overwhelming

• aggressive

• unpredictable


Useful for classrooms, parks, family events, therapy environments.


⸻


(d) Social Load Fusion Layer (SLFL)


Combines:

• SDS (density)

• PRS (proximity)

• IPC category

• ESE emotional state alignment

• baseline social tolerance profile


Output: Social Overload Index (SOI)


⸻


(e) Social Stabilization & Guidance Layer (SSGL)


Based on SOI, NI-AI produces:

• calming cues

• directional guidance

• simplified communication

• grounding reminders

• instructions for caregivers (“move back 3 steps”, “lower brightness”, “reduce verbal input”)


⸻


(f) Overload Intervention Protocol (OIP-S)


If SOI exceeds threshold:

• initiate calming voice tones

• reduce UI motion

• reduce visual complexity

• activate “Safe Interaction Mode”

• notify caregiver silently

• simplify instructions


⸻


315.7 — Safety Layer


SEIRE is bound by:

• parental controls

• EchoAuth identity match

• NI-AI Doctrine limits

• anti-AGI rules (DLRC)

• clinical safety caps

• privacy guardrails

• sensor permission protocols


SEIRE never:

• controls movement,

• interprets personal identities,

• issues complex predictions about others.


It simply measures social overload pressure and responds protectively.
