392.3 — Functional Definition


PPGE performs four core functions:


(a) Baseline Pattern Aggregation


Builds a continuously updated model of the child’s:

• daily routines

• preferred environments

• sensory thresholds

• typical reaction sequences

• behavior rhythms over time


(b) Change Detection Layer


Identifies:

• deviations

• unusual frequency shifts

• unexpected slowdowns or spikes

• new avoidance patterns

• routine fragmentation


(c) Future-State Projection


Using non-medical predictive modeling, the system projects:

• likely next behaviors

• upcoming sensory pressure points

• potential conflict zones

• optimal transition moments

• anticipated overload points


(d) Proactive Recommendation Engine


Outputs simple, safe, legal guidance:

• “Lower sensory load in next 10 minutes”

• “Transition early to avoid escalation”

• “Preferred object comfort signal rising”

• “Hydration + rest recommended soon”

• “Break pattern suggests fatigue”


No emotion, no diagnosis — only behavioral trajectories.
