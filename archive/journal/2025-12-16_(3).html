EchoAuth™ — CDFI Interview Talking Points


1) Why are you building this?

• I’ve seen how caregivers are expected to rely on tools that automate without clear boundaries.

• In sensitive environments, that creates risk and mistrust.

• EchoAuth is built to keep humans in control while still benefiting from AI assistance.


⸻


2) Why you (as the founder)?

• I’m approaching this as a responsible operator, not a hype-driven technologist.

• My background combines technical work with real-world caregiving awareness.

• I’m intentionally building slowly, with governance and accountability first.


⸻


3) What makes this different from other AI tools?

• Most AI systems optimize for autonomy. EchoAuth optimizes for permission and pause.

• AI cannot act unless a governance layer approves it.

• The system is designed to stop safely rather than push forward under uncertainty.


⸻


4) What exactly will the loan be used for?

• Refining and stabilizing the prototype

• Preparing caregiver-guided pilot testing

• Producing compliance-aligned documentation

• Engineering validation and review


Nothing speculative. No rapid scaling.


⸻


5) What are the main risks, and how are you managing them?

• Technology risk: mitigated by bounded AI and clear system roles

• Operational risk: mitigated by phased milestones and small pilots

• Financial risk: mitigated by existing income and conservative planning


If something isn’t ready, the system — and the business — pauses.


⸻


6) How will you repay the loan if progress is slower than expected?

• Repayment does not depend on future contracts alone.

• I have ongoing consulting and service income to cover baseline repayments.

• The project is structured so obligations are met even during slower phases.


⸻


7) What impact are you aiming for?

• Helping caregivers feel supported, not replaced

• Making AI safer and more understandable in human-centered environments

• Building trust-first technology that communities can actually adopt


⸻


8) What does success look like in 12–18 months?

• A stable, tested prototype

• Small, responsible pilot deployments

• Clear documentation and audit readiness

• On-time repayment and lender confidence
