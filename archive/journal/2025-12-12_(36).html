SECTION 1 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (1 PAGE)


Project Overview


EchoAuth is a safety-first, non-verbal authentication and regulation support system designed for neurodivergent and non-verbal individuals, including those on the autism spectrum (ASD). The system helps determine when it is safe and appropriate for a technology-mediated action to occur, particularly in emotionally sensitive or regulated environments.


At the core of EchoAuth is Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG v3.0) — a deterministic decision-governance layer that ensures the system pauses, regulates, or proceeds based on safety, clarity, and permission.


EchoAuth does not diagnose, label, or replace caregivers. It governs process, not people.


⸻


Problem Statement


Many individuals cannot reliably interact with systems that depend on verbal communication, passwords, or rigid behavioral compliance. In these cases, existing technologies may misinterpret stress signals or act prematurely, increasing distress and reducing access.


Caregivers and institutions lack tools that are both non-verbal-friendly and safety-governed.


⸻


Solution


EchoAuth addresses this gap by combining:

• non-verbal signal recognition, and

• a strict governance layer (MCG v3.0)


to ensure the system only acts when conditions are stable, clear, and permitted.


When uncertainty exists, the system stops safely instead of guessing.

SECTION 2 — CLINICAL & SAFETY JUSTIFICATION (¾–1 PAGE)


EchoAuth is designed as a medical-adjacent, safety-governed system. It does not make medical claims and does not perform diagnosis.


Safety Principles


MCG v3.0 enforces the following principles at all times:

• No Guessing: If signals are unclear or incomplete, actions are paused.

• No Action During Emotional Instability: If emotional regulation is below threshold, the system halts.

• Human Authority Is Final: Caregivers and clinicians retain full control.

• No Emotional Manipulation: The system does not induce, suppress, or predict emotions.

• No Identity Confusion: Caregiver and child states are evaluated separately.


⸻


Fail-Safe Design


If the system encounters:

• conflicting inputs

• sensor failure

• ambiguous states


It enters Safety Hold Mode, preventing action until conditions stabilize.


⸻


Transparency


Every system decision produces a human-readable explanation, such as:


“Action paused due to emotional overload. Regulation recommended before proceeding.”


This ensures trust, oversight, and accountability.


⸻


Ethical Alignment


EchoAuth aligns with principles of:

• harm prevention

• proportional response

• explainability

• respect for neurodivergent communication


The system supports caregivers; it does not override them.
