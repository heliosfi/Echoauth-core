⭐ SECTION 327 — NEURODIVERGENT SENSORY PROFILE ENGINE (NSPE)


(NI-AI Patent Module)

────────────────────────────────────────


The NSPE is the adaptive heart of NI-AI’s neurodivergent intelligence.

It allows the system to understand, predict, and accommodate autistic sensory needs in ways no system on Earth currently does.


⸻


327.1 — Technical Field


This invention relates to computational models and adaptive learning systems for capturing, storing, analyzing, and updating individualized sensory profiles for neurodivergent users, with specific emphasis on autism spectrum conditions, ADHD, sensory processing differences, and nonverbal children.


⸻


327.2 — Purpose


The NSPE enables NI-AI to:

• Understand the child’s unique sensory thresholds

• Learn what stimulates, calms, or overwhelms

• Predict sensory overload before it happens

• Tailor environments (sound, light, pace, tone)

• Automatically adjust digital and physical interfaces

• Provide real-time sensory-safe recommendations

• Synchronize with EchoAuth & NI-AI engines for multi-domain support


This is a medical-grade, ASD-centered, safety-critical engine.


⸻


327.3 — Functional Definition


The NSPE constructs a living sensory identity for a child by:

1. Collecting multi-source sensory data

2. Mapping tolerances, triggers, and stabilizers

3. Generating a dynamic Sensory Comfort Index (SCI)

4. Predicting sensory overload windows (time, place, environment)

5. Providing regulated responses across:

• visual

• auditory

• tactile

• motion

• environmental

• emotional

• social load


This is the child’s sensory fingerprint, continuously updated and never erased.


⸻

⸻


327.5 — Outputs


The NSPE produces:


(1) Sensory Comfort Index (SCI)


Scaled 0–100.

Shows how “safe” the current sensory environment is for the child.


(2) Sensory Trigger Map (STM)


Listing:

• probable triggers

• intensity

• location

• expected reaction


(3) Sensory Stability Recommendations


Suggestions for caregivers, teachers, and devices.


Examples:

• “Lower background noise by 20%.”

• “Avoid bright light for 2 hours after school.”

• “Offer headphones after appointment.”

• “Switch interaction mode to Calming.”


(4) Overload Prediction Window (OPW)


Real-time prediction of:

• incoming sensory overload,

• shutdown risk,

• distress escalation.


(5) Adaptive Threshold Package


Sent to NI-AI engines so the system automatically shifts tone, pace, or sensory output.


⸻


327.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Sensory Vector Collector (SVC)


Transforms raw sensory readings into normalized multi-dimensional vectors.


(b) Trigger Pattern Analyzer (TPA)


Identifies recurring overstimulation patterns:

• large crowds

• sudden noise

• bright screens

• motion sickness

• certain textures

• emotional overload

• high cognitive demand environments


(c) Sensory Tolerance Estimator (STE)


Learns thresholds over time:

• max volume tolerance

• preferred brightness

• pacing preference

• touch acceptance

• screen interaction limits


(d) Predictive Sensory Model (PSM)


Uses temporal modeling to predict overload 5–30 minutes in advance.


(e) Sensory Stabilization Engine (SSE)


Selects calming interventions based on historical success:

• music

• fidget tool

• movement break

• silence

• weighted item

• dimmed light

• simplified instructions


(f) Dynamic Profile Updater (DPU)


Continuously updates sensory profile based on:

• new interactions

• updated medical reports

• caregiver feedback

• real-time behavior
