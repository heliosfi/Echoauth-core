HOSTILE Q&A — ROUND 1


Parents · Regulators · Grant Reviewers


⸻


👪 PARENTS


Q1: “Is this system making decisions about my child?”


Answer:

No. The system does not decide outcomes. It enforces boundaries so nothing happens unless it is explicitly allowed.


⸻


Q2: “Does the wearable monitor my child emotionally or psychologically?”


Answer:

No. Wearables do not infer emotion, intent, or mental state. They only reflect the system’s current state.


⸻


Q3: “What if my child is overwhelmed?”


Answer:

Overwhelm means the system slows down or pauses. Non-action is treated as a successful outcome.


⸻


Q4: “Can someone push my child to move forward anyway?”


Answer:

No. Staff cannot force progress. Physical and digital boundaries prevent it.


⸻


Q5: “Can I stop everything?”


Answer:

Yes. You can stop at any time, without explanation. Stopping is protected.


⸻


🏛️ REGULATORS / COMPLIANCE


Q6: “What prevents autonomous escalation?”


Answer:

Execution is denied by default. Action requires alignment across state discipline, authorization, and human approval.


⸻


Q7: “Is there any behavioral profiling or prediction?”


Answer:

No. The system does not predict behavior or infer psychological traits.


⸻


Q8: “What happens if components fail?”


Answer:

Failures reduce capability. The system returns to observation and denies execution.


⸻


Q9: “How is accountability maintained?”


Answer:

All state transitions, authorizations, denials, and non-actions are logged and auditable.


⸻


Q10: “Can this system act without a human?”


Answer:

No. Human oversight is always required for execution, and humans can only stop or authorize within bounds.


⸻


💰 GRANT REVIEWERS


Q11: “What problem does this solve that others don’t?”


Answer:

It prevents unsafe action by design, rather than relying on prediction, pressure, or training.


⸻


Q12: “Isn’t this overly restrictive?”


Answer:

Restraint is intentional. In sensitive environments, preventing harm matters more than speed.


⸻


Q13: “How do you measure success if nothing happens?”


Answer:

Success is correct behavior under uncertainty — including correct refusal to act.


⸻


Q14: “What evidence will you provide?”


Answer:

Audit logs showing state discipline, authorization enforcement, and correct handling of non-action.


⸻


Q15: “Is this scalable?”


Answer:

Yes. The architecture scales because authority is separated and deterministic.


⸻


🔒 HARD STOP QUESTIONS (THESE COME UP)


Q16: “Isn’t this just common sense?”


Answer:

Common sense isn’t enforceable. This system makes restraint structural and auditable.


⸻


Q17: “Who is responsible if something goes wrong?”


Answer:

Responsibility is traceable through logs. The system is designed to prevent single-point failure.


⸻


Q18: “What if staff disagree with the system?”


Answer:

Staff can stop the system. They cannot override its safety boundaries.


⸻


ONE-LINE CLOSE (USE THIS)


The system is designed so doing nothing is often the safest and correct outcome.
