NI-AI Doctrine — Compliance Summary (v1.1)
Non-Clinical, Non-Diagnostic Control Framework for Runtime Stabilization & Governance
Date: December 27, 2025 | Reference: EchoAuth TSW, Section VI — Compliance, Safety & Governance

Purpose
NI-AI stabilizes AI around human emotional state, identity, and purpose in real-time, integrating NI mapping (intention, urgency, cognitive load) with AI for coherence, safety, and trust. It enforces guardrails in systems like authentication or neurodivergent support, prioritizing human sovereignty.

What NI-AI Is Not

• Not diagnostic: No medical/psychological assessments or treatments.

• Not autonomous: AI subordinate to NI rules; no unchecked decisions.

• Not general-purpose: Layered governor for defined ecosystems (e.g., EchoAuth).

• Not speculative: Implementable with explicit failures and audits.

Core Runtime Flow
Layered end-to-end process:

1 Input: Multimodal signals (text, voice, behavior, context).

2 NI & ESE: Maps to patterns; generates ESP.

3 Coherence (C1–C5): Validates context (C1), emotion (C2), logic (C3), time (C4), purpose (C5).

4 AI: Guided reasoning/retrieval/generation.

5 SRK: Filters instabilities; rewrites/rejects.

6 EchoAuth: Verifies via RMS; escalates on low scores.

7 NSM: Adjusts for neurodivergence (shorter, simpler delivery).

8 Output: Tailored responses; logs metrics (ESP, RMS, scores, flags).

Safety & Identity Controls
Proactive brakes:

• Coherence: Pauses, clarifies on low scores.

• Emotional: Softens/simplifies for distress.

• Identity: Blocks high-risk on low RMS.

• Audit: Reviewable sessions for admins in regulated domains.

Deployment Tiers

1 Assist: Suggests improvements.

2 Co-Pilot: Rewrites, enforces rules.

3 Guardian: Approves high-risk actions with validations.

Auditability Statement
Generates traceable records; adheres to human-in-the-loop. Details in NI-AI Kernel Engineering Reference. Aligns with NIH/NSF guidelines for ethical, inclusive AI without clinical scope.
