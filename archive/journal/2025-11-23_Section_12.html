⭐ 12.1 Overview


The Sensory Overload Detection Engine (SODE) measures, analyzes, and predicts sensory stress levels experienced by neurodivergent individuals by combining environmental sensing, behavioral micro-pattern recognition, and NI–AI Fusion intelligence.


SODE detects rising sensory load before the child becomes overwhelmed, enabling timely interventions and preventing escalation events such as:

• meltdowns

• shutdowns

• elopement

• panic responses

• medical stress complications


No existing commercial system provides multi-sensory, context-aware predictive overload detection.


────────────────────────


⭐ 12.2 Inputs to SODE


SODE receives inputs from multiple sensory and behavioral channels:


A. Audio Inputs

• ambient decibel levels

• sudden noise spikes

• frequency patterns

• irregular or sharp sound bursts


B. Visual Inputs

• brightness intensity

• flicker frequency

• moving object density

• crowd motion


C. Environmental Inputs

• temperature

• confined or open space

• transition noise (hallways, doors, buses)

• event-based changes (bells, lunchrooms, sirens)


D. Behavioral Indicators


(Because sensory overload is not purely environmental — the child’s body signals it.)

• eye shielding

• covering ears

• increased stimming intensity

• breath irregularity

• posture tightening

• pacing acceleration


E. Contextual Inputs

• current environment (home, school, community, medical)

• transition state (moving between environments)

• historical sensory thresholds

• caregiver NI annotations


────────────────────────


⭐ 12.3 Sensory Signal Processing Pipeline


Step 1: Sensory Data Normalization


Raw inputs are normalized to environment-specific baselines.


Step 2: Sensory Intensity Scoring (SIS)


Each sensory channel receives a weighted intensity score:

• noise intensity

• brightness fluctuation

• motion density

• temperature load


Step 3: Sensory Volatility Detection (SVD)


Detects unstable or fast-changing environments, e.g.:

• cafeteria

• playground

• subway

• grocery store

• classroom transition


Step 4: Behavioral Correlation Module (BCM)


Behavioral signals are cross-matched with sensory load:

• if noise↑ + stimming↑ → overload likelihood↑

• if light↑ + posture tightening → overload likelihood↑

• if crowd motion↑ + pacing↑ → danger↑


Step 5: NI–AI Fusion Application


NI modifies AI predictions:

• “My child is sound-sensitive → increase noise weight”

• “Bright lights usually trigger shutdowns → adjust thresholds”

• “Crowds are okay outdoors but not indoors → context tuning”


Step 6: Sensory Overload Risk Index (SORI)


Output:

Numeric score (0–100) indicating sensory overload level.
