EchoAuth — Core Delivery & Evidence Architecture


Stage-by-Stage System Specification (v1.0 Locked)


Purpose:

To deliver a child-first, safety-aligned coordination system for ASD support that scales responsibly, preserves parental authority, enables professional contribution, and produces auditable evidence suitable for grants, pilots, and institutional adoption.


⸻


STAGE 1 — Portal Architecture & Role Separation


EchoAuth is delivered through three distinct portals operating on a shared child record:

1. Parent / Caregiver Portal — Authority & Consent Plane

2. Child Access Interface — Non-Verbal Access Plane

3. School / Clinic Portal — Professional Contribution Plane


Each portal exposes only the capabilities appropriate to its role. No portal can impersonate another. This separation is enforced at the API and data-permission level.


Outcome: Clear role boundaries without fragmentation.


⸻


STAGE 2 — Parental Authority Lock (System Invariant)


Parental authority is enforced as a system invariant, not a policy preference.


Only parents/guardians can:

• Grant or revoke access

• Activate or deactivate care tasks

• Enable medication schedules and alerts

• Approve recommendations into execution

• Change child permissions or routines


School and clinic users cannot override, auto-activate, or escalate actions.


Outcome: Authority remains intact at scale.


⸻


STAGE 3 — Child Access Interface with Flame Login


The child interface supports non-verbal and minimally verbal access using calm, familiar cues.

• No passwords, PINs, or textual challenges

• Optional state-based entry (Flame Login)

• Passive capture of interaction continuity

• No failure language or rejection feedback


The child is never framed as “logging in,” only accessing support.


Outcome: Safe access without pressure or exclusion.


⸻


STAGE 4 — Confidence Gating & Pause-on-Uncertainty


Access is governed through a deterministic confidence ladder:


Deny → Pause → Limited → Trusted → Verified

• High confidence allows normal access

• Uncertainty triggers a pause, not a guess

• Low confidence restricts or blocks access calmly


Invariant: The system never infers identity under uncertainty.


Outcome: Spoof resistance without child stress.


⸻


STAGE 5 — Shared Child Development Record


EchoAuth maintains a single shared development timeline capturing:

• Home observations

• School observations

• Clinical observations

• Routine participation

• Contextual events


Every entry is role-stamped, org-stamped, time-stamped, and context-tagged.


Outcome: One source of truth with preserved perspective.


⸻


STAGE 6 — Professional Contribution Without Authority Leakage


School and clinic users can:

• Document observations and interventions

• Propose supports and strategies

• Track goals and outcomes

• Upload records and plans

• Flag concerns


No professional input becomes active unless a parent approves it.


Outcome: Professionals remain effective contributors, not decision holders.


⸻


STAGE 7 — Records Vault (Reference Integrity)


The system supports secure upload and storage of:

• Doctor visit summaries

• Prescriptions (as documents)

• Care plans

• IEPs and school reports


Documents are stored for continuity and reference only.

They do not trigger automated conclusions or decisions.


Outcome: Continuity without medical overreach.


⸻


STAGE 8 — Medication Schedule Tracking (Care Logistics)


Caregivers can enter medication schedules based on existing prescriptions.


The system can:

• Track elapsed time

• Issue reminders (“next dose due in X minutes”)

• Log doses administered

• Flag missed windows


The system never recommends dosage changes or treatment actions.


Outcome: Safety and consistency without clinical decision-making.


⸻


STAGE 9 — Task Execution & Outcome Loop


EchoAuth supports a structured workflow:


Propose → Parent Approves → Execute → Log Outcome

• Professionals propose tasks

• Parents activate tasks

• Caregivers/schools execute approved tasks

• Outcomes are logged factually


Outcome: Measurable progress without authority confusion.


⸻


STAGE 10 — Audit Logging & Accountability


EchoAuth maintains immutable audit logs for:

• Access and viewing

• Uploads and edits

• Recommendations submitted

• Parent approvals or declines

• Task execution

• Medication logs


Logs are append-only and exportable for review.


Outcome: Trust, accountability, and institutional readiness.


⸻


STAGE 11 — System Logging Framework (Foundation)


All system activity is recorded as event logs, not interpretations.


Each log records:

• What happened

• Who performed it

• When it occurred

• Under which role and authority


No system log attempts to infer intent or correctness.


Outcome: Evidence without narrative bias.


⸻


STAGE 12 — Access & Authentication Logs


The system logs:

• Child access session start/end

• Confidence band reached

• Pauses and caregiver confirmations

• Denials and cooldowns


Outcome: Proof of non-verbal access safety and pause-on-uncertainty behavior.


⸻


STAGE 13 — Development & Observation Logs


Observation logs capture:

• Category (communication, regulation, routine, engagement)

• Role and organization

• Time and context


No scoring, diagnosis, or labeling is applied.


Outcome: Longitudinal development evidence without medical claims.


⸻


STAGE 14 — Recommendation & Decision Logs


Two immutable streams exist:

1. Professional Recommendations

2. Parental Decisions


No recommendation can execute without a corresponding parental decision log.


Outcome: Verifiable preservation of parental authority.


⸻


STAGE 15 — Care Task & Outcome Logs


For each approved task:

• Activation time

• Executor role

• Outcome notes

• Completion timestamps


Outcome: Real-world usage evidence for pilots and grants.


⸻


STAGE 16 — Medication & Reminder Logs


Logs capture:

• Schedule creation (parent-only)

• Reminder issuance

• Dose logged

• Missed dose flags


Outcome: Safety assurance without clinical interpretation.


⸻


STAGE 17 — Data Access & Audit Logs


Every data access is logged:

• Who accessed what

• When

• For how long

• Under which role


Outcome: Transparency, compliance, and trust.


⸻


STAGE 18 — Aggregated, De-Identified Reporting


EchoAuth can generate anonymized metrics such as:

• Engagement trends

• Task adherence

• Reminder effectiveness

• System usage patterns


No personal identifiers are included.


Outcome: Grant-ready reporting and scalable evaluation.
