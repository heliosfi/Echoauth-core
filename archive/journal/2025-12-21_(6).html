CLINICAL PORTAL — DOCUMENTATION & PROFESSIONAL ADVICE


Purpose (professional, plain)


The Clinical Portal exists to allow licensed professionals to document, advise, and support continuity of care for the child without turning EchoAuth into a medical system or shifting authority away from parents.


This portal supports coordination, not treatment.


⸻


1️⃣ Clinical Documentation


What clinicians can do

• Upload visit summaries

• Upload treatment notes (reference-only)

• Upload prescriptions as documents

• Upload care plans and recommendations


What this means

• Documents support continuity

• Documents do not trigger actions

• Documents do not auto-update routines


Professional framing:

Clinical records are referenced, not executed.


⸻


2️⃣ Professional Recommendations


What clinicians can do

• Submit recommendations

• Suggest supports or routines

• Reference observations from clinical settings


What clinicians cannot do

• Activate supports

• Change schedules

• Direct caregivers

• Override parents


Professional framing:

Clinical input remains advisory until parent-approved.


⸻


3️⃣ Visibility Into Adherence (If Approved)


What clinicians may see

• Whether approved routines are followed

• Whether reminders were acknowledged

• High-level adherence summaries


What they do not see by default

• Private family notes

• Caregiver-only logs

• Sensor-level raw data


Professional framing:

Visibility is limited, consent-based, and contextual.


⸻


4️⃣ Coordination Without Control


How clinicians interact with the system

• They contribute information

• They observe outcomes (as allowed)

• They refine recommendations over time


What they never control

• Child-facing programs

• Caregiver tasking

• Daily routines


Professional framing:

Clinicians inform care — they do not run it.


⸻


5️⃣ Privacy & Compliance Boundaries


Built-in limits

• Role-based access

• Parent-controlled visibility

• Audit logging of all access

• No clinical decision automation


Professional framing:

The system supports ethical collaboration without regulatory overreach.


⸻


6️⃣ What the Clinical Portal Explicitly Does NOT Do

• ❌ Diagnose inside the platform

• ❌ Prescribe or dispense

• ❌ Trigger medical actions

• ❌ Replace EHRs

• ❌ Enforce clinical protocols


Professional framing:

EchoAuth complements clinical systems; it does not replace them.


⸻


7️⃣ How This Helps the Child

• Care is more consistent across environments

• Fewer conflicting recommendations

• Parents understand guidance in context

• Reduced stress from misalignment


The child benefits from clarity, not control.


⸻


One Clean Sentence (Store This)


“The EchoAuth Clinical Portal allows professionals to document and advise within a parent-governed coordination system, supporting continuity of care without clinical automation or authority transfer.”
