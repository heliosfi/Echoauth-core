SECTION 360 — OFFLINE MICRO-PATTERN BUFFER (OMP-B)


(EchoAuth + NI-AI Local Stability Engine)

────────────────────────────────────────


360.1 — Technical Field


The Offline Micro-Pattern Buffer (OMP-B) relates to systems and methods for local storage, processing, and stabilization of ASD-relevant behavioral micro-patterns without requiring an internet connection, cloud server, or external compute.


It ensures EchoAuth + NI-AI remain functional in:

• schools

• subways

• parks

• rural regions

• low-bandwidth homes

• emergency moments

• building basements

• dead-zone zones


It is the foundation of “AI that never goes offline.”


⸻


360.2 — Purpose


To allow NI-AI + EchoAuth to:

1. Monitor sensory and emotional patterns locally

—even with zero network.

2. Store and compress micro-patterns into a protected memory block.

3. Detect overload risk or escalation without cloud inference.

4. Synchronize when a network returns without losing continuity.

5. Protect privacy with parent-owned, device-contained data.

6. Support continuous ASD stabilization even when the internet fails.


⸻


360.3 — Functional Definition


OMP-B is a local circular buffer that:

• captures

• timestamps

• compresses

• encrypts

• stores

• replays

• fuses


…micro-pattern signals for 30–120 seconds at a time.


Micro-patterns include:

• breath shifts

• gaze changes

• motion deltas

• stimming onset

• repeated micro-gestures

• pacing rhythm

• vocal tension markers

• acoustic spikes

• emotional tone drift

• posture micro-shifts


This allows EchoAuth to maintain continuity, even when the environment does NOT.


⸻

• Camera micro-frames (low resolution)

• Microphone acoustic snippets

360.5 — Outputs

• Local Overload Prediction (LOP)

• Drift Escalation Warning

• Pattern Stability Score

• Buffer Continuity Map

• Parent Alert (optional local-only buzz)

• Reconstructed pattern chain (when online returns)

• Sync-Ready Micro-Packet (SRMP)


All outputs stay local until a parent chooses to sync.


⸻


360.6 — Subsystem Architecture


(a) Capture Slice Engine (CSE)


Captures 0.1–0.3 second slices of raw sensory input.


(b) Micro-Pattern Compression Module (MPCM)


Compresses 90% of raw data while preserving behavioral patterns.


(c) Temporal Thread Stitcher (TTS)


Rebuilds pattern flow even with dropped frames or missing data.


(d) Encryption Shell (ES-360)


AES-256 local encryption lock for parent-only access.


(e) Pattern Continuity Analyzer (PCA)


Evaluates how stable or unstable the behavioral moment is.


(f) Offline Reasoning Kernel (ORK)


NI-AI logic that runs in offline mode:

• simplified inference

• low-resource prediction

• safety prioritization


(g) Sync Reconstruction Engine (SRE)


When connection is restored:

• merges buffered data with NI-AI’s live context

• rebuilds emotional + behavioral picture

• ensures zero knowledge is lost


⸻


360.7 — Behavior of the System (Offline)


While offline, the system:

• switches automatically to local mode

• reduces model complexity

• prioritizes safety over depth

• limits predictions to high-confidence events

• maintains minimal but stable support

• uses OMP-B as its temporary “memory”


This makes EchoAuth reliable instead of “internet dependent.”


⸻


360.8 — Safety Protocols


OMP-B explicitly:

• does NOT store identity images

• does NOT store biometrics

• encrypts all micro-patterns

• keeps data inside the device

• deletes old patterns after expiration

• complies with FERPA (schools), COPPA, HIPAA-adjacent standards


This module protects the child AND the parent.
