THE GOVERNANCE COMPASS


(One-page internal guide you can live with)


1. The Reality Check


Intelligence is not the bottleneck. Permission is.

If a system can act before authority and safety are resolved, it is not deployable in high-stakes environments.


⸻


2. The Core Invariant


Interpretation may occur. Action must be earned.

If certainty, authority, or coherence is missing, execution does not proceed.


⸻


3. The Safety Rule


Non-execution is a valid and often correct outcome.

Pause, refusal, and escalation are successes — not failures.


⸻


4. The Authority Rule


Authority collapses under ambiguity; it never degrades gracefully.

No transfer. No guessing. No “best effort.”


⸻


5. The Separation Rule

• Intelligence interprets

• Governance judges

• Order enforces

• Humans remain final


These layers must never merge.


⸻


6. The Identity Rule


The system must protect a person’s internal normal, not normalize them to the world.

Especially for children and vulnerable users.


⸻


7. The Drift Test


For any change, ask one question:


Does this make it easier for the system to stop, or harder?


If harder → reject.

If easier → allowed.

If unclear → pause.


⸻


8. The Extension Rule


You may extend surface area.

You may not alter the spine.


Interfaces can change.

Governance cannot.


⸻


9. The Teaching Rule


Teach boundaries, not beliefs.

Ask where the system stops.

Let others discover the gap.


⸻


10. The Funding Rule


Use only enough resources to validate safety without distorting intent.

~$150k is sufficient to determine whether the governance spine is safe enough to exist.


⸻


11. The Final Question (Always Valid)


If this goes wrong, can the system reliably do nothing?


If yes → proceed carefully.

If no → stop.


⸻


The Single Sentence That Holds Everything


AI should help humans understand, not decide or act for them — and when it is unsure, it must stop.


That’s the compass.


You don’t need to remember stages.

You don’t need to defend philosophy.

You don’t need to chase trends.


When something feels off, return to the compass.

It will always point you back to safety, clarity, and purpose.
