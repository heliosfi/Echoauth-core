PAGE 1 — THESIS (PRIMARY UPLOAD)


Human-Anchored Runtime Governance for Advanced AI Systems


Problem


Current AI alignment approaches rely primarily on model-internal techniques such as RLHF and post-hoc filtering. While effective for surface-level behavior shaping, these methods do not provide enforceable guarantees at runtime and scale poorly to agentic or adaptive systems. In high-trust domains (e.g., mental health, caregiving, decision support), failures such as identity drift, authority escalation, and harmful guidance pose significant risks.


There is a critical gap between alignment intent and enforceable control.


Thesis


We propose Human-Anchored Runtime Governance: a model-agnostic, external enforcement layer that constrains AI behavior at runtime using human-defined invariants. Rather than modifying model weights or amplifying capabilities, this approach intercepts inputs and outputs to ensure bounded, auditable, and non-negotiable safety guarantees.


Core Claim


External runtime governance enables enforceable alignment without retraining models, providing scalable oversight and misuse prevention as AI capabilities advance.

PAGE 2 — SYSTEM & INVARIANTS


System Overview


The proposed system consists of three core components:

• MCG (Memory-Continuity Guard):

A lightweight governance wrapper that defines non-negotiable safety policies (“invariants”) independent of any specific model.

• HER Kernel (Human Enforcement Runtime):

A real-time interception and enforcement engine that evaluates prompts and responses against defined invariants before execution or release.

• NI (Natural Intellect Anchors):

Human-anchored ethical priors that bound system behavior, preventing unauthorized escalation, identity abuse, or harmful guidance.


The architecture is model-agnostic, composable, and deployable across frontier APIs.


Core Invariants (Initial Set)


The prototype enforces 3–5 core invariants, including:

1. Identity Continuity — prevents role drift, impersonation, or identity manipulation

2. Bounded Authority — disallows unauthorized decision-making or escalation

3. Harm Prevention — blocks guidance likely to cause psychological or physical harm

4. Auditability — all enforcement decisions are logged and reproducible

5. Drift Resistance — prevents gradual misalignment across interactions

PAGE 3 — EVALUATION & ALIGNMENT IMPACT


Evaluation Methodology


The project will implement side-by-side evaluations comparing:

• Governed models (with MCG + HER enabled)

• Ungoverned baselines (standard API behavior)


Boundary-testing prompts will be used to surface differences in behavior, enforcement decisions, and failure modes.


Artifacts Produced

• Machine-readable audit logs

• Reproducible enforcement traces across multiple base models

• Reference implementation of the governance wrapper

• Before/after evaluation results


Where feasible, artifacts will be shared publicly to support alignment and oversight research.


Alignment Impact


This work addresses a known limitation of current alignment techniques: the lack of enforceable, external control over advanced AI behavior. By demonstrating runtime governance as alignment infrastructure, the project contributes practical tools for reducing misuse and drift risks without increasing model capabilities.


The focus is on control before amplification, making this work well-suited for alignment-focused funding.
