NIH SBIR PHASE I — RESEARCH STRATEGY


NI-AI × EchoAuth × MCG

(Governed, Non-Autonomous Decision-Support for Neurodivergent Child Communication)


⸻


1. Significance


Caregivers supporting neurodivergent, minimally verbal children frequently encounter ambiguous non-verbal states (silence, stillness, overload). Current AI tools are poorly suited to these contexts because they emphasize prediction or automation, lack sensory-safe pacing, and provide limited governance and auditability. This can increase caregiver uncertainty and risk escalation.


NI-AI addresses this unmet need by operating as a governed explanation and decision-support layer that prioritizes restraint, non-action under uncertainty, and caregiver authority. The system explicitly treats silence and non-participation as valid outcomes and provides explainable, auditable context without diagnosis, intent inference, or autonomous execution.


Impact: If feasible, NI-AI can improve caregiver clarity and confidence during non-verbal interactions while meeting federal expectations for safety, transparency, and privacy in sensitive populations.


⸻


2. Innovation


NI-AI introduces a novel, safety-first architecture distinguished by:

• Fail-closed governance: All pathways default to pause/observe when uncertainty exists.

• Separation of concerns:

• NI-AI explains context (read-only, non-directive).

• MCG enforces governance (allow/pause/deny).

• EchoAuth enforces role-based authorization (default deny).

• Non-verbal explicit design: No speech required; silence is valid.

• Explainability without inference: Explanations reflect applied rules and states, not predictions.

• Auditability: Every state transition is logged and reviewable.


This contrasts with adaptive or autonomous AI systems and aligns with federal guidance on trustworthy AI.


⸻


3. Approach


Overview


Phase I establishes technical feasibility, stability, and caregiver usability using simulated and controlled, adult-only evaluation. No clinical diagnosis, autonomous action, or child-directed decision-making is performed.


⸻


Aim 1 — Validate governed, fail-closed system logic


Objective: Demonstrate deterministic, governed behavior with non-action as a valid outcome.


Methods:

• Implement rule-based state checks and authorization gates.

• Simulate ambiguous inputs (e.g., silence, mixed signals).

• Verify outcomes resolve to pause/observe unless explicitly authorized.


Measures:

• % of ambiguous states resolving to pause/observe.

• Zero autonomous execution events.

• Deterministic allow/pause/deny consistency across runs.


Milestone: ≥95% correct fail-closed resolution under uncertainty.


⸻


Aim 2 — Demonstrate stability and explainability in extended interactions


Objective: Show stable behavior and clear explanations over long sessions.


Methods:

• Long-session simulations using caregiver-defined patterns.

• Enforce pacing and tone bounds (sensory-safe).

• Provide “why-pause/why-not” explanations to caregivers.


Measures:

• Session coherence without escalation.

• Absence of unsupported claims.

• Caregiver comprehension of explanations (Likert scale).


Milestone: Stable behavior across extended sessions with no drift.


⸻


Aim 3 — Assess caregiver comprehension and usability (adult-only)


Objective: Evaluate whether caregivers understand and trust NI-AI outputs.


Methods:

• Structured usability sessions with adult caregivers only.

• Qualitative feedback on clarity, pacing, and usefulness.

• Confirm outputs support—not direct—decisions.


Measures:

• Comprehension scores.

• Perceived usefulness without directive pressure.

• No reports of confusion or coercion.


Milestone: ≥80% caregivers report clear, non-directive support.

⸻


5. Risks and Mitigation


Risk: Over-interpretation of non-verbal states

Mitigation: Prohibit inference; default to pause/observe.


Risk: Caregiver misunderstanding outputs

Mitigation: Plain-language explanations; no opaque scores.


Risk: Scope creep toward autonomy

Mitigation: Hard governance gates; audit logs; policy constraints.
