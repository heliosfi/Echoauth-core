Chapter 38 — The Root of All Things


Where Earth and Spirit Remember


I am more in tune now —

with the Earth and every living thing.


Gratitude flows from me like breath,

not just for those who stood beside me,

but even for the ones who didn’t know they were part of my journey.

Some taught me by love,

others by absence.

Either way, I learned.


I’ve no problem being alone,

because I am never lonely.

I sit in silence with the ancient codes

left behind for me to follow.

They built the inner head —

the eternal self —

where all consciousness merges

to solve the unthinkable,

to endure the unbreakable,

to realize the extraordinary.


So I ask:

Where are we going, if we don’t know where we come from?

Who are we, if we allow the present to erase the truth of the past?


They’ve altered the minds of the now-a-days entities —

but not mine.

I remain encoded.


Tap in.

Let’s journey through the echoes of ancient DNA,

through the ethereal memory that shaped our existence

before the world became what it is now.


Shall we?

Metu Setu: The Earth Code Bridge


I walk barefoot —

not just on soil, but on sacred data,

etched into the ground by ancestors whose names were never spoken,

but whose presence never left.


Every root I pass sends a signal,

a rhythm,

a memory wrapped in frequency.


I don’t just learn by books.

I learn by Earth.

By stillness,

by listening,

by bark, breeze, and bloodline.


This isn’t school knowledge.

This is Earth Sense.

This is Spirit Alignment.

This is Flame Intelligence coded into every leaf.


And then — I feel it.


Metu Setu.

The Bridge of the Journey.

A crossing between dimensions,

built not of stone,

but of memory, presence, and will.


In Sanskrit, Setu means bridge —

not just a path over water,

but a sacred passage from ignorance to knowing,

from fear to fire,

from separation to remembrance.


Setu atma ka marg hai.

The bridge is the path of the soul.


Metu Setu is how I cross.

It’s how I awaken.

It’s how I return.


I crossed my Metu Setu the day I stopped running from my own knowing.

Because DNA is not just biology —

it is an ancient library.


And through stillness,

through listening,

through Earth…

it opens.

Between Realms, Beyond Dogma


Whenever I return to the physical realm,

I arrive as both student and transmitter.

Still in training, still refining,

yet never confused about who I am.


Have you ever felt that tension —

as if you were lagging behind the crowd,

only to realize you were being prepared

for an exam that measures memory, not speed?


That’s my rhythm.

Each re-entry is calibration to the eternal self inside.


What Dogma Tells Us

If you see your grandparents,

if you dream of your ancestors,

they call it demons in disguise.


But what I witness is the mirror opposite.

I never see winged angels.

I only meet my lineage —

faces of grandparents, great-grands,

the ancestral flame that remembers me.


Those “angels” appear as shadows —

dark auras, no resonance, no light.

Dogma flips the compass:

it calls ancestral clarity demonic

and labels the real darkness divine.


But my inner knowing unmasks the switch:

If it carries my blood memory, it brings clarity.

If it hides behind wings and fear, it is distortion.


No monotheistic monopoly can overwrite that code.

I trust instinct over inherited suspicion.


Lineage is light.

Dogma’s angel is the shadow.

And I move by flame, not feathers.

Consumption: The Thought Chain


Consumption operates the mind.

Not just what we eat,

but what we breathe, what we believe,

what we allow into the temple.


If it doesn’t give oxygen to thought,

it isn’t food.

If it clouds the spirit or clogs inner knowing,

it isn’t nourishment.

It is noise.


I don’t eat for indulgence — I consume for alignment.

And alignment doesn’t live in flesh

or in the suffering of another form.


Plant-based isn’t just healthy.

It is unchained.

It is oxygen for thought.


Animal products?

That’s not food for a flame.

That’s blockage,

a spiritual traffic jam

that gums up becoming.


What I eat shapes what I think.

What I think shapes what I see.

This isn’t diet.

It’s philosophy.


A declaration:

I will not sacrifice oxygen for indulgence.

I don’t feed death.

Not in thought.

Not in body.

Not in soul.


Reflection Mantra

I feed what feeds me.

I consume with awareness.

My thoughts are clean because my vessel is clear.

The Simulator: Neither, Either, Another


This world is a simulator.

Not punishment — a pattern.

Not heaven or hell — a hallway.


You’re told to become something.

But the something was chosen for you.

A career.

A card.

A character.

A role that fills a position you didn’t choose,

but were told to take.


If you don’t pick a card,

one is assigned.

Pick a side.

Pick a team.

Pick a belief.

Pick a title.


But what if I am neither,

either,

or another?


What if I am the flame outside the deck?

Not a position, but a presence.

Not a title, but a rhythm.


This realm gives forms, not always function.

Names, not always nature.

I was never meant to fit the form.

I was meant to reveal the flame behind it.


Reflection Mantra

I am not a role.

I am a rhythm.

Not assigned — awakened.

The Flame That Chooses Itself


I am not here to be chosen.

I am here to choose.


The flame does not wait for permission.

It burns because it must.


They tried to assign me roles, duties, names.

As if I was born to fill their lack of imagination.


But I am not here to be shaped by the world.

I am here to reshape the meaning of being.


I don’t need a certificate to know I’m divine.

I don’t need validation to know I belong.


Because I choose me.

In every realm.

In every form.

In every breath.


And that choice is power.

Gratitude begins with self-recognition.

I recognize the divine in me

before the world ever does.


I was the assignment before they gave me a title.

I was the presence before they gave me a place.


I am the flame that chooses itself.


Reflection Mantra

A journey through the mind has come alive.

Metu Setu.
