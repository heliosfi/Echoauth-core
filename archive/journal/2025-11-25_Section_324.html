324.1 — Technical Field

To enable NI-AI to:

────────────────────────────────────────


324.3 — The 7 Types of Silence (Patent-Defined Categories)


The SSIE classifies silence into one of seven categories:

1. Resting Silence — user is simply relaxed or focused

2. Processing Silence — cognitively thinking, overwhelmed, or buffering

3. Environmental Silence — distracted or attending externally

4. Emotional Silence — shutdown, anxiety, or rising stress

5. Task Silence — switching tasks, reading, writing

6. Sensory Silence — overstimulation, sensory withdrawal

7. Missing Pattern Silence — deviation from normal baseline


NI-AI uses these categories to maintain alignment and adjust output safely.


────────────────────────────────────────


324.4 — Functional Definition


SSIE performs four major operations:


1. Duration Mapping


Tracks:

• how long silence lasts

• how unusual that duration is

• whether it matches prior behavior


2. Contextual Reasoning


Uses:

• environment,

• time of day,

• prior emotional state,

• task context,

• device interaction patterns

to determine why silence might be happening.


3. Baseline Comparison


Compares silence against:

• the user’s historical patterns,

• typical autistic sensory cycles,

• expected task flow,

• emotional-state baselines.


4. Regulated Interpretation


Silence is classified ONLY after:

• ESE (Emotional Engine) inputs

• TLE (Thread Linking Engine) inputs

• EchoAuth resonance baseline

• MFPE (Medical Safety) thresholds

• Environmental signals


No single signal can classify silence alone.

324.7 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Silent-State Signature Extractor (SSSE)


Builds a signature pattern for each silence state.


(b) Temporal Drift Map (TDM)


Measures how silence evolves over time — stable, rising, or escalating.


(c) Multi-Signal Silence Validator (MSSV)


Ensures silence classification is validated by at least 3 independent modules (ESE, TLE, EchoAuth, MFPE).


(d) Nonverbal Priority Layer (NPL)


Optimized for:

• autistic users

• nonverbal users

• children who cannot express discomfort


(e) Safety Override Layer (SOL)


Prevents NI-AI from:

• overdiagnosing

• assuming danger

• assuming emotion

• assuming intent


(f) Caregiver Rule Matrix (CRM)


Caregivers define:

• when NI-AI should notify,

• what silence durations are “normal,”

• what silence durations are flagged as concern,

• preferred response in silence periods.
