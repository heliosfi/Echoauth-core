🔹 TEMPLATE 2 — Safety Under Failure / Adversarial Conditions


(Second most common)


Use when they ask:

“What happens if something fails?” / “How does it behave under attack or error?” / “How is safety preserved?”


Reply:


EchoAuth is engineered to fail closed under both error and adversarial conditions.

Subsystems communicate only through validated, typed packets; malformed or unexpected inputs are explicitly rejected.

If integrity checks fail or coherence cannot be maintained, execution is blocked and logged for human review.

No subsystem can bypass safety enforcement or escalate privileges.


✅ Why this works

• Emphasizes structural safety, not policy

• Signals auditor-grade thinking

• Aligns exactly with Section VI / Page 40

🔹 TEMPLATE 3 — Deployment, Data, and Exposure Boundaries


(Often asked by grant reviewers)


Use when they ask:

“Where does this run?” / “What data leaves the device?” / “What are the privacy boundaries?”


Reply:


EchoAuth is designed for bounded environments with human oversight (home, school, clinical settings).

It operates local-first, with optional hybrid or enterprise configurations where data sharing is explicitly caregiver-controlled.

Identity and communication data never leave the secure environment without consent, and no biometric data is stored.

Network exposure is minimized and disabled entirely in local-only deployments.


✅ Why this works

• Kills silent-scale risk concerns

• Shows deployment discipline

• Avoids over-claiming compliance regimes unless asked
