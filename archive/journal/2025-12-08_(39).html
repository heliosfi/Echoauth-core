(Required for federal review, IRB approval, and ASD safety alignment)

Page 11 defines the ethical, clinical, and operational boundaries that ensure:

• user safety

• data protection

• predictable system behavior

• legal compliance across child and disability frameworks

11.1 Ethical Foundations


EchoAuth operates under four primary ethical pillars:


1. Respect for Neurodivergent Communication


The system must honor:

• diverse expression styles

• non-sequential communication

• atypical patterns

• sensory-driven responses


EchoAuth does not force conformity to neurotypical communication behaviors.


2. Non-Exploitation Principle


EchoAuth cannot:

• collect data beyond its functional need

• repurpose data for unrelated research

• be trained on user identities

• be monetized through data resale

• use user activity to improve external AI models


All data belongs to the user, guardian, or institution per contract.


3. Predictability Over Optimization


EchoAuth prioritizes:

• stability

• consistency

• safety


The system must not generate novel behaviors if they reduce predictability for ASD users.


4. Informed Consent & Guardian Control


Every session requires:

• explicit guardian consent

• staff oversight

• no recording without consent

• full transparency of how EchoAuth interprets user actions


⸻


11.2 Clinical Constraints (ASD-Specific Safety Rules)


These constraints ensure safe operation for autistic users who may be:

• non-verbal

• low-verbal

• sensory-sensitive

• prone to overload

• reliant on predictable routines


EchoAuth MUST follow these clinical constraints:


1. No Unpredictable Outputs


The system cannot:

• improvise

• guess beyond boundaries

• generate emotional or personal advice

• produce text unrelated to user intention


All outputs must be:

• structured

• pre-validated

• clinically appropriate


2. No Visual or Auditory Overstimulation


EchoAuth must:

• avoid flashing/high-saturation visuals

• maintain consistent color and layout

• control audio volume

• limit animations to near-zero


3. Limited Interaction Time


Clinicians determine maximum safe duration (10–20 minutes).

The system must:

• enforce session timers

• alert staff before user fatigue

• enter “calm mode” automatically if overstimulation is detected


4. Mandatory Presence of Responsible Adult


EchoAuth must not operate with minors:

• unsupervised

• in private

• during crisis without staff


Guardian or clinician presence is required.


⸻


11.3 Data Collection Limits (Legal + Ethical Boundaries)


EchoAuth collects only what is required for:

• session continuity

• stability of the ORI Identity Kernel

• safety enforcement

• clinical progress tracking


Data EchoAuth Does Collect:

• session timestamps

• user interaction selections

• system outputs

• identity session vector (non-biometric)

• safety-trigger logs

• optional clinician annotations


Data EchoAuth Never Collects:


❌ facial images

❌ voice recordings

❌ audio streams

❌ GPS location

❌ biometrics of any kind

❌ academic grades

❌ medical diagnoses

❌ therapy notes

❌ private messages

❌ internet activity

❌ external app data


These restrictions guarantee FERPA/HIPAA compliance.


⸻


11.4 Data Storage, Access, and Retention


1. Local-First Storage


All data stays on the device unless:

• guardian consents

• admin exports logs

• IRB requires audit


2. Encryption Requirements

• AES-256 on device

• TLS 1.3 for transmissions

• zero plain-text logs


3. Access Controls


Only:

• guardian

• clinician

• pilot site admin


…may access logs.


4. Retention Schedule


Default: 30-day rolling deletion

Unless:

• clinician marks data for retention

• user/guardian requests export

• pilot program requires longer retention for analysis


No data persists beyond contractual obligation.


⸻


11.5 Prohibited System Behaviors


These must be enforced at engineering and policy levels:


System must NOT:


❌ attempt emotional diagnosis

❌ use camera input

❌ use a microphone for processing

❌ make predictions about mental health

❌ provide medical recommendations

❌ model long-term user behavior

❌ generate free-form conversational output

❌ simulate autonomy or consciousness

❌ make promises to minors

❌ collect hidden data


EchoAuth remains a structured, bounded assistive technology, not a general-purpose AI.


⸻


11.6 IRB & SBIR Ethical Compliance Summary


EchoAuth meets requirements for:


1. Minimal Risk Category (IRB)


Because:

• no biometrics

• no invasive data collection

• no physical interaction

• no behavioral manipulation

• predictable output behavior


2. Child Privacy Protections


Aligned with:

• COPPA

• FERPA

• HIPAA

• Section 508 accessibility


3. Assistive Technology Classification


EchoAuth is defined as:


“A structured communication support tool that enhances intentional expression and identity confirmation for autistic individuals.”


NOT a chatbot

NOT an AI companion

NOT a medical device

NOT an autonomy engine
