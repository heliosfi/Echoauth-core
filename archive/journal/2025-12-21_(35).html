EchoAuth — Onboarding & Operational Integration


Stage-by-Stage (Role-Integrated, Child-First)


⸻


STAGE 19 — Role-Based Onboarding Entry


Each role enters EchoAuth through a role-specific onboarding path, not a generic signup.


Parent / Caregiver Onboarding

• Simple account creation

• Child profile creation

• Consent + sharing preferences

• Selection of allowed programs (sensory, music, routines)

• Selection of who else can help (school, clinic, caregivers)


Goal:

Parents feel supported, not burdened.


⸻


School / Clinic Onboarding

• Organization verification

• Role declaration (teacher, therapist, nurse, clinician)

• Scope acknowledgment (observe, propose, execute approved tasks)

• Access request to child profile (parent approves)


Goal:

Professionals understand their lane before they act.


⸻


Caregiver Delegate Onboarding

• Invitation by parent

• Clear task scope (what they can do)

• Alert and reminder setup

• Confirmation of responsibilities


Goal:

More hands, less confusion.


⸻


STAGE 20 — Training by Role (Not One-Size-Fits-All)


EchoAuth training is embedded, short, and contextual.


Parent Training

• How to approve or decline recommendations

• How to enable/disable sensory programs

• How alerts and reminders work

• How to read logs without stress


School / Clinic Training

• How to log observations correctly

• How to propose tasks and supports

• How to track outcomes

• What not to do (no authority override, no diagnosis)


Caregiver Training

• How to respond to alerts

• How to log care actions

• How to escalate concerns safely


Key rule:

Training teaches what helps the child, not system mechanics.


⸻


STAGE 21 — Role-to-Feature Mapping (No Guesswork)


Every role sees only the features they need, clearly labeled.


Parents see

• Child overview

• Development timeline

• Recommendations pending approval

• Medication & care reminders

• Sensory/music program controls


School / Clinic see

• Observation entry

• Approved task execution

• Outcome logging

• Read-only records

• Secure messaging


Caregivers see

• Active tasks

• Alerts

• Logging buttons

• Limited child context (no admin views)


Result:

No one wonders “should I be here?”


⸻


STAGE 22 — Live Operation Flow (Day-to-Day Use)


This is what real life looks like:

1. Child engages with sensory/music program via Child Interface

2. Engagement is logged automatically

3. School logs an observation

4. Clinic proposes a support strategy

5. Parent approves it once

6. Caregivers and school execute it

7. Outcomes are logged

8. Everyone sees the same record


No meetings required.

No re-explaining the child.

No lost notes.


⸻


STAGE 23 — Safety Reinforcement in Daily Use


Safety is reinforced operationally, not just in policy.

• Any uncertainty → system pauses

• Any scope violation → system blocks

• Any sensitive change → parent confirmation required

• Any access → logged


The system guides behavior, not just records it.


⸻


STAGE 24 — Scaling “More Hands” Without Chaos


EchoAuth enables more helpers without increasing risk by:

• Delegated roles with limits

• Task-based contribution

• Clear ownership

• Shared visibility

• Immutable logs


This is how you scale care, not confusion.


⸻


STAGE 25 — Continuous Learning Loop (Non-Clinical)


The system supports improvement by:

• Showing what supports are used most

• Highlighting consistency gaps

• Surfacing engagement patterns

• Allowing teams to adjust with parent approval


No scoring.

No labeling.

No pressure.
