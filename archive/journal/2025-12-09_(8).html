PAGE 54 — Glossary of System Terms


Adaptive Continuity


EchoAuth’s ability to maintain identity, session memory, and interpretation stability across interactions.


ASD Sensory Envelope


The controlled set of visual, auditory, and interaction parameters ensuring no overstimulation for autistic users.


Coherence Engine (UMCE)


Layer ensuring that all outputs are logically, temporally, and semantically aligned.


Fail-Closed Behavior


A safety mechanism that halts system operations to prevent incorrect or unsafe actions.


Guardian Override


A privileged control channel allowing authorized caregivers to confirm, correct, or override system interpretations.


Identity Kernel (ORI)


Non-biometric signature generator responsible for maintaining stable identity without facial, fingerprint, or voice data.


NI-AI Runtime


A structured interpretation model built on templates, signals, and predictable reasoning patterns—not generative autonomy.


Safety Envelope


The total protective architecture ensuring user safety, compliance, and error prevention.

PAGE 59 — Engineering Notes (Reference Spine)


This section contains high-level engineering items CTOs and labs use during the onboarding process.


1. Known Constraints

• System never uses biometrics.

• All actions must pass UMCE coherence filters.

• Safety Envelope supersedes functionality.


2. Developer Assumptions

• Deterministic behavior preferred over generative inference.

• ASD sensory compliance is non-negotiable.

• Guardian Overrides must always remain operable.


3. Risks

• Overfitting NI-AI to narrow communication styles

• Insufficient sensory testing

• Deployment misconfiguration


4. Mitigation

• Template-based interpretation

• Strict UI/UX stability rules

• Hybrid deployment with local safety layer

• Centralized logging & audit trails


5. Future Extensions

• Hardware-integrated sensory monitors

• Multilingual NI-AI templates

• Expanded ASD behavioral models

• Interoperability with IEP platforms
