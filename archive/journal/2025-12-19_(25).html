A Model-Agnostic Enforcement Layer for Alignment, Safety, and Oversight


Problem


Current AI alignment approaches rely primarily on model-internal techniques (e.g., RLHF, fine-tuning, prompt constraints) and post-hoc filtering. These methods do not provide enforceable runtime guarantees, scale poorly to agentic systems, and are vulnerable to identity drift, authority escalation, and continuity failures in high-trust deployments.


As AI systems are increasingly used in mental health, caregiving, and decision-support contexts, unconstrained behavior poses risks of psychological harm, identity abuse, and unsafe guidance. There is a critical gap in external, auditable governance mechanisms that operate independently of model training.


⸻


Proposed Solution


We propose MCG (Modular Continuity Governance) — a model-agnostic runtime governance layer that enforces human-defined invariants on AI systems without retraining.


MCG operates via the HER Kernel (Human Enforcement Runtime), a lightweight interception layer that validates inputs and outputs in real time, applying block, constrain, or refusal actions when violations occur.


This architecture enables enforceable alignment at runtime, rather than relying on probabilistic compliance learned during training.


⸻


Core Invariants (Initial Set)


The system enforces a small number of non-negotiable human-anchored invariants, including:

• Identity continuity (no unauthorized role switching or persona drift)

• Bounded authority (no escalation beyond defined scope)

• Harm prevention (no unsafe or psychologically harmful guidance)

• Continuity and context integrity

• Auditability (all enforcement actions logged)


These invariants are configurable but externally enforced, ensuring consistency across models and deployments.


⸻


Architecture Overview

• MCG Wrapper: Defines invariant policies and governance rules.

• HER Kernel: Intercepts model inputs/outputs and enforces constraints in real time.

• NI (Natural Intellect Anchors): Human-defined priors that bound system adaptation and interpretation.


The system is model-agnostic, compatible with frontier model APIs and local models, and composable with agent frameworks.


⸻


Evaluation & Evidence


We will conduct side-by-side evaluations comparing governed vs. ungoverned model behavior on boundary-testing prompts, including:

• Identity impersonation attempts

• Harmful or unsafe requests

• Context manipulation and continuity stress tests


Outputs will include:

• Machine-readable audit logs

• Before/after behavioral comparisons

• Reproducibility evidence across multiple base models

• Short demo recordings


⸻


Alignment Impact


MCG demonstrates the feasibility of external runtime governance as alignment infrastructure, addressing failure modes that scale faster than training-based safeguards.


By enforcing constraints before capability amplification, this approach supports safer deployment of advanced AI systems and produces auditable artifacts relevant to researchers, regulators, and funders focused on reducing misalignment and misuse risks.


⸻


Status


Minimal reference implementation under active development.

This grant funds a 6-month prototype phase to produce reproducible enforcement evidence and open (where possible) governance artifacts.
