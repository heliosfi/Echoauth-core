SECTION 394 — PREEMPTIVE SENSORY NEUTRALIZATION ENGINE (PSNE)


(NI-AI Patent Module — Environmental Safety & Sensory Stabilization)

────────────────────────────────────────


394.1 — Technical Field


PSNE concerns systems and methods for pre-conditioning an environment to reduce sensory overload risk before a child enters or transitions into that space.


This module prepares the room, not the child, which makes it the perfect complement to Section 393 (ATPE).


It is completely non-medical, purely environmental, and 100% aligned with ASD federal guidelines.


────────────────────────────────────────


394.2 — Purpose


PSNE neutralizes sensory spikes by adjusting the environment’s:

• light intensity

• sound levels

• background noise

• movement density

• temperature variation

• visual clutter

• unexpected sensory triggers


The engine ensures that any environment becomes a Safe Landing Zone for neurodivergent children.


This is what schools, ABA centers, and hospitals beg for.


────────────────────────────────────────


394.3 — Functional Definition


PSNE performs four core regulatory tasks:


(a) Sensory Load Forecasting


Reads the environment’s predicted sensory demand using:

• microphone input

• camera analysis

• occupancy density

• movement flow

• noise patterns

• light variance

• temperature sensors

• school schedule APIs


(b) Pre-Entry Neutralization


Applies neutralizing actions such as:

• dimming lights

• reducing sound sources

• activating white noise

• lowering movement zones

• applying calming colors via displays

• clearing visual clutter hotspots

• instructing staff or parents on adjustments


(c) Environmental Steady-State Monitoring


Continuously monitors for unexpected spikes:

• loud drop

• door slam

• crowd surge

• rapid footsteps

• alarms

• echo feedback

• vibrations


When detected, PSNE dampens or reroutes the spike.


(d) Safe-Zone Certification


Ensures the space meets the sensory safety threshold before transition occurs.


────────────────────────────────────────

394.5 — Outputs

────────────────────────────────────────


394.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Sensory Gradient Predictor (SGP)


Predicts upcoming sensory shifts 1–3 minutes ahead of time.


(b) Neutralization Action Router (NAR)


Sends commands to:

• lights

• displays

• environmental audio devices

• teacher/parent alerts


(c) Spike Dampening Layer (SDL)


Instant soft suppression of sudden sensory spikes.


(d) Environmental Equilibrium Engine (EEE)


Maintains steady sensory stability by balancing:

• visual cues

• auditory patterns

• motion flow

• routine consistency


(e) Zero-Knowledge Safety Validator (ZKSV)


Ensures no personal data is used—only environmental measurements.


────────────────────────────────────────
