⭐ SECTION 370 — AUTONOMY-ESCALATION SAFETY LADDER (AESL)


(Patent Language + Mini-Claims)

────────────────────────────────────


370.1 — Technical Field


This invention relates to AI-driven safety systems for neurodivergent users.

Specifically, AESL defines a structured, multi-level escalation sequence that determines how and when an AI system increases support as a user’s autonomy declines.


It ensures:

• predictable intervention

• non-invasive escalation

• preservation of user dignity

• compliance with school, medical, and caregiver standards


AESL forms a tiered safety ladder that prevents sudden or overreaching AI actions.


⸻


370.2 — Purpose


AESL solves three major industry failures:


1️⃣ AI systems intervene too early


→ Removing autonomy, creating dependency, and violating ethics.


2️⃣ AI systems intervene too late


→ Missing emotional overload cues, leading to crisis events.


3️⃣ AI systems intervene unpredictably


→ Parents and schools lose trust.


AESL creates a patented predictable ladder where every step up corresponds to a mathematically verified risk level.


⸻


370.3 — Core Function


AESL receives the Autonomy Range Band (AR) and Autonomy Risk Index (ARI) from UARE (Section 369).


Using these two signals, it decides which of the 7 escalation tiers should activate.


⸻


⭐ 370.4 — The 7-Step Escalation Ladder (AESL-7)


This is the patentable heart of the system.


Level 0 — Passive Observation Mode


NI-AI watches autonomic signals without intervening.

Used when autonomy is high and stable.


Outputs:

• Silent monitoring

• Zero prompts

• Normal resonance tracking


⸻


Level 1 — Micro-Correction Mode


Small, invisible adjustments inside NI-AI:

• Simplifies future responses

• Reduces complexity

• Adjusts tone slightly


NO user notification.


⸻


Level 2 — Gentle Adaptive Support


NI-AI begins:

• Slowing pacing

• Repeating key instructions

• Giving clarity prompts


Still non-intrusive.


Example:

“Let’s slow down and take this one step at a time.”


⸻


Level 3 — Direct Stabilization Prompt


NI-AI becomes more explicit.


Examples:

• “Would you like a simpler explanation?”

• “Want me to break this into steps?”


User autonomy is still priority.


⸻


Level 4 — Escalation to Caregiver/Teacher Assist


Only triggers if:

• ARI crosses defined threshold

• User’s autonomy dips below A_low

• Emotional overload is imminent


NI-AI sends a soft alert to the parent/teacher dashboard.


Not an emergency — “early assist” tier.


⸻


Level 5 — High-Risk Intervention


Triggered only when:

• Multi-thread drift detected

• Emotional regulation collapsing

• Behavioral overload patterns rising


Actions:

• Real-time grounding script

• Visual calming panel

• Simplified command mode


This is the “prevent meltdown” tier.


⸻


Level 6 — Emergency Safety Tier


(Top of the ladder)


Used only when:

• Risk of elopement

• Panic behavior

• Medical distress

• Physical danger

• Loss of environmental orientation


NI-AI triggers:

• Caregiver emergency alert

• Location ping (if enabled)

• Safety script

• Sensory-reset mode


This tier is heavily regulated, audited, and parent-configurable.


⸻


370.5 — Internal Mechanics


AESL uses five internal subsystems:


(a) Threshold Comparator Engine (TCE)


Compares ARI to ladder thresholds.


(b) Escalation Layer Selector (ELS)


Chooses escalation tier (0–6).


(c) Cross-Thread Drift Detector (CTDD)


Checks emotional, cognitive, and contextual alignment.


(d) Advance Warning Predictor (AWP)


Predicts autonomy collapse 3–15 seconds early.


(e) Safety Gatekeeper Module (SGM)


Prevents premature escalation unless:

• multiple threads agree

• UARE signals confirm

• parent-approved ladder is loaded


⸻


370.6 — Outputs

• Current Escalation Tier (ET0–ET6)

• Parent/Teacher Assist Requests

• Real-Time UI Adjustments

• Drift Clearance Report
