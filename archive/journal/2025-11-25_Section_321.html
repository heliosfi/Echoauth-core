────────────────────────────────────────


321.1 — Technical Field


The NEMF relates to machine interpretation correction systems designed to prevent false-positive escalation detection in neurodivergent contexts, ensuring accurate emotional reading and avoiding harmful misclassification.


────────────────────────────────────────


321.2 — Purpose


NEMF ensures that NI-AI ONLY activates support protocols when the signs truly indicate:

• distress

• sensory overload

• shutdown trajectory

• risk states


It filters out:

• harmless stimming

• energetic play

• excitement

• rapid movement caused by joy

• irregular breathing from laughter


This preserves safety, prevents unnecessary interventions, and builds trust between parent, child, and system.


────────────────────────────────────────


321.3 — Functional Definition


The NEMF performs multi-level verification before labeling any behavior as concerning.


It runs:

1. Behavior Type Check (BTC)

Compares movement pattern to profile library of harmless vs. distress movements.

2. Context Correlation Check (CCC)

Cross-checks whether movement fits environment (park ≠ classroom response).

3. Emotion Validity Filter (EVF)

Confirms emotional interpretation with facial cues, posture, and pace.

4. Stim-Safe Registry (SSR)

Recognizes individualized stimming behaviors and marks them as self-regulation, not distress.

5. Non-Agitated Fast Motion Filter (NAFMF)

Distinguishes:


• running in joy

vs

• running in fear.


6. Pattern Duration Check (PDC)

Short microbursts of movement are not treated as escalation unless repeated or prolonged.

7. Cross-Thread Truth Verification (CTTV)

Emotion, motion, sound, and context threads must ALL agree before labeling distress.

321.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Harmonic Pattern Recognizer (HPR)


Detects joy-based motion patterns (loose, rhythmic, upward energy).


(b) Distress Pattern Recognizer (DPR)


Detects fear-based motion (rigid, abrupt, inward energy).


(c) Contextual Override Rule Set (CORS)


If environment explains behavior, distress is not assumed.


(d) Stim Normalization Engine (SNE)


Learns unique stims for each child and marks them as “stability tools.”


(e) Emotional Confirmation Layer (ECL)


Ensures that emotional interpretation matches physical signals before acting.


(f) Multi-Signal Consensus Engine (MSCE)


Requires 4 out of 5 signals to confirm negative state before triggering stabilization.
