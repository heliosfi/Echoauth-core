CDFI NARRATIVE — COMMUNITY IMPACT & BORROWER READINESS


Borrower: Nicholas B. Carty

Business: Helios F.I LLC

Project: EchoAuth – Caregiver-First Communication & Authentication System


⸻


1. Community Need & Problem Statement


Families, caregivers, schools, and clinics supporting neurodivergent and non-verbal children face a persistent gap:

• Communication systems often assume verbal interaction

• Authentication and safety systems are not designed for caregivers and children

• Existing tools prioritize efficiency over emotional safety and predictability

• Caregivers lack systems that respect stillness, silence, and non-verbal states


This gap disproportionately affects:

• Low-income families

• Children with autism and developmental differences

• Community-based schools and clinics with limited resources


The result is increased caregiver stress, reduced trust in technology, and missed opportunities for safe support.


⸻


2. Project Description


EchoAuth is a caregiver-first system designed to support non-verbal and neurodivergent children through:

• Role-based caregiver authentication

• Predictable, non-invasive interaction patterns

• Non-verbal-safe system design

• Default non-action under uncertainty

• Transparent explanations for caregivers


EchoAuth does not replace caregivers, diagnose children, or automate decisions.

It exists to support, not override, human judgment.


⸻


3. Community Impact


Direct Impact

• Improves safety and clarity in caregiver-child interactions

• Reduces stress caused by unpredictable or intrusive systems

• Supports inclusive access to technology for families with limited resources


Indirect Impact

• Enables schools and clinics to adopt technology responsibly

• Reduces technology-related friction in care environments

• Builds trust between families and service providers


Equity Considerations

• Designed with neurodivergent needs as a baseline, not an afterthought

• Prioritizes accessibility over complexity

• Aligns with community-centered values rather than extractive technology models
