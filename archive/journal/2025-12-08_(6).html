PAGE 42 — CLINICAL VALIDATION & PILOT STUDY PROTOCOL


(Federal Reviewer Standard — NIH/NSF/NIDILRR Aligned)

────────────────────────────────────────


1. Study Purpose


The Phase I pilot study is designed to evaluate the functional performance, safety, and therapeutic utility of the EchoAuth NI-AI Neuro-Support System among autistic and non-verbal individuals in controlled educational and clinical environments. The study will assess:

• Accuracy of intention decoding

• Reliability of identity confirmation

• Reduction in communication delays

• User stress indicators and sensory responses

• Caregiver and clinician workload impact


The purpose is not commercialization — it is clinical validation, establishing EchoAuth as a medically relevant, evidence-based assistive technology.


────────────────────────────────────────


2. Study Sites


Pilot testing will be conducted in collaboration with:

1. ASD Therapy Clinics (Applied Behavior Analysis, Speech/OT clinics)

2. Special Education Classrooms (IEP-structured settings)

3. In-Home Care Settings (with guardians present)


All sites will receive an IRB-compliant briefing, consent procedures, and simple training for caregivers/educators on system operation.


────────────────────────────────────────


3. Participant Profile & Enrollment Criteria


Target Enrollment: 20–30 participants


Eligibility Criteria

• Diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

• Ages 5–21

• Non-verbal or low-verbal communication profile

• Ability to engage with tablet-based AAC or visual grids

• Guardian consent + clinician supervision


Exclusion Criteria

• Severe visual impairment preventing icon-based communication

• Unmanaged sensory aggression preventing device use

• Inability to participate in structured 5–10 minute tasks


────────────────────────────────────────


4. Study Design (Mixed-Methods Approach)


The pilot will consist of four structured task phases, repeated across all sites:


Phase I — Baseline Assessment


Participants use their current communication method (gestures, PECS, device, or none).

Data captured:

• Time-to-expression (request/need)

• Accuracy of caregiver interpretation

• Behavioral stress indicators


Phase II — EchoAuth Guided Communication


Participants perform identical tasks through the EchoAuth interface:

• Need expression patterns

• Identity confirmation workflows

• Emotion or state reporting


Phase III — Sensory Response Evaluation


Assessment of:

• Visual load tolerance

• Predictability and timing comfort

• Stress reduction or increase


Phase IV — Real-World Simulation


Short activities requiring:

• Requesting a break

• Signaling discomfort

• Confirming identity

• Asking for a preferred item/activity


────────────────────────────────────────


5. Outcome Measures (Quantitative Metrics)


Primary Outcomes

1. Communication Accuracy

Target improvement: 30–40% increase vs baseline.

2. Time-to-Expression Reduction

Goal: 20–35% faster articulation of needs.

3. Identity Confirmation Reliability

Goal: ≥ 95% accuracy with NI-AI contextual signature.

4. System Stability & Safety

Measured via UMCE safety logs and fail-closed behavior rates.


Secondary Outcomes

• Caregiver cognitive load reduction

• Clinician satisfaction scoring

• Student engagement duration

• Decrease in behavioral escalation triggers


────────────────────────────────────────


6. Data Collection Methods


Quantitative Data

• System logs (timing, accuracy, stability)

• Behavioral scoring sheets (clinician-reported)

• Task-completion metrics

• UMCE incident/no-incident records


Qualitative Data

• Clinician interviews

• Guardian feedback surveys

• Participant responses (verbal/non-verbal cue observations)


Data Security


All data stored in encrypted containers, FERPA/HIPAA-aligned.

Identifiers separated from functional data.


────────────────────────────────────────


7. Safety Protocols (Mandatory for Federal Reviewers)


EchoAuth operates with:

• Fail-closed execution (no unstable behaviors)

• Guardian override channels

• No biometrics collected

• Local-first processing option (protected mode)

• Strict input boundaries through UMCE


Participants are monitored by clinicians at all times.

If distress is observed, testing pauses immediately.


────────────────────────────────────────


8. Success Criteria (Defines Phase II Eligibility)


EchoAuth will be considered clinically validated for Phase II if:

• Communication accuracy improves ≥ 30%

• Identity confirmation success ≥ 95%

• Stress indicators decrease in ≥ 60% of participants

• Caregiver/clinician satisfaction scoring ≥ 4.0/5.0

• System stability ≥ 99% uptime during sessions


Meeting these thresholds positions EchoAuth as a federally fundable assistive technology eligible for $750k–$1.5M Phase II awards.
