NEXT STAGE — HOW ECHOAUTH IS USED (IMPLEMENTATION)


EchoAuth is implemented as a coordination layer that sits around the family — not on top of the child.


⸻


Step 1 — Parent Setup (Once)


Parents define:

• approved routines

• approved content and activities

• approved caregivers and roles


This is done once and can be updated at any time by the parent.


Nothing else happens without this step.


⸻


Step 2 — Child Access (Ongoing)


The child uses a single, calm, non-verbal portal.


The interface does not change based on who is present.

There are no tests, prompts, or evaluations.


The portal simply reflects what the parent has already approved.


⸻


Step 3 — Adult Participation (Role-Based)


Adults interact with the system based on their role:

• Parents approve and modify rules

• Caregivers follow approved routines

• Schools support and observe

• Clinicians document and advise


No role can act outside its permissions.

No role can override a parent.


⸻


Step 4 — Context Support (Optional)


If parents choose, cameras or wearables may be used to provide context such as time, location, or routine status.


This information supports adult awareness only.

It does not trigger actions or interpretations.


⸻


Step 5 — Safety by Design


All actions are logged.

When information is unclear, the system pauses.

No automated decisions are made for the child.


⸻


What This Enables


Parents can accept help without losing control.

Adults stay aligned without constant communication.

The child experiences consistency across environments.


⸻


Implementation Line (LOCKED)


EchoAuth is used to keep routines, roles, and authority consistent across everyone involved in a child’s care.
