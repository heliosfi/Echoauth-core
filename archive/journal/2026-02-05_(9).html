1. Executive Overview

The EchoAuth system employs a "Structural Spine" architecture to manage runtime governance. Unlike probabilistic alignment models, EchoAuth enforces safety through non-negotiable hardware and software gates. This ensures that the system remains a passive tool of the verified caregiver, incapable of autonomous decision-making or execution.

2. The Triple-Layer Enforcement Mechanism

The architecture is divided into three isolated layers with strictly defined interface contracts.

I. Interpretation Layer (NIAI_01)

Function: Processes environmental and sensor data into standardized pattern reports.

 * Safety Constraint: The layer is "Non-Identity." It lacks memory, persona, or the ability to issue commands.

 * Enforcement: All outputs must use a non-directive schema. If the interpretation is ambiguous, the system is contractually required to report a "Conflict Flag" rather than a "best guess."

II. Judgment Layer (MCG_01)

Function: Adjudicates authority based on real-time human presence and role-based permissions.

 * Safety Constraint: "Resolution, Not Evaluation." The system does not ask if an action is "good"; it only asks if it is "authorized" by a verified human.

 * Enforcement: Under any condition of conflict (e.g., caregiver disagreement) or signal absence, the judgment deterministically collapses to a refusal state.

III. Execution Layer (CEG_01)

Function: The physical gatekeeper for all system actions.

 * Safety Constraint: "Single Execution Token." Only one action can occur at a time, and only when the gate is explicitly opened by the Judgment layer.

 * Enforcement: A mandatory "Stabilization Window" creates a temporal pause before execution, allowing for late-arriving overrides or signal loss to trigger a hardware-level WAIT state.

3. Critical Risk Mitigations

| Risk Category | Structural Mitigation | Outcome |

|---|---|---|

| AI Agency | Non-Identity Interpretation (NIAI_01) | AI cannot form intent or issue commands. |

| Unauthorized Action | Binary Judgment Contract (MCG_01) | No execution occurs without verified human presence. |

| System Drift | Immutable Invariants | The safety rules are "sealed" and cannot be modified at runtime. |

| Uncertainty | Deterministic Collapse | Ambiguity triggers a "Stop" state by default. |

4. Conclusion for Evaluators

EchoAuth is designed for environments where the cost of a "false positive" (unauthorized action) is unacceptable. By removing agency from the AI and anchoring all authority in the caregiver, the system achieves a level of auditability and safety that exceeds current industry standards for assistive technology.
