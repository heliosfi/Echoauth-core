🔥 Spec Paragraph — Conflict Flag & Manual Review Loop


Conflict Flag and Manual Review Loop.

In one embodiment, the EchoAuth system includes a conflict-aware safety layer that automatically flags high-risk or ambiguous authentication events for human review. During each caregiver-initiated check-in, the NI-AI engine evaluates a structured cue packet describing the child’s current behavior and environment. The engine computes (i) a categorical output label (“Safe” or “Needs Attention”), (ii) a confidence score, (iii) a continuous emotion/distress score in the range 0.0–1.0 derived from mapped danger behaviors (e.g., self-harm gestures), discomfort behaviors (e.g., covering ears, pacing), and environmental triggers (e.g., loud or crowded spaces), and (iv) an internal conflict score reflecting disagreement between internal reasoning threads. The system then applies explicit safety rules: check-ins with high conflict, elevated emotion/distress, or low-confidence danger signals are automatically marked as needs_review and stored with a “pending” review status in persistent storage. A separate review interface allows a caregiver or qualified reviewer to view flagged events (including the system’s final label, confidence, and emotion score), add human-readable reviewer notes describing what actually occurred, and update the review status to “reviewed.” Internal NI-AI thread details and conflict dynamics remain in private audit logs and are not exposed to the child-facing interface. This loop ensures that uncertain or potentially dangerous situations are surfaced to a human decision-maker, while the child continues to receive a single, stable, and coherent output per check-in.
