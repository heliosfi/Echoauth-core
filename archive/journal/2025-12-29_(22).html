EchoAuth — Parent Portal


Canonical Explanation (Authority & Governance)


The EchoAuth Parent Portal is the authority layer of the system.


It exists so parents or legal guardians can define, control, and govern all access rules without relying on caregivers, institutions, or automated decision-making. The Parent Portal establishes the boundaries within which EchoAuth operates and ensures consistency across every environment the child enters.


⸻


Purpose of the Parent Portal


The Parent Portal solves a control problem:


When multiple caregivers, settings, or schedules are involved, rules can drift. EchoAuth prevents drift by anchoring all permissions, routines, and access boundaries to parent-defined policy.


The Parent Portal is where:

• rules are created

• permissions are assigned

• roles are enforced

• boundaries are locked


⸻


What Parents Control


Parents or legal guardians can:

• Define what the child can access

• Approve or revoke caregivers and roles

• Set identity recognition elements (symbols, colors, objects, tones)

• Establish routines and allowed activities

• Determine confirmation methods

• Review access history and audit logs


All system behavior flows from these definitions.


⸻


What the Parent Portal Does Not Do


The Parent Portal does not:

• Diagnose or assess the child

• Analyze behavior or emotional state

• Provide therapy or treatment recommendations

• Automate parenting decisions


It is a governance interface, not a clinical or advisory tool.


⸻


Authority Model (Critical)

• Parents / Legal Guardians are the sole policy authority

• Caregivers execute within assigned roles only

• EchoAuth enforces rules exactly as defined


Authority does not change based on environment, caregiver, or time.


⸻


Safety and Accountability

• All changes to rules or permissions are explicit

• No silent updates or hidden modes

• Every access attempt is logged and reviewable

• If a rule is unclear or conflicting, access pauses automatically


This protects:

• the child

• the parent

• the caregiver

• the institution


⸻


Relationship to Other Portals

• Child Portal: Experience-facing, minimal, non-verbal

• Caregiver Portal: Execution-only, no authority

• Parent Portal: Governance, configuration, and oversight


Each portal has a distinct role. None overlap.


⸻


Core Principle (Lock This)


Parents define the rules.

Caregivers support execution.

EchoAuth enforces consistency.
