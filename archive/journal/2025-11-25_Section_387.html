SECTION 387 — SCHOOL AUTHORITY SYNCHRONIZATION LAYER (SASL)


(Patent Module for District-Level, Teacher-Level, and Classroom-Level NI-AI Safety Governance)


⸻


387.1 — Technical Field


The SASL relates to multi-node governance, distributed safety authority, and synchronized behavioral insights for educational environments.

It is the subsystem that allows NI-AI to operate inside:

• Schools

• Classrooms

• District networks

• Specialized support rooms (IEP, SEL, ASD programs)


…while remaining parent-dominant, FERPA/COPPA compliant, and non-autonomous.


⸻


387.2 — Purpose


SASL solves the core problem every school superintendent has:


“How do we use this system district-wide without compromising individual student safety, privacy, or parental authority?”


Therefore, SASL ensures:

• Teachers can see what they need

• Schools can coordinate without accessing sensitive private data

• Parents retain the highest override authority

• NI-AI remains safe, bounded, and predictable inside learning environments

• Local policies + federal laws are automatically enforced


⸻


387.3 — Architecture Overview


SASL uses a three-tier synchronization model:


Tier 1 — Parent Sovereignty Node (PSN)


The root of all permissions.

School access is always subordinate to parent settings.


Tier 2 — School Governance Node (SGN)


District-level settings that define:

• what classrooms can receive,

• which safety parameters are active,

• escalation routes for crisis detection.


Tier 3 — Teacher Interaction Node (TIN)


The classroom-level interface that provides:

• real-time alerts

• overload predictions

• escalation cues

• behavior stabilization prompts

• sensory-overload early warnings


Teachers do not receive emotional data, medical indicators, or private family information.

They receive only safety signals and educational cues.


⸻


387.4 — Core Functional Components


(a) Classroom-Sync Bridge (CSB)


A low-latency channel that synchronizes student states with teacher dashboards.

• No raw audio/video

• Only abstracted pattern signals

• Zero identifiable personal data

• Safe for school-wide use


⸻


(b) FERPA/COPPA Auto-Enforcement Gateway (FCEG)


Every transmission is screened for legal compliance.


Prevents:

• Student identity leakage

• Health-sensitive data exposure

• Emotional-state misuse

• Unauthorized export of classroom data


⸻


(c) District Override Ledger (DOL)


Records any district-level change to permissions.

Parents can view the ledger and revoke permissions instantly.


This ledger ensures:


NO school can change safety settings without parental visibility.


⸻


(d) Multi-Teacher Access Matrix (MTAM)


Allows:

• Special-education teachers

• Classroom teachers

• Paraprofessionals

• Speech or OT specialists


…to see just the layer they need — nothing more.


⸻


(e) Safety Convergence Engine (SCE)


If multiple teachers flag risk simultaneously:

• NI-AI cross-checks states

• Verifies the pattern

• Prevents false positives

• Routes escalation to parents first, school second


⸻


387.5 — Safety Features

• Parent remains Tier 1 authority

• No biometric data leaves the device

• Schools cannot aggregate or profile students

• Audit logs ensure transparency

• No student is ever “scored” or labeled

• Pattern-only data keeps NI-AI within safe, bounded use


⸻


387.6 — Outputs

• Classroom Safety Indicators (CSI)

• Escalation Path Recommendations (EPR)

• District Compliance Logs (DCL)

• Multi-Classroom Synchronization Threads (MCST)

• Teacher-Safe Alerts (TSA)


⸻


387.7 — Claims


Claim 387.1

A multi-tier governance system that synchronizes safety rules between parent, school, and classroom nodes without sharing personal data.


Claim 387.2

A compliance gateway that auto-enforces FERPA, COPPA, and district policies on each data transmission.


Claim 387.3

A classroom-synchronization mechanism that transmits only pattern-level abstractions, not raw sensory input.


Claim 387.4

A parent-first override ledger ensuring schools cannot alter NI-AI permissions without parental visibility.


Claim 387.5

A multi-teacher access system that provides role-specific visibility while preserving student privacy and emotional sovereignty.
