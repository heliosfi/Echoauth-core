⭐ PATENT SECTION 14 — EMOTIONAL STATE RECOGNITION ENGINE (ESRE)


(Core emotional engine; heavily patentable)


────────────────────────


⭐ 14.1 Overview


The Emotional State Recognition Engine (ESRE) determines the emotional condition of neurodivergent individuals using:

• micro-movement patterns

• stimming modulation

• breath signatures

• posture changes

• gaze behavior

• environmental context

• NI–AI fusion rules

• historical emotional profiles


This engine does not rely on facial recognition (avoids privacy + bias issues).

Instead, it uses body-based emotional indicators, which are more accurate for autistic children.


────────────────────────


⭐ 14.2 Inputs to ESRE


ESRE integrates the following signals:


A. Body-Based Emotion Indicators

• shoulder tension

• hand compression or squeezing

• foot tapping pattern changes

• pacing rhythm variation

• micro-freeze events

• rocking acceleration

• breath sharpness or irregularity


B. Posture-Based Indicators

• curling inward → fear/anxiety/shutdown

• leaning forward → hope/curiosity

• rigid spine → anger/frustration

• withdrawal posture → sadness or overwhelm


C. Gaze & Attention Indicators


(Non-identifying, no facial data stored)

• gaze drop

• gaze freeze

• avoidance

• rapid scanning

• closing eyes under stress


D. Sensory Context Indicators


Provided by SODE:

• noise spikes

• visual overload

• crowd density

• temperature discomfort


E. Behavioral Transition Indicators


Provided by BPE:

• pacing shifts

• stimming modulation

• sudden stillness

• intensity spikes

• repetitive purpose changes


F. NI Overrides


Parent or caregiver emotional knowledge:

• “He bites his sleeve when scared.”

• “She rocks faster when frustrated.”

• “He freezes before crying.”


This makes the emotional interpretation child-specific.


────────────────────────


⭐ 14.3 Emotional Signal Processing Pipeline


ESRE processes emotional data in five steps:


Step 1 — Emotional Cue Extraction


Extract micro-signals:

• movement accents

• breath shape

• posture angles

• motion frequency

• stress rhythm


Step 2 — Emotional Pattern Matching


Match cues to emotional templates:

• fear pattern cluster

• frustration cluster

• sadness cluster

• sensory-overwhelm cluster

• shutdown cluster

• confusion cluster


Step 3 — Contextual Correction Layer


Environment modifies emotional meaning:

• fear at home ≠ fear in school

• pacing at playground ≠ pacing near exits

• stillness in noise ≠ stillness in quiet


Step 4 — NI–AI Fusion Layer


NI input adjusts:

• thresholds

• emotional meaning

• false-positive reduction

• cultural interpretation

• personal history


Step 5 — Emotion Confidence Output


Final emotional state + confidence score (0–100%).


────────────────────────


⭐ 14.4 Emotional States Classified by ESRE


ESRE identifies eight major emotional states:


1. Calm / Neutral


Body relaxed, movement stable.


2. Curious / Engaged


Forward lean, stable rhythms.


3. Anxious


Tight posture, scanning gaze, stimming increase.


4. Confused


Slow processing signs, gaze drop, hesitation.


5. Frustrated


Rigid movements, breath spikes, rhythmic intensity.


6. Sad / Low Mood


Curled posture, slower stims, fatigue indicators.


7. Overwhelmed


Rapid signals from multiple channels.


8. Shutdown State


Stillness, minimal motion, breath flattening.
