SECTION 369 — UNIVERSAL AUTONOMY-RANGE ENGINE (UARE)


(Patent Language + Mini-Claims)


⸻


369.1 Technical Field


The invention relates to adaptive AI systems that modulate assistance levels based on user-specific autonomy thresholds. It specifically concerns dynamically calculated autonomy ranges for neurodivergent individuals, enabling safe, personalized, context-sensitive support.


⸻


369.2 Purpose


The UARE determines:

• When NI-AI should intervene

• When NI-AI should NOT intervene

• When support should escalate

• When support should stay passive

• How much autonomy a child has in each environment

• How quickly NI-AI should adjust during emotional overload


This solves the global AI challenge of over-support vs under-support.


And it ensures NI-AI is legally classified as a support system, not an autonomy-damaging AI.


⸻


369.3 Core Function


UARE establishes a continuously updated numerical band called the Autonomy Range (AR):


AR = [A_low, A_high]


Where:

• A_low = minimum safe autonomy

• A_high = maximum safe autonomy


The engine updates this band using:

1. Emotional State Engine (ESE) signals

2. Behavioral Fusion (NGOBE + MCBFE)

3. Environmental context (school vs home vs community)

4. Wearable signals or Zero-Wearable Mode

5. Parent/Teacher preferences

6. NI-AI doctrine safety rails


⸻


369.4 System Layers


(a) Baseline Autonomy Model (BAM)


Captures a child’s natural autonomy levels across environments.


(b) Contextual Autonomy Modulator (CAM)


Adjusts autonomy based on current environment + sensory load.


(c) Dynamic Intervention Threshold Calculator (DITC)


Computes when NI-AI should:

• offer help

• remain silent

• escalate

• notify a parent/teacher


(d) Autonomy Risk Index (ARI)


A 0–1 score that predicts loss of regulation.


(e) Support Action Selector (SAS)


Chooses the safest action allowed inside the autonomy range.


⸻


369.5 Outputs

• Autonomy Range Band (AR)

• Contextual Intervention Recommendation (CIR)

• Autonomy-Safe Support Action (ASA)

• Intervention Suppression Flag (ISF)

• Autonomy Escalation Signal (AES)


⸻


369.6 Mini-Claims


Claim 369.1


A system that computes personalized autonomy ranges using emotional, behavioral, and contextual data.


Claim 369.2

A contextual autonomy modulation mechanism that adjusts user autonomy based on environment-specific sensory load.


Claim 369.4


An autonomy risk prediction model producing real-time intervention thresholds.


Claim 369.5


A safety system suppressing or enabling AI actions based on user-specific autonomy bounds.
