⭐ PAGE 25 — COMMERCIALIZATION ROADMAP (FULLY COMPLETED)


EchoAuth’s commercialization strategy is structured across three sequential phases—Prototype Validation, Early Revenue through Pilot Contracts, and National Scaling. Each phase reflects federal expectations for SBIR Phase I → Phase II progression, ASD clinic adoption, and school district procurement guidelines.


The roadmap is intentionally conservative, measurable, and grounded in real deployment pathways.


⸻


25.1 Phase I — Prototype Completion & Validation (Months 0–6)


Goal: Establish a clinically defensible, safe, predictable system that proves feasibility in ASD environments.


Deliverables:

• Fully functional EchoAuth MVP

• ASD sensory-optimized interface

• ORI Identity Kernel with non-biometric signature

• NI-AI interpretation and communication templates

• Coherence Engine + safety wrapper (fail-closed behavior)

• Testing results from 20–30 ASD users

• Pilot readiness certification

• Provisional patent filed


Revenue: None expected during this phase (pure validation).


Funding: Microloans + SBIR Phase I + foundation grants.


⸻


25.2 Phase II — Pilot Deployment & Early Revenue (Months 6–18)


Goal: Convert pilots into paying early adopters while collecting efficacy data.


Pilot Pricing:

• Schools: $5,000–$15,000 per site (annual)

• ASD Clinics: $3,000–$10,000 per deployment

• Home subscription tier: $20–$40 per month


Expected Year 1 Revenue Range:

$30,000–$80,000 (conservative)


Pilot Deliverables:

• MOUs with 2–5 schools

• MOUs with 1–3 ASD clinics

• IEP integration templates

• Caregiver training modules

• Pilot evaluation data

• Federal reporting packet (for Phase II submission)


Key Validation Metrics:

• 30–40% improvement in communication accuracy

• 95% identity confirmation reliability

• High sensory-tolerance scores

• Zero drift or safety incidents


This data is CRITICAL for scaling.


⸻


25.3 Phase III — Phase II Scaling & Institutional Partnerships (Months 18–36)


Goal: Transform EchoAuth from a pilot-ready tool into a scalable service for institutions nationwide.


Primary Activities:

• Expand engineering team (2–3 developers)

• Introduce cloud-assisted but privacy-protected workflow

• Roll out multi-user classroom dashboards

• Complete full patent filing

• Conduct larger clinical studies (100–200 participants)

• Engage district procurement boards

• Integrate with Medicaid waiver programs


Projected Phase II Funding:

$1,000,000–$1,500,000 non-dilutive (typical SBIR Phase II range)


Expected Year 2–3 Revenue:

$200,000–$600,000 (pilot expansions + subscriptions)


⸻


25.4 Market Entry Strategy (Institution-Focused)


EchoAuth will enter the market through three high-traction pathways:


1. ASD Clinics (fastest adoption cycle)

• Immediate clinical utility

• Lower bureaucratic barriers

• High demand for communication stabilizers

• Willingness to test new assistive tech


2. School Districts (largest scale potential)

• Aligns with special education mandates

• Strong funding streams: IDEA, ESSER, Title I, local grants

• Multi-year contracts


3. Families (long-term subscription base)

• Lower monthly cost

• High lifetime value

• Enables system familiarity outside clinic/school


This tri-path strategy accelerates adoption while diversifying revenue.


⸻


25.5 Competitive Advantage (Reviewer-Grade)


EchoAuth is differentiated by:

1. NI-AI Cognitive Interpretation (not a chatbot)

Predictable communication engine tuned to ASD behavior patterns.

2. Non-Biometric Identity Kernel

Safer for children and compliant with FERPA/COPPA.

3. Fail-Closed Safety Architecture

Zero unpredictable outputs — essential for vulnerable populations.

4. ASD-Specific UI & Sensory Design

Purpose-built for low stimulation and high-confidence use.

5. Dual Environment Capability

Works in both clinical and educational contexts.

6. Expandable to Other Neurodivergent Populations

ADHD, apraxia, selective mutism, cognitive delay, traumatic brain injury, etc.

25.7 Commercialization Risks & Mitigation (Required by Grant Reviewers)


Risk 1 — Slow institutional adoption


Mitigation: Start with ASD clinics → prove results → leverage clinical endorsements.


Risk 2 — Funding gaps between pilots


Mitigation: Subscription tier maintains steady revenue.


Risk 3 — UI sensory rejection


Mitigation: Continuous ASD-led UX testing with sensory-stability scoring.


Risk 4 — Compliance hurdles


Mitigation: Align early with FERPA/HIPAA/COPPA from prototype stage.
