MASTER NARRATIVE (ONE PAGE)


EchoAuth


What problem it solves


When multiple adults are involved in a child’s care, inconsistency happens—not from bad intent, but from lack of coordination. Routines drift, rules are applied differently, and the child absorbs that instability.


What EchoAuth is


EchoAuth is authority-gated coordination infrastructure for families and care teams.

It is not medical, not diagnostic, and not behavioral analysis.


How it works (plain)

• Parents approve once: routines, access, and roles.

• The child experience stays consistent via a calm, non-verbal portal—no matter who is present.

• Adults act by role: parents approve; caregivers follow; schools support; clinicians advise.

• Context is optional and limited: cameras/wearables provide time/place/routine context only—no interpretation.

• When something is unclear, the system pauses instead of guessing.


Why this is different


Most tools observe, analyze, or message. EchoAuth enforces coordination—who can do what, when, and under which approvals.


Why this helps the child


Consistency reduces stress. Clear boundaries prevent harm. Fewer guesses mean fewer disruptions.


One-line close


EchoAuth keeps everyone aligned, so the child doesn’t have to adapt to every adult.
