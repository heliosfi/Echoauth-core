Section 351 — Nonverbal Signal Intake Encoder (NSIE)


351.1 — Title


Nonverbal Signal Intake Encoder (NSIE)


351.2 — Technical Field


Computer-implemented system and device-mic interaction layer for encoding observable nonverbal and behavioral signals into standardized, non-clinical, parent-authorized data packets readable by the NI-AI kernel for coherence-preserving interpretation and cross-environment alignment.


351.3 — Core Problem


Autistic and nonverbal children express internal states through physical and temporal behavioral signals that are routinely:

• ignored or misread by caregivers and institutions,

• interpreted as clinical symptoms instead of communication,

• recorded in fragmented or conflicting formats across home, school, and medical environments.


This breaks trust and delays intervention.


351.4 — Solution Summary


NSIE introduces a structured encoding layer that:

✔ captures behavioral stims and expressive micro-actions,

✔ converts them into secure vector packets,

✔ flags only overload/focus, not medical conditions,

✔ remains exclusively controlled by parental override and user consent.


351.5 — Inputs Captured


NSIE encodes:

• movement clusters (fast/slow/action amplitude)

• stim rhythm signatures

• posture positional arcs

• eye-pattern scan vectors

• proximity pacing vectors

• silence-to-expression gradients

• caregiver override command flags


351.6 — Internal Functional Modules


(a) Stim-Rhythm Encoder (SRE)

Encodes stimming cadence into numeric rhythm hashes.


(b) Micro-Action Vectorizer (MAV)

Converts small gestures into time-stamped motion vectors.


(c) Posture Arc Encoder (PAE)

Encodes body position orientation safely for NI intake.


(d) Silence-Gradient Interpreter (SGI)

Numerically encodes silence as a communication signal, not “absence.”


(e) Parental Rail Compliance Gate (PRCG)

Ensures all output remains under parent authority.


351.7 — System Outputs


NSIE emits:

• Nonverbal Vector Packet (NVP)

• Timing Coherence Mark (TCM)

• Parent Override Compliance flag (POC_flag)

• Sync-ready hashed intake vector (SHIV)

• Encrypted Behavior Intake Event container (EBIE-container)


351.8 — Safety & Compliance


NSIE is:

• non-clinical,

• informational only,

• parent-authorized,

• encrypted on intake,

• FERPA/COPPA/HIPAA compliant on packet level,

• compatible with phone-only and wearable sensor sources.


351.9 — Claims


Claim 351.1 (Independent)

A bounded reasoning intake-encoding system for encoding nonverbal behavioral signals into parent-authorized emotional, cognitive, or sensory vector packets.


Claim 351.2 (Dependent)

The system of Claim 351.1, wherein stimulus-originated silent or motion-based cues are encoded numerically without assigning medical labels.


Claim 351.3 (Independent)

A parent-override enforcement gate that ensures encoded signals do not recursively self-optimize or cross-interpret into clinical diagnosis.


Claim 351.4 (Dependent)

The system of Claim 351.3, wherein output packets are encrypted and stored in minimal form via caregiver consent-bound retention constraints.
