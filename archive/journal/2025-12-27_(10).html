Appendix X: Crossroad Execution Gate (CEG)


(Runtime Execution Control within the MCG Spine)


Overview


The Crossroad Execution Gate (CEG) is a deterministic runtime control embedded within the Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) spine of the EchoAuth / NI-AI system. Its sole function is to regulate when approved actions may execute, not what actions are chosen. The CEG enforces sequencing, temporal safety, and identity-aligned authorization to prevent premature, cascading, or unsafe system outputs.


⸻


Functional Role


The CEG operates after MCG judgment and before system execution.

• MCG determines whether an action is permissible in principle.

• CEG determines whether the action is permitted at the current moment.


The CEG does not generate decisions, assess users, infer mental states, or adapt goals. It functions strictly as an execution-timing gate.


⸻


Core Mechanisms


1. Turn Authority (CEG Core)


A centralized, non-learning control that enforces a single rule:


No subsystem executes unless explicitly released.


All inbound subsystem requests (NI/AI processing, emotional adaptation, external input handling, identity verification) must wait until called forward.


⸻


2. MCG Signal Check


Verifies that the requested action:

• Aligns with governance axioms

• Respects role and authority boundaries

• Does not violate system invariants


Actions failing this check are blocked; actions passing may still be delayed.


⸻


3. Sequence Gate


Enforces correct execution order. Even valid actions are paused if they arrive out of sequence, preventing race conditions and reaction chaining.


⸻


4. Temporal Safety Window


Assesses system readiness (e.g., signal stability, contextual settling). If conditions are unstable, execution is delayed rather than rejected.


⸻


5. Execution Release


Only after all prior checks pass does the CEG release the action to the execution layer.

Safety & Compliance Positioning

• Non-autonomous: The CEG cannot initiate actions.

• Non-diagnostic: No clinical or psychological inference.

• Human-aligned: Supports pause, delay, and escalation rather than automated response.

• Audit-ready: All releases are loggable and reviewable.


This structure aligns with NIH expectations for responsible AI, risk mitigation, and human-in-the-loop governance without introducing autonomous agency.


⸻


Key Distinction for Reviewers


The CEG does not decide what to do.

It decides when doing is allowed.


This separation is fundamental to system safety.
