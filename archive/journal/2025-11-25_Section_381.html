381.1 — Technical Field


The Dynamic Threshold Stabilization Engine (DTSE) relates to adaptive safety threshold management in AI systems, particularly for neuro-sensitive contexts such as ASD support.

DTSE continuously adjusts emotional, sensory, cognitive, and predictive thresholds to maintain stable interaction and prevent unsafe escalation.


This module acts as the second-by-second stabilizer underneath the Safety Locus Matrix.


⸻


381.2 — Purpose


DTSE answers this question:


“How do we keep the system stable even when the user, environment, or device suddenly changes?”


It performs:

• Adaptive threshold tuning

• Multi-signal stability balancing

• Preventing overload spikes

• Preventing false-positive safety triggers

• Preventing false-negative safety misses

• Keeping safety thresholds optimal under any condition


It ensures NI-AI never becomes unstable, overly sensitive, or slow to react.

DTSE is a dynamic regulator that:
