What Engineers to Hire (Tailored to Your Tiny Team + EchoAuth’s Needs)

Keep it lean: Stick to the assumption of 1 full-time beast + 1 part-time ML brain. Focus on folks who grok Python/FastAPI (your stack), have hardware/ML vibes (sensors + HER kernel), and aren’t allergic to safety-critical code (autism support means zero tolerance for flaky shit). Prioritize remote US/EU for timezone sanity and ethics alignment—offshore can work for cost, but vet harder for HIPAA-adjacent compliance.

1 Full-Stack Engineer (The Workhorse – 100% time, Weeks 1–12)

◦ Profile: Mid-senior (3–7 years exp), Python ninja with FastAPI/Flask, Docker/CI-CD chops, and bonus for BLE/hardware integration (e.g., ESP32 or Empatica SDKs). They own Phases 1–2 + 4: Sensors swap, API hardening, governance rules, and that React dashboard.

◦ Where to Find ’Em:

▪ Freelance: Upwork/Toptal (filter for “Python FastAPI + hardware” gigs; aim for 4.8+ stars, 100+ hours logged).

▪ Full-time/contract: LinkedIn (search “full-stack Python engineer remote” + “healthtech” keywords), or Hired/Wellfound for startup vibes.

▪ Red Flags: No GitHub with open-source contribs? Pass. No experience with structured logging or env vars? Next.

◦ Why This One First: They unblock everything—your scaffold runs today, so they can ship PRs Day 1 (Docker + logging swaps).

2 ML/Research Intern (The HER Wizard – 20–30 hrs/week, Starts Week 4)

◦ Profile: Grad student or early-career (1–3 years), PyTorch/scikit-learn pro, with a thesis or project in biosignals (EDA/HR for stress detection) or autism-related datasets. They tackle Phase 3: Dataset curation, LSTM training, and that hybrid ML fallback. Keep it ethical—bonus if they’ve got IRB experience.

◦ Where to Find ’Em:

▪ Universities: Post on Handshake or reach out to CS/ML depts at Stanford/MIT/UC (your NI-AI manual’s safety angle is catnip).

▪ Platforms: Intern-specific like Parker Dewey or LinkedIn (query “ML intern biosignals remote”).

▪ Pro Tip: Offer equity stipend (1–2%) + mentorship shoutouts for their portfolio—turns it into a win-win.

◦ Why Part-Time: Weeks 4–10 only; they ramp on simulated data first, no rush.

If budget’s tight solo-start, freelance the full-stack first (they can solo Phase 1), then layer in the intern. Total headcount: 2. Scale to 3 if grants hit early.
