Appendix E — Alert & Reminder Feature Rules


(Care-Safe, Non-Interpretive, Parent-Governed)


Purpose


Define exactly what alerts exist, who receives them, and what alerts are never allowed to say or do, ensuring reminders support care without pressure, diagnosis, or authority leakage.


⸻


E1. Alert Categories (Allowed)


1) Medication & Care Timing

• “Next dose due in 15 minutes.”

• “Last dose logged 6 hours ago.”

• “Dose window missed — please check.”


Notes

• Timing-only.

• Based solely on caregiver-entered schedules.

• No guidance on dose changes.


⸻


2) Routine & Task Reminders

• “Routine start window open.”

• “Approved task scheduled for today.”

• “Task completion not yet logged.”


Notes

• Tasks must be parent-approved.

• Neutral language only.


⸻


3) Access & Safety Pauses

• “Session paused — caregiver confirmation needed.”

• “Access limited due to uncertainty.”


Notes

• Calm, factual.

• No blame language.


⸻


4) Administrative & Coordination

• “New recommendation pending review.”

• “Document uploaded.”

• “Access request awaiting approval.”


Notes

• Informational only.


⸻


E2. Alert Recipients (Strict Routing)


Alert Type Parent Caregiver School Clinic Child

Medication Timing ✅ ✅ ◻️ (if approved) ◻️ (if approved) ❌

Routine/Task ✅ ✅ ◻️ (approved tasks only) ◻️ ❌

Access Pause ✅ ◻️ ❌ ❌ ❌

Recommendations ✅ ❌ ◻️ ◻️ ❌

Admin/Docs ✅ ❌ ◻️ ◻️ ❌


◻️ = parent-approved scope only


Invariant:


No alert is ever delivered to the child.


⸻


E3. Language Rules (Non-Negotiable)


Alerts MUST be:

• factual

• time-based

• neutral in tone

• short and predictable


Alerts MUST NOT:

• recommend actions (“increase/decrease,” “skip,” “change”)

• imply fault (“missed,” “failed” without “please check”)

• infer causes or conditions

• suggest urgency beyond time (“urgent,” “critical”)

• include clinical interpretation


⸻


E4. Whitelisted Templates (Execution Control)


All alerts are generated from pre-approved templates (IDs), not free text.


Examples:

• MED_NEXT_DUE_X_MIN

• MED_WINDOW_MISSED_CHECK

• TASK_PENDING_TODAY

• SESSION_PAUSED_CONFIRM


No custom alert text can be authored by users or professionals.


Why: Prevents drift into advice or pressure.


⸻


E5. Frequency & Escalation Limits

• No alert storms

• Minimum cool-down between repeats

• Escalation requires parent configuration

• Default behavior: notify once, then wait


⸻


E6. Parent Governance


Parents control:

• which alert categories are enabled

• who receives alerts

• time windows (quiet hours)

• immediate disable at any time


⸻


E7. Logging & Audit


Every alert logs:

• template ID

• recipient role

• timestamp

• delivery status (sent/seen)

• linked event (schedule/task/session)


Logs are append-only and auditable.


⸻


E8. Safety Fallback


If alert logic encounters ambiguity:

• Do not send

• Do not infer

• Do not escalate

• Record a system event and wait


⸻


Appendix E Seal


EchoAuth alerts are factual, time-based, and parent-governed. They inform without instructing, support without pressuring, and pause instead of guessing.
