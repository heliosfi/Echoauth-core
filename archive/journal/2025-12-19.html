✅ STAGE 3 — HARD QUESTIONS (LENDER / NON-SBA)


1️⃣ “Why YOU? Why this team?”


✅ ANSWER (Monique leads, you anchor if needed)


“This problem sits at the intersection of caregiving, safety, and system design.

We’re not approaching it as a generic tech product — we’re building it from lived exposure to caregiver gaps and compliance realities.

Our advantage is that EchoAuth was designed governance-first, not feature-first.”


STOP.


❌ DO NOT SAY

• “We’re passionate”

• “There’s nothing like it”

• Personal hardship stories

• “AI will solve this”


⸻


2️⃣ “What if the pilot doesn’t work?”


✅ ANSWER


“Then the funding has still done its job.

This phase is explicitly to validate safety, usability, and adoption assumptions.

The system is modular — failed assumptions don’t collapse the architecture.”


This shows risk literacy, not fear.


⸻


3️⃣ “How do you make money eventually?”


✅ ANSWER


“Post-pilot, the model is institution- and caregiver-based licensing.

But revenue is intentionally not the focus before validation — safety and compliance come first.”


❌ DO NOT SAY

• Subscription details

• Pricing tiers

• Government reimbursement yet


⸻


4️⃣ “Why not grants instead?”


✅ ANSWER


“We are pursuing grants in parallel.

However, grant timelines are slow and restrictive.

Non-SBA capital allows us to complete the prototype and pilots that actually strengthen future grant applications.”


This frames funding as leverage, not avoidance.


⸻


5️⃣ “Why non-SBA specifically?”


✅ ANSWER


“SBA products are better once revenue or fixed operations exist.

At this stage, we need flexibility to refine safety-critical components without operational constraints.”


Short. Calm. Done.


⸻


6️⃣ “What stops someone bigger from copying this?”


✅ ANSWER


“The defensibility is not just technical — it’s architectural and governance-based.

EchoAuth’s control layer is what enables use in regulated environments.

That’s not something companies can bolt on after the fact.”


❌ DO NOT SAY

• “Patents will stop them” (unless asked)

• “First mover advantage”


⸻


7️⃣ “What do you need from us to move forward?”


✅ ANSWER (TURN IT BACK)


“Clarity on fit.

Specifically: what structure you’d consider viable, what documentation you’d need, and what would disqualify this quickly.”


This puts them in decision mode.
