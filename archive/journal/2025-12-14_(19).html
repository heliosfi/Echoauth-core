STAGE 5 — PRIVACY IMPACT ASSESSMENT (PIA)


NI-AI × EchoAuth × MCG

(Child-Centered, Non-Verbal, Governance-First)


⸻


5.1 System Identification


System Name:

NI-AI — Neuro-Integrated Artificial Intelligence System


Integrated Components:

• MCG (Motion / Context Governance)

• EchoAuth (Role-Based Authorization)

• NI-AI (Read-Only Explanation Layer)


System Owner:

Helios F.I LLC

Nicholas B. Carty (Founder / System Owner)


System Category:

Decision-support and explanation system with adaptive authentication, operating under human governance.


⸻


5.2 Purpose of the System


NI-AI exists to support safe, explainable, and restrained interaction in sensitive environments (including neurodivergent and non-verbal contexts).


The system’s purpose is to:

• Maintain safety and stability

• Enforce authorization boundaries

• Provide transparent explanations of system state

• Support caregivers as the sole decision authority


Explicit exclusion:

The system does not perform diagnosis, prediction, behavioral scoring, or autonomous decision-making.


⸻


5.3 Information Collected


NI-AI is designed around data minimization.


Data That May Be Collected (Configuration-Dependent):

• Session interaction signals (text input, timing, sequence)

• System state markers (allow / pause / deny)

• Role and authorization state (caregiver/admin)

• Optional device/session identifiers (non-persistent)


Data That Is Not Collected:

• No SSN

• No financial records

• No medical records

• No biometric identity by default

• No intent inference stored as fact

• No emotional diagnosis


⸻


5.4 Sources of Information

• Direct user or caregiver interaction

• System-generated governance states

• Administrator configuration inputs


No third-party data brokers or enrichment sources are used.


⸻


5.5 Intended Use of Information


Collected information is used only to:

• Enforce governance rules (MCG)

• Verify authorization (EchoAuth)

• Explain system behavior (NI-AI)

• Maintain auditability and safety


Information is not used for profiling, advertising, or behavioral prediction.


⸻


5.6 Data Retention & Minimization

• Session-bounded by default

• Ephemeral context expires automatically

• Logs store outcomes and timestamps, not raw interaction content (unless explicitly required by policy)

• Retention windows are configurable and reviewable


Principle enforced:

Collect the minimum required to remain safe.


⸻


5.7 Access & Security Controls


Privacy protections are enforced through:

• Role-based access (EchoAuth)

• Least-privilege permissions

• Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3)

• Encryption at rest (AES-256)

• Immutable audit logs

• No direct child access to configuration or logs


⸻


5.8 Privacy Risks & Mitigations


Risk Mitigation

Misinterpretation of silence Silence treated as valid state

Over-collection of sensitive data Data minimization + configuration limits

Unauthorized access EchoAuth + default deny

Context leakage Session isolation + expiration

AI overreach NI-AI is read-only; MCG is final authority



⸻


5.9 Consent & User Rights

• Explicit caregiver consent governs operation

• Users may opt out where applicable

• No forced participation

• No punitive outcome for non-interaction

• Data handling is transparent to authorized caregivers


⸻


5.10 Neurodivergent & Non-Verbal Protections


This system explicitly supports:

• Non-verbal interaction

• Sensory-safe pacing

• No requirement to respond

• No escalation due to silence

• Observe / pause as successful outcomes


These protections are enforced at the governance level, not inferred by AI.


⸻


5.11 Compliance Alignment


This PIA aligns with:

• NIST Privacy Framework

• FISMA privacy expectations

• FedRAMP privacy principles

• Federal child-safety expectations

• Executive Order 14110 (AI Safety)


⸻


5.12 Privacy Summary Statement


NI-AI is architected to respect privacy through restraint, minimization, and governance.

The system avoids inference-as-truth, defaults to non-action under uncertainty, and preserves caregiver authority at all times.
