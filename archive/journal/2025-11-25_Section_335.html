SECTION 335 — SENSORIAL HARMONIC ADAPTATION ENGINE (SHAE)


(NI-AI Patent Module)

────────────────────────


335.1 — Technical Field


The SHAE concerns adaptive modulation of AI output based on the user’s real-time sensory load, emotional state, and cognitive capacity. This subsystem is specifically engineered for neurodivergent support systems and dynamic AI-human interaction environments.


⸻


335.2 — Purpose


The engine ensures NI-AI never overwhelms, overstimulates, or under-stimulates an ASD user, caregiver, or sensitive environment. It harmonizes communication intensity using sensory, emotional, and environmental signatures.


It is a core safety engine proven essential in:

• EchoAuth environmental mode

• NI-AI coherent communication

• ASD sensory safety

• Emotional stabilization


⸻


335.3 — Functional Definition


The SHAE performs real-time sensory balancing through three coordinated mechanisms:

1. Sensory Load Detection (SLD)

Detects incoming sensory strain through:

• message pacing

• emotional load

• environmental context tags

• device signals (camera, mic, touchscreen intensity)

2. Harmonic Adjustment Layer (HAL)

Adjusts NI-AI output intensity across:

• complexity

• pacing

• tone

• length

• sensory density (images, steps, instructions)

3. Adaptive Resonance Sync (ARS)

Aligns the output with the user’s resonance profile (baseline, fatigue state, emotional pattern).


⸻

• Neurodivergent Support Mode data

⸻


335.5 — Outputs

• Harmonic Output Profile (HOP)

⸻


335.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Sensory Delta Calculation (SDC)

Measures deviation between user’s usual sensory tolerance and current load.


(b) Dynamic Tone Regulator (DTR)

Caps tone intensity and adjusts emotional energy output.


(c) Instruction Density Modifier (IDM)

Breaks complex instructions into absorbable segments when sensory load is elevated.


(d) Resonance Rebalancing Loop (RRL)

Self-correcting loop ensuring no escalation or sensory fatigue occurs during long interactions.


(e) Caregiver-Defined Sensory Thresholds (CDST)

Allows parents/therapists to set personalized sensory safety limits.
