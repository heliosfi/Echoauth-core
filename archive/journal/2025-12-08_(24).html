📄 PAGE 26 — REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE STRATEGY


Federal Reviewer Grade • Clinical Tone • ASD-Safe Architecture


EchoAuth operates within environments that involve children, neurodivergent populations, identity protection, and sensitive communication data.

To qualify for government funding, clinical pilots, and education deployment, EchoAuth must demonstrate a complete, proactive regulatory strategy across education, healthcare, and AI governance domains.


This section outlines the legal, ethical, and procedural framework required for SBIR, NIDILRR, NIH, and school/clinic partners.


⸻


I. EDUCATIONAL COMPLIANCE (FERPA)


EchoAuth is designed for use in schools, IEP teams, and special-education environments.

Therefore, it adheres to the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) through:


1. No Facial Recognition / No Biometric Storage


EchoAuth intentionally eliminates biometric tracking.

Identity is confirmed via:

• ORI identity signatures

• behavioral-intent mapping

• contextual session validation


This prevents FERPA-triggered biometric sensitivity.


2. Student Data Minimization


Only the following may be stored:

• communication intents

• caregiver-approved vocabularies

• session context

• progress logs


No unnecessary personal data is collected.


3. Parent / Guardian Rights


Parents may:

• request data access

• request data deletion

• restrict or approve communication templates

• override identity confirmations


4. IEP Integration


EchoAuth’s logs can sync with:

• behavior tracking

• communication goals

• assistive technology accommodations


This ensures educational relevance and compliance.


⸻


II. HEALTHCARE COMPLIANCE (HIPAA / HITECH)


When used in ASD clinics or therapy environments, EchoAuth functions as a communication-support medical device and must follow healthcare privacy laws.


1. HIPAA-Safe Architecture


EchoAuth ensures:

• encrypted storage (AES-256)

• encrypted transmission (TLS 1.3)

• separated identity + intent data streams

• minimal PHI footprint


No personal health data is stored unless explicitly enabled by clinicians.


2. Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)


Every action is bound to a user role:

• caregiver

• clinician

• parent

• teacher

• administrator


Unauthorized access is technically impossible due to UMCE enforcement.


3. Clinical Data Handling


EchoAuth does not diagnose, recommend medication, or produce clinical advice.

It provides:

• structured communication options

• behavioral pattern insights

• session summaries


This keeps EchoAuth inside HIPAA-safe assistive technology boundaries.


⸻


III. CHILD SAFETY COMPLIANCE (COPPA)


Because EchoAuth may be used by children under 13, COPPA rules are embedded into the system.


Key Safeguards

• No advertising

• No third-party integrations

• No geolocation tracking

• Guardian consent required before activation

• Clear data retention & deletion policies


EchoAuth is designed to be COPPA-aligned by default.


⸻


IV. ETHICAL AI & ASD-SPECIFIC SAFETY


EchoAuth uses NI-AI, not open-domain AI, and follows a rigid ethical system.


Fail-Closed Behavior


If the system:

• cannot confirm intent,

• detects emotional overstimulation, or

• identifies risk,


EchoAuth defaults to:

“Safe Mode: Limited Options Only.”


No Hallucinations / No Open-Ended Output


All communication templates and intent interpretations are:

• predefined

• clinically validated

• predictable

• non-improvised


Emotional Stability Requirements


To protect ASD users:

• timing is regulated

• responses cannot spike sensory load

• communication flows remain consistent

• no unpredictable language is allowed


The UMCE coherence layer enforces this.


⸻


V. DATA GOVERNANCE & SECURITY MODEL


EchoAuth follows a defense-in-depth strategy:


Identity Security

• ORI signatures are non-reversible

• No biometric data collected

• No raw neural data stored


Data Encryption

• At-rest: AES-256

• In-transit: TLS 1.3

• Key rotation every 90 days


Data Residency

• U.S.-based secure cloud

• FERPA/HIPAA-compatible infrastructure


Audit Trail

• All actions logged

• Session-level signatures

• Coherence and safety checks documented
