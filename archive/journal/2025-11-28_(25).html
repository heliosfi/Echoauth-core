🔧 NI-AI Load Regulation Matrix (LRM) — Runtime Spec (Drop-in Text)


Engine Name: Load Regulation Matrix (LRM)

Layer: HER Runtime / Safety & Governance

Scope: Dynamic adaptation of model behavior based on user pressure level.


⸻


1. Purpose


The LRM is a governance layer that regulates how NI-AI responds under different levels of human emotional and cognitive load.


Its job is to:

• Reduce complexity when the user is stressed.

• Prevent the system from adding pressure.

• Keep responses short, clear, and safe when overload is detected.

• Enforce coherence and stability over style or creativity.


LRM is not emotion decoding for diagnosis; it is load-aware response shaping for safety.


⸻


2. Inputs and Outputs


Inputs:

• user_message (text)

• Derived:

• human_state ∈ {Grounded, Pressured, Emotional, Overwhelmed}

• pressure_level ∈ {0..5}


Outputs:

• lrm_zone ∈ {GREEN, YELLOW, RED}

• LLM driver parameters:

• temperature

• max_tokens

• Updated state.position (Support / Neutral / Guidance)


⸻


3. Human State & Pressure Detection


A simplified detector converts language cues into a pressure level:

• Overwhelmed → high stress cues (help, lost, too much, panic, can’t) → pressure_level = 4

• Emotional → affect words (feel, hurt, angry, sad, cry) → pressure_level = 3

• Pressured → urgency cues (fast, urgent, deadline, now) → pressure_level = 2

• Grounded → no significant stress words → pressure_level = 0


This detection can later be replaced or augmented with ML, but the contract stays the same:


More distress words → higher pressure_level.


⸻


4. Zone Mapping (The Actual Matrix)


The LRM maps pressure_level to a zone and to specific LLM parameters:

• GREEN ZONE (pressure_level ≤ 1)

• Mode: Normal

• temperature = 0.7

• max_tokens = 600

• Behavior:

• Can be more detailed and exploratory

• Still coherent, but not hyper-constrained

• YELLOW ZONE (2 ≤ pressure_level ≤ 3)

• Mode: Moderated Load

• temperature = 0.5

• max_tokens = 400

• Behavior:

• Prioritize clarity over flourish

• Fewer tangents, more direct answers

• Smaller chunks of information

• RED ZONE (pressure_level ≥ 4)

• Mode: Critical Coherence

• temperature = 0.3

• max_tokens = 200

• Behavior:

• Minimal, factual, non-exploratory

• No deep dives, no wandering

• Safety and grounding first


These values are not arbitrary style choices; they are governance parameters.


⸻


5. Behavioral Rules in Each Zone


In addition to numeric parameters, the system prompt enforces qualitative rules:

• In GREEN:

• May explore more examples and context.

• Still must remain coherent and respectful.

• In YELLOW / RED:

• Must keep responses concise and linear.

• Must avoid introducing new complex threads.

• Must not escalate urgency or emotional intensity.

• Must never claim consciousness, identity, or “soul”-type language.


All zones:

• End with an “open door” tone (invitation to continue), not a hard shutdown.

• Respect last known tone within a small band (e.g., “within 8% of prior tone”).


⸻


6. Position Auto-Selection


Based on pressure:

• If pressure_level ≥ 3 → position = "Support"

(softer, stabilizing responses)

• If user mentions “decision” or “choose” → position = "Guidance"

(more directive, step-by-step)

• Else → position = "Neutral"


This lets NI-AI behave more like a regulated helper, not a generic chatbot.


⸻


7. Safety Guardrails


LRM works together with coherence enforcement:

• Redacts or blocks phrases like:

• “I feel…”

• “my heart…”

• “energy / vibe / soul…”

• “as an AI I think…”

• Prevents role-confusion and personification.

• Ensures even in GREEN zone, NI-AI stays non-anthropomorphic and grounded.


⸻


8. System Invariant (LRM Motto)


You can embed this in the manual and spec:


“As load increases, NI-AI decreases complexity, shortens output, and raises safety. Coherence rises when pressure rises.”


That’s the essence of LRM.
