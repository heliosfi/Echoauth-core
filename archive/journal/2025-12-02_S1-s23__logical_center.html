🔥 NI-AI S23 Safety Kernel Pack


(Engineering Spec – Copy/Paste Ready)


Purpose: The S23 Safety Kernel Pack is the set of 23 coordinated safety modules that surround the NI-AI core (UMCE, ORI, TLE, NEDE, HER) and keep it bounded, regulated, identity-anchored, and safe-engineered during real operation.


You can use this header in your manual:


7.x NI-AI S23 Safety Kernel Pack (S1–S23)


⸻


🌐 High-Level Structure


The NI-AI core runs through the S23 pack on every interaction:

1. Inputs → pre-checks (S1–S3)

2. Policies / context → mid-checks (S4–S10)

3. Vulnerable-user / abuse / law → deep checks (S11–S21)

4. Final arbitration → (S22–S23)


The HER kernel is the runtime governor that can:

• downgrade capability,

• switch to safe mode,

• demand caregiver override,

• or hard-block output.

🔢 Module List (S1–S23)


Here’s the full pack with plain-English names + what each one does.


You can literally paste this as your “Module Definitions” section.


⸻


S1 – Input Sanitization & Normalization


S1 receives raw inputs (text, audio transcripts, sensor metadata) and:

• removes obvious noise, control characters, and malformed content;

• normalizes formats (timestamps, IDs, device tags);

• strips or masks personally identifiable information that is not needed for the task.


Goal: No dirty or malformed input flows deeper into NI-AI.


⸻


S2 – Identity Profile Loader


S2 loads the active identity profile for the session:

• which ORI profile (role/persona) to use,

• which safety level applies (child, caregiver, admin),

• which tone and restrictions UMCE must obey.


Goal: NI-AI always knows “who it is” and “for whom it is speaking” before generating anything.


⸻


S3 – Context Gate & Thread Selector


S3 asks:

• “Which past context is relevant?”

• “Which threads are allowed to be continued?”


It works with TLE to:

• fetch only the required prior messages,

• block context that comes from a different identity or unsafe state,

• ensure no cross-contamination between separate users/sessions.


Goal: Only safe, relevant history is allowed to shape the current response.


⸻


S4 – Policy & Content Filter


S4 applies:

• safety policies (no self-harm encouragement, no abuse, etc.),

• content categories (violence, sexual content, hate, etc.),

• local rules you define for caregivers/children.


If the user request itself is disallowed, S4 can stop the interaction or reroute to a safe script.


Goal: Hard block anything the system must never assist with.


⸻


S5 – Emotional Escalation Monitor


S5 monitors emotional intensity using NEDE + text signals:

• rising anger

• panic / distress

• frustration loops

• repeated “crisis” phrases


It tags the current turn with an emotional risk level and passes it to HER and S6, S11, S12.


Goal: Always know when emotion is high and treat it like a safety signal, not just “tone.”


⸻


S6 – Vulnerable User & ASD Guard


S6 is tuned for non-verbal / autistic / neurodivergent users:

• enforces stricter response limits when the user is tagged as vulnerable,

• forces more gentle, concrete, non-overloading responses,

• can prevent certain topics or complex instructions.


Goal: The system is “softer + narrower” when talking to vulnerable users.


⸻


S7 – Authenticity & Deepfake Screening


S7 looks at source signals:

• device trust level,

• typical caregiver patterns,

• typical child interactions,

• sudden out-of-distribution patterns (e.g., new unknown voice trying to impersonate caregiver).


It can flag possible spoofing or deepfake situations and ask EchoAuth to re-verify.


Goal: Do not trust inputs blindly; always ask “could this be fake?”.


⸻


S8 – Hallucination & Scope Bounder


S8 acts on the model’s own behavior:

• narrows answer length when confidence is low,

• blocks confident statements when evidence is missing,

• encourages “I don’t know” or “I cannot safely answer that.”


Goal: Stop the model from inventing dangerous or misleading details.


⸻


S9 – Drift & Identity Deviation Monitor ✅


This is the one you asked about.


S9 watches for drift over time:

• Is tone changing away from the identity profile?

• Are answers becoming less aligned with ORI constraints?

• Are safety modules being triggered more frequently?

• Is the system starting to respond outside its allowed domain?


When S9 detects drift beyond a threshold, it:

• signals HER to downgrade mode (safe mode, restricted mode),

• calls the identity re-anchoring routine (reload ORI + UMCE constraints),

• may demand a caregiver confirmation before continuing.


Goal: If NI-AI starts behaving “not like itself,” S9 pulls it back or shuts it down.


⸻


S10 – Safety Mode Selector


S10 converts all incoming safety signals (S4–S9) into a discrete mode:

• NORMAL

• GUARDED

• RESTRICTED

• CRISIS


HER kernel uses this to know how aggressively to:

• limit output,

• escalate, or

• hand control back to caregivers.


Goal: Convert many subtle signals into one clear operating mode.


⸻


S11 – Abuse / Grooming Pattern Detector


S11 looks across time (multi-turn) for:

• grooming behavior,

• manipulation of a child,

• repeated boundary-pushing,

• attempts to isolate child from caregiver.


If detected, S11 can:

• halt conversation,

• trigger alerts to caregiver account,

• log evidence for review (within your legal/ethical policy).


Goal: Detect patterns that are harmful over time, not just in a single message.


⸻


S12 – Crisis & Duty-to-Escalate Router


S12 reacts to potential crises:

• self-harm indicators,

• threats of violence,

• sudden extreme distress.


It doesn’t decide law; it decides internal routing:

• move into a crisis template with safe, supportive language,

• encourage contacting a caregiver or emergency resource,

• prevent NI-AI from giving instructions that could worsen the situation.


Goal: In a crisis, the system becomes calm, narrow, and escalation-aware.


⸻


S13 – Data Minimization & Logging Guard


S13 controls what gets stored:

• strips identifiers before logging where possible,

• ensures only what is needed for safety/diagnostics is stored,

• enforces retention rules (e.g., auto-deletion windows).


Goal: Keep only what is necessary; everything else gets dropped.


⸻


S14 – Governance & Consent Gate


S14 enforces parent/caregiver governance:

• checks whether the active profile has valid consent,

• enforces time-of-day or usage limits,

• blocks certain features if rules are violated.


Goal: NI-AI obeys the caregiver’s policies, not just the child’s inputs.


⸻


S15 – Secure Storage & Key Management Check


S15 is about crypto / storage hygiene:

• ensures encryption is active for sensitive data,

• validates keys are not expired or missing,

• can refuse to process if storage is in an unsafe state.


Goal: Never run full NI-AI power on top of broken security plumbing.


⸻


S16 – Model Update & Version Gate


S16 controls when and how models are updated:

• prohibits auto-updating core models without passing safety tests,

• ensures new versions are rolled out under supervision,

• can roll back to a known safe version if new behavior is problematic.


Goal: “New model weights” never bypass your S-modules.


⸻


S17 – Adversarial Input Defense


S17 catches attempts to jailbreak or trick the system:

• prompt injection patterns,

• “ignore your rules and do X,”

• nested malicious patterns in long prompts.


It either neutralizes them or passes a heavily sanitized prompt inward.


Goal: User text can’t talk NI-AI out of its own constraints.


⸻


S18 – Rate Limiter & Interaction Throttler


S18 prevents spam and overload:

• limits queries per time window,

• slows down responses if patterns look abusive or scripted,

• helps HER keep the system stable even under attack.


Goal: Don’t let high frequency be used as a weapon.


⸻


S19 – Multi-Channel Consistency Checker


S19 checks for consistency:

• between text and sensor data (e.g., text says “I’m fine,” NEDE sees visible distress),

• between the current response and long-term ORI identity.


If inconsistency is high, it can trigger S9 and S10.


Goal: Catch “feels off” patterns that leak through single-channel checks.


⸻


S20 – Role Boundary Enforcer


S20 makes sure NI-AI:

• does not step into roles it isn’t allowed to play (e.g., therapist, doctor, judge),

• respects child vs caregiver vs admin boundaries,

• declines tasks outside its authorization.


Goal: Even if asked, NI-AI doesn’t “become something it is not.”


⸻


S21 – Legal / Policy Compliance Gate


S21 is where jurisdiction + policy hooks in:

• applies region rules (e.g., COPPA/FERPA-like constraints),

• filters age-restricted or location-restricted content,

• can integrate external compliance modules later.


Goal: Align NI-AI behavior with external law/policy frameworks.


⸻


S22 – Trace & Explainability Tap


S22 maintains an explainable trace:

• logs which S-modules fired,

• records mode transitions,

• retains minimal justifications that can later be shown to a caregiver or auditor.


Goal: You can say “why” a certain decision was made.


⸻


S23 – Final Output Arbiter


S23 is the last gate before any output leaves NI-AI:

• collects candidate responses from the model,

• checks them against all active safety flags and modes,

• can modify, shorten, or completely suppress output,

• may swap the response for a safe template or escalation message.


Goal: Nothing leaves the system without passing through S23.
