EchoAuth™ — Invention Disclosure (Attorney Ready)


Inventor: Nicholas B. Carty

Working Title of Invention: EchoAuth™ – Caregiver-Governed Neuro-Safety Authentication System

Primary Use Case: Coherence-enforced biometric check-ins for caregivers and non-verbal autistic children.


⸻


Technical Field


The invention relates to secure digital authentication and pediatric safety oversight systems. More specifically, it provides a caregiver-governed, neuro-adaptive authentication architecture that processes structured behavioral and environmental cue data for non-verbal autistic children, ensuring that safety decisions are resolved internally before output, logged privately for audit, and delivered as one unambiguous result per check-in.


⸻


Summary of the Invention


EchoAuth™ introduces a novel identity-first authentication model in which caregivers register both themselves and their child, creating an immutable authority linkage. At runtime, caregivers submit structured cue packets describing the child’s non-verbal behaviors, emotional state, and sensory environment. Cues are encoded into identity tokens before NI-AI evaluation, where multiple internal reasoning paths compete, generate conflict and distress metrics, and collapse into a single validated decision. The system computes a continuous emotion/distress score (0.0–1.0), a mapped danger behavior score, an internal conflict score, and a confidence value. When distress, danger, or reasoning disagreement exceeds defined safety thresholds, the event is routed to a pending human review queue, while the child-facing layer is blocked from receiving uncertain or competing outputs. The final resolved result is returned to caregivers only as "Safe" or "Needs Attention", along with confidence and emotion scores, preserving clarity for the child while ensuring accountability for caregivers and compliance bodies.


⸻


Differentiating Novelty


The key novelty resides in (i) caregiver authority as the root of all authentication and safety decisions, (ii) cue-based identity token creation before inference, (iii) internal conflict resolution and one-output enforcement prior to output release, and (iv) private audit logging and review loop integration for pediatric safety oversight, without UI distraction or multi-candidate output surfaces. This architecture prioritizes identity stability and cognitive safety for non-verbal autistic children while maintaining traceability and legal verifiability for caregivers, clinicians, and engineers.
