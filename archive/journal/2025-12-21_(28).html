1️⃣ NIH (SBIR / R01-adjacent, Non-Clinical Support Framing)


Title


EchoAuth: A Child-First Care Coordination and Non-Verbal Access Platform for Autistic Children


Project Summary


EchoAuth is a non-clinical, child-first care coordination and access platform designed to support autistic and non-verbal children across home, school, and clinical environments. The system enables sensory-safe engagement for children while providing parents, caregivers, schools, and clinicians with shared visibility, time-based reminders, and auditable documentation—without diagnosing, prescribing, or delivering medical treatment.


EchoAuth preserves parental authority as a system invariant and enforces pause-on-uncertainty safety behavior. The platform records observations, stores clinician-provided documents, tracks caregiver-entered schedules, and supports coordination through non-interpretive logging suitable for evaluation and research contexts.


Relevance to NIH Mission

• Supports family-centered care coordination

• Reduces caregiver burden

• Improves continuity for children with communication barriers

• Enables safe, non-clinical infrastructure for care adherence and engagement


Outcomes (Non-Clinical)

• Reduced missed care actions

• Improved coordination across environments

• Increased safe engagement for non-verbal children

2️⃣ NSF (SBIR / Civic Tech / Accessibility Framing)


Title


EchoAuth: Safe, Non-Verbal Access and Care Coordination Infrastructure for Autistic Children


Project Description


EchoAuth is a socio-technical system addressing a core accessibility gap: most digital systems assume verbal communication, authentication, and executive function. EchoAuth replaces these assumptions with a child-first access interface, parent-governed authority controls, and a structured coordination layer for caregivers, schools, and clinicians.


The platform integrates sensory-safe engagement modules for children and a rigorous logging and audit architecture that enables accountability, scalability, and evaluation without interpretation or behavioral scoring.


Innovation

• Non-verbal access without credentials

• Role-bounded collaboration model

• Pause-on-uncertainty safety logic

• Event-based, non-interpretive evidence logging


Broader Impacts

• Improves inclusion for neurodivergent children

• Reduces coordination friction in public systems

• Enables scalable, ethical deployment across institutions

Title

• Giving parents full control over decisions

• Logging everything safely for accountability


Impact

• Children experience consistency and calm

• Families retain agency and trust
