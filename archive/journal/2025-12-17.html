Request for Limited School Pilot


Non-Medical Sensory Awareness Support for Neurodivergent Students


Submitted by:

NI-AI™ / EchoAuth™

Founder: Nicholas B. Carty

(New York City)


⸻


Purpose of This Request


We are requesting approval to conduct a limited, non-medical pilot within your school to evaluate a caregiver-supervised technology designed to help non-verbal and neurodivergent students communicate sensory distress before meltdowns, shutdowns, or safety incidents occur.


This pilot is not diagnostic, not therapeutic, and not disciplinary.

It is a support and awareness tool for staff and caregivers.


⸻


The Problem We Are Addressing


Many neurodivergent and non-verbal students experience:

• sensory overload (noise, crowding, lighting, temperature)

• distress they cannot clearly communicate

• meltdowns or elopement without warning

• increased classroom disruption and safety risk


Teachers and staff often recognize distress only after escalation.


This pilot evaluates whether early, private alerts can help adults intervene calmly before escalation.


⸻


What the Pilot Does (Plain Language)


During the pilot:

• The system monitors environmental and behavioral signals (via school-approved devices)

• When sensory stress thresholds are exceeded, a private alert is sent to:

• a designated caregiver

• and/or a designated school staff member

• Alerts say what is happening, not why

(e.g., “High noise + crowd pressure detected”)


There is:

• ❌ no diagnosis

• ❌ no behavior labeling

• ❌ no audio/video recording

• ❌ no autonomous action

• ❌ no student data sold or shared


Adults remain in full control at all times.


⸻


Pilot Scope (Proposed)

• Duration: 8–12 weeks

• Students: 10–25 (IEP or caregiver-approved only)

• Technology:

• Phase 1: Smartphone-based sensing only

• (Optional later phase: wearable, pending approval)

• Setting: Classroom, hallway, cafeteria, transitions

• Staff Burden: Minimal (alerts only, no dashboards required)


⸻


Safety & Compliance Commitments

• Fully non-medical and non-diagnostic

• FERPA-aligned data handling

• Parent / guardian explicit consent required

• Parent and school override at all times

• No permanent biometric storage

• All alerts logged for transparency (IEP-safe language)


⸻


What the School Gains

• Earlier awareness of student distress

• Reduced classroom disruptions

• Fewer emergency escalations

• Better caregiver–school coordination

• No financial cost during pilot phase

• Optional written summary for internal review
