OPTION A — SYSTEM-WIDE ROLE INTERACTION MAP


(Authority, Visibility, and Execution Boundaries)


This map answers one core reviewer question:


“Who can do what, who can see what, and who can never override whom?”


⸻


A1. Roles Defined (Canonical)

• Child — recipient of experience only

• Parent / Guardian — sole authority holder

• Caregiver — task executor

• School Staff — observer + educational implementer

• Clinician — documenter + advisor

• System (EchoAuth) — coordination enforcement only


⸻


A2. Authority Hierarchy (Non-Negotiable)

1. Parent / Guardian — final authority

2. System Governance (EchoAuth rules) — enforces parent decisions

3. Caregivers / Schools / Clinicians — act within approved scope

4. Child — never required to decide, explain, or comply


No role may escalate authority upward.

No role may bypass the parent.


⸻


A3. Action Rights Matrix (Who Can Do What)


Action / Capability Child Parent Caregiver School Clinician

Approve programs ❌ ✅ ❌ ❌ ❌

Approve routines ❌ ✅ ❌ ❌ ❌

Assign caregivers ❌ ✅ ❌ ❌ ❌

Execute tasks ❌ ❌ ✅ ✅ (school scope) ❌

Remove access ❌ ✅ ❌ ❌ ❌


Invariant:

Execution ≠ Authority. Recommendation ≠ Permission.

Rule:

No role sees more than is required to perform their function.


⸻


A5. Decision Flow (Simplified)

1. Recommendation submitted (school / clinician)

2. Parent reviews

3. Parent approves or declines

4. EchoAuth enforces scope

5. Caregiver / School executes

6. Action logged

7. Parent retains oversight


If any step is unclear → system pauses.


⸻


A6. What the System Never Allows

• ❌ Cross-role overrides

• ❌ Silent authority transfer

• ❌ Automatic activation

• ❌ Interpretation of child behavior

• ❌ Pressure on the child to respond

OPTION B — PILOT READINESS SUMMARY


(Funders, Institutions, and Government Reviewers)


This answers:


“Can this be piloted safely, legally, and responsibly?”


⸻


B1. Pilot Purpose


Validate a parent-governed coordination system that improves consistency of care for neurodivergent children without clinical intervention, diagnosis, or automation of decisions.


⸻


B2. Pilot Scope (Intentionally Narrow)


Included

• Parent Portal

• Child Portal (ASD-safe programs only)

• Caregiver Portal (task execution)

• Optional School Portal (observation only)


Excluded

• Clinical decision-making

• Medical treatment

• Behavioral scoring

• Outcome claims


⸻


B3. Pilot Population

• Small cohort (5–20 families)

• Parent-opt-in only

• Caregivers approved by parents

• No human subjects research (infrastructure validation only)


⸻


B4. What Is Being Tested

• Authority enforcement

• Role separation

• Consistency across caregivers

• Pause-on-uncertainty behavior

• Parent trust and usability


Not testing child outcomes.


⸻


B5. Safety & Compliance Posture

• No diagnosis

• No therapy

• No autonomous decisions

• No forced child interaction

• Silence and non-participation treated as valid states


Aligned with:

• NIH Phase I expectations

• NIST governance principles

• Non-verbal accessibility standards


⸻


B6. Success Criteria (Fundable & Safe)

• Parents report reduced coordination stress

• Caregivers follow routines without drift

• Schools contribute observations without control

• System pauses correctly under ambiguity

• No escalation or pressure on children


⸻


B7. Why Institutions Say Yes

• Low risk

• Clear authority boundaries

• No regulatory overreach

• Complements existing systems

• Scales without changing child experience


⸻


One-Line Pilot Close


“This pilot proves coordination safety first—before intelligence, automation, or scale.”
