──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.1 — EMOTIONAL LITERALIZATION SYSTEM (ELS)


Autistic brains process emotions better when:


✔ simplified

✔ clarified

✔ literal

✔ predictable


So EchoAuth expresses emotions in simple, clear formats:


Examples:


“You feel hot.” (anger)

“You feel heavy.” (sad)

“You feel fast.” (anxiety)

“You feel full.” (overwhelmed)

“You feel quiet.” (shutdown)


This bypasses abstract emotion labels.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.2 — THE 4 SENSORY-EMOTION PAIRS (SEP)


Emotions in ASD users often show FIRST in the body.


EchoAuth teaches this connection:


⭐ 1 — Heat → Anger / Frustration


Chest, head, hands warm


⭐ 2 — Pressure → Sadness


Chest feels tight, heavy


⭐ 3 — Speed → Anxiety


Thoughts quick, heartbeat fast


⭐ 4 — Silence → Shutdown


Body slows, thinking quiet


Matching sensory → emotion helps the child identify feelings early.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.3 — EMOTION NAMING LADDER (ENL)


Emotions are learned step-by-step:


Step 1 — Sensation


“I feel tight.”


Step 2 — State


“I feel upset.”


Step 3 — Emotion


“I feel sad.”


This ladder makes emotional vocabulary easy and pressure-free.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.4 — SAFE EXPRESSION METHODS (SEM)


EchoAuth teaches ASD-safe ways of expressing feelings:


✔ drawing

✔ tapping patterns

✔ pointing to icons

✔ selecting visuals

✔ using short phrases

✔ hand signals

✔ step-away options


No forced talking. No forced eye contact.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.5 — REGULATION ROUTINES (RR)


EchoAuth provides literal emotional self-regulation:


✔ “Sit down.”

✔ “Slow breathing.”

✔ “Count 1–4.”

✔ “Hands in lap.”

✔ “Hug pillow.”

✔ “Quiet corner.”


Not emotional lectures — action-based calming.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.6 — MELTDOWN EARLY-WARNING SYSTEM (MEWS)


EchoAuth detects subtle signs before the child melts down:

• increasing movement

• voice shifts

• confusion

• sensory withdrawal

• pacing

• sudden silence

• hands covering ears

• avoidance


EchoAuth signals:


“You are getting full.”

“Time for regulation.”


Meltdowns become preventable.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.7 — SHUTDOWN SUPPORT ENGINE (SSE)


Shutdowns are not defiance.

They are nervous system exhaustion.


EchoAuth guides caregivers:


✔ reduce talking

✔ reduce sensory load

✔ do not touch

✔ wait quietly

✔ offer soft grounding

✔ allow space

✔ restore safety


This protects the child’s nervous system.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.8 — THE EMOTION-TO-ACTION BRIDGE (EAB)


EchoAuth teaches:


“Emotion → Action” in clean formulas.


Example:


✔ Feeling: fast → Action: slow breathing

✔ Feeling: heavy → Action: hug pillow

✔ Feeling: hot → Action: pause + hands down

✔ Feeling: quiet → Action: rest


This gives children emotional control, not confusion.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 41.9 — CAREGIVER EMOTIONAL SUPPORT PROTOCOL (CESP)


EchoAuth teaches caregivers:


✔ stay neutral

✔ avoid emotional reactions

✔ avoid overwhelming language

✔ don’t take behavior personal

✔ reduce sensory input

✔ redirect calmly

✔ offer predictable choices


These rules reduce emotional explosions at home.
