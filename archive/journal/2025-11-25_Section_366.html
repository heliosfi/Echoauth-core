SECTION 366 — Neurobehavioral Global Outcome Engine (NGOBE)


(Clinical-Grade Autism Outcome Measurement & Standardization System)


366.1 — Technical Field


This disclosure relates to neurobehavioral analytics, clinical outcome measurement, longitudinal emotional tracking, and standardized behavioral-progress computation for neurodivergent individuals.

NGOBE enables EchoAuth + NI-AI to generate clinical-grade, regulatory-compatible outcomes without performing diagnosis or medical inference.


⸻


366.2 — Purpose


NGOBE exists to solve the core problem in autism support:


❌ No unified standard for emotional or behavioral progress


❌ No continuous measurement across environments


❌ No framework for school, home, and clinic alignment


❌ No way for caregivers to show measurable improvement


NGOBE introduces the world’s first non-clinical, non-diagnostic, multi-environment outcome engine that:

• quantifies emotional stabilization

• tracks sensory resilience over time

• measures recovery speed

• evaluates intervention effectiveness

• builds a clinical-style growth trajectory

• exports formats compatible with IEPs, therapists, and providers


This creates fundable, research-grade evidence of support impact.


⸻


366.3 — Core Functional Architecture


NGOBE consists of five synchronized processing layers:


(A) Clinical Outcomes Matrix (COM)


Transforms raw emotional, contextual, and sensory signals into standardized clinical metrics, including:

• emotional variance index

• overload frequency

• overload intensity

• stabilization time

• intervention response profile

• recovery curve gradient


(B) Neurobehavioral Progress Tracker (NPT)


Longitudinal graph engine capturing:

• weekly emotional trajectory

• sensory tolerance drift

• resilience growth

• baseline shifts

• trigger-specific patterns


Produces 30-day, 90-day, and 180-day outcome charts.


(C) Resilience & Recovery Graph (RRG)


Measures:

• time to regulate

• duration of dysregulation

• recovery slope

• stabilization efficiency


This is the heart of what schools and therapists want.


(D) Intervention Effectiveness Engine (IEE)


Compares intervention types:

• calming sound

• sensory redirection

• breathing cues

• parent-specific strategies

• environment exits

• routine-reset sequences


Outputs an objective “Intervention Effectiveness Score” (IES).


(E) Clinical Export Engine (CEE)


Generates:

• IEP-compatible summaries

• ABA-compatible trackers

• therapist-aligned reports

• school-dashboard progress sheets

• ZERO diagnosis, ZERO medical claims


Fully FERPA/COPPA/HIPAA-compliant (connected to Section 361).
