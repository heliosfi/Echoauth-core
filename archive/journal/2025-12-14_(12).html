NIH SBIR PHASE I — SPECIFIC AIMS


Project Title


NI-AI: A Governed, Non-Autonomous AI Decision-Support System for Neurodivergent Child Communication


Overall Goal


The long-term goal of this project is to improve caregiver understanding and response during interactions with neurodivergent, minimally verbal children by providing safe, explainable, and caregiver-mediated decision support.


The objective of this Phase I project is to establish the technical feasibility, safety, and usability of NI-AI as a non-diagnostic, non-autonomous AI explanation and stabilization system operating under strict governance and authorization controls.


Significance


Caregivers supporting neurodivergent children often face uncertainty during non-verbal communication moments, which can lead to misinterpretation, escalation, or inaction. Existing AI systems are poorly suited for these contexts due to:

• reliance on inference or prediction,

• lack of sensory-safe design,

• insufficient governance and auditability.


NI-AI addresses this gap by prioritizing restraint over action, explanation over inference, and caregiver authority over automation.


Innovation


NI-AI introduces a novel architecture that:

• treats non-action as a successful outcome,

• enforces fail-closed defaults under uncertainty,

• separates explanation from execution through governance (MCG) and authorization (EchoAuth),

• explicitly supports non-verbal and minimally verbal communication states, and

• provides auditable, explainable outputs suitable for sensitive environments.


This approach differs fundamentally from adaptive or autonomous AI systems.


⸻


Specific Aim 1 — Validate governed, fail-closed system logic


Objective: Demonstrate that NI-AI operates deterministically under governance, with no autonomous action or escalation.


Approach:

• Implement rule-based state checks and authorization gates.

• Verify allow / pause / deny behavior under simulated ambiguous inputs.

• Confirm default behavior resolves to pause or observation when uncertainty is detected.


Expected Outcome:

Evidence that the system consistently prioritizes safety, non-action, and caregiver mediation.


⸻


Specific Aim 2 — Demonstrate stability and explainability in extended interactions


Objective: Evaluate system stability and clarity across long-session, non-verbal interaction scenarios.


Approach:

• Conduct long-duration simulated sessions using caregiver-defined interaction patterns.

• Measure consistency of explanations, absence of escalation, and coherence over time.

• Validate that silence and stillness are treated as valid states.


Expected Outcome:

Demonstration of stable, predictable behavior without drift or hallucination pressure.


⸻


Specific Aim 3 — Assess caregiver comprehension and usability


Objective: Determine whether caregivers understand and trust NI-AI explanations.


Approach:

• Conduct structured usability sessions with adult caregivers only.

• Collect qualitative feedback on clarity, pacing, and perceived usefulness.

• Evaluate whether outputs support decision-making without directing action.


Expected Outcome:

Evidence that NI-AI explanations are understandable, non-directive, and supportive.


⸻


Phase I Deliverables

• Verified governance and authorization logic

• Stability and safety evaluation results

• Caregiver usability findings

• Phase II technical roadmap


Phase II Vision


Phase II will focus on expanded caregiver pilots, integration readiness, and broader evaluation in non-clinical support settings, without introducing autonomous or diagnostic behavior.
