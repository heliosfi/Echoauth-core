MCG 3.0 — Clinical & Safety Summary


Plain Language | Non-Diagnostic | Human-Governed


⸻


1. Purpose of the System (Clinical Framing)


The Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) system is designed to support communication, safety, and situational awareness for individuals who may be:

• non-verbal or minimally verbal

• neurodivergent

• under high cognitive or sensory load


The system does not diagnose, does not treat, and does not replace clinicians or caregivers.

It exists solely to assist interpretation and routing of signals within clearly defined safety boundaries.


⸻


2. What the System Does


MCG functions as a governance layer that:

• receives structured inputs (behavioral, environmental, caregiver-provided)

• stabilizes and routes those inputs

• enforces safety constraints

• determines whether actions are allowed, denied, or escalated to a human


The system prioritizes clarity, restraint, and human oversight at all times.


⸻


3. What the System Explicitly Does NOT Do


For clinical and ethical clarity, MCG will never:

• infer emotional states

• infer intent

• diagnose conditions

• label behaviors

• make clinical judgments

• act autonomously

• override human authority

• learn or adapt without explicit permission


Any interpretation beyond signal routing is intentionally excluded.


⸻


4. Human-in-the-Loop Requirement


All actionable outcomes are governed by human-in-the-loop control.


The system may:

• recommend escalation

• recommend pausing interaction

• recommend caregiver review


The system may not:

• initiate irreversible actions

• operate without supervision

• proceed when signals are ambiguous


When uncertainty is detected, the default response is escalation to a human caregiver or professional.


⸻


5. Safety-First Design Principles


MCG is built on the following safety principles:

• Default-Deny

If the system is unsure, it does not proceed.

• Graceful Degradation

Under stress, overload, or signal loss, the system reduces functionality rather than expanding it.

• Explainability

Every system output can be explained in plain language.

• Auditability

All actions and decisions are logged and reviewable.


⸻


6. Risk Management & Failure Modes


The system includes safeguards for:

• signal ambiguity

• sensory overload

• conflicting inputs

• system faults


In these cases, MCG will:

• pause output

• notify a human

• log the event

• await instruction


The system is designed to fail safely, not silently.


⸻


7. Clinical Compatibility


MCG is compatible with:

• caregiver-led environments

• educational settings

• supervised clinical support contexts


It is not positioned as a medical device in its initial form and makes no therapeutic claims.


Future clinical integrations, if pursued, would require:

• independent validation

• regulatory review

• separate approval pathways


⸻


8. Ethical Positioning


MCG prioritizes:

• dignity of the individual

• respect for autonomy

• transparency in operation

• non-manipulative interaction


The system is intentionally conservative by design.


⸻


9. Summary (Reviewer-Facing)


In summary, MCG is a safety-governed cognitive support system that:

• assists interpretation without diagnosis

• enforces human oversight

• reduces risk rather than increasing capability

• is designed for responsible deployment
