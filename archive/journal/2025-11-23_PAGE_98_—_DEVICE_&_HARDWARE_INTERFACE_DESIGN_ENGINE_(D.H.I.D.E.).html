⭐ 98.2 — INTERFACE TIERS (4-TIER MODEL)


EchoAuth uses four interface tiers:

– toys, tablets, wearables, music devices

2️⃣ Caregiver & Professional Devices

– phones, tablets, laptops, clinic screens

⭐ 98.3 — CHILD-FACING DEVICES INTERFACE (CFDI)


These are the frontline tools your son and other kids directly interact with.


📱 A. Tablet / Phone (Child Mode UI)


Inputs:

• simple touches

• swipes

• yes/no buttons

• emotion icons

• visual choices

• music selections


Outputs:

• calming visuals

• countdown animations

• color-coded emotions

• short instructions

• music playback (regulation engine)

• “safe zone” and “break time” visuals


Design Rules:

• large buttons

• minimal text

• color-coded states (calm, alert, break, done)

• no clutter

• always predictable layout


⸻


⌚ B. Wearable Band / Bracelet


Inputs:

• heart rate

• movement level

• skin temp (optional)

• tap patterns (fast x3 = “help”)


Outputs:

• gentle vibration to regulate

• “breathe with me” haptic pulses

• small LED color (green = calm, yellow = rising, red = break)


This works with:

• Overstimulation prediction (88)

• Emotional regulation (84)

• Community safety (96)


⸻


🧸 C. Smart Sensory Toy (Plush, Cube, or Figurine)


Inputs:

• squeeze (pressure intensity)

• tap patterns

• movement (shaking, rocking)


Outputs:

• soft light pulses

• music/tones

• vibration

• breathing rhythm

• spoken or tone-based affirmations


Toy = portable regulation + silent communication.

⭐ 98.4 — CAREGIVER & PROFESSIONAL DEVICES INTERFACE (CPDI)


These are the command consoles for adults.


📲 A. Caregiver App (Parent / Guardian UI)


Panels:

• Today Overview (sleep, mood, load, hydration, risks)

• Behavior Forecast (88)

• Cognitive Load & Learning Readiness (90)

• Nonverbal → Meaning translation (83, 91)

• Health & Intake (85, 94)

• Appointments & Shift Logs (93)

• Live Alerts (overload, wandering, meltdown risk)


Actions:

• log notes

• adjust preferences

• confirm meds & meals

• mark events (meltdown, doctor visit, therapy)

• override AI suggestions (NI leads AI)


⸻


🧑‍⚕️ B. Professional Dashboard (Doctor / Therapist UI)


Views:

• symptom timeline

• intake history

• behavior patterns

• sensory triggers

• emotional memory map (95)

• medication & nutrition correlation (94)

• recommended questions/tests (85, 94)


Outputs:

• printable/secure summary for EMR

• graphs showing pattern, not just single events


⸻


🧑‍🏫 C. School / Teacher Interface


Panels:

• transition difficulty (89)

• classroom load (noise, crowd, movement) (87, 96)

• student emotional / behavior risk forecast (88, 95)

• suggested teaching window (90, 91)

• meltdown-safe zones / escape routes (96)


Outputs:

• simple traffic-light indicators for teachers

• recommended adjustments: break, reduce noise, change seat, use music, shorten instructions


────────────────────────


⭐ 98.5 — ENVIRONMENT & SENSOR DEVICES INTERFACE (ESDI)


This layer never “talks” like a human — it just senses and adjusts.


🎥 A. Cameras (Vision Edge Units)


Inputs:

• posture

• pacing

• freezing

• wandering direction

• proximity to exits

• gross-motor stimming patterns


Local Logic:

• posture → stress

• pacing → overload risk

• wandering angle → exit risk

• stillness → shutdown risk


Outputs:

• alerts to parent/teacher device

• triggers room-level adjustments (lights, sound, etc.)


No facial ID for surveillance —

Only emotional/behavioral state estimation for support.


⸻


🎙️ B. Microphones & Sound Sensors


Inputs:

• decibel level

• sudden spikes

• background chatter

• machinery hum

• echo/reverb


Local Logic:

• maps noise → sensory risk

• triggers early overstimulation warnings (88)


Outputs:

• “noise too high” alerts

• suggestion: headphones, move, lower volume

• can auto-lower smart speaker / TV


⸻


💡 C. Lights, Doors, IoT


Lights

• dim when overload risk rises

• shift to softer spectrum near bedtime or meltdown-prevention zones


Doors

• monitor open/close + child proximity (wandering)


Other IoT

• adjust room temp

• activate air purifier

• flag unsafe devices (hot stove, etc.)

⭐ 98.7 — DATA FLOW LOOP (D.F.L.) — HOW IT ALL MOVES


A typical live loop:

1. Camera + mic + wearable detect: pacing + rising noise.

2. F.I.K calculates:

– overstimulation rising

– meltdown risk = 68%

3. EchoAuth:

– sends gentle vibration to bracelet

– shifts toy to calming pattern

– dims room lights (if smart)

– plays selected music profile (92)

– notifies caregiver app: “Break recommended now.”

4. Caregiver taps “Confirm Break.”

5. System tracks result:

– meltdown avoided → marks intervention as successful.

6. F.I.K learns that this intervention works for this child in this context.


That’s NI–AI: human plus system, not system replacing human.

⭐ 98.8 — CHILD vs. ADULT UI PRINCIPLES


Child UI (Screen / Toy)

• low text, high symbols

• consistent layout

• predictable responses

• music + color + gentle motion

• zero clutter

• no complex menus


Adult UI (App / Dashboard)

• layered detail (quick view + deep drilldown)

• clear alerts

• easy logs

• daily summary

• secure role-based view (parent vs. therapist vs. teacher)


Both sides see what they need, nothing extra.
