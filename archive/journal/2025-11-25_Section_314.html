⭐ SECTION 314 — MULTI-CHANNEL SENSORY LOAD REGULATION ENGINE (MSLRE)


(NI-AI Patent Module)

────────────────────────────────────────


314.1 — Technical Field


This invention relates to real-time sensory analysis and adaptive modulation of environmental inputs for neurodivergent users.

More specifically, the MSLRE governs NI-AI’s ability to detect, measure, classify, and regulate multi-channel sensory load (audio, visual, motion, tactile, environmental) to prevent overstimulation.


This applies to:

• autistic children

• sensory-processing-sensitive individuals

• education and clinical environments

• home and community settings


⸻


314.2 — Purpose


Autistic children may experience overload from:

• sound

• light

• screen motion

• vibrations

• crowded environments

• multiple simultaneous sensory channels


When sensory load exceeds safe thresholds:

• communication breaks

• emotional regulation collapses

• panic, shutdown, or meltdown risks escalate

• medical/behavioral emergencies occur


MSLRE is designed to prevent these outcomes by providing:


✔ early overload detection

✔ multi-channel sensory scoring

✔ dynamic sensory reduction

✔ environment-specific modulation

✔ predictive safety alerts

✔ real-time caregiver notification


This engine is critical to NI-AI’s ASD support mission.


⸻


314.3 — Functional Definition


The MSLRE is a five-channel sensory control system:

1. Audio-Load Channel

• background noise

• sharp sounds

• repetitive sounds

• crowd volume

2. Visual-Load Channel

• screen brightness

• color intensity

• motion frequency

• flashing patterns

• environmental lighting

3. Tactile-Load Channel

• clothing pressure

• seat vibration

• temperature changes

• textures

4. Spatial-Motion Channel

• room movement

• crowd proximity

• object motion

5. Atmospheric Channel

• temperature

• humidity

• airflow

• pressure


Each channel generates its own sensory score, and the engine combines them to produce a single Sensory Load Index (SLI) ranging from 0–100.

314.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Multi-Channel Sensory Parser (MCSP)


Extracts raw sensory data from all available device inputs.


(b) Sensory Classification Engine (SCE)


Classifies signals into the five channels:

• Audio

• Visual

• Tactile

• Spatial

• Atmospheric


(c) Load Measurement Layer (LML)


Measures intensity, frequency, unpredictability, and duration.

Produces: Channel Load Score (CLS) for each channel.


(d) Personal Sensory Baseline Mapping (PSBM)


Every child has different thresholds.

PSBM holds:

• quiet tolerance

• brightness tolerance

• motion sensitivity

• temperature sensitivity

• stimming-to-overload markers


This personal baseline is ESSENTIAL for accuracy.


(e) Predictive Sensory Escalation Model (PSEM)


Predicts overload 30–120 seconds before it occurs.


Powered by:

• NSM

• UNDSE

• Real-time risk signals

• Historical overload patterns


(f) Adaptive Sensory Output Layer (ASOL)


Dynamically adjusts:

• screen brightness

• sound volume

• motion intensity

• animation frequency

• UI simplification

• color palette

• tactile feedback

• environmental prompts


(g) Overload Intervention Protocol (OIP)


Triggered if SLI passes threshold.

Actions include:

• calming auditory tones

• motion freeze

• low-sensory mode activation

• sending quick caregiver alert

• pausing overwhelming activities
