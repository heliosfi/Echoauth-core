# MCG_01_Judgment_Semantics.md

## Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) — Judgment Layer


Status: SEALED

## 1. Purpose & Scope


This document specifies the **judgment semantics** of the Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) layer.


MCG functions as a **constitutional authority resolver**.  

Its sole responsibility is to collapse potentially conflicting human and institutional signals into a **single, deterministic authority verdict**.


MCG:

- Does not execute actions

- Does not sequence execution

- Does not interpret meaning

- Does not optimize outcomes


MCG outputs **judgment only**, which is consumed downstream by the Crossroad Execution Gate (CEG).


---


## 2. Core Judgment Invariants (Always Enforced)


### Invariant 1 — Inversion of Trust

All requests are treated as **unauthorized by default**.


Authorization must be actively demonstrated by satisfying explicit role, context, and presence requirements.


Absence of proof is equivalent to refusal.


---


### Invariant 2 — Zero-Inference Policy

MCG is prohibited from inferring intent.


MCG may not:

- Guess meaning

- Interpret partial signals

- Resolve ambiguity through prediction


Ambiguous or indeterminate signals resolve to **NOT AUTHORIZED**.


---


### Invariant 3 — Resolution, Not Evaluation

MCG does not assess desirability, benefit, or correctness.


MCG asks only:

> “Does the current configuration of verified authority permit this specific motion?”


MCG never asks:

- “Is this a good idea?”

- “Is this optimal?”

- “Is this likely intended?”


---


### Invariant 4 — Human Authority Supremacy

All authority is human-anchored.


MCG may consider institutional or system inputs only insofar as they are:

- Explicitly permitted

- Context-bounded

- Subordinate to caregiver authority


---


## 3. Authority Resolution Matrix


MCG resolves authority using a strict precedence hierarchy.


| Signal Source | Authority Weight | Scope | Override Capability |

|---|---|---|---|

| Primary Caregiver (EchoAuth Verified) | Absolute | Global | May veto all other signals |

| Secondary Caregiver (Role-Bounded) | High | Context-specific | Valid only within defined domains |

| Institutional / Clinical Authority | Conditional | Policy-defined | Subject to caregiver override-lock |

| System / NI-AI Suggestion | Zero | N/A | Cannot authorize or override |


Notes:

- Authority does not accumulate across sources.

- Higher-precedence signals override lower-precedence signals.

- NI-AI outputs are advisory only and carry no authority.


---


## 4. Conflict Resolution Semantics


MCG resolves conflicts deterministically.


### 4.1 Caregiver vs Caregiver

- If two caregivers issue conflicting instructions:

  - **Primary caregiver authority prevails**

  - If authority tier is equal and conflict persists → **NOT AUTHORIZED**


---


### 4.2 Caregiver vs Institutional

- Caregiver authority always supersedes institutional signals.

- Institutional authority may only authorize when:

  - Explicitly enabled

  - Not contradicted by caregiver settings


Any contradiction collapses to **NOT AUTHORIZED**.


---


### 4.3 Absence or Loss of Signal

- Missing authority

- Expired credentials

- Stale presence verification


All resolve to **HOLD** or **NOT AUTHORIZED**, never execution.


---


### 4.4 Ambiguity

- Partial confirmations

- Conflicting context markers

- Unclear role applicability


Ambiguity resolves as **NOT AUTHORIZED**.


---


## 5. Escalation & Presence Verification


When authority cannot be resolved, MCG follows a fixed escalation path:


1. **HOLD**  

   No authorization issued. Downstream execution blocked.


2. **NOTIFICATION**  

   Presence verification request sent to primary caregiver.


3. **REFUSAL**  

   If verification is not received within the allowed stabilization window, the request is purged.


No silent continuation is permitted.


---


## 6. MCG → CEG Interface Contract


MCG outputs **only binary or ternary verdicts**:


- `AUTHORIZED`

- `NOT AUTHORIZED`

- `HOLD`


No explanatory context, confidence scores, or recommendations are passed downstream.


CEG is responsible for sequencing and execution control; MCG does not participate beyond judgment.


---


## 7. Prohibited Behaviors (By Construction)


MCG cannot:

- Execute actions

- Sequence execution

- Override CEG behavior

- Accumulate authority over time

- Learn or adapt authority rules

- Apply probabilistic or heuristic reasoning
