SECTION 368 — MULTI-CONTEXT BEHAVIOR FUSION ENGINE (MCBFE)


(Patent Language + Mini-Claims)


⸻


368.1 Technical Field


The present invention relates to systems and methods for multi-environment behavioral data fusion, specifically for neurodivergent user support. More particularly, it concerns a contextual signal ingestion, normalization, and temporal-relational fusion architecture enabling predictive modeling of behavioral risk, emotional regulation, and adaptive support.


⸻


368.2 Summary of the Invention


The MCBFE provides a unified methodology for:

1. Collecting heterogeneous behavioral signals from multiple environments (home, school, community, digital interactions, and wearables).

2. Normalizing all signal types into a common behavioral schema.

3. Encoding environment-specific behavior signatures to preserve contextual nuance.

4. Aligning behavior timelines from different environments on a shared temporal axis.

5. Computing fused vectors indicating risk, resilience, context tension, and cross-environment correlations.

6. Feeding fused insights into parent, teacher, and clinical dashboards, as well as predictive engines within NI-AI and EchoAuth.


The system enables unified, child-specific behavioral understanding without compromising privacy or autonomy.


⸻


368.3 System Architecture


The MCBFE comprises:


(a) Contextual Ingestion Layer (CIL)


Configured to ingest raw behavior events from:

• Home environment devices

• School dashboards and teacher inputs

• Community event sources

• Wearable devices

• Mobile applications and EchoAuth sessions


Each raw event is stored as a structured record tagged with:

• context_type

• timestamp_utc

• source_id

• raw_payload

• privacy_scope


(b) Behavior Event Normalization Layer (BENL)


Processes raw event records into a normalized structure containing:

• trigger_type

• intensity_score

• valence_score

• regulation_state

• duration_seconds

• actor_role

• annotations

• link_to_previous_event_id


(c) Context Signature Encoding Layer (CSEL)


Generates environment-specific signatures comprising:

• overload_frequency

• mean_intensity

• trigger_distribution

• recovery_time distribution

• protective_factor counts


(d) Temporal-Relational Fusion Core (TRFC)


Aligns normalized events into time-segmented timelines and computes:

• Inter-context correlations

• Cross-context synergy scores

• Cross-context tension scores

• A fused behavior vector (“BFV”) for the child


(e) Outcome & Risk Vector Generator (ORVG)


Produces:

• Overload risk predictions

• Recommended pre-event, in-event, and post-event supports

• Environment alignment scores


(f) Feedback & Adaptation Layer (FAL)


Receives parent/teacher feedback and recalibrates weighting functions while remaining constrained by the NI-AI doctrine and safety rails.

368.4 Functional Claims (Mini-Claims)


Claim 368.1


A system for fusing behavior signals from multiple physical and digital contexts into a unified behavioral model through event normalization and contextual signature encoding.


Claim 368.2


The system of Claim 368.1 wherein a temporal-relational fusion core aligns normalized behavior events into coherent time-segmented behavioral timelines.


Claim 368.3


The system of Claim 368.1 wherein cross-context correlation maps are generated to quantify behavioral relationships across environments.


Claim 368.4


The system of Claim 368.1 further comprising a fused behavior vector representing environment-specific risk, resilience, and regulatory profiles.


Claim 368.5


The system of Claim 368.1 wherein predictive risk vectors and recommended support actions are generated based on fused behavioral signals.


Claim 368.6


The system of Claim 368.1 wherein feedback from caregivers or educators adaptively recalibrates fusion weightings without exceeding predefined safety constraints.


Claim 368.7


The system of Claim 368.1 wherein child-specific privacy rules dynamically govern context inclusion, exclusion, and downstream data availability.
