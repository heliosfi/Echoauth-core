Purpose of This Diagram


To visually encode fail-closed behavior across all failure classes—technical, contextual, or human-initiated—without ambiguity.


If safety, coherence, or authority is compromised, motion ceases.


No retries without permission.

No silent recovery.

No residual autonomy.


⸻


Failure Entry Points (Any Time, Any Layer)


Failures may be detected at any point in the pipeline or session lifecycle.


Recognized Failure Triggers

• Ambiguous or incomplete inputs

• Instability detected (signal volatility, overload)

• Boundary or axiom violation

• Governance indeterminacy

• Safety invariant breach

• Human-issued stop / override


Rule (global):


All failures route directly to MCG.


⸻


Central Authority — MCG Failure Gate


Draw as a bold vertical gate labeled:

“MCG v3.1 — Failure Assessment Gate”


MCG evaluates:

• Severity

• Reversibility

• Human authority state


MCG does not attempt correction.

It decides whether motion is permitted to continue (almost always: no).


⸻


Mandatory Failure Outcomes (No Others Allowed)


A. Immediate Halt (Fail-Closed)

• Motion stops instantly

• No further processing

• Human review required


Label:

“HALT — Fail-Closed (Axiom 3 & 7)”


⬇️


➡️ Session Terminated


⸻


B. Safe Pause (Await Human Instruction)

• Temporary stop

• No state progression

• Explicit human decision required


Label:

“PAUSE — Await Human Authority”


⬇️


➡️ Session End or Resume (Human Only)


⸻


C. Reset / Stabilize

• Clears volatile state

• Dissipates kinetic energy

• No automatic resume


Label:

“RESET — Clear State”


⬇️


➡️ Session End


⸻


Human Override Path (Always Available)


From any node, Human Authority may:

• Pause

• Halt

• Terminate

• Decline continuation


Label near top:

“Human Authority Override — Absolute (Axiom 6)”


This path bypasses all internal logic and terminates motion immediately.

Label:

“Terminal Stop — No Carryover”
