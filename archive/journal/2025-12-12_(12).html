SECTION VI — COMPLIANCE, SAFETY & GOVERNANCE


6.1 Compliance Positioning (Clear + Defensible)


EchoAuth is a non-clinical, caregiver-guided support system designed to assist with non-verbal communication observation and documentation for children, including non-verbal and ASD users.


EchoAuth:

• Does not diagnose

• Does not treat

• Does not infer internal states

• Does not replace caregiver judgment


The system operates as supportive infrastructure, not a medical device or clinical decision system.


⸻


6.2 Child Safety & Caregiver Authority


Primary Safety Principle


All interpretation, decisions, and responses remain with the caregiver or parent.


EchoAuth:

• Never initiates interaction with a child

• Never responds autonomously to child behavior

• Never provides recommendations about child intent or condition


Children are beneficiaries, not operators.


Caregivers and parents are the sole authorized decision-makers.


⸻


6.3 Non-Verbal Communication Scope (Explicit)


EchoAuth supports observation and logging of non-verbal cues, including but not limited to:

• Gestures

• Vocalizations (non-linguistic)

• Movement patterns

• Environmental context


All entries are:

• Human-observed

• Human-recorded

• Human-reviewed


EchoAuth does not interpret meaning or assign emotional, cognitive, or psychological labels.


⸻


6.4 Governance Architecture (MCG Enforcement)


MCG (Melanin Cognitive Governance) provides system-level safeguards implemented as middleware.


Governance Controls:


Identity Anchoring

• Every system action is tied to a verified adult user

• No anonymous or child-initiated actions permitted


Interpretability

• All data is human-entered

• All system behavior is transparent and reviewable

• No black-box processing


Safety Gating

• Blocks:

• clinical inference

• emotional prediction

• behavioral diagnosis

• autonomous action

• Allows:

• observation storage

• historical review

• caregiver annotations

• export for review


Governance checks occur before data persistence or display.


⸻


6.5 Data Protection & Auditability


EchoAuth enforces:

• Role-based access control (RBAC)

• Least-privilege access

• Append-only audit logs


All actions are:

• Timestamped

• User-attributed

• Reviewable


Audit logs cannot be modified by standard users.


This supports:

• Internal accountability

• External review

• Grant and lab requirements


⸻


6.6 Regulatory Alignment (Non-Exhaustive)


EchoAuth is designed to align with:

• Child data protection principles

• Caregiver-first responsibility models

• Non-diagnostic digital health guidance

• Research and pilot program safety expectations


EchoAuth avoids regulated classifications by design, not by disclaimer.


⸻


6.7 Explicit Exclusions (Hard Guardrails)


The following are intentionally excluded from the MVP and any pilot deployment:


❌ Medical diagnosis

❌ Emotional inference

❌ Cognitive assessment

❌ Behavioral prediction

❌ Autonomous child interaction

❌ Child-controlled workflows

❌ Consciousness modeling


Any feature violating these constraints requires formal governance review.


⸻


6.8 Grant, Lab & Pilot Readiness Statement


EchoAuth is suitable for:

• Caregiver-supported environments

• Non-verbal communication research support

• Early-stage pilots

• Institutional review under non-clinical frameworks


The system prioritizes:

• Safety

• Accountability

• Transparency

• Human authority


EchoAuth is supportive infrastructure, not an evaluative system.
