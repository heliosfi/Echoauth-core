346.1 — Technical Field


The CENIE concerns systems and methods for interpreting autistic and neurodivergent nonverbal behaviors with context-dependent accuracy across multiple environments, including home, school, clinic, public spaces, and digital environments.


This module creates a unified interpretation model that adjusts meaning based on where the child is, who is present, and what the environmental demands are.


It is the world’s first cross-environment, context-aware nonverbal ASD communication interpreter.

346.3 — Functional Definition


CENIE is a multi-layer engine that:

1. Captures nonverbal signals from wearables, cameras, behavior logs, and environmental sensors

2. Classifies ASD-relevant nonverbal patterns

3. Maps each signal to different meanings depending on the environment

4. Predicts emotional/behavioral escalation

5. Adapts interpretation over time using NI-AI’s coherence core

6. Outputs actionable guidance to parents, teachers, therapists, or EchoAuth UI


This prevents mislabeling such as:

• “agitation” → actually overstimulation

• “defiance” → actually sensory avoidance

• “withdrawal” → actually shutdown

• “noncompliance” → actually delayed processing

346.5 — Multi-Environment Context Mapping


CENIE maps nonverbal behavior differently depending on where the child is:


Home Interpretation Layer (HIL)

• Safety assumed highest

• Behaviors likely mean comfort, boredom, or sensory seeking


School Interpretation Layer (SIL)

• Behaviors may indicate:

• overstimulation

• confusion

• delayed processing

• need for a break

• miscommunication with teacher


Clinic Interpretation Layer (CLIL)

• Behaviors may indicate:

• anxiety

• pain/discomfort

• exam stress

• sensory overload from tools


Public Interpretation Layer (PIL)

• Behaviors may indicate:

• fear

• overstimulation

• unsafe environment

• need to retreat


Digital/Device Interpretation Layer (DIL)

• Reading/typing delays

• Communication fatigue

• Cognitive overload
