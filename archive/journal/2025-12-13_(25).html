NIH SBIR / STTR ABSTRACT (HEALTH-TUNED)


EchoAuth × NI-AI: Safety-First Infrastructure Supporting Non-Verbal Interaction


Significance


Non-verbal individuals—particularly autistic children and others with communication differences—are frequently placed in care and educational environments that implicitly require speech, interpretation, or behavioral inference to proceed safely. These demands introduce pressure, misinterpretation, and escalation risk. Many digital health and assistive tools rely on predictive or optimization-based approaches that are poorly suited to non-verbal contexts and raise safety, bias, and auditability concerns.


There is a critical need for non-diagnostic infrastructure that protects non-verbal interaction by design—without inferring mental state, emotion, or intent.


⸻


Innovation


This project introduces a deterministic, safety-first control architecture that supports non-verbal interaction by removing the need for speech, explanation, or behavioral interpretation. The innovation is architectural rather than predictive: restraint is enforced structurally, and non-action (silence, stillness, withdrawal) is treated as a valid and successful outcome.


Unlike AI-driven systems that infer or optimize behavior, this system defaults to deny execution unless explicit authorization and human oversight are present. Decisions are transparent and auditable.


⸻


Approach


The system integrates three bounded components:

• MCG (Motion / Context Governance):

Deterministic management of physical presence, time, and transitions without psychological or behavioral inference.

• EchoAuth:

Authorization and boundary enforcement that prevents action without explicit conditions and human oversight.

• NI-AI (Natural-Intellect AI):

A read-only explanation and audit layer providing transparency without prediction, scoring, or autonomous control.


Optional wearables may be used during pilots solely for passive state reflection and orientation cues. Wearables have no authority, no triggers, and no inference pathways. The system operates safely without wearables.


Phase I will validate default-deny execution, correct handling of non-verbal interaction, graceful degradation under failure, and complete audit logging. No diagnostic claims, behavioral profiling, or predictive modeling will be developed or tested.


⸻


Expected Outcomes

• Demonstrated prevention of unsafe action under uncertainty

• Verified support for non-verbal interaction without escalation or pressure

• Complete, reviewable audit logs suitable for regulatory and institutional review


Failure to act under uncertainty is considered a successful outcome.


⸻


Relevance to NIH Mission


This work advances safe, equitable infrastructure supporting individuals with communication differences by reducing harm risk in real-world care and educational environments. The approach aligns with NIH priorities emphasizing safety, transparency, and responsible technology use—without making diagnostic or treatment claims.


⸻


Funding Alignment Statement (Use Verbatim)


Funds support validation of deterministic safety controls, authorization enforcement, auditability, and non-verbal interaction handling. No funds support diagnostic activity, predictive modeling, behavioral inference, or outcome optimization.
