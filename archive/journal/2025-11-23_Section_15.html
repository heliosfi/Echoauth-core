⭐ 15.1 Overview


The Medical Early Warning Engine (MEWE) identifies subtle physiological, behavioral, and environmental indicators that signal:

• dehydration

• fever onset

• acute pain

• internal discomfort

• abnormal breathing

• early infection

• sudden fatigue

• medical stress escalation

• gastrointestinal distress

• kidney-related stress signs

• pain masking (common in ASD)


MEWE provides early detection — BEFORE visible symptoms appear.


For neurodivergent children (especially nonverbal ones), this solves a problem that hospitals, pediatricians, and caregivers have struggled with for decades.

MEWE fuses data from multiple systems:


A. Physiological Pattern Indicators (Non-contact sensing)


Detected through movement + breath + posture changes:

• irregular breathing

• shallow breathing

• body tightening

• slow chest expansion

• slowed motor response

• posture collapse

• sudden stillness

B. Behavioral Pain Indicators


(NEVER detected by typical AI systems)

• inconsistent pacing

Certain sensory stress patterns reveal medical issues:

• sensory irritability ↑ during fever onset

D. Environment Factors

• hot day → dehydration risk ↑

Step 2 — Pain Behavior Correlation

Step 3 — Medical Baseline Comparison

• movement becomes delayed


Step 4 — Environment Risk Amplification


Adjusts interpretation:

Step 5 — NI–AI Medical Fusion Layer

⭐ 15.4 Medical States Classified by MEWE


MEWE identifies nine medical states:


1. Stable


No concerning signs.


2. Mild Physiological Stress


Breath changes, mild fatigue.


3. Sensory-Linked Medical Stress


Light/sound sensitivity triggered by internal discomfort.


4. Pain Suspected (Mild)


Micro-freezes, stiff posture.


5. Pain Suspected (Moderate)


Hand pressure to body, reduced movement.


6. Dehydration Risk


Breath shallow + posture collapse + sensory irritability.


7. Fever/Illness Pre-Onset


Lower energy + increased scanning gaze.


8. Acute Pain Signal


Rapid freezing, guarding behavior.


9. Medical Emergency Candidate


Shutdown + breathing irregularity + movement collapse.
