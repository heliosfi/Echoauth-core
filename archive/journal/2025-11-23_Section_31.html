⭐ 31.3 TIER 1 — Critical Safety & Medical Priority (CSMP)


(If any safety or medical rule activates, all other rules are paused.)


This tier includes:

• elopement risk

• wandering toward danger

• collapse detection

• dehydration risk

• fever spike

• irregular breathing

• shutdown with medical markers

• high-risk pacing

• traffic proximity alerts

• escape attempt in vehicle


When CSMP activates:


✔ wearable triggers strong vibration

✔ caregiver receives urgent alert

✔ toy activates emergency pulse

✔ safety engines override all others

✔ calming engines follow safety, not vice versa

✔ prediction systems switch into emergency mode


Nothing—NOTHING—overrides CSMP.


──────────────────────────────


⭐ 31.4 TIER 2 — Emotional & Sensory Stability Priority (ESSP)


(Used when safety is secure, but distress is rising.)


This tier includes:

• sensory overload

• fear

• confusion

• frustration

• shutdown onset

• overwhelm

• classroom stress

• transition anxiety

• public space overload


ESSP triggers:


✔ wearable calming patterns

✔ toy comfort pulse

✔ audio regulation tone

✔ environment modulation (lights, sound, crowd avoidance)

✔ caregiver suggestion: “Slow transition”, “Offer break”

✔ school hub sensory adjustments


ESSP protects mental state and prevents escalation.


──────────────────────────────


⭐ 31.5 TIER 3 — Behavioral & Preference Priority (BPP)


(Used only when Tier 1 and Tier 2 are stable.)


This tier handles:

• task refusal

• pacing

• communication attempts

• restlessness

• comfort preferences

• stimming patterns

BPP triggers:


✔ preference-based tones

✔ favorite music

✔ routine-based suggestions

✔ motivational cues

✔ communication support


This tier never overrides Tiers 1 or 2.

⭐ 31.6 Decision Conflict Resolution


If engines conflict, EDT resolves using strict precedence:


Case Example A — Emotional Distress vs Medical Risk

• child rocking from pain (medical)

• but crying loudly (emotional)


EDT chooses medical, because Tier 1 overrides Tier 2.


Case Example B — Sensory Overload vs Behavior Escalation

• noise spike

• pacing increases


EDT chooses sensory, because Tier 2 overrides Tier 3.


Case Example C — Preference vs Emotional Distress

• child prefers blue tone

• emotional fear detected


EDT chooses emotional, not preference.


Case Example D — Behavioral Refusal vs Safety

• child refuses to move

• but environment shows safety risk


EDT chooses safety, instantly.

⭐ 31.7 NI–AI Fusion Influence on the Triad


The caregiver’s Natural Intellect modifies:

• thresholds

• patterns

• interpretations

• meaning

• escalation timing

• personalized exceptions


But NI cannot override critical safety or medical rules.

This protects the child and protects you legally.


However NI can modify:

• calming tone selection

• sensory strategies

• emotional interpretations

• transition timing

• behavior meaning


Example:

If parent says:

“He paces when excited, not distressed.”

EchoAuth adjusts pacing interpretation in Tiers 2 and 3.

But if pacing occurs near a street → Tier 1 wins.
