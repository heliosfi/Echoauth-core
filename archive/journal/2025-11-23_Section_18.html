⭐ 18.5 Communication Modes (Patent Critical)


CAE produces communication in FOUR forms:


A. Expressive Output Mode (EOM)


The child’s internal signals → output as speech/text.


B. Predictive Communication Mode (PCM)


Outputs meaning BEFORE the child escalates.


Example:

“Rising confusion. Child likely needs instructions repeated.”


C. Emergency Communication Mode (ECM)


Triggered by MEWE + MESE:

• pain

• dehydration

• medical collapse

• high danger

• elopement risk


Example:

“Critical alert: medical distress likely. Child still, breathing irregular.”


D. Environment-Adaptive Mode (EAM)


CAE modifies communication based on environment:

• quiet environments → text

• school → icon + text combo

• transitions → audio to caregiver

• medical → symptom summary
