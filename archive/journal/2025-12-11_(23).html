⭐ SECTION 1 — STATE MACHINE OVERVIEW


There are 5 primary states:


BASELINE

SENSITIVE

OVERLOAD

REGULATE

LOCKOUT


And one meta-state:


SAFETY OVERRIDE (forced state)


These 6 cover every possible ASD, caregiver, or system condition.


⸻


⭐ SECTION 2 — STATE DEFINITIONS


1. BASELINE


Normal regulated interaction.


Conditions:

• child stable

• caregiver coherent

• environment neutral


Actions:

• normal prompts

• standard chamber lighting/sound

• default unlock logic


⸻


2. SENSITIVE


Child or caregiver shows early signs of dysregulation.


Conditions:

• bio signal inconsistency

• rising stress markers

• tone shift


Actions:

• softer audio

• reduced sensory load

• NI-AI increases monitoring

• unlock slowed or delayed


⸻


3. OVERLOAD


Child or caregiver enters high dysregulation.


Conditions:

• pacing

• rapid breathing

• emotional overload

• high stress signature

• BioBridge instability


Actions:

• unlock suspended

• calming environment mode

• redirect caregiver

• offer regulation prompts


⸻


4. REGULATE


Active calming mode.


Conditions:

• child recovering

• caregiver stabilizing

• signals normalizing


Actions:

• slow visuals

• guided breathing prompts (optional)

• smooth chamber lighting


⸻


5. LOCKOUT


System refuses unlock due to:

• safety risk

• unclear intention

• caregiver emotional threat

• system mismatch


Actions:

• hard stop

• no unlock until stability restored

• guidance to caregiver


⸻


META-STATE: SAFETY OVERRIDE


Activated when:

• any safety invariant is violated

• ambiguity persists

• child endangered

• caregiver dysregulated


Overrides EVERYTHING.
