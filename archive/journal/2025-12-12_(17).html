📄 DOCUMENT 1 — SYSTEM OVERVIEW (1 PAGE)


EchoAuth — Caregiver-Governed Support for Non-Verbal Children


Overview


EchoAuth is a caregiver-governed system designed to support non-verbal children by providing safe, accountable access control and structured observation tools. The system improves consistency, accuracy, and safety in caregiving environments by ensuring that all interactions are mediated by verified caregivers or parents.


EchoAuth does not interpret internal states or make clinical decisions. It exists to support caregivers in observing patterns, responding consistently, and maintaining accountability over time.


⸻


Who It Serves

• Non-verbal children

• Parents and caregivers

• Care environments (home, educational, therapeutic support settings)


⸻


The Problem


Caregivers supporting non-verbal children often face:

• Inconsistent responses across caregivers

• Loss of observational history over time

• Increased risk of misinterpretation

• Lack of structured accountability in interactions


These challenges create safety risks and reduce the effectiveness of care.


⸻


What EchoAuth Does

• Provides secure, identity-anchored access for caregivers and parents

• Enables structured logging of non-verbal communication cues as observations, not interpretations

• Maintains reviewable interaction history for accountability

• Supports consistent, safe caregiver responses over time


⸻


What EchoAuth Does NOT Do

• No diagnosis

• No emotional or cognitive inference

• No autonomous decision-making

• No clinical or therapeutic recommendations


EchoAuth is not a diagnostic, therapeutic, or autonomous decision-making system.

📄 DOCUMENT 2 — GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE (MCG) (1 PAGE)


Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG)


Purpose


MCG is a decision-governance framework that ensures all system actions remain identity-anchored, interpretable, and safe. It governs how decisions are allowed to occur, not what intelligence the system possesses.


⸻


Layer 1 — Identity Anchoring

• All access and actions are tied to verified caregiver or parent identities

• Role-based permissions determine what actions are allowed

• No anonymous or autonomous execution


Result: Clear accountability at all times.


⸻


Layer 2 — Interpretability & Constraint

• All system actions are logged and reviewable

• No opaque or unexplained system behavior

• Outputs are constrained to predefined, human-reviewable functions


Result: Transparency and traceability.


⸻


Layer 3 — Safety Gating

• Blocks unsafe, coercive, or unauthorized actions

• Prevents child-facing execution without caregiver authorization

• Enforces non-clinical system posture by design


Result: Harm prevention and institutional safety.


⸻


Governance Rule


Identity Verified → Action Constrained → Safety Validated → Execution Allowed

If any condition fails, execution stops.
