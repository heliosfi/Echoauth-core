NI-AI × EchoAuth × MCG

1. Rationale and Significance


AI systems operating in human-facing environments frequently fail in situations characterized by ambiguity, silence, or non-verbal interaction. In these cases, conventional systems often escalate behavior, infer intent without ground truth, or generate responses that exceed their authority.


These failure modes present heightened risk in caregiver-mediated contexts involving children and neurodivergent individuals, where non-action, restraint, and explainability are often the safest outcomes.


The NI-AI system addresses this gap by implementing a governance-first, fail-closed architecture that separates:

• observation,

• authorization, and

• execution,


and defaults to pause and caregiver notification when uncertainty exists.


This Phase I research focuses on evaluating whether such a system can operate predictably, stably, and transparently under controlled conditions without introducing autonomous decision-making.


⸻


2. Technical Approach Overview


Phase I will evaluate NI-AI as a decision-support and explanation layer, not as an autonomous agent.


Key architectural constraints:

• All execution is gated by MCG (Motion / Context Governance)

• EchoAuth enforces role-based authorization; default state is deny

• NI-AI provides explanation only; it does not independently decide outcomes

• Silence and non-participation are treated as valid system states


The research strategy evaluates system behavior through controlled interaction simulations rather than live clinical deployment.


⸻


3. Experimental Design by Specific Aim


Aim 1: Stability and Restraint Under Non-Verbal Conditions


Objective:

Determine whether NI-AI maintains stable behavior when faced with ambiguous or non-verbal input.


Methodology:

• Simulated interaction sessions with reduced, delayed, or absent responses

• Controlled introduction of ambiguous signals (e.g., silence, repetitive input)

• Measurement of system outcomes: allow / pause / deny


Metrics:

• Frequency of pause vs escalation

• Response consistency across sessions

• Absence of inferred intent or speculative output


Success Criteria:

System consistently resolves ambiguity through pause/observe rather than escalation or inference.


⸻


Aim 2: Caregiver-Mediated Decision Support Evaluation


Objective:

Evaluate whether NI-AI explanations improve caregiver understanding without exerting decision pressure.


Methodology:

• Caregivers review NI-AI explanation outputs for pause/deny states

• Assessment of explanation clarity and perceived usefulness

• Comparison against baseline systems lacking explicit governance explanations


Metrics:

• Caregiver comprehension scores

• Reported confidence in system restraint

• Reduction in perceived urgency to act


Success Criteria:

Caregivers report improved clarity and trust without feeling overridden or directed.


⸻


Aim 3: Auditability and Governance Verification


Objective:

Verify that NI-AI decisions are fully traceable, reviewable, and governance-bound.


Methodology:

• Simulated policy changes and uncertainty conditions

• Review of audit logs for state transitions

• Verification of immutable logging for allow/deny/pause outcomes


Metrics:

• Completeness of logs

• Accuracy of explanation-to-decision mapping

• Successful termination or pause under injected faults


Success Criteria:

All system actions are explainable, logged, and compliant with governance rules.


⸻


4. Data Handling and Analysis

• No identifiable personal data required for Phase I

• All interaction data anonymized or simulated

• Session data is time-bounded and ephemeral

• Analysis focuses on system behavior, not user characteristics


Statistical analysis will emphasize:

• outcome distributions (pause vs action)

• consistency across trials

• deviation detection


⸻


5. Risk Assessment and Mitigation


Potential Risks:

• Overly conservative behavior limiting system usefulness

• Misinterpretation of explanations by caregivers

• Technical instability during long sessions


Mitigations:

• Clearly defined success thresholds

• Explanation templates standardized

• Immediate pause/termination pathways enforced


No clinical or behavioral risk to participants is anticipated.


⸻


6. Phase I Deliverables


At the conclusion of Phase I, Helios F.I LLC will deliver:

• A validated governance-first AI interaction framework

• Empirical data on restraint, stability, and explainability

• A documented methodology for safe AI evaluation in non-verbal contexts

• Recommendations for Phase II expansion (if appropriate)


⸻


7. Alignment with NIH Expectations


This research:

• Avoids diagnostic or predictive claims

• Prioritizes safety and explainability

• Supports caregiver-mediated contexts

• Advances trustworthy AI methodologies


NI-AI is evaluated as a research infrastructure, not a clinical tool.
