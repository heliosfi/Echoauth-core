⭐ SECTION 372 — STATE TRANSITION AUDITOR (STA)


The system that verifies, logs, and certifies every NI-AI cognitive state transition.


⸻


372.1 — Technical Field


The State Transition Auditor (STA) pertains to artificial intelligence safety, auditability, state-machine regulation, cognitive-state validation, and meta-governance oversight within context-regulated AI systems built for ASD support, parent-controlled adaptive reasoning, and multi-environment compliance.


The STA ensures that all internal cognitive state transitions are:

• monitored

• verified

• logged

• traceable

• externally auditable

• aligned with parent/caregiver permissions


⸻


372.2 — Purpose


The STA exists to answer four critical safety questions:


1. Did NI-AI switch states when it was supposed to?


(e.g., S1 → S2 “Sensory-Sensitive Mode”)


2. Did NI-AI avoid switching states when it was NOT authorized?


(e.g., S2 → S4 “Analytical Mode” — forbidden without permission)


3. Is the reasoning behind the transition defensible, traceable, and safe?


4. Can regulators, parents, schools, and medical staff independently verify this?


Without the STA, state transitions cannot be legally certified.


⸻


372.3 — Functional Definition


The STA is a meta-layer that:

1. Observes every requested state transition

2. Validates transition legality using pre-defined boundary rules

3. Confirms external authorization (parent → EchoAuth → Doctrine → SRK)

4. Records the reasoning behind the transition

5. Rejects unauthorized or unsafe transitions

6. Logs transition outcomes for audits


It acts as the independent witness inside NI-AI.


⸻


372.4 — Inputs


The STA receives data from:


• State Transition Requests (STRs)


Generated when NI-AI detects a need to change cognitive state.


• Permission Arbitration Engine (PAE)


Confirms parent or caregiver authorization level.


• Multi-State Cognitive Boundary Framework (371)


Provides allowed vs forbidden transitions.


• Emotional State Engine (ESE)


Provides emotional justification for transitions.


• SRK (Stability Regulation Kernel)


Provides safety thresholds.


• EchoAuth Identity Vector


Ensures the transition request came from the correct user.


⸻


372.5 — Outputs


The STA produces:


• State Transition Certificate (STC)


Formal proof the transition was legal and safe.


• Denied Transition Record (DTR)


Logged anytime NI-AI blocks an unsafe or unauthorized transition.


• Transition Safety Score (TSS)


Internal numeric rating used by SRK and Doctrine.


• Reasoning Trace Packet (RTP)


Snapshot of WHY the state transition occurred.


• Compliance Log Entry (CLE)


Stored in the regulatory ledger (Section 378).


⸻


372.6 — Internal Mechanics


The STA operates through five modules:


⸻


(a) Transition Intent Validator (TIV)


Confirms the purpose of the transition:

• safety

• emotional regulation

• sensory overload

• parent instruction

Invalid intent = automatic denial.


⸻


(b) Boundary Compliance Engine (BCE)


Cross-checks the transition with 371’s matrix of allowed/forbidden moves.


⸻


(c) Authorization Checker (ACX)


Validates:

• parent override settings

• EchoAuth identity

• permissions level

• environmental rules (school/home/hospital)


If ANY mismatch → transition stops.


⸻


(d) Safety & Load Verifier (SLV)


Checks:

• emotional load

• sensory overload probability

• cognitive strain

• risk of meltdown

• risk of drift


If thresholds exceed safe limits → STA blocks the move.


⸻


(e) Forensic Transition Recorder (FTR)


Creates a forensic-grade record for regulators, doctors, or parents.


⸻


372.7 — Safety Logic


The STA ensures NI-AI:

• can NEVER self-escalate into higher reasoning states

• cannot escape sensory-safe boundaries

• cannot jump between cognitive domains without approval

• cannot bypass caregiver authority

• cannot behave unpredictably in emergencies

• cannot drift toward AGI-like autonomy


It also guarantees:

• full transparency

• traceability

• reproducibility

• external auditability
