STAFF BEHAVIOR RULES


Inside the Physical + Wearable Space

MCG · NI-AI · EchoAuth


Core Rule (Non-Negotiable)


Staff do not decide outcomes. Staff protect the process.


If staff behavior can override the system, the system is broken.


⸻


1️⃣ STAFF ROLE DEFINITION


Staff are:

• Facilitators

• Observers

• Safety enforcers


Staff are not:

• Decision-makers

• Interpreters of intent

• Executors of urgency

• Substitutes for the system


⸻


2️⃣ STAFF AUTHORITY (WHAT THEY CAN DO)


✅ Allowed Actions

• Guide users between zones

• Explain what a zone is for (not what to do)

• Help users pause or return to Observation

• Revoke or stop execution if safety requires

• Call attention to system state changes

• Document incidents factually


Stopping is always allowed. Advancing is never forced.


⸻


3️⃣ STAFF RESTRICTIONS (WHAT THEY CANNOT DO)


❌ Forbidden Actions

• Push users forward through zones

• Authorize on someone’s behalf

• Interpret emotional state as readiness

• Create urgency (“we need to move now”)

• Bypass physical or digital boundaries

• Use wearables as control tools


If staff feel pressure to “help it along,” they must stop.


⸻


4️⃣ STAFF LANGUAGE RULES (CRITICAL)


❌ Do NOT say

• “You’re ready now”

• “Just one more step”

• “This will help you”

• “We need to hurry”

• “The system wants you to…”


✅ DO say

• “We can stay here”

• “Nothing needs to happen right now”

• “We can return to observation”

• “Stopping is okay”

• “The system is waiting”


Language must lower momentum, not increase it.


⸻


5️⃣ STAFF & WEARABLE INTERACTION


Staff may:

• Point out what a wearable is indicating

• Help a user notice state changes


Staff may NOT:

• Interpret wearable signals as commands

• Say “your wearable says you should…”

• Override wearable neutrality


Wearables inform awareness — not behavior.


⸻


6️⃣ STAFF RESPONSE TO DISTRESS OR ESCALATION


When distress appears:

• Do not escalate system state

• Do not attempt to “push through”

• Guide calmly back to Observation or Entry

• Allow time without explanation pressure


The correct response to distress is less system, not more.


⸻


7️⃣ STAFF FAILURE HANDLING


If staff make a mistake:

• System should already block unsafe action

• Staff acknowledge and log the incident

• No justification, no retroactive approval


Mistakes are handled through structure, not blame.


⸻


8️⃣ STAFF TRAINING RULE (SIMPLE)


Train staff on three truths only:

1. Nothing has to happen

2. Stopping is success

3. Boundaries protect everyone


If staff understand those three, the system holds.


⸻


9️⃣ STAFF AUDIT EXPECTATIONS


Staff actions are:

• Logged (when interacting with system)

• Reviewable

• Bounded by role


This protects staff as much as it protects users.


⸻

If you feel the urge to hurry, you are already outside the system’s intent.


That line corrects behavior instantly.
