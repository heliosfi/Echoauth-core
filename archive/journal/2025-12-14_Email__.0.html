📄 1-PAGE TECHNICAL SUMMARY (ATTACHMENT)


Project Title


NI-AI: Governed AI Decision-Support for Neurodivergent Child Communication


Applicant


Helios F.I LLC

Principal Investigator: Nicholas B. Carty


Problem Statement


Many neurodivergent children—particularly minimally verbal or non-verbal—experience breakdowns in communication that lead to caregiver uncertainty, misinterpretation, and escalation. Existing AI systems are not designed for sensory safety, governance, or caregiver-mediated restraint, making them unsuitable for this population.


Proposed Solution


NI-AI is a non-autonomous, governed AI decision-support system that provides explanations, stabilization cues, and pause recommendations to caregivers during child interactions. The system does not diagnose, predict, or act independently.


All outputs are gated through:

• MCG (Manual Control Governance): final authority for allow / pause / deny

• EchoAuth: role-based authorization (default = deny)


NI-AI functions only as a read-only explanation layer.


Technical Innovation

• Deterministic, rule-based logic (no self-modification)

• Fail-closed defaults under uncertainty

• Sensory-safe pacing and tone constraints

• Explicit support for non-verbal communication states

• Full auditability and explainability


Phase I Objectives

1. Validate technical feasibility of governed, fail-closed AI logic

2. Demonstrate stable, non-escalatory behavior in long sessions

3. Evaluate caregiver comprehension and trust in explanations

4. Confirm alignment with federal AI safety and privacy expectations


Expected Outcomes

• Demonstrated feasibility of restrained AI support

• Evidence of reduced caregiver uncertainty

• Clear path to Phase II caregiver-assisted deployment


Human Subjects


Phase I involves no clinical intervention and no diagnostic use.

Interaction testing is limited to adult caregivers and simulated data.


Alignment


This work aligns with NIH interests in:

• Neurodevelopmental support technologies

• Assistive and adaptive systems

• Safe, explainable AI in sensitive contexts
