Section 352 — Kernel Meaning Security Layer (KMSL)


352.1 — Title


Kernel Meaning Security Layer (KMSL)


352.2 — Technical Field


A computing safety layer that governs meaning formation and interpretation within NI-AI kernels, ensuring that all decoded input (including nonverbal, emotional range, and environmental signals) is processed securely, reproducibly, and without clinical diagnosis, while remaining controlled by parent-override governance and consent-based data storage.


352.3 — Core Problem


Modern AI systems fail to secure the meaning layer of child-expressed, caregiver-interpreted inputs, which causes:

• Meaning hallucination,

• Cross-domain inference contamination,

• Mis-scoring of child needs as medical symptoms,

• Untraceable interpretation chains,

• Caregiver mistrust and institutional rejection.


352.4 — Solution Summary


KMSL ensures NI-AI:

✔ interprets expressions as communication, not symptoms,

✔ locks meaning strictly to parental intent corridors,

✔ encrypts meaning-vectors before they inform decisions,

✔ produces a verifiable reasoning trail for audits and pilots.


352.5 — System Functions


KMSL performs:

1. Meaning vector encryption

2. Authorized-lane meaning verification

3. Cross-engine contamination blocking

4. Context-based signal-mode switching

5. Secure archival of behavioral baselines

6. Tamper-proof reasoning trail generation


352.6 — Internal Modules


(a) Meaning-Vector Encryptor (MVE)

Encrypts meaning signals into non-modifiable hashes.


(b) Lane-Bound Meaning Interpreter (LBMI)

Restricts all meaning interpretation to parent authorized lanes only.


(c) Cross-Kernel Isolation Shield (CKIS)

Prevents rules and inferences leaking between kernels or domains.


(d) Audit-Ready Meaning Certificate Emitter (AMCE)

Generates meaning certificates traceable for legal or pilot review.


(e) Parent-Override Meaning Lock (POM-Lock)

Parental authority supersedes all machine assumptions.


352.7 — Outputs


KMSL emits:

• Encrypted Meaning Vector (EMV)

• Meaning Safety Certificate (MSC)

• Drift Probability Score (DPS)

• Reasoning Audit Trail (RAT)

• Parent Override Proof Hash (POPH)


352.8 — Safety Specification


KMSL prohibits:

• medical diagnosis,

• psychological diagnosis,

• unsupervised meaning expansion,

• recursive self-optimization,

• cross-domain speculative synthesis.


It ensures:

• Parent-controlled interpretation

• Consent-bound data retention

• Secure vector translation only

• Auditable, non-AGI reasoning


352.9 — Claims


Claim 352.1 (Independent)

A system for encrypting and securing meaning-vectors before they inform NI-AI decision engines.


Claim 352.2 (Independent)

A meaning-interpretation corridor that only reasons inside parent-authorized lanes.


Claim 352.3 (Dependent)

The system of Claim 352.1, wherein meaning-vectors are encrypted into tamper-proof hashes.


Claim 352.4 (Independent)

A cross-kernel security shield preventing meaning contamination between NI-AI kernel states or environment modes.
