EchoAuth — Internal Governance & Intelligence Alignment Appendix


MCG / NI-AI Canonical Reference


1. Architectural Intent


EchoAuth is designed as a governance-first system.

Intelligence is subordinate to authority.

Automation is subordinate to safety.


The system assumes:

• Human judgment is primary

• Uncertainty is unavoidable

• Defaults must favor protection over optimization


All technical decisions inherit from this ordering.


⸻


2. Core Stack Overview


Ordering (non-negotiable):

1. MCG — Melanin Cognitive Governance

Decision authority & invariant enforcement

2. NI — Natural Intellect Layer

Human-defined intent, rules, and approvals

3. AI — Bounded Computational Support

Organization, assistance, and load reduction


No layer may bypass or override the layer above it.


⸻


3. MCG (Governance Layer)


MCG functions as the judge, not the processor.


Responsibilities:

• Enforce invariants

• Validate permissions

• Halt execution under ambiguity

• Log decisions and transitions


MCG Invariants (examples):

• No action without explicit authorization

• No inference about individuals

• No silent state changes

• No irreversible actions without confirmation


If a request violates an invariant, execution halts.


⸻


4. NI Layer (Human Authority Encoding)


The NI layer represents human-defined intent, including:

• Parent or guardian rules

• Approved routines and permissions

• Role definitions (who can do what)

• Contextual boundaries (where/when actions apply)


NI inputs are:

• Explicit

• Revocable

• Auditable


NI does not “learn” autonomously.

Changes require human action.


⸻


5. AI Layer (Bounded Support)


AI components are strictly non-authoritative.


Permitted functions:

• Organizing approved information

• Surfacing relevant options

• Reducing cognitive load

• Maintaining continuity across sessions


Prohibited functions:

• Behavioral prediction

• Diagnostic classification

• Autonomous decision-making

• Hidden optimization objectives


AI output is advisory only and must pass through MCG validation.


⸻


6. Decision Flow (Canonical)

1. Input received (caregiver action, system event)

2. MCG checks invariants

3. NI-defined rules are evaluated

4. AI may assist with organization or suggestion

5. MCG validates final action

6. Action executes or halts

7. Event is logged


There is no shortcut path.


⸻


7. Uncertainty Handling


Uncertainty is treated as a stop condition, not a prompt to guess.


When uncertainty is detected:

• The system pauses

• Authority is requested

• No fallback automation occurs


This prevents:

• Silent errors

• Overreach

• Harmful assumptions


⸻


8. Safety Posture


EchoAuth assumes:

• Good intentions are insufficient

• Misuse is possible

• Drift occurs without enforcement


Therefore:

• Defaults are conservative

• Permissions are explicit

• Logs are immutable

• Failures are contained


Protection precedes convenience.


⸻


9. Audit & Compliance Readiness


System design supports:

• Clear role delineation

• Transparent decision paths

• Human accountability

• Non-clinical classification


EchoAuth is coordination infrastructure, not assessment technology.


⸻


10. Scalability Constraints


EchoAuth scales through:

• Repetition of governed patterns

• Stability of rules

• Predictability of enforcement


It does not scale through:

• Data extraction

• Behavioral profiling

• Model autonomy expansion


Scale without harm is a design requirement.


⸻


11. Canonical Rules (Do Not Break)

• Governance > Intelligence

• Authority > Automation

• Safety > Speed

• Clarity > Cleverness

• Halt > Guess


Any change that violates these rules is rejected.
