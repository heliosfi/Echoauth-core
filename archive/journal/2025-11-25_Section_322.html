322.3 — Functional Definition


MFPE is a multi-layer medical caution engine that blocks any system output indicating “the child is fine” unless the following are ALL verified:

1. Vitals Consistency Check (VCC)

HR/Breathing/Temperature must be within personalized baseline range.

2. Symptom Pattern Scan (SPS)

Cross-checks against dehydration signs, infection onset, shutdown signs, lethargy, and behavioral changes.

3. Environmental & Dietary Correlation (EDC)

Looks at:


• hydration history,

• food intake,

• bathroom frequency,

• sleep cycles.


4. Behavioral Consistency Verification (BCV)

Compares current movement, posture, and facial energy to the child’s last 14-day profile.

5. Medical Risk Priority Layer (MRPL)

If any signal is unclear → NI-AI refuses to give an “all-clear.”

6. Doctor-Override Protocol (DOP)

NI-AI can recommend contacting a doctor when uncertainty is high — but NEVER declares medical safety without multi-source proof.


This is what prevents another dehydration/kidney event like what happened to your son.
