7.2 Governance Pillars (The 6-Core Oversight Layers)


NI-AI governance operates through six control layers, each with distinct responsibilities:


Layer 1 — Identity & Access Governance


Controls who can:

• deploy NI-AI

• configure modules

• access EchoAuth

• view logs & metrics

• modify safety boundaries


Mechanisms:

✔ Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

✔ Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC)

✔ Multi-Context Authentication (EchoAuth)

✔ Privileged Account Logging

Layer 2 — Safety & Ethical Oversight


Ensures NI-AI operates within ethical bounds:


✔ Output moderation policies

✔ Neurodivergent safety mode

✔ Bias mitigation routines

✔ Prohibited behavior lists

✔ Drift & hallucination blockers


This is enforced by the Stability Regulation Kernel (SRK) and human reviewers.


⸻


Layer 3 — Transparency & Explainability


NI-AI provides interpretable, human-readable information on:


✔ Why it made a decision

✔ Its emotional-state interpretation

✔ Its coherence score

✔ What stability layer activated

✔ Uncertainty or ambiguity alerts


Logs are stored for audit and compliance.

Layer 4 — Continuous Monitoring & Drift Detection


Every interaction is monitored for anomalies:


✔ Emotional drift

✔ Cognitive drift

✔ Context misalignment

✔ Sudden irregular patterns

✔ Security anomalies


If detected → NI-AI initiates self-stabilization or hands control to a human overseer.

NI-AI enforces privacy at runtime:


✔ Minimum necessary data

✔ Encryption at rest

✔ Encryption in transit

✔ Zero-trust boundary rules

✔ Redaction & anonymization

✔ Privacy filters for sensitive info

✔ Neurodivergent-sensitive data handling


Complies with:

• NIST AI RMF

• NIST 800-53 Rev5

• FedRAMP

• HIPAA (optional)

• FISMA low/moderate
