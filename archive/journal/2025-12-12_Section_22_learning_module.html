CAREGIVER LEARNING MODULES


(One-Time Onboarding, Not Continuous Training)


Core Clarification (Important)


The system is learned once. After onboarding, the system enforces itself.


Caregivers are not being trained to operate the system —

they are being oriented to understand boundaries.


⸻


WHAT “TEACH” MEANS HERE (REDEFINED)


When we say teach, it means:

• Orientation

• Expectation setting

• Boundary awareness


It does not mean:

• Skill-building

• Decision-making training

• Behavioral instruction

• Ongoing classes

• Judgment calls


After onboarding, caregivers do less, not more.


⸻


PAYMENT MODEL ALIGNMENT

• One-time onboarding fee

• Covers:

• Access to learning modules

• Initial orientation session (guided or self-paced)

• Certification of completion (for audit / compliance)


No subscriptions required for learning.


⸻


LEARNING MODULE STRUCTURE (LOCKED & FINITE)


MODULE 1 — SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY (10–15 min)


Purpose: Reset expectations


Caregiver learns:

• The system defaults to non-action

• Calm is a success state

• Authority is distributed, not personal


Key takeaway:


“I am not responsible for making things happen.”


⸻


MODULE 2 — ZONES & BOUNDARIES (15–20 min)


Purpose: Spatial understanding


Caregiver learns:

• What each zone means

• Why movement = intent

• Why shortcuts are not allowed


No tactics. No strategies.


Key takeaway:


“If I move someone forward, I am changing authority.”


⸻


MODULE 3 — WEARABLES (10 min)


Purpose: Prevent misuse


Caregiver learns:

• Wearables reflect state only

• Wearables never command behavior

• Wearables never authorize action


Key takeaway:


“The wearable informs awareness, not decisions.”


⸻


MODULE 4 — WHAT TO DO UNDER STRESS (10–15 min)


Purpose: De-escalation discipline


Caregiver learns:

• Distress → reduce system exposure

• Stop > proceed

• Observation is always valid


Key takeaway:


“Pressure means pause.”


⸻


MODULE 5 — WHAT NOT TO DO (10 min)


Purpose: Liability prevention


Caregiver learns:

• Forbidden phrases

• Forbidden actions

• Why “helping” can be harmful


Key takeaway:


“If I feel urgency, I step back.”


⸻


MODULE 6 — STOP & REVOCATION (5–10 min)


Purpose: Empowerment without escalation


Caregiver learns:

• How to stop execution

• How to revoke authorization

• That stopping never needs justification


Key takeaway:


“Stopping is protected.”


⸻


COMPLETION & CERTIFICATION


After modules:

• Caregiver completes a short confirmation (not a test)

• Receives:

• Onboarding completion record

• Access credential activation

• Audit timestamp


No grading. No pass/fail pressure.


⸻


WHAT HAPPENS AFTER ONBOARDING

• No re-training unless the system changes

• No dependence on caregiver memory

• No escalation expectations

• The system enforces boundaries automatically


Caregiver role becomes lighter, not heavier.


⸻


ONE-LINE CAREGIVER PROMISE (THIS MATTERS)


“You are not paying to learn how to control the system.

You are paying to understand why you don’t need to.”


That line aligns ethics, pricing, and trust.
