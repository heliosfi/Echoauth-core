PAGE 20 — OPTIONS EXTENSION (CANONICAL v1.0)


Greeks-Aware • Spread-Safe • Time-Decay-Controlled • Non-Drifting


This page extends SniperBot to options without breaking Pages 16–19.

Options add leverage and hidden risk, so the system becomes stricter, not looser.


Rule: Options trading is allowed only when the same core gates pass plus options-specific gates.


⸻


1) Options Trade Card (Additional Required Fields)


A standard Trade Card becomes an OptionsTradeCard with these required fields:

• underlying_ticker

• option_type (CALL / PUT)

• expiration_date

• strike

• contract_multiplier (typically 100)

• contracts (computed)

• mid_price (not just last price)

• bid, ask, spread

• spread_percent

• iv_percentile (or proxy)

• delta (target range)

• gamma_risk_flag

• theta_per_day

• max_slippage_allowed

• fill_rule (MID only / LIMIT only / no market orders)


If any field is missing → invalid card → cannot execute.


⸻


2) Options-Specific Gates (Must PASS)


SpreadGate (Options) — Hard Block


Fail if:

• spread_percent > threshold (ex: > 8–12% depending on liquidity)

• Wide spreads on near-dated contracts

• Low open interest / low volume


Action:

• Reject card (no execution)


⸻


LiquidityGate (Options) — Hard Block


Fail if:

• OI below minimum

• Volume below minimum

• Bid/ask “jumps” (illiquid quotes)


Action:

• Reject card


⸻


FillGate — No Market Orders


Pass only if:

• Order type = LIMIT

• Limit price anchored to MID

• Slippage cap is enforced


Fail action:

• Reject card


⸻


DeltaGate — Control Exposure


Allowed delta ranges (defaults; adjustable):

• Swing: 0.35–0.60

• Day/Scalp: 0.20–0.45

• Lotto/speculative: DISALLOWED in Auto-Mode


Fail action:

• Reject card


⸻


ThetaGate — Time Decay Control


Fail if:

• Theta per day exceeds limit for the strategy window

• Expiration too close for intended hold time (unless explicitly allowed)


Default:

• Auto-Mode disallows 0DTE / 1DTE unless whitelisted by operator


Fail action:

• Reject card


⸻


IVRegimeGate — Avoid Buying Expensive Premium


Fail if:

• IV percentile too high for long premium entries (calls/puts)

• Or IV crush risk present (pre-earnings long options)


Action:

• Reject card or force strategy switch to defined alternative (manual only)


⸻


3) Options Risk Engine (Different from Shares)


Options sizing is based on premium-at-risk, not just stop distance.


Risk inputs

• account_equity

• risk_percent

• max_contracts

• premium_per_contract = mid_price × 100


Contracts calculation (basic)


max_risk_dollars = account_equity × (risk_percent / 100)

contracts = floor(max_risk_dollars / (premium_per_contract))


Reject if:

• contracts < 1

• contracts > max_contracts

• premium too high for the plan


Note: For stop-based options trades, use the tighter of:

• premium-at-risk limit OR

• underlying-structure stop mapped to option pricing sensitivity


⸻


4) Stop/TP for Options (Two Valid Methods)


Method A — Underlying-Based (Preferred)

• Stops and targets are defined on the underlying chart

• Option exits are triggered when underlying levels are hit


Why:

• Options price can distort; the underlying is truth.


⸻


Method B — Premium-Based (Allowed in Strict Form)

• Stop = % loss on premium (ex: -25% / -35%)

• TP1/TP2 = % gain levels (ex: +20% / +40% / runner)


This is allowed only if:

• Liquidity is strong

• Spread is tight

• The strategy window is short


Auto-Mode defaults to underlying-based exits.


⸻


5) Auto-Mode Restrictions for Options (Stricter)


Auto-Mode options execution is allowed only if:

• SpreadGate PASS

• LiquidityGate PASS

• FillGate PASS (LIMIT/MID)

• DeltaGate PASS

• ThetaGate PASS

• IVRegimeGate PASS

• Expiration >= minimum days (default 7+ unless manually overridden)


Default disallowed in Auto-Mode:

• 0DTE

• Earnings week long-premium (unless hedged strategy exists)

• Very low volume contracts

• Wide spread contracts


⸻


6) Options Trade Management (In-Trade)


In IN_TRADE for options:

• No averaging down

• No widening stops

• Partial exits encouraged

• Runner allowed only with trailing rule or underlying-based trail

• Time-stop recommended (options decay makes time a risk factor)
