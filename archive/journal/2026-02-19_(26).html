EchoAuth — Runtime Governance Constraint Layer


Technical Abstract


EchoAuth is a deterministic runtime authorization gate designed for vulnerable care contexts where execution must be strictly governed by parent-anchored authority. The system addresses a core structural gap in existing safety approaches: most systems attempt to improve behavior through monitoring, filtering, or adaptive intelligence, while still allowing execution under ambiguity. EchoAuth instead enforces authority resolution before execution occurs.


The architecture is built on strict separation of powers. The Identity Binding Layer (UMCE) establishes actor and role context but has no execution capability. The Management and Control Gate (MCG) is the sole authority-resolution layer, mapping requests into one of four governed outcomes: Approve, Deny, Hold, or Escalate. The Control and Execution Gate (CEG) performs no judgment; it executes only when presented with a cryptographically bound, single-use authorization grant issued by the MCG.


Each authorization grant is time-bounded, nonce-protected, and bound to a specific instruction hash, envelope version, and target. Execution cannot proceed without a valid, fresh grant. Ambiguity defaults to Hold. Authority channel loss defaults to Hold. Instruction drift triggers Halt. Concurrency is managed through exclusive state locks to prevent race-condition execution.


The system does not rely on behavioral compliance, intent inference, or post-hoc veto mechanisms. Unauthorized execution is structurally prevented rather than procedurally discouraged. Parent or root authority defines governed envelopes in advance, allowing scalable delegation without relinquishing oversight.


EchoAuth prioritizes verifiable authority over convenience. In high-stakes care environments, the cost of unauthorized intervention exceeds the cost of governed delay. By decoupling identity, judgment, and execution, and by making unsafe shortcuts mechanically impossible, EchoAuth provides a deterministic governance constraint layer suitable for environments where safety must be architectural, not aspirational.
