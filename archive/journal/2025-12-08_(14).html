📄 PAGE 34 — CONSENT FRAMEWORK (GUARDIAN, SCHOOL, CLINICAL, RESEARCH)


EchoAuth Consent, Permission, and Ethical Use Architecture


EchoAuth requires explicit, informed, role-specific consent before the system becomes active for any user under age 18 or any neurodivergent adult requiring supported decision-making.


This framework includes five parallel consent streams:

1. Guardian Consent (Primary)

2. Minor Assent (Developmentally Appropriate)

3. School or District Authorization

4. Clinical/Provider Authorization

5. Research & IRB Consent (Pilot Programs Only)


Every channel reinforces data minimization, role-specific permissions, and fail-closed safety behavior.


⸻


SECTION A — GUARDIAN CONSENT (PRIMARY CONTROL LAYER)


Required for all uses—home, school, clinical, and research.


A1. Core Guardian Permissions


Guardians must explicitly approve:

• Activation of EchoAuth for their child

• Collection of minimal communication metadata

• Use of device-level preferences (sensory mode, layout)

• Storage of communication logs for up to 30 days

• Role-based access for teachers/clinicians interacting with the system


A2. Guardian Rights


Guardians retain:

• The right to pause or disable the system at any time

• The right to delete all stored data permanently

• The right to export session summaries

• The right to approve or deny school/clinical access

• The right to revoke all permissions immediately


A3. Consent Language (Formal)


“I understand that EchoAuth is an assistive communication and identity-support tool designed for autistic and neurodivergent individuals. I authorize the use of this technology for the purpose of improving communication, supporting safety, and enabling consistent interaction patterns. I understand that EchoAuth does not collect biometrics, behavioral profiles, location data, photos, or audio recordings. I may revoke consent at any time.”


⸻


SECTION B — MINOR ASSENT (AGE-ADAPTED)


Required for children 7–17 when developmentally appropriate.


Assent must be simple, sensory-safe, and non-coercive.


B1. Example Assent Prompt


“This tool helps you show what you need or how you feel.

You can use it if you want to.

You can stop any time.”


B2. Requirements

• No child can be forced to use EchoAuth.

• Children must understand they can stop at any time.

• Assent is re-confirmed at the start of each new pilot session.


⸻


SECTION C — SCHOOL / DISTRICT CONSENT (FERPA-LAYER)


Required for educational deployment.


C1. School Responsibilities


Schools must confirm:

• FERPA-compliant handling of communication logs

• Controlled permission for each teacher or aide accessing EchoAuth

• Integration with existing IEP supports

• Secure device management (passwords, physical protection, network settings)


C2. Teacher/Aide Authorization


Each instructor must sign:

• A role-specific access agreement

• A confidentiality and non-disclosure statement

• A training acknowledgment confirming they understand:

• sensory considerations

• predictable communication timing

• safe intervention procedures


C3. Data Sharing Boundary


Schools may access:

• communication summaries

• preference settings

• usage patterns


Schools may NOT access:

• medical information

• private family logs

• any disallowed personally identifiable information


⸻


SECTION D — CLINICAL CONSENT (HIPAA-LAYER)


Required for use in ASD therapy centers, psychology practices, or clinics.


D1. Clinician Responsibilities


Clinicians must confirm:

• EchoAuth is NOT used for diagnosis

• EchoAuth is NOT used for psychological profiling

• Only minimal data required for communication support is accessed

• Clinical notes remain separate from EchoAuth’s system


D2. Authorization Boundary


Clinicians may view:

• user-selected communication

• session context patterns

• assistive communication logs


Clinicians may NOT:

• upload PHI into EchoAuth

• connect EchoAuth to EHR systems

• use EchoAuth logs as clinical assessments


⸻


SECTION E — RESEARCH CONSENT (IRB PILOT PROGRAMS)


Required only when data is used for research or system validation.


E1. IRB Requirements

• Full IRB protocol approval must precede UX or communication studies

• All user data is anonymized before analysis

• Guardians receive clear explanation of study purpose

• Participants may withdraw at any time

• No compensation tied to system use for minors


E2. IRB-Grade Consent Language


“This study examines how assistive communication tools support autistic individuals. Participation involves using EchoAuth during structured activities. No personal identifiers, medical data, photos, or audio/video recordings will be collected. Participation is voluntary, and you may withdraw at any time without penalty.”


E3. Risk Assessment


EchoAuth qualifies as minimal risk because:

• no biometrics collected

• no emotional inference

• no behavioral scoring

• communication metadata only


⸻


SECTION F — CONSENT LOGGING & ENFORCEMENT


EchoAuth enforces:


1. Multi-Layer Verification


Consent must be confirmed in three layers before activation:

• guardian

• institution (school/clinic)

• system signature (EchoAuth internal)


2. Automatic Consent Expiration


Consent must be renewed:

• every academic year for schools

• every 6 months for clinical settings

• every study cycle for research


3. Consent Revocation


If consent is revoked:

• system immediately stops

• all stored data is deleted within 60 seconds

• all institution access is revoked

• the system returns to inactive state
