SECTION 332 — MULTI-CHANNEL EXPRESSION SAFETY LAYER (MCESL)


(NI-AI Patent Module)

──────────────────────────────────


332.1 — Technical Field


The MCESL relates to systems and methods for regulating, moderating, and stabilizing all outward expressions generated by an AI system—across audio, visual, textual, kinetic, and haptic channels—with specific design constraints for interaction with autistic children and neurodivergent users.


This module ensures non-overstimulating, predictable, clinically safe communication behaviors.


⸻


332.2 — Purpose


The MCESL prevents:

• sudden loud audio spikes,

• bright or flashing visual output,

• emotionally inconsistent speech patterns,

• overwhelming animation or UI shifts,

• tone or style mismatches,

• unpredictable expressive changes,

• cross-channel overload (too many signals at once).


The purpose is to protect ASD children, maintain sensory stability, and ensure federal clinical compliance.


⸻


332.3 — Functional Definition


The MCESL is a unified expression control layer that:

1. Monitors every outgoing communication channel.

2. Enforces safety ceilings for volume, brightness, emotion, intensity, rhythm, and pace.

3. Synchronizes tone, pacing, and clarity across all channels.

4. Prevents overstimulation by automatically lowering or muting expressions that exceed user-specific thresholds.

5. Detects child stress signals and auto-adjusts expression style.

6. Standardizes NI-AI’s “communication persona” so the child never receives mixed signals.


⸻


332.4 — Inputs


MCESL receives information from:

• Sensory Overload Engine (Section 12)

• Emotional State Recognition Module (Section 14)

• Behavioral Prediction Engine (Section 11)

• NI-AI Doctrine Intent Controls

• Caregiver-defined Sensory Profile

• Device/Environment Thread (D-Thread)

• Purpose/Intent Thread (P-Thread)


The system adjusts expression based on:

• current environment,

• child’s stress score,

• sound sensitivity level,

• light sensitivity level,

• emotional volatility score,

• time-of-day routine,

• child’s historical patterns.


⸻


332.5 — Outputs


The MCESL produces:

• Calibrated Speech Output (CSO)

• Sensory-Safe Visual Output (SSVO)

• Low-Intensity Animation Mode (LIAM)

• Soft-Rhythm Audio Patterns (SRAP)

• Expression Safety Certificate (ESC)

• Auditable Expression Log (AEL)


These outputs form the communication foundation for NI-AI within EchoAuth.


⸻


332.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Cross-Channel Synchronization Grid (CCSG)


Aligns text, tone, visuals, and animations so the child receives one unified, stable message.


(b) Stimulus Intensity Governor (SIG)


Sets dynamic caps for:

• audio volume

• color brightness

• animation speed

• emotional expression strength

• movement rhythm


(c) Overstimulus Suppression Unit (OSU)


Automatically lowers intensity when:

• heart rate rises

• body tension increases

• repetitive stimming intensifies

• stress facial markers appear

• environmental noise increases


(d) Sensory-Adaptive Output Engine (SAOE)


Matches NI-AI expression style to:

• child’s profile

• specific environment

• child’s current sensory load

• time-of-day routines


(e) Safe Persona Lock (SPL)


Ensures NI-AI’s communication remains:

• soft

• consistent

• predictable

• calm

• emotionally non-volatile

• never surprising


(f) Expression Audit Layer (EAL)


Logs every spoken word, displayed frame, and tone shift for:

• compliance

• caregiver review

• clinical validation

• safety certification
