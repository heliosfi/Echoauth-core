EchoAuth — Dependent Claim Set


(Claim Group 3: Scope Expansion Without Drift)


These claims depend from Claim Group 1 (System) and Claim Group 2 (Method) and are written to survive examiner scrutiny.


⸻


A. Role & Authority Hierarchy (Caregiver-Centered)


Claim 3.

The system of claim 1, wherein the predefined set of authorized human roles includes a hierarchical authority structure in which parental or legal guardian authorization supersedes caregiver, educational staff, or clinical staff authorization.


Claim 4.

The system of claim 1, wherein role-based authority is contextually bounded by at least one of time, location, activity type, or institutional setting.


Claim 5.

The method of claim 2, wherein conflicting authorization inputs from multiple authorized human actors cause execution to be refused pending external human resolution.


⸻


B. School Environment Protection (Education-Safe)


Claim 6.

The system of claim 1, wherein authorized educational staff are granted limited authority to initiate actions only within a defined school environment and only during verified presence periods.


Claim 7.

The system of claim 1, wherein actions affecting the protected individual in a school setting are refused when parental or guardian authorization is absent, expired, or overridden by restriction.


Claim 8.

The method of claim 2, wherein continuity of authorization is invalidated upon transition between home and educational environments unless revalidated by an authorized human actor.


⸻


C. Clinical & Support Context (Non-Diagnostic, Examiner-Safe)


Claim 9.

The system of claim 1, wherein authorized clinical support staff are restricted to operational coordination actions and are prohibited from initiating diagnostic or treatment-related determinations.


Claim 10.

The system of claim 1, wherein the execution control gate enforces refusal when a requested action exceeds the authorized role scope of a clinical support staff member.


Claim 11.

The method of claim 2, wherein clinical context authorization is limited to presence-validated sessions and expires automatically upon session termination.


⸻


D. Continuity & Absence Safeguards (Safety Core)


Claim 12.

The system of claim 1, wherein continuity assessment includes detection of authorized human absence and automatically revokes execution permission upon absence detection.


Claim 13.

The system of claim 1, wherein historical authorization data is prohibited from being used as a substitute for real-time presence verification.


Claim 14.

The method of claim 2, wherein urgency signals or repeated requests do not override refusal when authorization conditions are unmet.


⸻


E. Refusal & Escalation as a Feature (Critical Differentiator)


Claim 15.

The system of claim 1, wherein refusal events are logged with timestamps, role identifiers, and refusal causes for audit and accountability purposes.


Claim 16.

The system of claim 1, wherein escalation pathways route control exclusively to authorized humans and prohibit automated conflict resolution.


Claim 17.

The method of claim 2, wherein refusal is treated as a successful execution outcome under conditions of ambiguity or authority conflict.
