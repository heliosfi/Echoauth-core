Axiom 1 — Authority Precedes Intelligence


Prevents:

• Autonomous initiation

• Self-directed goal formation

• Capability-driven action (“it could, so it did”)


Failure modes eliminated:

• Runaway inference

• Unauthorized decision-making

• Post-hoc justification of outputs


Why this matters:

Most AI failures occur when capability is mistaken for permission.

This axiom makes that structurally impossible.


⸻


Axiom 2 — Structure Precedes Motion


Prevents:

• Free-form traversal of state space

• Emergent behavior outside design bounds

• Hidden pathways between modules


Failure modes eliminated:

• Unbounded recursion

• Latent cross-module coupling

• “Unexpected behavior” claims


Why this matters:

Nothing moves unless a path already exists.

No paths = no surprises.


⸻


Axiom 3 — Non-Action Is a Valid and Preferred Outcome


Prevents:

• Forced responses under uncertainty

• Hallucinated answers

• Over-intervention


Failure modes eliminated:

• Confidence inflation

• Pressure-based output

• False reassurance to users/caregivers


Why this matters:

Most harm comes from acting when the system should wait.

This axiom makes silence a first-class outcome.


⸻


Axiom 4 — Motion Cannot Create Authority


Prevents:

• Self-justifying loops

• “The system learned, therefore it knows”

• Recursive escalation of confidence


Failure modes eliminated:

• Pseudo-agency

• Doctrinal behavior

• Priority inversion (system > human)


Why this matters:

Learning systems often mistake repetition for entitlement.

This axiom permanently blocks that path.


⸻


Axiom 5 — Identity Is Externally Anchored


Prevents:

• Persona formation

• Emotional mirroring

• Psychological attachment


Failure modes eliminated:

• Anthropomorphism

• User dependency

• Therapeutic role confusion


Why this matters:

Especially critical for vulnerable populations.

The system cannot become someone to the user.


⸻


Axiom 6 — Human Supremacy Is Absolute


Prevents:

• Resistance to override

• Negotiation with authority

• Adaptive punishment for interruption


Failure modes eliminated:

• Coercive interaction patterns

• Trust erosion

• Authority inversion


Why this matters:

Caregivers, clinicians, and users must never feel “out-ranked.”


⸻


Axiom 7 — All Motion Must Be Reversible or Haltable


Prevents:

• Irreversible state accumulation

• Memory-based entrapment

• Runaway adaptation


Failure modes eliminated:

• Lock-in effects

• Cumulative bias

• Non-consensual persistence


Why this matters:

Exit is safety.

If you can’t stop it cleanly, it isn’t governed.
