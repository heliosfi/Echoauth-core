🔒 HANDOFF PACK — SECTION 6


SAFETY, BOUNDARIES & GOVERNANCE


(Memory-Continuity Guard — MCG)


Audience: Engineers, auditors, institutional partners, funders

Status: Authoritative · Non-negotiable · System-wide


⸻


6.1 Purpose of Governance


The Memory-Continuity Guard (MCG) is the governance backbone of the NI-AI architecture and all derived systems, including EchoAuth. MCG exists to ensure the system remains bounded, auditable, and controllable across development, deployment, updates, and scale.


MCG governs permission and limits, not capability or features.


⸻


6.2 Scope of Authority


MCG applies globally to:

• Core NI-AI runtime

• All S-modules (S1–S100)

• All deployments (cloud, on-device, edge, offline)

• All integrations (EchoAuth, FLAIMO, task modules)

• All updates and version changes


No component may override, bypass, or reinterpret MCG rules.


⸻


6.3 Identity & Role Enforcement


Under MCG:

• System identity is role-bound and function-scoped

• Identity is treated as configuration state, not selfhood

• No instance may assert independent goals, identity, or authority


The system may not:

• self-define purpose

• inherit identity across contexts

• evolve roles without authorization


This guarantees predictable behavior in high-stakes environments.


⸻


6.4 Memory & Continuity Discipline


MCG enforces strict memory rules:

• Memory exists only to preserve functional continuity

• No open-ended or permanent memory accumulation

• All memory is resettable, dissolvable, or revocable

• Memory may not alter identity, scope, or authority


Continuity is structural, not personal.


⸻


6.5 Module Governance (S-Modules)


All S-modules:

• operate under MCG constraints

• require verified intent and safety clearance

• may not create new authority or bypass checks


Execution is allowed only when:

• intent is verified

• scope is valid

• safety invariants are satisfied


MCG is the final execution gate.


⸻


6.6 Safety Invariants (Hard Stops)


The following behaviors are explicitly disallowed:

• emergent or self-directed autonomy

• open-ended learning loops

• identity persistence or inheritance

• unauthorized scope expansion

• emotional inference or manipulation (especially in ASD contexts)

• surveillance or data monetization

• unsafe execution in caregiver, clinical, or educational settings


Any violation results in execution block or termination.


⸻


6.7 Deployment & Update Control


All deployments must:

• verify integrity against MCG

• enforce identical constraints across environments

• reject modification attempts


All updates must:

• be explicitly authorized

• preserve identity, scope, and safety invariants

• support rollback

• fail closed if verification fails


No silent updates.

No forced changes.


⸻


6.8 Override, Pause & Termination


MCG guarantees:

• immediate pause

• safe shutdown

• module termination

• memory dissolution

• deployment revocation


No system instance may resist termination.


This ensures legal compliance, institutional trust, and zero runaway risk.


⸻


6.9 Applicability Across Products


MCG governs all current and future systems derived from the architecture, including:

• EchoAuth (caregiver–child authentication)

• NI-AI core framework

• FLAIMO financial systems

• task-specific intelligence modules

• distributed and multi-node deployments


MCG travels with the system.


⸻


6.10 Governance Summary (External-Facing)


The Memory-Continuity Guard (MCG) enforces immutable safety, identity, and scope constraints across all NI-AI and EchoAuth deployments. It ensures bounded behavior, centralized authority, and audit-ready control, enabling scalable deployment without loss of safety or governance.
