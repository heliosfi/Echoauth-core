Elaboration on MCG Governance Details

Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) v3.0 serves as the core decision-governance layer within EchoAuth and NI-AI systems, ensuring all processes remain safe, deterministic, explainable, and aligned with human authority. Below, I elaborate on its key details, drawing from its locked architecture, roles, invariants, logic, outputs, and positioning. This is structured for clarity, suitable for engineers, clinicians, funders, and reviewers.

1. Architectural Positioning and Pipeline Integration

MCG is positioned as the “decision judge” in the 7-stage EchoAuth pipeline, sitting precisely between interpretation (Stage 4: NI-AI Interpretation) and final safety verification (Stage 6: Safety Invariant Engine, SIE). It does not handle raw input sensing (Stages 1-3: Input Acquisition, BioBridge Extraction, State Stabilization Engine) or execution (Stage 7: Chamber State & Action Engine).

• Full Pipeline Reminder:

1 Input Acquisition: Raw biological/environmental signals.

2 BioBridge Extraction: Neutral feature conversion.

3 State Stabilization Engine (SSE): Noise correction and normalization.

4 NI-AI Interpretation: Pattern framing (descriptive, non-judgmental).

5 MCG Governance: Evaluates safety, permission, coherence.

6 Safety Invariant Engine (SIE): Redundant invariant checks.

7 Chamber State & Action Engine (SCE): Governed outputs only.

This placement ensures MCG acts as a mandatory gate: no progression without explicit approval, preventing unaligned actions in sensitive contexts like non-verbal ASD support.

2. Core Function and Single-Question Focus

MCG’s sole responsibility is to evaluate: “Is it safe, permitted, and coherent to proceed right now?”

• It processes outputs from prior stages (e.g., stabilized signals and interpreted patterns) but never generates raw predictions or judgments.

• Emphasis on determinism: Decisions are rule-based, not probabilistic or learned, to enable full traceability and testing.

• In non-verbal/neurodivergent scenarios, it prioritizes signal clarity over assumption, defaulting to pause if ambiguity arises.

3. The Three Governance Roles (Always Active)

MCG operates through a triad of interdependent roles, ensuring balanced, multi-faceted oversight:

• Navigator: Determines directional alignment with declared intent or future state. Frames “where to head” without dictating actions.
Example Output: “Proceed toward stabilization mode; defer non-essential.”

• Guardian: Enforces ethical, safety, and boundary constraints. Detects risks like instability, overload, or identity mismatch. Holds absolute veto power.
Example Output: “Hold — signal coherence below threshold; escalate to human.”

• Architect: Translates evaluations into explainable, human-readable logic. Provides rationale and structured next steps (not commands).
Example Output: “Pause for 30 seconds → reassess stability → log justification.”

These roles run concurrently for every decision, creating redundancy without complexity.

4. Safety Invariants (Hard-Coded, Non-Overrideable Rules)

MCG embeds unbreakable invariants to prevent harm, misalignment, or overreach:

• No guessing on unclear or incomplete data (defaults to reassess/pause).

• No action during detected emotional, physiological, or field instability.

• No cross-identity confusion (e.g., child signals ≠ caregiver authority).

• No emotion creation, manipulation, or labeling.

• Human authority is always final (MCG cannot self-approve escalations).

• Fail-safe defaults: Uncertainty → halt; always prioritize safe inaction over incorrect progression.

• System stops gracefully on any violation (e.g., log, notify, memory wipe if terminated).

These are implemented as independent checks, separate from roles, for defense-in-depth.

5. Decision Boundary Logic (Simplified, Testable Pseudocode)

MCG’s logic is explicit and predictable, using conditional branching to enforce invariants:

IF risk > threshold → SAFETY HOLD (Guardian veto; log and notify)

ELIF signal coherence < minimum → PAUSE / REASSESS (Navigator defer; Architect explain)

ELIF emotional/physiological instability detected → REGULATE (stabilize first; no action)

ELIF field/context instability detected → RESET (return to baseline; human input required)

ELSE → PROCEED WITH GOVERNED DIRECTION (triad-aligned; constrained output only)

• Thresholds are predefined (e.g., coherence minimum = 80% signal fidelity).

• Every branch produces traceable rationale.

• Logic is fully testable via simulations (e.g., inject ambiguity → verify halt).

6. Outputs and Traceability

MCG never produces free-form or raw data. Outputs are structured governance objects for auditability:

• Direction (from Navigator: e.g., “Toward support mode”).

• Warnings/Constraints (from Guardian: e.g., “Boundary: No identity crossover”).

• Explanation + Next Steps (from Architect: e.g., “Rationale: Instability detected; Reassess in 30s”).

• State Justification (for logs: full trace of invariants checked).

All outputs are logged immutably, enabling post-hoc reconstruction by reviewers or clinicians.

7. What MCG Explicitly Avoids (Boundary Definitions)

To prevent scope creep:

• Not diagnostic: No labeling of states, emotions, or conditions.

• Not influential: No manipulation of user affect or behavior.

• Not autonomous: No self-initiated actions; always gated.

• Not a replacer: Augments caregivers/clinicians, never substitutes.

• Not predictive: Frames patterns only, no intent/behavior forecasting.

MCG governs processes, not people — ensuring ASD-safe, ethical operation.

8. Status, Readiness, and Evolution

• Readiness: Prototype-ready (75-80% scaffolded; engineer handoff viable).

• Why It Matters: Makes EchoAuth certifiable (e.g., HIPAA-aligned logs), clinically defensible (invariant-protected), and investor-credible (testable boundaries).
