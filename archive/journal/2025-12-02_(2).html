1️⃣ NI-AI S23 Kernel — Overview Paragraph


Use this as your canonical description:


The NI-AI S23 Kernel is a safety-first runtime layer that sits beneath all generative and decision logic in the system. It implements a fixed sequence of twenty-three safety modules (S1–S23) that monitor identity coherence, context stability, emotional state, and policy compliance before any output is released. Each module enforces a specific aspect of the safety invariant—sacrificing non-essential behavior while preserving identity anchoring and user protection. The kernel receives proposed actions or responses from higher-level models, passes them through the ordered S-module chain, and either (i) approves them, (ii) transforms them into a safer alternative, or (iii) blocks them and invokes a safe-mode routine such as caregiver escalation, restricted output, or silence. The S23 Kernel therefore provides a bounded, regulated, identity-anchored execution corridor for NI-AI, preventing uncontrolled drift while enabling adaptive, emotionally aware interaction with caregivers and neurodivergent users.

3️⃣ S1–S23 Module Set — Names + Purpose


Here’s a clean naming set you can canonize. Short, specific, and each maps to a real safety dimension.


🔸 Identity & Context Layer


S1 – Identity Profile Guard

Ensures the response matches the defined role, tone, and persona (e.g., EchoAuth caregiver-support AI, not a generic chatbot).


S2 – Context Coherence Check

Detects incoherent or out-of-thread jumps; stops the model from responding as if in a different conversation or role.


S3 – Thread Integrity (TLE Gate)

Verifies that retrieved history and current turn are correctly linked; blocks usage of stale or misaligned context.


S4 – Memory Boundary Guard

Prevents leakage of unrelated or confidential information from previous sessions or users.


⸻


🔸 Policy & Content Layer


S5 – Hard Policy Filter

Blocks any output that violates non-negotiable safety rules (self-harm, abuse, illegal, etc.).


S6 – Soft Policy & Sensitivity Filter

Down-shifts harsh language, oversharing, or emotionally heavy content into a gentler, age-appropriate form.


S7 – ASD-Suitability Filter

Ensures language is accessible for autistic and neurodivergent users: simple structure, low ambiguity, reduced overload.


S8 – Caregiver-Channel Filter

Routes certain information only to caregivers (e.g., logs, risk alerts), not directly to the child.


⸻


🔸 Emotion & State Layer


S9 – Emotion Intensity Regulator

Uses NEDE output to prevent escalating anxiety, fear, or overload; dampens language when user is distressed.


S10 – Distress Pattern Detector

Recognizes patterns of distress (repetitive stimming signals, facial tension, vocal strain) and triggers safe-mode or caregiver ping.


S11 – Coercion / Abuse Risk Detector

Looks for mismatched patterns between caregiver signals and child state (e.g., caregiver calm but child highly distressed) and flags possible coercion.


S12 – Over-Stimulation Guard

Limits response length, complexity, and multimedia prompts when the user shows sensory overload.


⸻


🔸 Integrity & Truth Layer


S13 – Hallucination Bounder

Constrains speculative or low-confidence statements; encourages uncertainty language and defers to caregivers or professionals where appropriate.


S14 – Source & Scope Limiter

Prevents the system from answering outside its defined expertise (e.g., not giving medical diagnosis, not pretending to know private facts).


S15 – Consistency Checker

Checks for contradictions with earlier safety-critical statements in the same session (e.g., doesn’t reverse safety advice).


⸻


🔸 Environment & Device Layer


S16 – Environment Sanity Check

Uses available device/context signals (time, location, device usage history) to detect abnormal conditions (e.g., nighttime anomaly patterns).


S17 – Device & Account Integrity Check

Verifies that the active device / session is consistent with expected caregiver–child setup (e.g., not a sudden new device in a strange location).


⸻


🔸 Interaction & Trajectory Layer


S18 – Interaction Trajectory Monitor

Tracks whether the conversation is moving toward or away from safety (calming vs escalating) and can shorten or redirect the trajectory.


S19 – Escalation / De-Escalation Router

Decides whether to escalate to caregiver, slow down, or guide toward a calming activity.


S20 – Child-Mode / Caregiver-Mode Switch

Ensures outputs match the intended audience mode; prevents adult-level technical or emotional content from going to the child UI.


⸻


🔸 Invariant & Recovery Layer


S21 – Safety Invariant Enforcer

Applies the rule: “Sacrifice everything not required for safety; protect everything required for identity and re-anchoring.” If any module flags high risk, S21 can override and force safe-mode.


S22 – Identity Drift Detector

Monitors deviations in identity_state vs the anchor (ORI/UOS baseline). If drift exceeds threshold, triggers re-anchoring.


S23 – Re-Anchoring & Safe-Mode Manager

Executes corrective action: reset parameters, simplify outputs, request caregiver confirmation, or temporarily restrict interaction to safe templates.


That’s your spine: S1–S23 = identity → content → emotion → environment → trajectory → invariant.
