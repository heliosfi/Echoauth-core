⭐ SECTION 316 — AUTONOMOUS SOCIAL WITHDRAWAL & SAFE-EXIT PROTOCOL (ASW-SEP)


(Real-Time ASD Safety Module • NI-AI Patent Block)

────────────────────────────────────────


This module protects autistic children and sensory-sensitive users in real-world environments by detecting when they need to withdraw, exit, or create space — before a meltdown or panic occurs.


It is a behavioral safety module, not AGI, not autonomy — just protection, prediction, and calm redirection.


────────────────────────────────────────


316.1 — Technical Field


The invention concerns adaptive AI safety mechanisms engineered to:

• detect emotional & sensory overload,

• predict imminent shutdown or meltdown behavior,

• identify environmental escape routes,

• recommend safe micro-exits,

• guide the caregiver through the process.


────────────────────────────────────────


316.2 — Purpose


Autistic users often cannot verbally express:

• “I need space,”

• “This is too much,”

• “I need to leave,”

• “People are too close,”

• “I’m overwhelmed.”


ASW-SEP provides a silent, intelligent protection layer to:

• prevent sensory escalation,

• provide social distance strategies,

• guide safe withdrawal,

• produce calm, regulated transitions,

• protect both parent and child from environmental stress.


This is a life-saving module.


────────────────────────────────────────

316.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Overload Prediction Layer (OPL)


Predicts escalation 5–20 seconds in advance using:

• emotional trend slope

• proximity acceleration

• noise increase delta

• social density movement


⸻


(b) Withdrawal Need Estimator (WNE)


Calculates the user’s withdrawal need score (0–100).

Factors include:

• heart rate patterns (if wearable)

• body freezing / stimming increase

• abrupt quietness

• gaze aversion

• repetitive micro-movements

• rapid environmental scanning


⸻


(c) Safe Exit Path Generator (SEPG)


Generates a simple, human-friendly exit plan:

• “Two steps back”

• “Move toward the bench”

• “Turn left — less noise”

• “Go near the wall for safety”

• “Step outside for 1 minute”


No autonomy, only suggestions.


⸻


(d) Caregiver Micro-Script Engine (CMSE)


Provides immediate instructions like:

• “Lower your voice.”

• “Gently place a hand on his shoulder.”

• “Say: ‘You’re safe. We’re stepping aside.’”

• “Walk at half pace.”

• “Exit slowly — don’t rush.”


⸻


(e) Sensory Reset Layer (SRL)


Recommends:

• deep-pressure hug

• quiet corner

• ear covers

• breathing rhythm

• reduced lights


⸻


(f) Recovery Monitoring Layer (RML)


Tracks stabilization and advises when:

• user is ready to re-enter

• user should remain withdrawn longer

• environment is still too stimulating
