54 — Glossary of System Terms


A precise, engineering-grade glossary for CTOs, labs, and grant reviewers.


A


ASD — Autism Spectrum Disorder; refers to neurodivergent sensory, cognitive, and communication considerations integrated into EchoAuth’s design.


ASD-Centered Interface — UI designed to minimize sensory overload and maximize predictable, structured interaction.


⸻


C


Caregiver Override Channel (COC) — A protected pathway permitting caregivers/clinicians to confirm or override restricted-domain actions.


Coherence Engine (UMCE) — Logical, temporal, and narrative validator ensuring consistent system behavior across all subsystems.


⸻


E


E-Modules — Extended conceptual modules defining identity, interpretation, memory, governance, field-awareness, and coherence frameworks.


Execution & Safety Layer (ESL) — Enforcement layer that blocks unsafe actions, maintains policy compliance, and applies fail-closed behavior.


⸻


F


Fail-Closed — A safety principle where any ambiguity, contradiction, or instability results in system shutdown of the specific path—not a guess.


⸻


N


NI-AI — Natural Intelligence–Aligned Artificial Intelligence; constrained cognition that operates within human-governed safety, predictability, and explicit identity rules.


NI Layer — The structured, ASD-safe interpretation engine converting user signals into stable intent packets.


⸻


O


ORI Kernel — Ontological Residual Identity Kernel; the core identity model anchoring all EchoAuth sessions, ensuring predictable, personal continuity without biometrics.


⸻


P


Pattern Stabilization — A NI-AI process where repeated user signals are normalized into predictable patterns, enabling non-verbal communication.


⸻


S


S-Modules — The 23-system canonical engineering modules that define the operational runtime (boot, interpretation, coherence, safety, identity, etc.).


Safety Envelope — The non-negotiable operational boundaries regulating what the system may or may not express or perform.


⸻


T


Temporal Context Engine (TCE) — Runtime engine that considers timing, sequence, and context windows to maintain communication stability.


⸻


U


UMCE — Unified Meta-Control Engine; controls logic, coherence, state alignment, and prevents drift or contradictory output.


⸻


W


Workflow Grid — ASD-centered structured communication grid defining controlled inputs, predictable responses, and regulated output channels.

59 — Engineering Notes (Raw Technical Addendum)


This appendix stores engineering assumptions and future implementation notes that CTOs and labs expect to see.


Key Notes:

• NI Layer can run rule-based, hybrid symbolic, or constrained ML models.

• ORI Kernel must be deterministic and encrypted.

• Safety Envelope overrides all subsystem outputs.

• Temporal windows (TCE) should be adjustable per user.

• All ASD-facing UI components must follow sensory guidelines.

• Logging must remain de-identified at all times.

• No raw LLM output may be surfaced to ASD users.

• All cloud connections optional and user-consented.

• Override channel must produce audit trails.
