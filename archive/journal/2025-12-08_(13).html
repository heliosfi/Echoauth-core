SECTION A — ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (AUP)


EchoAuth may ONLY be used for the following purposes:


A1. Communication Support

• Supporting intentional expression for autistic or non-verbal users

• Providing structured communication pathways

• Enabling user-driven choices and confirmations


A2. Safety & Identity Confirmation

• Non-biometric identity confirmation

• Guardian-approved safety workflows

• Supporting school/clinic safety protocols


A3. Educational & Clinical Support

• IEP-aligned communication

• Therapist-approved communication tasks

• Supporting regulated clinical or educational sessions


A4. Research (IRB-Approved Only)

• Structured human factors testing

• Communication accuracy testing

• Non-identifiable system performance research


⸻


SECTION B — PROHIBITED USES (ZERO-TOLERANCE)


EchoAuth must NOT be used for ANY of the following:


B1. Diagnostic or Psychological Evaluation

• EchoAuth cannot diagnose

• EchoAuth cannot classify mental states

• EchoAuth cannot measure emotional or behavioral health


B2. Behavioral Surveillance or Monitoring


Institutions may NOT use EchoAuth to:

• track emotional state

• score behavior

• predict outbursts

• profile children


B3. Discipline, Punishment, or Control


EchoAuth cannot be used to:

• enforce compliance

• discipline a student

• monitor rule-breaking

• restrict movement


B4. Data Exploitation


Forbidden:

• data resale

• commercial advertising

• uploading PHI

• connecting EchoAuth to non-approved systems


B5. Unauthorized System Modification


No altering:

• safety wrapper

• NI-AI core

• identity kernel

• system limits

• coherence engine


These subsystems are protected and must not be disabled.


⸻


SECTION C — DUTY OF CARE POLICY (LEGAL PROTECTION FOR YOU)


Institutions using EchoAuth must agree to the following:


C1. Primary Responsibility


EchoAuth supplements human care — it does not replace human supervision.

Schools/clinics remain responsible for:

• physical safety

• behavioral intervention

• emergency responses


C2. User Autonomy


EchoAuth must:

• respect user autonomy

• allow the user to opt-out

• never force communication


C3. Assisted Decision Support


EchoAuth supports:

• expressing needs

• confirming preferences

• identifying discomfort


It does not:

• direct medical decisions

• override guardian authority

• override clinician judgment


C4. System Integrity


All deployments must maintain:

• device security

• safety wrapper intact

• role-based access

• encrypted system boundaries


C5. Abuse Prevention


Staff must immediately stop system use if:

• user shows signs of stress

• sensory overload occurs

• the session becomes non-consensual


⸻


SECTION D — LEGAL DISCLAIMER FOR DEPLOYERS


EchoAuth is:

• an assistive technology, not a diagnostic device

• a communication tool, not a psychological assessor

• a safety support engine, not a risk predictor

• a structured NI-AI system, not a conversational model


Institutions must operate EchoAuth strictly within approved boundaries, or system access will be revoked.
