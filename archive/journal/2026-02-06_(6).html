EchoAuth with NI-AI Subsystem

(Federal / Institutional Submission Version)


⸻


System Identity Statement (Anti-Drift Anchor)


EchoAuth is the parent system. NI-AI is a bounded subsystem within EchoAuth that provides interpretation and contextual risk modeling only. NI-AI does not execute actions, authorize access, or assert authority. Execution is gated by a separate sequencing mechanism (CEG), and identity and authorization are enforced by EchoAuth. Human authority is final. The system is refusal-first and halts under uncertainty.


⸻


1. Executive Risk Summary


EchoAuth with the NI-AI subsystem is designed as a risk-reducing infrastructure, not an autonomous system.


From a federal risk perspective, the system:

• Does not introduce independent execution authority

• Does not displace human judgment

• Does not act under uncertainty

• Does not degrade into autonomous behavior during failure


Risk is managed structurally rather than procedurally.


⸻


2. Authority & Autonomy Posture


The system enforces a non-authoritative-by-default posture.


Key properties:

• All execution requires explicit permission

• No component can self-authorize

• Authority never migrates from human operators to the system

• Disagreement collapses agency rather than redistributing it


This posture minimizes:

• Decision liability

• Delegation drift

• Over-trust risk


⸻


3. Failure & Degradation Behavior


The system is engineered to fail safely and visibly.


Under adverse conditions:

• Identity loss → no execution

• Context instability → refusal or halt

• Policy conflict → execution denied

• Sequencing failure → system pause


There is no “best effort” execution mode under uncertainty.


⸻


4. Incident Response Compatibility


EchoAuth integrates with standard federal incident response processes.

• Security-relevant events are logged

• Halt and refusal events are auditable

• Escalation paths are predefined

• Manual intervention is supported at all times


The system does not conceal failure states.


⸻


5. AI-Specific Risk Controls


AI-related risks are addressed through design constraints:

• Context drift is detected and logged

• Modeling outputs are advisory only

• No autonomous action is taken on probabilistic signals

• Risk signals increase restriction, not action


This aligns with NIST AI Risk Management Framework guidance.


⸻


6. Ethical & Human-Centered Safeguards


EchoAuth preserves:

• Human decision authority

• Transparency of enforcement actions

• Clear audit trails

• Explicit refusal states


The system does not perform:

• Behavioral diagnosis

• Psychological classification

• Value optimization


⸻


7. Pilot Suitability Statement


EchoAuth with NI-AI is suitable for:

• Time-bounded pilots

• Research deployments

• Non-authoritative operational support


Because the system is interruptible, auditable, and refusal-first, pilots can be conducted without committing to long-term operational reliance.


⸻


8. Exit & Shutdown Assurance


The system supports:

• Immediate shutdown

• Gradual disengagement

• Scope reduction

• Full exit without residual authority


No component persists authority after shutdown.
