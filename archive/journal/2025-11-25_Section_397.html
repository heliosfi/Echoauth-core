SECTION 397 — ADAPTIVE PUBLIC-SPACE SENSORY SUPPRESSION LAYER (APSSL)


(NI-AI Patent Module — Real-Time Suppression of Harmful Sensory Spikes for ASD)

────────────────────────────────────────


397.1 — Technical Field


APSSL concerns systems and methods for detecting, predicting, and controlling exposure to sudden sensory events in public environments that may cause distress for ASD individuals.


This includes:

• sudden loud noises (sirens, alarms, yelling)

• sharp acoustic spikes (dropping objects, doors slamming)

• abrupt lighting changes (flashes, strobes, flickers)

• chaotic motion bursts

• unexpected social density jumps


APSSL runs on phone-only mode, wearable mode, or hybrid mode.


────────────────────────────────────────


397.2 — Purpose


Children with ASD often experience meltdowns, shutdowns, or sensory overload bursts because of unexpected sensory shocks — not because they can’t handle environments, but because environments shift too fast.


APSSL eliminates the “surprise factor.”


It:

• predicts dangerous sensory spikes

• filters/softens them

• warns the caregiver

• shields the child through adaptive mitigation

• prevents escalation into crisis


This is one of the most powerful protective layers in the entire NI-AI architecture.


────────────────────────────────────────


397.3 — Functional Definition


APSSL performs four key processes:


(a) Spike Detection Layer (SDL)


Identifies sudden changes in:

• loudness

• frequency

• brightness

• color intensity

• motion vectors


(b) Pre-Shock Prediction Engine (PSPE)


Forecasts sensory shocks before they occur by analyzing:

• crowd tension (from 396 PSDFE)

• environment signatures

• historic shock patterns

• acoustic swell patterns

• light-field fluctuations


(c) Sensory Softening Output (SSO)


Delivers mitigation through:

• audio smoothing (phone speaker, wearable audio)

• visual dimming (screen dim overlay, AR filter)

• vibration cues

• parent alert

• environmental rerouting


(d) Crisis-Avoidance Orchestrator (CAO)


If a shock is unavoidable (ex: ambulance siren nearby):

• increases distance recommendation

• switches child to a calming mode

• activates grounding micro-pattern

• signals parent with highest-priority alert

397.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Acoustic Spike Anticipation Grid (ASAG)


Predicts loud bursts using sound-field micro-swells.


(b) Light-Field Anomaly Tracker (LFAT)


Detects and forecasts abrupt brightness changes.


(c) Motion Burst Predictor (MBP)


Forecasts chaotic motion (running children, sudden crowds).


(d) Sensory Impact Model (SIM)


Estimates how harmful the upcoming spike is for that specific child.


(e) Protective Path Switching Engine (PPSE)


Chooses least-harm environments nearby.


(f) Caregiver Override Reinforcement Layer (CORL)


Ensures parent commands always take precedence.
