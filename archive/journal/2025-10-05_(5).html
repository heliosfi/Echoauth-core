Summary

EchoAuth is a pioneering neuroadaptive AI ecosystem empowering non-verbal autistic individuals with secure, sensory-responsive communication and record-sharing. Unlike fragmented autism apps (e.g., Proloquo2Go for AAC 0 , The Listening Program for sensory therapy 1 ), EchoAuth integrates biometric authentication (face/voice), augmentative communication (AAC with TTS), adaptive binaural beats (e.g., 10Hz alpha for calm), and HIPAA-grade encrypted records into a CUDA-accelerated, offline-capable platform. Designed by a parent for neurodivergent sovereignty, it ensures privacy, reduces sensory overload, and fosters collaboration among families, educators, and clinicians. Future expansion includes SniperBot (AI trading) and FLAIMO (financial governance) for economic empowerment.

Innovation

EchoAuth redefines assistive technology with a unified, AI-driven membrane:

1 Biometric Chamber (EchoAuth): CUDA/TensorRT-powered face/voice verification (<10ms inference) secures access, protecting sensitive data without cloud reliance. 2 

2 Adaptive AAC: Offline TTS (pyttsx3) with customizable symbol boards enables rapid expression, logged for therapy insights.

3 Sensory Engine: Real-time solfeggio tones (528Hz) and binaural beats (4–10Hz) adjust volume based on environmental loudness, reducing overstimulation. 4 8 

4 Encrypted Records: SQLite + Fernet encryption enables secure sharing of communication and sensory logs with caregivers.

5 Scalable AI Core: PyTorch/CUDA ensures low-latency, local processing, extensible to SniperBot (decision-intelligence) and FLAIMO (financial autonomy).

No existing platform combines these in a neurodivergent-centric, privacy-first system.
