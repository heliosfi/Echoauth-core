NIH SPECIFIC AIMS


NI-AI × EchoAuth × MCG

Governed Decision-Support Infrastructure for Stable Non-Verbal Interaction


⸻


Overall Objective


The overall objective of this project is to evaluate a governance-first AI decision-support system designed to operate safely in non-verbal, caregiver-mediated contexts where traditional AI systems fail due to ambiguity, escalation, or misinterpretation.


Current AI interaction systems frequently:

• Escalate behavior under uncertainty

• Infer intent or emotional state without verifiable ground truth

• Treat silence or non-participation as failure

• Allow autonomous behavior without enforceable restraint


These limitations present significant risks in environments involving children, neurodivergent individuals, and caregiver-dependent decision pathways, where safety, predictability, and explainability are essential.


This project evaluates NI-AI, integrated with EchoAuth and governed by MCG (Motion / Context Governance), as a non-autonomous, fail-closed decision-support and explanation layer that defaults to non-action when uncertainty exists and preserves caregiver authority at all times.


⸻


Central Hypothesis


We hypothesize that a governance-first, explanation-only AI architecture—in which observation, authorization, and execution are structurally separated—will demonstrate greater stability, reduced escalation, and improved caregiver trust in non-verbal interaction scenarios compared to conventional AI interaction systems.


This hypothesis is testable, non-diagnostic, and aligned with NIH priorities for safe, interpretable, and human-centered technologies.


⸻


Specific Aim 1


Evaluate stability and restraint of NI-AI during non-verbal interaction scenarios.


We will assess whether NI-AI maintains stable behavior when interacting with non-verbal or minimally responsive participants by measuring:

• Frequency of pause vs. escalation under ambiguous input

• Consistency of system responses across extended sessions

• Adherence to fail-closed, non-action defaults


Outcome: Demonstration that NI-AI resolves ambiguity through observation and pause rather than inference or action.


⸻


Specific Aim 2


Assess caregiver-mediated decision-support effectiveness under MCG governance.


We will evaluate how caregivers interact with NI-AI explanations and authorization gates by measuring:

• Clarity of system “why-pause / why-deny” explanations

• Caregiver comprehension and confidence in system behavior

• Reduction in perceived pressure to act during uncertain states


Outcome: Evidence that NI-AI supports caregiver decision-making without replacing or overriding human judgment.


⸻


Specific Aim 3


Measure system safety, auditability, and explainability under controlled pilot conditions.


We will test NI-AI’s governance and audit features by evaluating:

• Completeness and consistency of logged state transitions

• Traceability of allow / deny / pause decisions

• System behavior during simulated failure and uncertainty conditions


Outcome: Validation that NI-AI produces reviewable, auditable outputs aligned with NIH expectations for safe AI research systems.


⸻


Expected Outcomes and Impact


This project is expected to produce:

• Empirical evidence supporting governance-first AI design

• A validated framework for safe AI interaction in non-verbal contexts

• Reusable methods for explainability, restraint, and caregiver mediation


Impact: The results will inform NIH-funded research on trustworthy AI systems suitable for sensitive human environments, without introducing autonomous decision-making or clinical claims.


⸻


Relevance to NIH Mission


This work advances NIH interests in:

• Safe human-centered technologies

• Non-invasive support systems

• Ethical AI deployment in vulnerable populations

• Research-grade evaluation of AI restraint and explainability


The system is not diagnostic, not predictive, and not autonomous, making it appropriate for early-stage NIH research funding and controlled pilot studies.
