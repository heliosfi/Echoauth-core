NIH FEDERAL USE CASE — NI-AI & EchoAuth


National Institutes of Health (NIH)

Primary Alignment:

• NIMH (National Institute of Mental Health)

• NICHD (National Institute of Child Health and Human Development)

• NINDS (Neurological Disorders)

• NIDCD (Communication Disorders)


NI-AI directly addresses NIH priorities in mental health, neurodevelopment, cognitive load reduction, autism support, and equitable healthcare access.


⸻


1. Key NIH Problem Areas NI-AI Solves


A. Cognitive Overload & Emotional Dysregulation


Individuals with ASD, ADHD, anxiety, or trauma often experience:

• communication strain

• difficulty processing emotional cues

• inconsistent support

• sensory overload


NI-AI stabilizes emotional + cognitive states in real time through:

• emotional-state detection

• rhythm-based pacing adjustment

• sensory-load modulation

• coherence scoring algorithms


⸻


B. Inaccessible Authentication & Digital Health Tools


Most healthcare tech assumes:

• neurotypical behavior

• consistent attention

• predictable device usage


EchoAuth introduces:

• resonance-based authentication

• behavior-adaptive login

• stress-aware access systems

This makes digital health tools more accessible to neurodivergent users.


⸻


C. Burnout Among Caregivers & Clinicians


Healthcare workers face:

• cognitive strain

• lack of emotional-support tools

• high administrative load


NI-AI reduces clinician burden by:

• stabilizing user interaction

• supporting clearer communication

• reducing misinterpretation

• providing adaptive, context-aware digital assistance
