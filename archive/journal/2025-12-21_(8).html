CAREGIVER PORTAL — QUALIFIED HUMAN SUPPORT & EXECUTION


Purpose (professional, plain)


The Caregiver Portal exists to provide hands-on support for the child while keeping all authority with the parent.

It enables help without confusion, without overreach, and without burning out family members.


This portal is about execution, not decisions.


⸻


1️⃣ Caregiver Qualification & Entry


Who can be a caregiver

• ASD-trained caregivers

• Professional aides

• Approved family helpers (if parent chooses)


What qualification means

• Verification of experience or training

• Completion of EchoAuth caregiver onboarding

• Agreement to task-scoped responsibility

• Acceptance of parent authority rules


Professional framing:

Caregivers are qualified, accountable participants, not ad-hoc helpers.


⸻


2️⃣ Task-Scoped Execution


What caregivers can do

• Carry out parent-approved tasks only

• Follow approved routines and supports

• Assist with transitions, activities, and care

• Support child engagement using approved programs


What caregivers cannot do

• ❌ Approve plans

• ❌ Change routines

• ❌ Add programs

• ❌ Override parents or professionals


Professional framing:

Caregivers operate within explicitly defined task boundaries.


⸻


3️⃣ Alerts & Reminders


What caregivers receive

• Time-based reminders

• Task notifications

• Check-in prompts


What the system expects

• Execution or acknowledgement

• Factual logging of completion or issues


Professional framing:

Reminders support consistency, not supervision.


⸻


4️⃣ Care Action Logging


What is logged

• What task was done

• When it was done

• Any factual notes (no interpretation)


Why this matters

• Parents see what’s happening

• Continuity across shifts

• Accountability without micromanagement


Professional framing:

Logs capture actions, not judgments.


⸻


5️⃣ Visibility & Privacy Limits


What caregivers can see

• Only what is required for their tasks

• Only during approved time windows

• Only data the parent allows


What they never see

• Full child history (unless approved)

• Clinical records by default

• Parent decision discussions


Professional framing:

Caregiver access is least-privilege by design.


⸻


6️⃣ Removal & Change Control


What parents can do

• Remove a caregiver instantly

• Change task scope at any time

• Adjust access without explanation


Professional framing:

Control remains continuous, not one-time.


⸻


7️⃣ What the Caregiver Portal Explicitly Does NOT Do

• ❌ Make decisions

• ❌ Interpret child behavior

• ❌ Diagnose

• ❌ Recommend treatment

• ❌ Communicate directives to other roles


Professional framing:

The Caregiver Portal supports care delivery, not care direction.


⸻


8️⃣ How This Helps the Child (Important)

• Child receives consistent support

• Routines are followed across environments

• Less stress from handoffs

• More presence, less confusion


Caregivers help around the child, not through the child.


⸻
