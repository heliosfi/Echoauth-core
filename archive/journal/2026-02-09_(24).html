Delegate Training Sheet — School Version


Working Safely Within the EchoAuth System


For Teachers, Aides, Administrators, and School Staff


⸻


Purpose of This Sheet


This guide explains how school staff interact with EchoAuth without triggering safety halts and without assuming caregiver authority.


EchoAuth exists to protect the child by ensuring that only parents or legal caregivers authorize actions.

School staff participate in execution, observation, and documentation only.


⸻


1. Your Role in the System


As a School Delegate, you may:

• Carry out caregiver-approved schedules and activities

• Observe behavior, engagement, and regulation

• Document incidents, progress, or concerns

• Propose adjustments for caregiver review


You may not:

• Approve or deny activities

• Modify schedules, permissions, or access

• Override caregiver decisions

• Resolve disagreements between caregivers

• Force the system to continue during a halt


The system enforces these limits automatically.


⸻


2. Common School Actions That Trigger Halts (Avoid These)

• Issuing a command that contradicts the home schedule

• Attempting to extend or shorten activities without approval

• Sending repeated commands after a pause

• Acting on verbal instructions not reflected in the system


If the system halts, do not attempt correction.


⸻


3. How to Work Safely Day-to-Day


A. Follow the Active Schedule Only

• Treat the system schedule as authoritative

• If something seems outdated or incorrect, document and notify


B. Submit Recommendations, Not Commands


Examples:

• ✔ “Student appears dysregulated after activity X”

• ✔ “Recommend adjustment to task duration”

• ✘ “Disable task”

• ✘ “Override schedule”


C. During Transitions (Most Common Failure Point)

• Avoid issuing commands during:

• Class changes

• Arrival/dismissal

• Substitute coverage

• When in doubt: pause and document


⸻


4. If the System Locks (CEG = LOCKED)

1. Stop issuing commands immediately

2. Do not retry or escalate within the system

3. Document the situation (time, activity, context)

4. Notify the caregiver through the approved channel


Only a caregiver can restore execution.


⸻


5. School-Specific Best Practices

• Coordinate schedules with caregivers weekly

• Inform caregivers of planned special events in advance

• Avoid manual overrides during behavioral escalations

• Treat system halts as safety signals, not disruptions


⸻


School Delegate Principle


Follow the plan. Document the exception. Never override authority.

Delegate Training Sheet — Clinical Version


Working Safely Within the EchoAuth System


For Therapists, Clinicians, Specialists, and Clinical Staff


⸻


Purpose of This Sheet


This guide explains how clinicians interact with EchoAuth while respecting caregiver authority and refusal-first safety constraints.


EchoAuth does not replace clinical judgment.

It ensures that clinical activity never executes without caregiver authorization.


⸻


1. Your Role in the System


As a Clinical Delegate, you may:

• Execute caregiver-approved therapeutic activities

• Observe responses, tolerance, and outcomes

• Document clinical observations and recommendations

• Propose care plan adjustments for caregiver approval


You may not:

• Initiate new therapies autonomously

• Modify access, permissions, or schedules

• Override caregiver refusals

• Resolve authority conflicts

• Proceed during a system halt


⸻


2. Clinical Actions That Commonly Cause Halts

• Introducing unapproved therapeutic variations

• Adjusting intensity or duration without authorization

• Acting during ambiguous consent windows

• Attempting to proceed during caregiver disagreement


Halts are protective, not diagnostic.


⸻


3. Safe Clinical Workflow


A. Treat Authorization as a Clinical Constraint

• If consent is unclear, do not proceed

• The system is enforcing ethical boundaries, not limiting care


B. Keep Clinical Judgment Non-Executable

• Record insights and recommendations

• Submit proposals for caregiver review

• Do not embed intent into execution


C. During Dysregulation or Escalation

• Do not attempt new interventions without approval

• Use observation and documentation

• Allow caregiver to resolve authority if needed


⸻


4. If the System Locks (CEG = LOCKED)

1. Stop the session activity if required

2. Do not attempt system resolution

3. Document observed conditions and timing

4. Notify the caregiver


Execution resumes only after caregiver reauthorization.


⸻


5. Clinical Best Practices

• Align care plans with caregiver expectations

• Avoid “temporary” changes without explicit approval

• Treat refusal as a valid outcome

• Never infer consent based on prior sessions


⸻


Clinical Delegate Principle


Clinical insight informs care. Caregivers authorize care.


⸻


Final Shared Reminder (Both Contexts)


EchoAuth exists because some children cannot reliably self-advocate.

Your discipline within your role is part of the safety system.


If the system halts, it is protecting the child.
