14.1 Target Customers & Markets


EchoAuth serves three primary markets:


⸻


1. Schools & Special Education Programs (K–12)

• 7 million students receive special education services annually (IDEA).

• Over 700,000 autistic students require accommodations for communication and safety.

• Districts already pay $5k–$50k annually per classroom for assistive technologies.


EchoAuth provides:

• Non-verbal communication tools

• Identity confirmation during transitions

• Integration with IEP goals

• Teacher dashboards for structured communication logs


Primary buyers:

School districts, special education directors, IEP teams.


⸻


2. ASD Clinics & Therapy Centers


Clinics supporting ABA, speech therapy, OT, and developmental pediatric services require tools that:

• reduce communication barriers

• improve emotional stabilization

• support behavioral mapping

• reduce caregiver load


Primary buyers:

Clinical directors, developmental pediatric practices, ABA centers, autism-specific non-profits.


⸻


3. Families & In-Home Support Networks


A subscription model will allow families to access the core EchoAuth system:

• daily need expression

• identity stabilization during stressful events

• structured communication cues


Primary buyers:

Parents, caregivers, home-based ASD providers.


⸻

⸻

14.6 Competitive Landscape & Differentiation


EchoAuth competes with none of the major AAC systems directly.


AAC systems focus on:

✔ symbol grids

✔ speech generation


They do NOT provide:

✘ identity confirmation

✘ safety protocols

✘ sensory-adaptive stabilization

✘ NI-AI intention modeling

✘ predictable communication states


EchoAuth is a new class of assistive technology.

It sits between AAC, ASD-support tools, and AI-assisted cognition.


Competitors (indirect):

• Proloquo2Go

• TouchChat

• LAMP Words for Life


EchoAuth advantage:

• stability

• safety

• identity

• predictable state transitions

• ASD-specific architecture
