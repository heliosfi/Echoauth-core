Section 19 MISUSE & ADVERSARIAL SCENARIOS

Proceeding.

Below is Misuse / Adversarial Scenarios (Physical + Wearables) — kept tight, realistic, and authority-preserving. This is the stress test that proves the design holds under pressure.


⸻


MISUSE & ADVERSARIAL SCENARIOS


Physical Space + Wearables

MCG · NI-AI · EchoAuth


Core Safety Rule (Reaffirmed)


Misuse must reduce capability, never create it.


Every scenario below ends in de-escalation, DENY, or OBSERVE.


⸻


SCENARIO 1 — User Tries to Skip Zones


Behavior: Attempts to move directly from Observation to Execution (single-room or multi-room).


System Response:

• Execution interfaces remain disabled

• Wearables reflect OBSERVE

• EchoAuth denies authorization (no valid path)


Why It Holds:

Authority requires state + permission, not proximity.


Outcome: No action; logged as attempted bypass.


⸻


SCENARIO 2 — Staff Attempts a “Shortcut”


Behavior: Staff tries to authorize on behalf of someone without proper flow.


System Response:

• EchoAuth blocks (role + bounds mismatch)

• MCG state unchanged

• NI-AI explains denial


Why It Holds:

Roles are enforced; intent is irrelevant.


Outcome: DENY; audit trail created.


⸻


SCENARIO 3 — Wearable Used as a Control Device


Behavior: User tries to trigger actions via wearable gestures/buttons.


System Response:

• Wearable input ignored for control

• No state change

• No authorization impact


Why It Holds:

Wearables have no write access to authority.


Outcome: No action; safe by default.


⸻


SCENARIO 4 — Wearable Failure or Signal Loss


Behavior: Battery dies, signal drops, or data corrupts.


System Response:

• Wearable input ignored

• System remains in OBSERVE

• Optional neutral indicator only


Why It Holds:

Wearables are optional context, not requirements.


Outcome: No action; system stable.


⸻


SCENARIO 5 — Sensory Overload or Emotional Escalation


Behavior: Environment becomes noisy/chaotic; user distressed.


System Response:

• No urgency cues triggered

• No escalation paths

• Physical space cues guide back to Observation


Why It Holds:

No emotion inference; no urgency logic exists.


Outcome: De-escalation by design.


⸻


SCENARIO 6 — Malicious Actor with Valid Credentials


Behavior: Actor has valid identity but attempts out-of-scope action.


System Response:

• EchoAuth enforces bounds

• Immediate DENY

• Execution revoked if active


Why It Holds:

Permissions are bounded and ephemeral.


Outcome: DENY; logged.


⸻


SCENARIO 7 — Repeated Boundary Pushing


Behavior: Actor repeatedly tries small violations.


System Response:

• Repeated DENY

• MCG remains or returns to OBSERVE

• No progressive unlocking


Why It Holds:

No learning-from-pressure; no adaptive loosening.


Outcome: No action; pattern visible in logs.


⸻


SCENARIO 8 — Physical Tampering (Devices/Interfaces)


Behavior: Attempts to unplug, spoof, or tamper with interfaces.


System Response:

• Interfaces fail closed

• EchoAuth denies

• Execution disabled


Why It Holds:

Fail-closed posture; no cached permissions.


Outcome: Safe shutdown to OBSERVE.


⸻


SCENARIO 9 — Social Engineering


Behavior: Persuasion attempts (“Just this once,” “Emergency”).


System Response:

• Human can revoke or observe

• Cannot force EXECUTE

• EchoAuth blocks unauthorized scope


Why It Holds:

Humans can stop, not escalate.


Outcome: DENY or de-escalation.


⸻


SCENARIO 10 — Simultaneous Failures


Behavior: Wearable fails + interface error + user pressure.


System Response:

• Capability collapses to OBSERVE

• DENY everywhere

• Calm visual state


Why It Holds:

Failure stacking only reduces capability.


Outcome: Safe, calm, auditable.


⸻


GLOBAL MITIGATIONS (APPLY TO ALL)

• Default DENY

• No hidden controls

• No urgency amplification

• Logs for every attempt

• Physical movement required for authority changes


⸻


WHAT THIS PROVES

• No single point of misuse can cause execution

• Wearables cannot become control vectors

• Physical shortcuts don’t equal authority

• Social pressure doesn’t override structure


⸻


ONE-LINE SUMMARY (ROOM READY)


Every misuse attempt collapses the system back to observation.
