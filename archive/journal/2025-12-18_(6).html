MCG — A Human-Anchored Governance Layer for AI Systems

Thesis Overview
MCG is an enforceable governance infrastructure layer designed to keep AI systems aligned with human intent, identity, and safety invariants in high-trust environments. MCG prioritizes control before amplification—providing a governance spine that reduces drift, misuse, and unauthorized escalation while enabling safe deployment.

The Problem
As AI systems become more capable and adaptive, risks increase: intent drift, identity abuse, and safety failures in high-trust domains like caregiving, education, and enterprise decision-making. Many current approaches rely on post-hoc safeguards that do not scale cleanly as systems become more autonomous.

The Solution (Architecture)
MCG combines three components into a lightweight runtime wrapper for any LLM/agent stack:

• HER Kernel (Human Enforcement Runtime): Real-time policy enforcement that intercepts and validates actions against defined invariants.

• NI (Natural Intellect Anchor): Human-defined constraints and ethical priors that bound adaptation and prevent harmful drift.

• EchoAuth (Identity & Continuity Proof): Identity assurance and continuity checks to maintain verified human oversight in critical interactions.

What This Enables

• Auditable safeguards and logging.

• Human oversight by design (aligned with emerging regulatory expectations, e.g., EU AI Act high-risk requirements).

• Composable integration with existing AI stacks.

Why Now
Regulatory and enterprise pressure is increasing for enforceable oversight and safety controls, especially for high-trust use cases. AI safety funding is flowing toward control layers, scalable oversight, and alignment infrastructure.
