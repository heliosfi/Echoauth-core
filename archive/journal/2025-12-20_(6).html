EchoAuth — Explanation for Robert (Final, Spoken-Ready)


1. One-sentence opener (start here)


“EchoAuth is a non-clinical safety and coordination platform for families and care networks supporting autistic or non-verbal children, especially when care is shared across people or settings.”

⸻


2. The problem (make it real)


“The real problem isn’t diagnosis or therapy — it’s fragmentation. Parents, aides, teachers, and caregivers often have different reports about the same child. Progress looks slow, risks increase, and parents lose confidence when they’re not present.”


Key words Robert will register:

• fragmentation

• shared care

• risk

• trust


⸻


3. What EchoAuth actually does (plain language)


“EchoAuth creates a shared, continuous understanding of the child across caregivers. It doesn’t interpret emotions or speak for the child — it records verified patterns, preserves continuity, and enforces caregiver authority so everyone is working from the same picture.”


Important:

You’re saying what it does, not how deep it goes.

4. What makes it different (this is where MCG comes in)


“Under the hood, EchoAuth is built around a governance layer we call MCG — Master Control Governance. That layer enforces hard rules: no diagnosis, no substitution of human judgment, and halt-on-uncertainty behavior.”

“That’s what keeps it safe, compliant, and scalable.”

5. If Robert asks “AI? Biometrics? Emotion?” (this is the safe clarification)
