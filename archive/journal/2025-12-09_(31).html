48. Grant Pack Abstract (NIH/NSF/NIDILRR Ready)


Length: Reviewer-compliant, ~225 words


EchoAuth is an assistive NI-AI system designed to improve communication accuracy, identity stability, and emotional safety for autistic and non-verbal individuals. The system integrates a structured identity kernel (ORI), constrained NI-AI interpretation layers, and ASD-optimized sensory interfaces to support clinical, educational, and in-home environments. EchoAuth does not operate as a conversational model; it is a deterministic, rule-bound assistive engine that reduces ambiguity, enhances predictability, and increases user independence.


This Phase I proposal seeks federal support to finalize EchoAuth’s prototype, strengthen its NI-AI parsing logic, conduct usability testing with ASD clinicians, and validate the safety, reliability, and communication benefits of the system. The project includes developing a structured test harness, refining FERPA/HIPAA compliance layers, and conducting evidence-based trials with 20–30 ASD participants across home, school, and clinic settings.


Requested funds ($200,000–$350,000) will support engineering completion, sensory-adaptive interface refinement, compliance work, and pilot preparation. Expected outcomes include measurable improvements in communication accuracy (target: +30%), stable caregiver override protocols, validated fail-closed responses, and fully documented pilot-readiness for Phase II scaling.


EchoAuth aligns with federal priorities in neurodivergent empowerment, AI safety, communication science, and equitable education. Successful completion positions EchoAuth for multi-site pilots and long-term commercialization in the ASD assistive technology domain.
