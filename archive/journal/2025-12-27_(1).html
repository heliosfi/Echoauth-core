A. Purpose of SET 9 (Reviewer Logic)


SET 9 exists to demonstrate that EchoAuth / NI-AI is governed, not expressive.


It shows:

• Risks are anticipated, not discovered late

• Controls are runtime-enforced, not aspirational

• Humans remain final authority, not AI


This satisfies:

• NIH: Human-subject protection, autonomy, non-diagnostic AI

• NSF: Ethical & Responsible Research (ER2), accountability, transparency


⸻


B. Core Risk Categories & Controls (Canonical)


1️⃣ Autonomy Risk (AI Acting Beyond Intent)


Risk: AI outputs exceed intended scope or user purpose.

Control:

• NI Layer defines intent & purpose before AI generation

• C5 (Purpose Check) blocks misaligned outputs

• CEG (Execution Gate) enforces turn-based execution


Reviewer Signal: Non-autonomous AI ✔


⸻


2️⃣ Identity & Misuse Risk


Risk: Impersonation, session drift, or unauthorized action.

Control:

• EchoAuth computes Resonance Match Score (RMS)

• Low RMS → escalate, pause, or block

• No silent continuation allowed


Reviewer Signal: Trust & security ✔


⸻


3️⃣ Hallucination / Drift Risk


Risk: Fabricated or unstable AI outputs.

Control:

• Stability Regulation Kernel (SRK)

• Rejects or rewrites outputs failing coherence

• Downgrades complexity under cognitive overload


Reviewer Signal: Technical risk mitigation ✔


⸻


4️⃣ Emotional or Cognitive Harm Risk


Risk: Overstimulating or inappropriate responses.

Control:

• Emotional State Engine (ESE)

• Emotional Guardrail softens tone, slows pacing

• NSM activates simplified delivery when flagged


Reviewer Signal: Inclusivity without clinical claims ✔


⸻


5️⃣ Bias & Overreach Risk


Risk: Demographic inference or diagnostic interpretation.

Control:

• Behavioral signals only (timing, interaction patterns)

• No demographic, medical, or psychological labeling

• Explicit “What NI-AI Is Not” boundary language


Reviewer Signal: NIH-safe non-diagnostic framing ✔


⸻


C. Human-in-the-Loop Oversight


At no point does the system override human authority.


Human oversight exists at:

• Design time (MCG axioms define allow/hold/escalate)

• Runtime (CEG gates execution)

• Post-runtime (audit logs reviewable)


Admins or supervisors can:

• Inspect logs (ESP, RMS, coherence scores)

• Disable modes (e.g., Guardian Mode only)

• Halt execution pathways


⸻


D. Auditability & Traceability


Every interaction produces:

• ESP (Emotional State Profile)

• RMS (Identity continuity)

• Coherence scores (C1–C5)

• Execution decisions (allow / hold / block)


Logs are:

• Read-only

• Time-ordered

• Reviewable for compliance, safety, or investigation


No adaptive learning occurs without explicit governance approval.


⸻


E. NIH-Safe Ethics Statement (2–3 Sentences)


“The NI-AI framework does not diagnose, treat, or assess medical or psychological conditions. All emotional and behavioral mappings are used solely for interaction adaptation and safety control. Human oversight, identity verification, and auditability are enforced at runtime to protect autonomy, privacy, and welfare.”


✅ Ready for R21 appendices.
