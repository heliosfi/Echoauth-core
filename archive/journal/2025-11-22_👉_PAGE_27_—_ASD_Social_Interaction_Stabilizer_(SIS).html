⭐ PAGE 27 — ASD SOCIAL INTERACTION STABILIZER (SIS)


“How EchoAuth protects, regulates, and guides ASD users during social interaction.”


──────────────────────────────────


Autistic social overwhelm is NOT caused by “lack of social skills.”

It is caused by:

• sensory saturation

• unpredictable timing

• fast-changing emotional cues

• too many signals at once

• unclear expectations

• high cognitive demand

• fear of misunderstanding

• rapid shifting attention

• noise in social environments

• emotional pressure from others


Most systems try to teach social skills.


EchoAuth instead stabilizes the user’s nervous system AND the social environment — reducing pressure and making interaction safe.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.1 — THE 4 SOCIAL STATES (SIS MAP)


ASD users shift between 4 Social States:


State Description Goal

S1 — Observing Watching, listening, quiet Keep low load

S2 — Participating Interacting lightly Maintain control

S3 — Overloaded Too many signals at once Reduce inputs

S4 — Retreating / Shutdown Pullback, quiet, freeze Protect & restore


SIS auto-detects these states.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.2 — SOCIAL SIGNAL FILTER (SSF)


EchoAuth filters overwhelming social signals:


✔ tone of voice

✔ multiple people speaking

✔ emotional intensity

✔ unpredictable movement

✔ fast conversations

✔ social expectations

✔ eye contact pressure


SSF reduces the signal-to-noise ratio so the child is not overloaded.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.2 — SOCIAL EXPECTATION TRANSLATOR (SET)


This makes EchoAuth the bridge between child and environment.


SET translates unclear social input into simple, literal meaning:


Examples:


“Come here” → “Walk to the adult.”

“You should know this” → “They want you to answer.”

“Look at me” → “Turn your face toward them.”

“Be respectful” → “Talk softly + no yelling.”

“They’re waiting for you” → “It’s your turn.”


No metaphors.

No emotional overload.

No pressure.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.4 — SOCIAL TIMING REGULATOR (STR)


ASD social breakdowns often occur due to timing mismatch.


STR adjusts timing to protect the child:


✔ delays EchoAuth’s responses

✔ slows pacing

✔ helps child pause before reacting

✔ gives them more time to process

✔ prevents interruptions

✔ manages turn-taking


EchoAuth becomes their rhythm keeper in social spaces.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.5 — SOCIAL EXIT PATHWAYS (SEP)


EchoAuth teaches the child how to exit a social situation without escalation.


Examples:

• “I need a break.”

• “One second.”

• “Can I step away?”

• “I need quiet.”


SEP provides scripts + visual buttons for the child to leave safely and respectfully.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.6 — SOCIAL ENERGY GAUGE (SEG)


EchoAuth monitors how much “social energy” the child has left.


When SEG drops below threshold:

• reduce conversation

• shorten sentences

• move to visual mode

• suggest break

• prompt caregiver

• avoid new interactions


SEG prevents emotional collapse.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.7 — EMERGENT SOCIAL OVERLOAD DETECTION (ESOD)


EchoAuth detects overload patterns such as:


✔ fidgeting

✔ looking down

✔ going quiet

✔ shorter words

✔ losing track

✔ freezing

✔ repetitive movements

✔ hand pressure

✔ frustration building


If ESOD rises, SIS activates risk-level response.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.8 — CAREGIVER SOCIAL COACHING (CSC)


EchoAuth guides caregivers in real time with:

• “Lower your voice.”

• “Give them space.”

• “Don’t force eye contact.”

• “Simplify instructions.”

• “Wait before repeating.”

• “Let them breathe.”


This prevents accidental social overload from adults.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 27.9 — SIS RESPONSE LADDER


EchoAuth’s Social Stabilizer flows in ladders:


⭐ Ladder 1 — Mild


→ reduce pacing

→ simplify wording

→ support focus


⭐ Ladder 2 — Moderate


→ switch to visual mode

→ protect from noise


⭐ Ladder 3 — High

→ begin regulation protocols
