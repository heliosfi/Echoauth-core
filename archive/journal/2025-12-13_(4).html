🌱 KIVA FOUNDER STORY


EchoAuth


My name is Nicholas, and EchoAuth was born from watching how easily children lose continuity when the world around them changes.


For many children—especially non-verbal or minimally verbal children—expression doesn’t travel well. A child may be understood at home, but once they enter school, a clinic, or a new caregiving environment, that understanding resets. Different devices, different apps, different routines. What the child was able to express yesterday may not be recognized today.


I saw how stressful and unfair that is for a child.


EchoAuth exists to solve that one problem: how to help a child carry their identity and expression with them, even when environments, adults, or tools change.


EchoAuth is not therapy, not diagnosis, and not surveillance. It is a smart, non-verbal communication and login system that helps children express needs, preferences, and routines consistently across parents, caregivers, and schools—when other systems cannot.


The system is designed for the child first. Parents, caregivers, and schools are there to support the child, not control them. EchoAuth simply helps everyone stay aligned around the same child context so the child does not have to start over each time.


I designed EchoAuth with strict boundaries so it stays safe and predictable. It does not act on its own, does not collect unnecessary data, and does not change purpose as it grows. Those rules are intentional. Families and communities deserve technology they can trust.


I am seeking a Kiva loan to help complete the prototype, acquire initial devices, and deploy small pilot programs. This funding helps bridge EchoAuth from development into real-world use, where families and caregivers can benefit immediately.


EchoAuth is about dignity, continuity, and giving children a way to be understood when words are not enough.


Thank you for supporting this work.
