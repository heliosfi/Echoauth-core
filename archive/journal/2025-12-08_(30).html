⭐ PAGE 20 — REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE ROADMAP


EchoAuth is designed as a neuro-support assistive technology operating in sensitive domains including education, pediatric clinical care, and home-based ASD support. As such, its regulatory pathway is structured around three pillars:

1. Data privacy & security compliance

2. Clinical & educational policy alignment

3. Technical safety & reliability standards


This roadmap outlines EchoAuth’s staged plan to meet all required legal, ethical, and institutional protections for deployment in schools, clinics, and homes.

20.1 Data Privacy Framework (Federal & State Requirements)


EchoAuth complies with the following federal statutes relevant to minors, neurodivergent users, and institutional environments:


FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)


Applicable for school deployments.

EchoAuth ensures:

• No unauthorized sharing of student data

• Clear parental consent models

• Minimal data retention

• Role-based access controls for educators


HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)


Applicable for clinical or therapeutic settings.

EchoAuth uses:

• Encrypted communication channels

• No storage of PHI unless explicitly required

• Compliant audit logs

• Secure guardian-accessible data pathways


COPPA (Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act)


Applicable for users under age 13.

EchoAuth enforces:

• Explicit parental/guardian consent

• No behavioral tracking

• No advertising or profiling

• Restricted data usage strictly for communication support


State-Level Education & Disability Laws


EchoAuth satisfies:

• State Assistive Technology mandates

• Special Education regulatory requirements

• Informed consent and school district procurement rules


This positions EchoAuth as a legally compliant, child-safe assistive technology.


⸻


20.2 Technical Security Controls


EchoAuth’s security model is built on four layers:


1. Encryption & Secure Transport

• AES-256 encryption at rest

• TLS 1.3+ encryption in transit

• Zero plaintext storage of sensitive identity markers


2. Identity Kernel & Access Control

• Non-biometric identity signatures

• Multi-layer NI-AI validation

• Guardian override channels

• Session-level authentication constraints


3. Data Minimization & Ephemeral Processing

• Only essential communication data is stored

• Sensitive data can be configured as session-only

• No third-party data sharing


4. Safety-First Runtime Behavior

• Fail-closed activation (system defaults to safe mode)

• Coherence checks to prevent invalid outputs

• Role-based caps on system capabilities


These layers meet or exceed expectations for tools supporting vulnerable populations.

20.5 Safety & Human Oversight Protocols


EchoAuth is designed to ensure that:


Human oversight is always mandatory.


The system never replaces clinician or caregiver judgment.


Safety Modes

• “Guardian Lock Mode” for high-sensitivity sessions

• “Low-Sensory Mode” to minimize visual overload

• “Communication Freeze Mode” during emotional escalations


Escalation Triggers

• System pauses when communication becomes ambiguous

• Prompts for human interpretation


These policies match the expectations of ASD therapy centers and federal reviewers.
