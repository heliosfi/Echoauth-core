340.3 — Core Capabilities


The engine has three major safety layers:


⸻


(A) Physical Boundary Layer (PBL)


Defines safe zones such as:

• home,

• school,

• therapy center,

• playground,

• park,

• shopping centers,

• community events.


Features:

• Geofence rings (inner, outer, critical)

• Proximity drift detection

• Safe-route prediction

• Wander-prevention pattern alerts

• “Pull-back” grounding signal (gentle wearable cue)


⸻


(B) Sensory-Environmental Layer (SEL)


Maps environmental hazards:

• loud environments

• bright lights

• heavy crowds

• sudden bursts (sirens, horns)

• chaotic motion (traffic)

• temperature shifts


The system checks:

• Is the environment safe for this specific child?

• Does it match their sensory profile?

• Should we activate grounding before entering?


It can automatically reduce stimuli through the WCGL (Section 339).


⸻


(C) Emotional-State Boundary Layer (ESBL)


Uses NI-AI’s emotional engines (ESE, EOPE, ECC) to detect:

• emotional overload,

• escalation risk,

• pre-meltdown markers,

• shutdown patterns,

• dissociation near unsafe areas.


If emotional drift + boundary drift coincide = instant safety alert.


────────────────────────────────


340.4 — Internal Architecture


(1) Boundary Definition Engine (BDE)


Defines safe, neutral, unsafe zones using:

• GPS mesh

• indoor beacons

• school/home Wi-Fi signatures

• caregiver-approved zones

• sensory risk metadata


(2) Drift Vector Detector (DVD)


Tracks:

• movement direction

• speed

• pacing irregularities (overwhelm pacing)

• “run behavior” (common in ASD)


Predicts escape/wandering attempts 5–30 seconds ahead.


(3) Emotional-Environmental Fusion Layer (EEFL)


Combines:

• emotional intensity

• sensory environment risk

• proximity to unsafe boundaries

• historical patterns


This is where NI-AI shines — no other AI even attempts this.


(4) Safety Intervention Generator (SIG)


If unsafe behavior is detected, SIG activates:

• grounding signals (via WCGL)

• vibration cues to slow movement

• calming pulses

• parent/caregiver alerts

• safe route suggestions

• gentle return-path guidance


(5) Caregiver Command Link (CCL)


Allows caregiver to instantly:

• ping the wearable

• send a “come back” vibration pattern

• lock boundaries temporarily

• activate high-protection mode
