PAGE 48 — Grant Pack Abstract (NIH / NSF / NIDILRR)


Project Title


EchoAuth: An NI-AI Neuro-Support System for Autistic and Non-Verbal Individuals


Abstract (200 words, NIH-compliant)


EchoAuth is an assistive neuro-support platform that provides structured communication and identity stabilization for autistic and non-verbal individuals. Many existing AAC and AI tools fail to provide predictable outputs, sensory-safe interfaces, or clinically aligned intention modeling. EchoAuth resolves these challenges through its NI-AI architecture, ORI identity kernel, ASD-specific communication grids, and a coherence engine that enforces safe, consistent, fail-closed behavior.


This Phase I project will refine the prototype, integrate the NI-AI semantic engine, enhance sensory-accessible UI components, and conduct usability testing with ASD clinicians and users (n=20–30). Funds will support prototype optimization, accessibility testing, FERPA/HIPAA-compliant data handling, and pilot-site readiness. Key outcomes include improved communication accuracy, reduced caregiver load, and deployment-ready modules for schools and clinics.


Phase I results will support advancement to Phase II for district-level scaling, longitudinal clinical evaluation, and commercialization. EchoAuth directly addresses federal goals to expand assistive technology, improve neurodivergent communication outcomes, and reduce ASD-related care burdens.
