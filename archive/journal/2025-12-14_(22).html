STAGE 2 — AI ASSURANCE REPORT


NI-AI × EchoAuth × MCG

(Stability, Restraint, Explainability)


2.1 Purpose of This Assurance


This report demonstrates that NI-AI operates as a governed decision-support and explanation layer whose behavior is deterministic, constrained, and auditable. All execution is fail-closed, caregiver-mediated, and non-inferential, with governance enforced by MCG and authorization by EchoAuth.


Scope note: This assurance does not claim autonomous decision-making, diagnosis, intent inference, or independent action.


⸻


2.2 System Role Clarification (No Flag Language)

• NI-AI: Read-only explanation and decision-support layer that evaluates interaction context against predefined safety and policy rules.

• MCG: Governance spine and final authority for allow / pause / deny.

• EchoAuth: Role-based authorization; default state is deny.


Canonical statement:

NI-AI does not independently decide outcomes; all execution is gated by MCG and EchoAuth. When uncertainty exists, the system defaults to non-action and caregiver notification.


⸻


2.3 Logic & Behavior Model

• Rule-based, state-driven logic

• Deterministic checks (no self-modifying behavior)

• Fail-closed defaults

• Non-action is a valid and successful outcome


Prohibited behaviors (by design):

• No emotion/intent inference as truth

• No prediction of child behavior

• No autonomous actions affecting the child


⸻


2.4 Stability & Drift Prevention


NI-AI stability is enforced through layered constraints:

• State validation before any output

• Context coherence checks (session-bounded)

• Pacing and tone bounds (sensory-safe)

• Governance re-evaluation at each transition


Outcome: Long-session interactions remain stable without escalation; ambiguous states resolve to pause/observe.


⸻


2.5 Hallucination Mitigation

• Outputs are constrained by policy rules

• Uncertain states trigger explicit non-action

• Explanations reflect applied rules, not speculation


Result: Reduced unsupported claims and no pressure to “fill silence.”


⸻


2.6 Explainability & Transparency

• Why-not / why-pause explanations available to caregivers and reviewers

• No opaque scoring presented to end users

• Audit-friendly summaries without raw inference claims


⸻


2.7 Safety Posture (Child-Centered)

• Silence and stillness are valid states

• The system never requires verbal confirmation

• Caregivers remain the sole decision authority


⸻


2.8 Oversight & Audit

• All state transitions logged

• Allow / deny / pause decisions recorded

• Logs are reviewable and immutable per policy


⸻


2.9 Assurance Summary


NI-AI demonstrates predictable, restrained, and explainable behavior under governance. The architecture prioritizes non-action under uncertainty, caregiver mediation, and auditability, aligning with federal expectations for safe, trustworthy AI in sensitive contexts.
