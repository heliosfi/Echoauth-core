SECTION 393 — ADAPTIVE TRANSITION PREPARATION ENGINE (ATPE)


(NI-AI Patent Module)

────────────────────────────────────────


393.1 — Technical Field


ATPE relates to systems and methods for preparing children—especially neurodivergent children—for upcoming transitions that are known to increase stress, sensory overload, or behavioral dysregulation.


This module does not use emotional inference or medical logic.

It relies strictly on:

• behavior sequences

• routine timing

• sensory load patterns

• environmental mapping

• caregiver-defined triggers


────────────────────────────────────────


393.2 — Purpose


ATPE reduces meltdown likelihood by:

• predicting transition difficulty

• adjusting timing

• providing preparatory cues

• staging sensory environments

• reducing uncertainty

• offering parent/teacher guidance


This is a major federal grant qualifier, because transitions are the #1 stressor for ASD children.


────────────────────────────────────────


393.3 — Functional Definition


ATPE performs five synchronized tasks:


(a) Transition Detection


Identifies:

• scheduled transitions

• unscheduled transitions

• environmental shifts

• social or physical transitions

• high-risk transitions (school → home, recess → class, etc.)


(b) Difficulty Forecasting


Calculates:

• Sensory Load Change Score (SLCS)

• Behavioral Resistance Likelihood (BRL)

• Routine Disruption Impact (RDI)


(c) Pre-Transition Preparation Plan (PTPP)


Creates a short actionable plan for caregivers or teachers, e.g.:

• “Begin soft countdown (5 minutes).”

• “Shift lighting softer before transition.”

• “Offer preferred object.”

• “Use visual cue: door → hallway → destination.”


(d) Real-Time Adjustment Layer


If the child reacts unexpectedly, ATPE adapts instantly.


(e) Post-Transition Calibration


Learns:

• what worked

• what failed

• how future transitions should be adapted


────────────────────────────────────────


393.4 — Inputs

• Routine logs

• Sensory load readings

• Environmental tags

• Time-of-day mapping

• School schedule API

• Parent schedule input

• Wearable or ZWSM micro-signals


────────────────────────────────────────


393.5 — Outputs

• Transition Difficulty Score (TDS)

• Preparation Action Pack (PAP)

• Real-Time Adjustment Signal (RTAS)

• Post-Transition Calibration File (PTCF)


────────────────────────────────────────


393.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Transition Anticipation Layer (TAL)


Predicts transition difficulty based on the child’s historical patterns.


(b) Sensory Gradient Calculator (SGC)


Measures difference between “current sensory load” vs “upcoming sensory load.”


(c) Micro-Adjustment Directive Layer (MADL)


Delivers real-time “soft support commands.”


(d) Predictive Drift Guard (PDG)


Prevents unapproved reasoning or emotional inference.


(e) Transition Success Memory (TSM)


Stores what worked for future optimization.


────────────────────────────────────────


393.7 — Safety


ATPE is:

• non-medical

• non-emotional

• behavioral only

• parent-directed

• evidence-based

• legally compliant with federal ASD support standards


────────────────────────────────────────
