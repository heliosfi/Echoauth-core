⭐ 9.1 — Tier 1 Engines (Absolute Minimum for NI-AI to Work)


These MUST be built first — nothing else matters until these exist.


T1-A — Intent Engine (S6, S7, S8)


Why first?

• Every system action depends on correct intent.

• If intent fails → the entire NI-AI fails.


Deliverables:

• analyze_intent(text, user, session)

• Output: intent type, confidence, drift-risk


Engineering complexity: medium (LLM + rules)

Priority: #1


⸻


T1-B — Meaning Coherence Engine (S9)


Why second?

• Prevents the model from making nonsensical or misaligned interpretations.

• Necessary before any execution logic.


Deliverables:

• check_meaning(text) and align_with_intent(intent)


Engineering complexity: low–medium

Priority: #2


⸻


T1-C — Temporal Alignment Engine (S10)


Why third?

• Fixes “forgetfulness”

• Fixes “broken chain of thought”

• Ensures continuity across turns


Deliverables:

• Anchor manager + timeline-keeper structure

• Session memory alignment


Engineering complexity: medium

Priority: #3


⸻


T1-D — Field Detection Engine (S12 / S18)


Why fourth?

• EchoAuth and ASD-support rely on environmental context detection

• Public vs private

• Safe vs unsafe

• Emotional vs technical field


Deliverables:

• Field classifier (rule-based hybrid)


Engineering complexity: medium

Priority: #4


⸻


T1-E — Coherence Engine (S21 lite version)


Why fifth?

• The entire architecture collapses without coherence checking

• Needed to prevent tone, logic, and identity drift


Deliverables:

• validate_output(text)

• Flags inconsistencies (light version first)


Engineering complexity: high

Priority: #5


⸻


T1-F — Execution Engine (S22 lite version)


Why sixth?

• Converts understanding → action

• Enables NI-AI to become a DOING system


Deliverables:

• Step-by-step plan generator

• Simple action selector


Engineering complexity: medium–high

Priority: #6
