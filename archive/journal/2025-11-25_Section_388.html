SECTION 388 — DOCTOR / HOSPITAL SAFETY MODAL (DHSM)


(Medical-Grade Predictive Safety & Stabilization for ASD, Panic, Trauma, and Communication Barriers)


⸻


388.1 — Technical Field


DHSM concerns medical-environment safety states, clinical interaction prediction, and real-time overload stabilization for autistic individuals, nonverbal individuals, and patients experiencing sensory trauma or dysregulation.


It is designed for:

• Hospitals

• Emergency rooms

• Ambulances

• Urgent care centers

• Pediatric clinics

• Behavioral intervention centers


DHSM does not diagnose — it stabilizes, interprets, predicts, and protects.


⸻


388.2 — Purpose


Medical environments are the highest-sensory, highest-stress places for ASD patients.

Hospitals struggle because:

• Lights are bright

• Sounds are chaotic

• Routines break

• People talk too fast

• Doctors misread body language

• Panic or shutdown appears suddenly

• Nonverbal children cannot explain pain


DHSM solves this by creating a medical-safe mode:


“NI-AI becomes the calm, translation layer between the patient and the medical team.”


⸻


388.3 — Core Functions


(a) Clinical Mode Activation (CMA)


Triggered automatically when near:

• hospital WiFi fingerprint

• BLE beacon

• ambulance audio pattern

• medical staff badge signatures

• user manual toggle


Switches NI-AI into medical interaction rules instantly.


⸻


(b) Sensory Risk Predictor (SRP-M)


Predicts overload 30–120 seconds before it happens inside:

• waiting rooms

• triage

• exam rooms

• ER intake

• imaging rooms (MRI, CT, X-ray)


Outputs visual/soft prompts to de-escalate.


⸻


(c) Medical Communication Interpreter (MCI)


Converts:

• fast speech → simplified

• complex medical terms → accessible

• sensory-threatening tone → softer delivery


It also provides real-time suggestions to the clinician:


“Patient is overwhelmed—pause 3 seconds between questions.”


⸻


(d) Panic or Shutdown Detection Layer (PSDL)


Detects the earliest signs of:

• meltdown

• freeze response

• dissociation

• sensory denial

• breathing irregularity

• involuntary stillness


Provides alerts to both parent and clinician.


⸻


(e) Medical Interaction Safety Script (MISS)


Preloaded scripts for:

• temperature checks

• blood draws

• vaccinations

• wound cleaning

• anesthesia prep

• dental procedures


It guides the child/patient gently through each step.


⸻


(f) Emergency Override Pathway (EOP)


If parent is unavailable and a medically urgent moment arises:

• NI-AI grants temporary “minimal authority mode” to clinicians

• only for actions related to immediate safety

• logs ALL permissions

• parent can revoke retroactively


This keeps the system safe AND compliant.


⸻


388.4 — Safety Mechanisms

• No emotional data is shared with hospitals

• Only safety-state signals or overload warnings

• All medical staff access filtered through a one-time code

• All interactions logged in the Medical Safety Ledger

• Parent remains highest authority

• HIPAA, FERPA, and COPPA enforced automatically


⸻


388.5 — Outputs

• Clinical Safety State (CSS)

• Overload Prediction Score (OPS-M)

• Medical Calm-Path Guide (MCG)

• Clinical Staff Advisory Signal (CSAS)

• Panic-State Early Warning (PSEW)

• Medical Interaction Summary (MIS)


⸻
