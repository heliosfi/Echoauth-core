NI-AI System Security Plan (SSP)


2. System Overview


Purpose


NI-AI (Natural Intellect Artificial Intelligence) is a governance-oriented cognitive support framework designed to provide controlled, auditable, and authority-aligned human-AI interaction.


The system is architected to maintain contextual continuity, identity assurance, operational stability, and authorization integrity across supported environments.


Unlike autonomous decision-making systems, NI-AI functions as an interpretation and coordination layer. Final authority remains with approved human stakeholders and established governance policies.


Architectural Principles


NI-AI is built upon four foundational functions:


Meaning


Interpretation of user input and contextual information.

Context preservation across interactions.

Traceable reasoning support.


Judgment


Governance validation and policy enforcement.

Risk evaluation and escalation management.

Authority verification before execution.


Order


Controlled execution of approved actions.

Sequential processing safeguards.

Operational consistency controls.


Identity


Authentication, authorization, and accountability.

Verification of user authority and permissions.

Audit-ready access control mechanisms.


Core Components


The NI-AI platform consists of:


NI-AI Interpretation Layer

EchoAuth Identity and Authorization Framework

Meaning Coordination Governance (MCG)

Controlled Execution Governor (CEG)

Stability Regulation Services

Audit and Traceability Services

Context Preservation Engine

Monitoring and Integrity Services


Deployment Model


NI-AI may operate within:


Government Cloud Environments

Hybrid Cloud Architectures

On-Premises Secure Facilities

Edge Computing Deployments


Deployment configurations are subject to organizational security requirements and applicable regulatory controls.


Security Philosophy


NI-AI is designed around a refusal-first governance model.


When authority, context, identity, or policy requirements cannot be verified, the system defaults to a restricted or non-executing state until validation requirements are satisfied.


This approach supports:


Least privilege principles

Human oversight requirements

Auditability

Deterministic authorization

Operational safety


Intended Use Cases


NI-AI is intended to support environments requiring:


Identity assurance

Governance-controlled workflows

Context-aware decision support

Human-supervised AI interaction

Traceable authorization processes

Secure multi-party coordination


The system is not intended to operate as an autonomous decision-maker and does not replace human authority, organizational governance, or regulatory oversight.
