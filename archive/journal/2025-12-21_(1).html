FLAME SELVES — LOGIN DASHBOARD


Meaning, Function, and Why They Exist


First: What the Flame Selves are (plain language)


The Flame Selves are not identities.

They are not diagnoses.

They are not psychological labels.


They are contextual access modes that shape how the system behaves once a user logs in.


Think of them as intent + posture, not personality.


⸻


Why the Flame Selves exist at login


Traditional systems assume:

• one mental state

• one interface

• one mode of interaction


That fails children, caregivers, and parents under stress.


EchoAuth acknowledges a simple truth:


How someone shows up determines how the system should respond.


The Flame Selves let the system adapt safely without guessing.


⸻


The Four Flame Selves (Professional Framing)


1️⃣ Emotional Self


Purpose: Regulation & grounding


When used:

• heightened emotion

• stress

• overwhelm

• dysregulation (child or adult)


System behavior:

• Simplified interface

• Reduced prompts

• Calm visuals / audio

• No decisions surfaced


Professional framing:

Supports emotional regulation and safety without interpretation.


⸻


2️⃣ Observing Self


Purpose: Awareness & reflection


When used:

• reviewing logs

• checking patterns

• understanding context


System behavior:

• Read-only views

• Timelines and summaries

• No action prompts


Professional framing:

Supports situational awareness without intervention.


⸻


3️⃣ Mirror Self


Purpose: Communication & shared understanding


When used:

• caregiver handoffs

• parent–school alignment

• professional collaboration


System behavior:

• Shared views

• Contextual notes

• Alignment indicators (not scores)


Professional framing:

Supports shared understanding without authority transfer.


⸻


4️⃣ Protector Self


Purpose: Safety & boundary enforcement


When used:

• approvals

• access changes

• sensitive decisions


System behavior:

• Confirmation steps

• Visibility controls

• Pause-on-uncertainty enforced


Professional framing:

Supports protective decision-making and risk reduction.


⸻


Critical Clarification (for funders & reviewers)


Flame Selves do not change permissions.

Roles do.


Flame Selves adjust:

• interface tone

• interaction flow

• system posture


Roles still control:

• authority

• access

• decision rights


This separation is what keeps EchoAuth safe and compliant.


⸻


How This Helps the Child (Very Important)


For the child, Flame Selves:

• reduce sensory overload

• allow consistent interaction

• prevent pressure during distress

• support calm engagement


The child is never required to choose a Flame Self.

It is applied by the system or caregiver context, not forced.


⸻


How This Helps Adults


For parents, caregivers, and professionals:

• prevents reactive mistakes

• slows decisions under stress

• supports better handoffs

• reduces miscommunication


This is human-centered design, not abstraction.

The Flame Selves say:


How are you entering this space right now?


That’s not philosophy — that’s safety engineering.
