⭐ PATENT SECTION 6 — DEFINITIONS


(Exact form used in professional patents — legally binding terminology)


6.1 “Neuroadaptive System”


A computing framework capable of adjusting its analysis, predictions, and interventions based on the real-time behavioral, sensory, emotional, and medical states of a neurodivergent individual.


⸻


6.2 “Neurodivergent Individual”


A person exhibiting cognitive, sensory, or behavioral patterns associated with autism spectrum disorder (ASD), ADHD, sensory processing disorder, or other neurological profiles that diverge from neurotypical norms.


⸻


6.3 “Nonverbal Signals”


Any communicative behavior not expressed through spoken language, including but not limited to:

• stimming

• posture

• breathing patterns

• movement intensity

• gaze shifts

• micro-expressions

• pacing

• withdrawal

• motor hesitation

• vocalizations without semantic content


⸻


6.4 “Behavioral Micro-Patterns”


Subtle, early-phase movement or physiological changes that precede escalation, shutdown, overload, or emotional dysregulation.


Examples include:

• increased pacing speed

• breath irregularities

• hand tension

• rocking rhythm changes

• stillness before shutdown


⸻


6.5 “Sensory Load”


The total effect of environmental stimuli—including sound, light, motion, temperature, and crowding—on the user’s nervous system.


⸻


6.6 “Overload State”


A condition in which sensory or emotional stimuli exceed the user’s processing capacity, potentially leading to escalation, meltdown, or shutdown.


⸻


6.7 “NI–AI Fusion Intelligence (F.I.K)”


A proprietary hybrid computing methodology that combines:

• Natural Intellect (NI):

Human insight, caregiver intuition, child-specific understanding, cultural context, emotional knowledge.

• Artificial Intelligence (AI):

Machine learning models that analyze sensor data, detect patterns, classify behaviors, predict escalation, and trigger interventions.


The fusion determines system outputs, ensuring AI is guided by NI rather than replacing it.


⸻


6.8 “Natural Intellect (NI)”


Human-derived knowledge, including:

• parental intuition

• lived experience

• emotional resonance

• cultural and contextual understanding

• situational awareness

• direct observation of the child’s patterns


NI includes pattern knowledge that cannot be mathematically computed but can be translated into rule-based systems.


⸻


6.9 “Artificial Intelligence (AI)”


Machine learning, neural networks, or algorithmic processes that interpret behavioral, sensory, environmental, or medical data to produce predictions or classifications.


⸻


6.10 “Adaptive Intervention”


Any automatically or manually triggered support action generated by the system, including:

• music regulation

• caregiver prompts

• sensory adjustments

• predictive safety alerts

• emotional guidance

• environmental recommendations


⸻


6.11 “Music Regulation Engine (MRE)”


The system module that uses frequency-specific auditory tracks to regulate emotional and sensory states, including:

• calming

• focusing

• grounding

• shutdown-prevention loops


⸻


6.12 “Multi-Environment Integration”


EchoAuth’s ability to operate across:

• home

• school

• medical/clinical settings

• public/community environments


while adjusting its logic to the sensory and behavioral expectations of each environment.


⸻


6.13 “Environmental Signature”


The unique sensory and behavioral profile of an environment, defined by:

• noise levels

• lighting

• motion density

• crowding

• transitions

• temperature

• risk patterns

• event frequency


⸻


6.14 “Nonverbal Expression Decoding Engine (NEDE)”


A computational module that interprets nonverbal signals into structured communicative meaning, enabling caregivers, educators, and clinicians to understand internal states.


⸻


6.15 “Medical Early Warning Pattern”


A combination of behavioral, physiological, or environmental signals that indicate potential medical issues such as dehydration, fatigue, infection, pain, or systemic distress.


⸻


6.16 “Ethical Camera Protocol (ECP)”


A sensor-processing workflow that analyzes movement and behavioral patterns without facial recognition, biometric identification, or long-term storage of images, ensuring privacy and ethical compliance.


⸻


6.17 “State Classification Model”


A predictive model that assigns the user to one of several defined states, including:

• calm

• rising stress

• sensory overload

• emotional dysregulation

• shutdown

• medical risk

• environmental danger


⸻


6.18 “Caregiver Override Rule”


A mechanism that ensures NI (caregiver intuition or observation) supersedes AI decisions, allowing parents or guardians to adjust, correct, or override system classifications.


⸻


6.19 “Fusion Weighting Logic”


The algorithm that determines how much influence NI or AI holds when interpreting specific signals or generating interventions.


⸻


6.20 “Neuroadaptive Intervention Threshold”


The minimum combination of signals required to trigger a predictive alert, a sensory adjustment, a musical regulation track, or a medical recommendation.
