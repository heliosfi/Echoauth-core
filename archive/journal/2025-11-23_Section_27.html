⭐ 27.6 Behavior & Emotional Interpretation in Vehicles


ETSM uses NEDE + ESRE + BPE to interpret:

• seat rocking

• toy gripping

• foot tapping

• covering ears

• freezing

• rapid breathing

• pacing attempt

• sudden silence

• repetitive motion change


Each becomes a vehicle-safe meaning.


Examples:

• rocking → self-regulation

• covering ears → sensory overload

• leaning forward abruptly → fear

• toy drop + slump → shutdown

• seatbelt fight → distress spike

• turning head from window → overstimulation

• hand squeeze intensifies → emotional overload

⭐ 27.7 Elopement & Escape Detection


One of the biggest dangers during transport.


ETSM detects:

• unbuckling patterns

• reaching for door

• positioning near exit

• sudden movement spikes

• attempt to stand while vehicle moving

• attempt to flee when vehicle stops


If detected:


ETSM triggers:

1. Wearable strong vibration

2. Caregiver/driver vibration alert

3. Vehicle-safe alert sound (soft tone)

4. Lock-down suggestion (for school buses)
