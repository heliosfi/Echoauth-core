⭐ 194.1 — Micro-Pattern Extraction Framework (MPEF)


Extracts ultra-small behavior signals such as:

• micro-pauses

• subtle facial shifts

• blink rate irregularities

• decreased movement

• sudden increase in rigidity

• finger tension patterns

• micro-shifts in breathing

• object fixation

• change in vocal rhythm

• repetitive scanning of environment


Outputs:

• Micro-Pattern Vector (MPV)

• Deviation Score (DS)

⭐ 194.2 — Behavior Arc Modeling Layer (BAML)


This subsystem maps where the child’s behavior is trending.


Examples of behavior arc types:

• calm → curious

• calm → anxious

• anxious → shutdown

• curious → overstimulated

• overwhelmed → meltdown risk

• focused → fatigued

• stable → dysregulated


This creates a predictive trajectory called:

• Behavior Arc Projection (BAP)

⭐ 194.3 — Hidden-State Transition Predictor (HSTP)


Many ASD children do NOT show clear emotional signals.


HSTP infers the “hidden emotional state” based on:

• micro-movement

• energy drop

• gaze withdrawal

• stiffness or stillness

• muted responses

• pacing changes

• breathing suppression


Outputs:

• Hidden State Estimate (HSE)

• Transition Probability (TP)

(e.g., 72% chance of shutdown in 2–3 minutes)

This predicts cognitive fatigue by measuring:

• typing quality

• engagement rhythm

• attention drift

• delay in response

• decrease in instruction-following

• pacing changes

• irritability markers


Outputs:

• Cognitive Fatigue Index (CFI)

• Expected Decline Timeline (EDT)
