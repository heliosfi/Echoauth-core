SECTION 380 — SAFETY LOCUS MATRIX (SLM)


(Safety Map Layer 2 — The Central Safety “Brainstem” of NI-AI)


⸻


380.1 — Technical Field


The Safety Locus Matrix (SLM) concerns integrated AI safety frameworks, specifically systems for building a centralized safety-coordinate map that controls, prioritizes, and enforces safe behavior across all NI-AI subsystems, wearables, devices, and cloud/federated environments.


SLM forms the core safety authority of NI-AI.


⸻


380.2 — Purpose


The SLM answers one question:


“What is the safest possible action NI-AI can take in this moment?”


It does this by:

• Pulling real-time safety data from every subsystem

• Calculating global safety scores

• Applying parent/caregiver + school rules

• Blocking unsafe reasoning or actions

• Selecting the safest next step

• Broadcasting that decision to the entire system


It is the final decision maker during safety events.


⸻


380.3 — Functional Definition


The SLM functions as a unified safety-coordination matrix by performing:


(a) Safety Signal Aggregation


Collects inputs from:

• ESE (Emotional State Engine)

• SLE (Sensory Load Engine)

• ESS (Environmental Safety Scanner)

• BPE (Behavior Prediction Engine)

• MSSR (State Synchronization Relay)

• Wearable safety signals

• Offline/phone-only ZWSM mode

• Parent intent rules

• School district safety policy


(b) Safety Vector Scoring


Creates a multi-dimensional vector representing:

• Physical safety

• Emotional safety

• Sensory safety

• Social safety

• Predictive safety

• Context continuity


(c) Safety Priority Mapping


Ranks possible system actions from safest → least safe.


(d) Locus Calculation


The “locus” is the exact safety position NI-AI is allowed to operate within at that moment:

• Range-limited

• Intensity-limited

• Direction-limited

• Depth-limited

• Risk-locked


(e) Block / Allow Engine


If a subsystem chooses an unsafe action, SLM overrides it immediately.


(f) Safety Broadcast


Pushes the final safety state & restrictions system-wide.


⸻

Safety Locus Matrix receives:

• Emotional Load Index (ELI)

• Sensory Overload Index (SOI)

• Drift Risk Score (DRS)

• Real-time environmental classification (home, school, public, vehicle)

• Behavioral risk predictions

• Parent/Caregiver Allowed Zones

• School District Fatigue Rules

• Device hardware limitations

• Offline/no-signal fallback status

• Threat or conflict warnings

• ESCALATION ladder levels (AESL)


⸻


380.5 — Outputs


SLM produces:


(a) Global Safety Locus (GSL)


The unified safety position of the entire system.


(b) Safety Constraint Packet (SCP)


A set of mandatory restrictions distributed to:

• ESE

• Prediction Engine

• Wearables

• ZWSM

• FSDL (school deployment layer)

• F.I.-Kernel


(c) Priority Safety Response (PSR)


The “safest next action.”


(d) System-Wide Safety State (SWSS)


A timestamped global safety snapshot.


(e) Safety Override Notice (SON)


Logs any override events for regulatory compliance.


⸻


380.6 — Internal Mechanisms


(a) Multi-Signal Fusion Core (MSFC)


Combines all safety signals into one multidimensional tensor.


(b) Locus Stability Controller (LSC)


Makes sure the safety locus does not abruptly change unless necessary.


(c) Risk Gradient Mapper (RGM)


Calculates how risk changes over time and across states.


(d) Safety Pressure Valve (SPV)


Prevents overload of any single safety domain (emotional, sensory, or predictive).


(e) Cross-State Locus Harmonizer (CSLH)


Ensures the locus remains consistent across:

• Offline mode

• Wearable mode

• School federated mode

• Home environment

• High-stress events


(f) Safety Ledger Writer (SLW)


Records every safety decision for:

• Parents

• Teachers

• Medical professionals

• Regulators
