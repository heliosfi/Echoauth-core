⭐ 17.4 Safety Processing Pipeline


MESE executes safety logic in 5 major steps:


Step 1 — Hazard Aggregation


Combines all sensory, behavioral, emotional, and environmental hazard inputs.


Step 2 — Risk Scoring


Uses the Unified Safety Risk Index (USRI):

• 0–20: Safe

• 21–40: Mild

• 41–60: Moderate

• 61–80: High

• 81–100: Critical


Step 3 — Cross-Engine Correlation


Example connections:

• Crowds + pacing → elopement risk

• Noise + anxiety → sensory overload

• Closed posture + fatigue → shutdown approaching

• Breath irregularity + movement collapse → medical alert


Step 4 — NI–AI Safety Fusion


Parent caregiver safety rules override generic logic.


Examples:

• “He runs when overwhelmed → increase exit danger sensitivity.”

• “She hides under tables when scared → indoor hazards increased.”

• “He has kidney history → dehydration risk doubled.”


This makes MESE hyper-personalized.


Step 5 — Output Generation


Produces action steps based on environment, risk level, and signals.

⭐ 17.5 Safety States Identified by MESE


MESE categorizes safety into five clear levels:


1. Safe Zone


Predictable environment, child stable.


2. Mild Alert


Some sensory/behavior changes.


3. Moderate Danger


Pacing, sensory spikes, or environment shifts.


4. High Danger


Elopement probability, sensory overload, emotional escalation.


5. Critical Danger


Medical emergency, meltdown, wandering attempt, shutdown.
