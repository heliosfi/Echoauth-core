PARENT PORTAL — AUTHORITY, COORDINATION, AND PROTECTION


Purpose (professional, plain)


The Parent Portal exists to preserve parental authority, reduce coordination burden, and allow parents to safely organize support around their child without surrendering control to institutions or systems.


This is the decision center of EchoAuth.


⸻


1️⃣ Authority & Consent (Core Function)


What the parent can do

• Approve or decline:

• programs

• supports

• tasks

• caregiver participation

• school or clinical recommendations


What this means

• Nothing becomes active for the child without parent approval

• No automatic activation

• No professional override


Professional framing:

Parental authority is enforced structurally, not procedurally.


⸻


2️⃣ Program & Content Control


What the parent controls

• Which child programs are enabled

• Which sensory or music supports are available

• Which educational content the child can access


How this works

• Schools and clinicians may recommend

• Parents decide

• Caregivers follow approved use


Professional framing:

Content access is curated and consistent across environments.


⸻


3️⃣ Caregiver Management (Human Support)


What the parent can do

• Invite or remove caregivers

• Assign task scope (what they can do)

• Control what caregivers can see

• Limit access by time or role


Why this matters

• Parents are not forced to rely only on family

• Help is available without chaos or loss of control


Professional framing:

Caregiver participation is parent-governed and task-scoped.


⸻


4️⃣ Routine, Schedule, and Reminder Control


What the parent sets

• Daily routines

• Care schedules

• Reminder timing

• Check-in expectations


What the system does

• Sends factual reminders

• Logs completion or missed actions

• Does not interpret or escalate without permission


Professional framing:

This supports continuity of care, not clinical decision-making.


⸻


5️⃣ Data Visibility & Privacy Control


What the parent controls

• Who can see what data

• Which logs are visible to which roles

• Whether schools or clinics can view summaries


Why this matters

• Prevents overexposure

• Maintains trust

• Supports compliance


Professional framing:

Privacy is role-based and parent-controlled.


⸻


6️⃣ Cameras & Wearables Governance


What the parent controls

• Whether cameras are enabled

• Whether wearables are connected

• Where and when sensors can be active

• Who can see summaries


What the parent does NOT lose

• Sensors never make decisions

• Sensors never diagnose

• Sensors never override parent authority


Professional framing:

Contextual tools operate only under explicit parental consent.


⸻


7️⃣ Oversight & Accountability


What the parent can see

• Activity logs

• Task execution history

• Recommendations vs decisions

• System pauses and alerts


Why this matters

• Parents are never “out of the loop”

• Accountability is visible, not assumed


Professional framing:

The Parent Portal provides oversight without micromanagement.


⸻


8️⃣ What the Parent Portal Explicitly Does NOT Do

• ❌ Diagnose

• ❌ Prescribe

• ❌ Automate decisions

• ❌ Replace professional judgment

• ❌ Force workflows on the child


Professional framing:

The Parent Portal governs coordination, not care delivery.


⸻
