⭐ 46.2 — THE 4-LAYER LANGUAGE DECODING STACK (LDS)


EchoAuth decodes language for the child in layers:


⭐ Layer 1 — Literal Meaning


“What do the words mean?”


⭐ Layer 2 — Intent Meaning


“What is the person trying to do?”


⭐ Layer 3 — Emotional Meaning


“What feeling is behind the words?”


⭐ Layer 4 — Social Meaning


“What does this mean for the situation?”


This avoids confusion and misinterpretation.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 46.3 — ASD-SAFE QUESTION MODEL (ASQM)


Questions are simplified into three types:


⭐ Type 1 — Choice


“Do you want A or B?”


⭐ Type 2 — Action


“What do you want to do next?”


⭐ Type 3 — Information


“What do you need?”


No trick questions.

No hidden expectations.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 46.4 — COMMUNICATION REGULATION ENGINE (CRE)


EchoAuth regulates communication based on:


✔ sensory load

✔ processing speed

✔ emotional state

✔ focus level

✔ environmental noise


If overloaded, EchoAuth switches to:


✔ fewer words

✔ slower pace

✔ visuals only

✔ single-step prompts

✔ quiet-tone guidance


Communication never overwhelms.

• icons

⭐ 46.6 — THE DELAYED-PROCESSING SUPPORT LOOP (DPSL)


ASD children often need extra time to process language.


EchoAuth provides:


✔ silence

✔ waiting

✔ countdowns

✔ slow transitions

✔ patience

✔ predictable pauses


Never:


❌ “Answer me now”

❌ “Hurry”

❌ “Why aren’t you speaking?”


EchoAuth matches the brain’s timing.

⭐ 46.7 — THE CALM EXPRESSION MODEL (CEM)


EchoAuth teaches children to express themselves using:


✔ short phrases

✔ visual choices

✔ pointing

✔ gestures

✔ calm tone

✔ safe scripts

• “I feel fast.”

• “Help please.”

• “Not now.”

• “I want quiet.”


This builds functional communication without pressure.
