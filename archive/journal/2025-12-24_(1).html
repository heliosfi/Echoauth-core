EchoAuth Regulatory Positioning Memo

Date: December 23, 2025
Prepared by: [Your Name/Team]
Purpose: To outline EchoAuth’s alignment with key U.S. regulatory frameworks, emphasizing its role as a governance-first coordination tool for caregivers. This memo positions EchoAuth as infrastructure that supports compliance without engaging in regulated activities like data mining, profiling, or clinical assessment.

Overview of EchoAuth
EchoAuth is a layered system designed to assist caregivers in managing routines and permissions for dependents (e.g., children or individuals with support needs). It prioritizes human authority through:

• MCG (Melanin Cognitive Governance): Enforces invariants and halts under ambiguity.

• NI (Natural Intellect Layer): Encodes explicit human-defined rules and approvals.

• AI (Bounded Support Layer): Provides advisory organization only, with no autonomous decision-making or prediction.

The system logs actions transparently, defaults to conservative protections, and requires explicit consents. It does not collect, analyze, or infer sensitive personal data beyond what is necessary for user-directed coordination.

Alignment with FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)

• Key FERPA Requirements: Protects student education records from unauthorized disclosure; requires consent for release; mandates safeguards for directory information.

• EchoAuth Positioning: EchoAuth is not an educational record system and does not store or process “education records” as defined under FERPA (e.g., no grades, assessments, or institutional data). It functions as a caregiver tool for non-educational routines (e.g., daily schedules). If integrated with educational contexts:

◦ All data inputs are explicit and revocable by guardians.

◦ No sharing occurs without affirmative consent.

◦ Logs support auditability, ensuring compliance with parental inspection rights.

• Risk Mitigation: Defaults halt execution without authorization, preventing accidental disclosures. EchoAuth avoids “directory information” handling to sidestep opt-out complexities.

Alignment with COPPA (Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act)

• Key COPPA Requirements: Applies to operators collecting personal information from children under 13; requires verifiable parental consent; limits data use; mandates data security and deletion options.

• EchoAuth Positioning: EchoAuth is not directed at children and does not collect data from or about users under 13 without direct parental control. It operates as a parent/guardian-managed platform:

◦ No child-facing interfaces; all inputs and outputs route through authorized adults.

◦ Data is minimal, explicit, and not used for advertising, profiling, or third-party sharing.

◦ Revocable consents and immutable logs align with parental control and notice requirements.

• Risk Mitigation: Prohibitions on AI behavioral prediction or inference prevent any “personal information” derivation. System design assumes no direct child interaction, qualifying for COPPA exemptions where applicable.

Alignment with ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)

• Key ADA Requirements: Prohibits discrimination based on disability; requires reasonable accommodations in public services, employment, and communications; emphasizes accessibility in technology.

• EchoAuth Positioning: EchoAuth supports inclusive caregiving without classifying or diagnosing disabilities. It is positioned as an accessibility-enhancing tool:

◦ Customizable routines accommodate diverse needs (e.g., reminders, permissions) without assumptions.

◦ No diagnostic functions; AI is bounded to organization, reducing barriers for caregivers.

◦ Design favors clarity and human oversight, aligning with ADA’s emphasis on equal access.

• Risk Mitigation: Uncertainty handling pauses actions and seeks authority, avoiding overreach that could impact individuals with disabilities. Future integrations can include WCAG-compliant interfaces for broader accessibility.

General Compliance Strategy

• Data Minimization: EchoAuth collects only what is explicitly provided for coordination, with no aggregation or secondary uses.

• Transparency & Accountability: Immutable logs and clear decision flows enable audits and demonstrate human-centric control.

• Non-Clinical Stance: EchoAuth is coordination infrastructure, not a medical or diagnostic device, avoiding FDA/HIPAA overlaps.

• Recommendations: Conduct third-party audits for certification; include opt-in consents in onboarding; monitor for regulatory updates. This positions EchoAuth as a low-risk, supportive tool that empowers caregivers while respecting protections.

Next Steps: Review for legal vetting; integrate into funding or partnership materials. If adjustments are needed, provide feedback.
