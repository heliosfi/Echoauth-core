DECLARATION OF DAILY CARE PRACTICES


(Administrative / Agency Use)


I, __________________________, declare as follows:


⸻


1.


I am a caregiver and member of a household providing continuous, adaptive care to a minor whose needs vary in intensity, timing, and expression.


2.


This declaration is made to provide a factual description of daily operational conditions relevant to administrative review, eligibility consideration, or case documentation.


3.


The household consists of two parents and two children. One child requires support that exceeds the capacity of a single caregiver.


4.


Care responsibilities within the household are allocated dynamically in response to daily conditions rather than fixed role assignments.


5.


No caregiver within the household is fully off duty at any point during the day or night.


6.


Daily conditions are non-deterministic and may change without advance notice, including but not limited to changes in sleep patterns, appetite, bathroom needs, activity tolerance, and supervision requirements.


7.


Planning and response are therefore adaptive and adjusted on an ongoing basis. Fixed schedules are used when feasible but are not assumed to remain stable.


8.


The minor demonstrates the ability to express certain preferences and needs, including food selection, bathroom use, and activity engagement.


9.


Execution of expressed preferences is subject to caregiver evaluation to ensure safety, health, and daily functional stability.


10.


Decision authority remains with caregivers when expressed preferences conflict with safety, health, or stability considerations.


11.


Available food options are limited. On certain days, items not aligned with ideal nutritional standards are intentionally provided to support behavioral regulation and completion of daily activities.


12.


Such food-related decisions are time-limited, context-specific, and reviewed continuously based on observed outcomes.


13.


Behavioral signals are continuously monitored. Deviations from expected engagement patterns, including extended or unusual silence, are treated as indicators requiring increased observation.


14.


Indicators such as extended silence may correspond to physical discomfort, unmet needs, or engagement in unsafe activity.


15.


When potential risk is identified, the initial response consists of physical presence, visual confirmation, and environmental assessment.


16.


Sensory input may be reduced, and supervision increased, as part of initial safety response procedures.


17.


Directive intervention is applied only when necessary to prevent harm.


18.


Safety within the household is primarily maintained through environmental controls rather than reliance on behavioral compliance.


19.


Potential hazards are identified and removed in advance. The environment is arranged to minimize reliance on perfect memory or continuous corrective action.


20.


Safety planning assumes the possibility of human error.


21.


The sibling participates in shared living space and daily activities but is not assigned responsibility for safety monitoring or risk mitigation.


22.


Care decisions are made with incomplete information and under time constraints.


23.


While not all decisions yield optimal outcomes, repeated exposure to similar conditions improves response time and recognition accuracy.


24.


Daily outcomes are evaluated on a per-day basis.


25.


A day is considered successful if safety is maintained and required activities are completed without significant disruption.


26.


Long-term optimization is secondary to maintaining daily continuity.


27.


External observations, advice, or assumptions may not reflect the specific conditions present on a given day.


28.


Decision authority resides with the caregivers responsible for daily management and direct consequences.


29.


Care decisions prioritize what is required on the current day to maintain safety and functional stability.


30.


This declaration is descriptive, non-diagnostic, and does not request evaluation, approval, or clinical determination.
