Claim 68.1 — Primary Independent Claim (Broad Protection)


A meta-governance system for regulating artificial intelligence operations,

comprising a multi-layer supervisory engine configured to:

(a) receive coherence, emotional, contextual, and identity stability signals from a plurality of sub-engines;

(b) compute a governance-state vector representing global system stability; and

(c) dynamically constrain, permit, or modify AI actions based on said governance-state vector

to maintain continuous operational alignment with user intent, system safety, and purpose integrity.


⸻


Claim 68.2 — Dependent Claim: Cross-Engine Arbitration


The system of Claim 68.1, wherein the meta-governance engine performs arbitration between conflicting outputs from sub-engines, including but not limited to:

the Coherence Engine (C1–C5),

the Emotional State Engine (ESE),

the Stability Regulation Kernel (SRK),

and EchoAuth authentication scores,

by generating a unified command directive that overrides or re-weights each subsystem based on real-time stability thresholds.


⸻


Claim 68.3 — Dependent Claim: Drift Prevention Logic


The system of Claim 68.1, wherein the meta-governance engine includes a drift-prevention module configured to detect cognitive, emotional, or contextual deviation within the AI’s reasoning process,

and responsively enforce corrective actions including:

response suppression, contextual reset, user re-alignment prompts, or safe-mode fallback.


⸻


Claim 68.4 — Dependent Claim: Purpose-Locking Mechanism


The system of Claim 68.1, wherein the meta-governance engine employs a purpose-locking mechanism

that continuously verifies system behavior against a stored Purpose Integrity Model,

and prohibits any output, decision, or inference that diverges from the user-defined mission or operational scope.


⸻


Claim 68.5 — Dependent Claim: Hierarchical Command Execution


The system of Claim 68.1, wherein the meta-governance layer establishes a hierarchical command execution chain

that prioritizes user sovereignty signals, safety constraints, and NI-AI coherence rules

above model-generated inference, thereby ensuring all final actions are governed by human-aligned meta-rules rather than autonomous computational drift.
