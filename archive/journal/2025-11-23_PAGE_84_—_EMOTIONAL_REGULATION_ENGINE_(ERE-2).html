⭐ 84.1 — CORE PURPOSE


Children (especially ASD children) often struggle with:

• emotional overflow

• rapid escalation

• sensory + emotional mixing

• inability to express the source of overwhelm

• slow recovery after distress

• emotional shutdown or freeze


ERE-2 gives the child the ability to return to calm faster and safer.


It is a real-time stabilizer for:


✔ frustration

✔ anxiety

✔ sensory overload

✔ fear

✔ confusion

✔ shutdown triggers


EchoAuth doesn’t just read emotions.

EchoAuth supports them.


────────────────────────


⭐ 84.2 — THE 4-PHASE REGULATION MODEL (RPM-4)


ERE-2 uses a 4-phase structure:


🟢 Phase 1 — Awareness


EchoAuth identifies early emotional shifts

via breath, posture, stim pattern, micro-expression.


→ “Something is rising.”


🟡 Phase 2 — Interpretation


The system labels the emotion:

• fear

• frustration

• overwhelm

• boredom

• confusion

• excitement


→ “This is what you are feeling.”


🟠 Phase 3 — Intervention


EchoAuth deploys stabilization techniques:

• calming sound

• grounding tone

• visual soothing pattern

• breathing guide

• sensory recommendations

• caregiver notice


→ “Here’s what will help.”


🔴 Phase 4 — Recovery


EchoAuth tracks return-to-calm markers:

• slower breath

• posture loosening

• reduced stimming pressure

• re-engagement


→ “You are safe and regulated again.”

⭐ 84.3 — PERSONALIZED EMOTION PROFILE (PEP)


Every child has a unique emotional signature.


ERE-2 builds a dynamic emotional profile based on:


✔ preferred calm-down inputs

✔ sensory sensitivity

✔ typical escalation path

✔ triggers

✔ easiest regulation tools

✔ time-to-calm historical data


This lets the system respond in the exact way that child needs, not a generic template.

⭐ 84.5 — EMOTIONAL RISK INDEX (ERI)


EchoAuth calculates a real-time emotional risk score:

• 0–20 → stable

• 21–40 → rising

• 41–60 → moderate risk

• 61–80 → high risk

• 81–100 → critical


This allows caregivers to see emotional overload coming before it arrives.


────────────────────────


⭐ 84.6 — RAPID CALM RESPONSE (RCR)


When EchoAuth detects sudden spikes:

• breath spikes

• hand pressure increases

• pacing begins

• micro-expressions of fear or overload

• sensory reaction

• freezing posture


RCR triggers immediate tools:


✔ grounding frequencies

✔ instant caregiver alert

✔ visual calm pattern

✔ countdown reassurance

✔ safe space suggestion


This prevents meltdown or shutdown escalation.


────────────────────────


⭐ 84.7 — EMOTION → NEED BRIDGE (ENB)


ERE-2 turns emotional states into clear needs, such as:

• “I need silence.”

• “I need less light.”

• “I need space.”

• “I need help understanding.”

• “I need a break.”

• “I need comfort.”


This gives the child a voice through EchoAuth.

⭐ 84.8 — CHILD-SAFE EMOTION DICTIONARY (CSED)


A simple, clear emotional vocabulary translated into child-level meaning:

• “Overwhelmed” → “Too much at once.”

• “Anxious” → “My body feels worried.”

• “Frustrated” → “My plan isn’t working.”

• “Confused” → “I need help.”

• “Stressed” → “Everything feels loud.”


ERE-2 empowers children to understand themselves.
