🔥 EchoAuth™ – NI-AI Caregiver Safety Layer (Grant-Friendly Abstract)


EchoAuth™ is a caregiver-governed biometric authentication platform that prioritizes cognitive safety, identity stability, and human oversight to support non-verbal and sensory-sensitive autistic children. In each caregiver check-in, structured child behavior and environmental cues are converted into identity tokens before inference, and an adaptive NI-AI reasoning engine evaluates cue packets using real-world distress signals, discomfort behaviors, and environmental triggers. The system generates a single, contradiction-free output label (e.g., “Safe” or “Needs Attention”) with accompanying confidence and emotion/distress scores. Critically, when low confidence, elevated distress, or multi-thread signal disagreement appears internally, the system automatically activates a Conflict Flag and Manual Review Loop, marking the event as pending review in auditable local storage. Caregivers or authorized reviewers can view flagged check-ins, apply human judgment, document what actually happened in reviewer notes, and resolve the alert without exposing internal logs to the child interface. This architecture ensures that children receive clarity, while caregivers receive accountability, enabling safer, scalable neuro-inclusive deployments across families, therapy centers, and community supervision networks. By embedding a human-in-the-loop decision spine in a traditionally underserved high-stakes use case, EchoAuth™ aims to set a new standard for ethical, regulated, parent-governed, non-AGI compliance, and auditable child safety systems.
