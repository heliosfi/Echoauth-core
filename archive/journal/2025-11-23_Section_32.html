⭐ 32.1 Overview


The EchoAuth Signal Language (ESL) is a structured, multi-layer communication system that:


✔ translates autistic nonverbal cues into clear, standardized meaning

✔ expresses EchoAuth system decisions using universal symbols

✔ synchronizes communication across home, school, clinic, and community

✔ provides a common language between parent, teacher, doctor, and device

✔ bridges the gap between nonverbal behavior and human understanding

✔ displays consistent output across ALL EchoAuth equipment

✔ works without text, speech, or cognitive load

✔ preserves dignity, reduces confusion, and prevents miscommunication


ESL is the first ASD communication meta-language.


────────────────────────────────


⭐ 32.2 ESL Architecture


ESL consists of five communication layers:


Layer A — Behavioral Signals


Decodes child movement, posture, actions.


Layer B — Sensory Signals


Expresses sensory overload, discomfort, or needs.


Layer C — Emotional Signals


Represents emotional states across devices.


Layer D — Safety & Medical Signals


Priority communication for danger or health.


Layer E — System Output Signals


EchoAuth-to-human communication (visual, haptic, audio, color codes).


Each layer has:

• Codes

• Patterns

• Intensities

• Priorities

• Meanings

⭐ 32.8 ESL System Output Signals (ESL-O)


How EchoAuth communicates to humans.


A. Color Codes


These appear on all devices (wearable LED, toy glow, hub indicators):

• Blue — Calm

• Green — Focus / Stable

• Yellow — Mild stress

• Orange — High stress / sensory overload

• Red — Emergency / danger

• Purple — Medical concern

• White — System neutral

• Teal — Learning / CPIE update


B. Haptic Patterns (Wearable & Toy)

• Short pulse → Attention

• Double pulse → Minor discomfort

• Triple pulse → Sensory overload

• Long vibration → Emotional distress

• Rapid pulses → Safety risk

• Low rumble → Calming support

• Strong stop pulse → Elopement danger


C. Audio Tones


Not words, just safe rhythmic signals:

• soft hum → calming

• ocean pulse → sensory grounding

• chime → transition alert

• low bass → danger approaching

• heartbeat → emotional regulation
