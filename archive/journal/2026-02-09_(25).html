Delegate Training Sheet


Working Safely Within the EchoAuth System


For School & Clinical Delegates (Execution-Only Roles)


⸻


Purpose of This Sheet


This document explains how delegates (school staff, therapists, aides, clinicians) interact with the EchoAuth system without triggering safety halts and without exceeding their role.


EchoAuth exists to protect the child by ensuring that only caregivers authorize actions.

Delegates support execution and observation — not decision-making.


⸻


1. Your Role in the System (Read Carefully)


As a Delegate, you are authorized to:

• Execute caregiver-approved tasks

• Observe child responses and system behavior

• Document outcomes, concerns, or recommendations

• Propose changes for caregiver review


You are not authorized to:

• Approve actions

• Override caregiver decisions

• Change schedules, permissions, or constraints

• Resolve conflicts between caregivers

• Force the system to proceed during a halt


The system enforces this automatically.


⸻


2. What Triggers a System Halt (Agency Collapse)


The system will intentionally lock (halt execution) if it detects:

• Conflicting instructions affecting the same task

• Commands issued outside your role scope

• Timing collisions between caregiver and delegate actions

• Unclear or expired authorization


This is not an error.

It is a safety lock to protect the child.


⸻


3. How to Avoid Triggering Halts


A. Do Not Issue Competing Instructions


Before executing or proposing an action, confirm that it:

• Matches the current caregiver-approved schedule

• Does not contradict an existing instruction


If unsure, pause and document — do not act.


⸻


B. Do Not “Correct” the System


If the system pauses, locks, or refuses:

• Do not retry the action repeatedly

• Do not escalate through workarounds

• Do not assume intent


Instead:

• Document what you observed

• Notify the caregiver through the proper channel


⸻


C. Keep Proposals Non-Executable


When suggesting changes (e.g., schedule adjustments, sensory settings):

• Submit them as recommendations

• Never frame them as commands

• Expect caregiver review before execution


⸻


4. What to Do If the System Locks (CEG = LOCKED)


If you see a halt message:

1. Stop issuing commands

2. Do not attempt resolution

3. Document the context (time, activity, child state)

4. Notify the caregiver


Only a Parent / Caregiver can resolve an Agency Collapse.


⸻


5. Key Rules to Remember (Non-Negotiable)

• The system does not “know best” — caregivers do

• Speed is never more important than safety

• Refusal is a valid and expected system state

• Your job is to support clarity, not bypass it


If the system stops, it is protecting the child.


⸻


6. Common Scenarios (Quick Reference)


Scenario: You want to extend screen time

Correct Action: Submit a recommendation

Incorrect Action: Attempt to modify the task directly


Scenario: A task pauses unexpectedly

Correct Action: Document + notify caregiver

Incorrect Action: Retry or override


Scenario: Two adults disagree

Correct Action: Step back

Incorrect Action: Choose a side or proceed anyway


⸻


Final Reminder


EchoAuth is designed for environments where children may not be able to self-advocate reliably.

Your restraint and role discipline are part of the safety system.


When in doubt: pause, document, escalate to caregiver.


⸻


One-Line Delegate Principle


If you didn’t authorize it, don’t force it. If the system pauses, let it.
