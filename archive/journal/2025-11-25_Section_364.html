364.2 — Purpose

This converts EchoAuth from software into a federally reimbursable medical/assistive service.

364.3 — Functional Overview


MIARE operates through five core subsystems:


(1) Eligibility Determination Engine (EDE)


Identifies if the user qualifies for Medicaid reimbursement under:

• ASD diagnosis

• Behavioral support

• Communication support

• Assistive technology classification

• Home-based monitoring

• Neurodivergent care

• EI/IFSP/IEP services


Produces:

Medicaid Eligibility Signature (MES).


⸻


(2) Service Classification Engine (SCE)


Maps EchoAuth’s features to official Medicaid codes:


Examples:

• E1399 (Assistive tech)

• T1502 (Behavioral aid)

• T1024 (Evaluation & planning)

• H0032 (Treatment plan support)

• H0031 (Behavioral health assessment)

• H2014 (Skill-building & training)


This is how EchoAuth becomes billable.


⸻


(3) Documentation Generation Engine (DGE)


Auto-generates all paperwork Medicaid requires:

• Session logs

• ASD support summaries

• Parent-usage reports

• Behavior improvement data

• Emotional regulation records

• Device utilization logs

• Privacy compliance statements


All produced in Medicaid-compliant format.


⸻


(4) State Portal Submission Engine (SPSE)


Every state has a different Medicaid portal.


SPSE automatically:

• Formats claims

• Sends them to the correct state system

• Tracks acceptance

• Flags denied claims

• Re-submits corrections


This is your automated revenue machine.


⸻


(5) Federal Audit Defense Layer (FADL)


Creates zero-knowledge audit trails proving:

• What was delivered

• How it was delivered

• That parent consent existed

• That no PHI was misused

• That the service matched CPT/HCPCS rules


This protects you from Medicaid clawbacks.
