📄 PAGE 32 — PARTICIPANT SAFEGUARDS & CRISIS PROTOCOL (IRB + Pilot Standard)


EchoAuth Safety Protocols for ASD Users in Research & Pilot Programs


⸻


SECTION A — PURPOSE OF SAFEGUARDS


EchoAuth is designed for autistic and non-verbal individuals, many of whom experience sensory sensitivities, communication stress, or behavioral escalations.

This page defines the required safety procedures for:

• meltdown risk

• sensory overload

• emotional escalation

• communication failure

• identity confusion

• user fatigue


These safeguards ensure minimal risk, in alignment with:

• IRB standards (minimal-risk human research)

• Clinical ASD best practices

• School district safety guidelines

• Federal disability protocols (IDEA, ADA, FERPA)

• EchoAuth’s internal NI-AI safety wrapper


⸻


SECTION B — SESSION SAFETY ENVIRONMENT


Each interaction with EchoAuth must occur in a setting that meets the following criteria:


1. Low-Stimulation Environment

• Soft lighting

• Minimal background noise

• No bright or flashing screens

• Predictable room arrangement


2. Presence of a Familiar Adult


At least one of the following must be present:

• Parent/guardian

• Clinician

• Teacher or paraprofessional


3. Immediate Break Access


The user must be able to:

• step away

• take a sensory break

• request termination of the session


Break areas must include:

• water

• quiet seating

• sensory supports (fidgets, weighted blanket, etc.)


⸻


SECTION C — MELTDOWN PREVENTION PROTOCOL


EchoAuth incorporates early-warning logic, but staff must follow this 5-step meltdown prevention process.


1. Identify Early Signs


Staff monitor for:

• pacing, increased movement

• tensing hands, fidgeting

• increased vocalizations

• avoidance behaviors

• sudden withdrawal

• rapid tapping or repetitive gestures


2. Pause the System


Staff must immediately:

• pause all prompts

• reduce stimuli

• stop audio or animations

• offer a break


3. Provide Predictable Choices


Offer one of three predictable options:


Option A: “Take a break now.”

Option B: “Continue for one more minute.”

Option C: “Stop completely.”


Autistic individuals rely heavily on predictable structure — these options stabilize emotional load.


4. Initiate Calm Reset Protocol


Staff provide:

• calm voice

• slow gestures

• no sudden changes

• reassurance:

“You are safe. We can pause anytime.”


5. Terminate Session if Needed


If escalation continues for >15 seconds, staff must safely end the session.


⸻


SECTION D — SENSORY OVERLOAD PROTOCOL


If a participant shows signs of sensory overwhelm:


1. Reduce Sensory Load Immediately

• Dim brightness

• Lower or mute audio

• Remove distracting visual elements


2. Activate EchoAuth’s Low-Sensory Mode


The system automatically switches to:

• monochrome palette

• simplified icons

• slower transitions

• longer response windows


3. Allow Unlimited Breaks


Participant may step away with no consequences.


⸻


SECTION E — COMMUNICATION FAILURE PROTOCOL


If a user is unable to express needs:


1. Default to Caregiver Interpretation


The caregiver can interpret gestures, movements, or known behaviors.


2. Switch to Non-Verbal Mode


EchoAuth shows:

• Yes/No tiles

• Emotion indicators

• Basic needs icons

• Safety check prompts


3. End the Session if Stress Persists


If frustration continues, terminate without penalty.


⸻


SECTION F — IDENTITY CONFUSION OR MIS-ASSOCIATION


EchoAuth NEVER traps a user in a wrong identity.


If the system is uncertain:


1. It halts automatically (fail-closed).


2. It requests caregiver confirmation.


3. It resets the session without saving data.


This protects:

• user autonomy

• system trust

• safety integrity


⸻


SECTION G — EMERGENCY PROTOCOL


This protocol is rarely needed and is designed for severe distress only.


Staff must terminate the session immediately and follow institutional procedure if:

• participant becomes physically unsafe

• severe meltdown occurs

• self-injury risk is present

• user becomes non-responsive to prompts or caregiver cues


EchoAuth does not continue operating in emergency states.


⸻


SECTION H — POST-SESSION SAFETY CHECK


After each session, staff complete a 1-minute checklist:

• Did the participant show signs of fatigue?

• Did they require breaks?

• Did sensory settings need adjustment?

• Were any stress indicators observed?

• Should future sessions be shorter?


This allows the system to be adapted individually for each user.
