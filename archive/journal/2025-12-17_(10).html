Prototype Use-of-Funds (Internal Draft)


Business: EchoAuth

Stage: Pre-revenue / Prototype & Pilot Preparation

Founder: Nicholas B. Carty

Purpose: Complete, test, and validate a functional technology prototype prior to revenue generation.


⸻


1. What Is Being Built


EchoAuth is a coherence-governed human–AI support system designed for caregiver-assisted access and communication workflows, including non-verbal and neurodivergent contexts. The system prioritizes safety, auditability, and human-in-the-loop governance.


The current focus is prototype completion and controlled testing, not commercialization or scale. No funds will be used for real estate, acquisitions, or unrelated operating expenses.


⸻


2. Use of Funds (Primary Allocation)


Loan funds will be used strictly for prototype readiness and validation, including:

• Engineering & Development

• Finalizing core system components

• Integration testing and error handling

• Testing & Validation

• Controlled pilot testing

• Reliability and safety verification

• Infrastructure & Tools

• Secure development environment

• Necessary software, hardware, and testing tools

• Compliance & Preparation

• Documentation

• Security and audit readiness

• Pre-deployment checks


Funds are not intended for salaries beyond limited technical support, marketing, or revenue expansion activities at this stage.


⸻


3. Milestones Enabled by Funding


Funding enables the following defined milestones:

1. Functional prototype completion

2. Internal testing and iteration

3. Controlled pilot readiness

4. Documentation and validation outputs

5. Go / No-Go decision for next phase (pilot expansion or partnerships)


Each milestone reduces technical risk and increases readiness for partnerships, grants, or revenue-based programs.


⸻


4. Timeline


Estimated timeline: 6–12 months

• Months 1–3: Prototype completion & integration

• Months 4–6: Testing, refinement, validation

• Months 7–12: Pilot preparation, documentation, next-phase planning


This timeline aligns with longer-term amortization structures and avoids short-term cash-flow pressure.


⸻


5. Repayment Logic (Path, Not Revenue Claims)


At this stage, repayment is not dependent on existing revenue, but on progression through defined milestones that unlock next-stage opportunities, including:

• Pilot partnerships

• Non-dilutive funding (grants, research programs)

• Institutional or mission-aligned follow-on capital

• Structured revenue programs once pilots validate use cases


The funding requested supports disciplined execution toward these outcomes, not speculative expansion.


⸻


6. Risk Management & Discipline

• No real estate exposure

• No daily or weekly repayment structures

• No high-pressure short-term obligations

• Clear scope control and milestone-based execution


The objective is measured progress, not aggressive scaling.
