⭐ SECTION 317 — CROWD DENSITY & FLOW RISK ENGINE (CDFRE)


(NI-AI Patent Module — Real-Time Environmental Safety Layer)

────────────────────────────────────────


This is the environmental counterpart to Section 316 (Withdrawal Safety).

Section 317 analyzes crowd behavior, movement dynamics, and human flow pressure to predict safety risks before a child or neurodivergent user becomes overwhelmed.


Autistic children often struggle in:

• airports

• subway platforms

• parades

• malls

• hallways during school transitions

• amusement parks

• busy lines

• waiting areas


This engine forecasts danger before the caregiver even notices.


────────────────────────────────────────


317.1 — Technical Field


The invention relates to AI-based environmental risk assessment systems designed to:

• measure crowd density patterns,

• detect hazardous human flow dynamics,

• predict proximity pressure,

• identify potential escape blocks,

• support neurodivergent users during mobility events.


It sits between ASW-SEP (316) and Environmental Prediction Module (315).


────────────────────────────────────────


317.2 — Purpose


CDFRE protects autistic children and sensitive individuals from:

• accidental physical pressure

• being boxed-in

• sudden crowd surges

• unpredictable approaching strangers

• chaotic movement patterns

• spatial overwhelm

• risk of bolting or panic reaction

• sensory-induced freeze states


It generates warnings and caregiver guidance before the environment becomes dangerous.


────────────────────────────────────────


317.3 — Functional Definition


CDFRE analyzes:

1. Static Density

• How many people are near the user?

2. Dynamic Flow

• How fast is the crowd moving and in what direction?

3. Compression Risk

• Is the space tightening? Are escape paths shrinking?

4. Proximity Pressure

• Are strangers drifting too close too quickly?

5. Flow Reversal Events

• Are people suddenly turning around, causing chaos?

6. Environmental Noise Delta

• Are sounds rising faster than expected?


It outputs:

• Risk Class (1–5)

• Predictive Surge Warning

• Safe Direction Arrow (text-based)

• Stop/Hold Guidance

• Caregiver Alert Sequence


────────────────────────────────────────

────────────────────────────────────────


317.5 — Outputs

• Crowd Density Score

• Flow Risk Level

• Proximity Pressure Indicator

• Spatial Stability Rating

• Safe-Path Recommendation

• “Move Now / Hold” Command

• Calming Space Suggestion

• Caregiver Micro-Decision Guide


Example:


“Hold position. Crowd surge detected from behind. Move two steps right toward the open space.”


────────────────────────────────────────


317.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Density Field Analyzer (DFA)


Breaks the environment into invisible grid “blocks” and estimates:

• number of bodies

• density gradient

• movement direction


(b) Flow Velocity Mapper (FVM)


Estimates:

• crowd walking speed

• direction

• irregular jitters (sign of panic)

• turbulence patterns


(c) Compression Prediction Engine (CPE)


Predicts:

• narrowing walkways

• bottlenecks

• accidental shoulder-to-shoulder compression


(d) Escape Path Estimator (EPE)


Identifies human-safe movements:

• micro-step retreat

• lateral sidestep

• back-to-wall safety

• caregiver shield positioning


(e) Behavioral Overwhelm Layer (BOL)


Uses emotional data from ESE to decide:

• if the user is at risk of freezing,

• if fast withdrawal is required,

• if slow movement is safer.


(f) Caregiver Signal Engine (CSE)


Generates:

• clear 1–2 step instructions

• safety scripts

• caution warnings
