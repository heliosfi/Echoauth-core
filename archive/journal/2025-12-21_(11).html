PART 2 — PROFESSIONAL TALK TRACK (CANONICAL)


You can deliver this in 60–90 seconds.

You can stop at any sentence if needed.


⸻


Opening — What It Is


“EchoAuth is a child-first coordination system for autistic and non-verbal children.

It’s not a medical app and it doesn’t diagnose or make decisions.”


⸻


Core Rule — Authority


“The core rule is simple: parents stay in control.

Nothing becomes active for a child unless a parent approves it.”


⸻


Child Experience


“The child doesn’t interact with the system like an adult.

They have a calm, non-verbal interface with sensory and music programs designed for regulation — no tests, no scores, no pressure.”


⸻


Adult Roles (One Sentence Each)


“Parents decide and control access.

Caregivers carry out approved tasks.

Schools observe and support.

Clinics document and advise.”


⸻


Qualified Caregiver Support


“Parents can also bring in qualified ASD-trained caregivers through the system, so support doesn’t fall only on family members.

Caregivers operate within strict, parent-defined boundaries.”


⸻


Cameras & Wearables (Context Only)


“If parents choose, EchoAuth can use wearables or cameras to provide safety and routine context.

These are optional, parent-controlled, and non-clinical.

They provide context — they do not diagnose or decide.”


⸻


System Behavior (Safety)


“Everything is logged, reminders are factual, and the system pauses instead of guessing when something is uncertain.”


⸻


Close — Why It Exists


“EchoAuth lets more hands help autistic children without increasing risk, by organizing people, routines, and context around the family.”


⸻


One-Line Fallback (Memorize This)


“It’s coordination infrastructure that existing systems don’t provide.”
