1. Technical Problem (NSF Framing)


AI systems intended for human-facing environments—especially those involving children, neurodivergent individuals, or caregiver-mediated contexts—often fail NSF’s core requirements for predictability, explainability, and risk containment.


Current approaches:

• Escalate behavior under uncertainty

• Infer intent or emotional state without ground truth

• Allow autonomous action without enforceable governance

• Treat silence or non-participation as system failure


These characteristics introduce unacceptable technical and safety risk, limiting applicability in education, healthcare, and public-sector research pilots.


⸻


2. Technical Innovation


Helios F.I LLC has developed NI-AI, integrated with EchoAuth and governed by MCG (Motion / Context Governance)—a fail-closed, governance-first AI architecture.


Core Innovation:


A deterministic governance spine that:

• Separates observation, authorization, and execution

• Defaults to non-action under uncertainty

• Enforces caregiver or institutional authority

• Produces explainable, auditable outcomes


This is not an autonomous agent.

It is decision-support and explanation infrastructure.


⸻


3. System Architecture (Concise)

• NI-AI: Read-only explanation and decision-support layer

• MCG: Final authority for allow / pause / deny (non-overridable)

• EchoAuth: Role-based authentication; default = deny


Key properties:

• Rule-based, state-driven logic

• No self-modifying behavior

• No behavioral prediction claims

• Silence and stillness treated as valid states


⸻


4. Research Objectives (Phase I Appropriate)


Objective 1:

Validate governance-first AI behavior in non-verbal interaction scenarios.


Objective 2:

Demonstrate stability, restraint, and explainability across long sessions.


Objective 3:

Measure system behavior when uncertainty is introduced (expected outcome: pause, not escalation).


These objectives are researchable, measurable, and NSF-appropriate.
