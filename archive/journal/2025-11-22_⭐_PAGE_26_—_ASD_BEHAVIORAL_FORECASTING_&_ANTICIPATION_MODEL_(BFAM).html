──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 26.1 — THE 3 FOUNDATIONS OF BEHAVIORAL FORECASTING


EchoAuth forecasts behavior using three types of signals:


⭐ 1. Micro-Behavioral Indicators (MBI)


These tiny changes often show up before full distress:

• shorter messages

• delayed responses

• repeated words

• stillness → freeze

• head tilt

• looking away

• fast breathing

• sudden quiet

• mis-typing

• tapping or fidgeting


Micro-signals = early warnings.


⸻


⭐ 2. Physiological Rhythm Shifts (PRS)


If enabled (wearables, camera, or microphone):

• breath rhythm

• movement rhythm

• micro-expressions

• fidget rhythm

• vocal tone shifts


ASD nervous systems show rhythm changes before distress.


⸻


⭐ 3. Cognitive Pacing Drift (CPD)


When the brain slows down or struggles:

• “I don’t know.”

• hesitation

• losing track

• difficulty choosing

• repeating questions

• jumping topics


EchoAuth detects CPD instantly.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 26.2 — THE BFAM TRI-LAYER ENGINE


EchoAuth predicts behavior using three fused layers:


⭐ Layer 1 — Sensory Prediction


Predicts whether:

• light

• noise

• movement

• touch

• textures

• temperature


will increase sensory load.


⸻


⭐ Layer 2 — Emotional Prediction


Forecasts emotional shifts:

• rising frustration

• anxiety spikes

• shutdown risk

• freeze behavior

• confusion


⸻


⭐ Layer 3 — Cognitive Prediction


Forecasts thinking capacity:

• access dropping

• cognitive fatigue

• difficulty maintaining steps

• blocked decisions


All three layers unite to create one predictive output.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 26.3 — THE 4-STEP BFAM ALGORITHM


EchoAuth uses a simple but powerful 4-step forecasting sequence:


⭐ Step 1 — Detect Drift


EchoAuth tracks changes across:

• sensory load

• emotional rhythm

• word choice

• response delay

• physical cues


If drift exceeds threshold → prediction triggered.


⸻


⭐ Step 2 — Classify Risk


Levels:


Risk Level Meaning

1 – Stable No action needed

2 – Mild Drift Prep early support

3 – Escalation Likely Switch to grounding + slower pace

4 – High Risk Safe Mode required

5 – Immediate Danger Activate de-escalation protocol



⸻


⭐ Step 3 — Preemptive Adjustment


EchoAuth changes:

• tone

• pacing

• step size

• sensory demands

• visual load

• cognitive complexity

• default communication mode


BEFORE behavior shifts.


⸻


⭐ Step 4 — Caregiver Guidance


EchoAuth alerts caregiver:

• “Reduce noise.”

• “Give space.”

• “Lower voice.”

• “Slow transitions.”

• “Sit beside them.”


This is how EchoAuth prevents escalation in real environments.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 26.4 — THE 5 PREDICTIVE PATHWAYS (BFAM MAP)


EchoAuth predicts:


⭐ 1. Sensory Escalation


Before the child covers ears, cries, or escapes.


⭐ 2. Emotional Surges


Before distress or shutdown.


⭐ 3. Cognitive Blocks


Before confusion causes anxiety.


⭐ 4. Behavioral Expressions


Before stimming spreads or escalates.


⭐ 5. Social Overload


Before large groups or social pressure overwhelm them.


Forecasting prevents meltdowns.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 26.5 — EARLY WARNING SIGNALS (EWS)


EchoAuth watches for specific patterns:


✔ words getting shorter

✔ messages becoming delayed

✔ sudden silence

✔ repetitive questions

✔ “I don’t want to”

✔ “stop” / “wait”

✔ sudden emotional drop

✔ withdrawal

✔ freeze

✔ scanning surroundings

✔ hand pressure

✔ breath tightening


When 3 or more appear → risk state rises.


──────────────────────────────────


⭐ 26.6 — BFAM RESPONSE LIBRARY


EchoAuth adapts based on predicted risk:


⭐ If Risk Level = 2


→ slow pacing

→ add grounding phrases

→ reduce sensory input


⭐ If Risk Level = 3


→ activate Safe Mode

→ remove choices

→ use minimal words


⭐ If Risk Level = 4


→ start De-escalation Protocol (Page 18)


⭐ If Risk Level = 5


→ Initiate caregiver alert

→ Environmental exit guidance

→ Immediate safety measures
