SECTION 407 — DEVICE ROLE HIERARCHY ENGINE (DRHE)


(NI-AI Patent Module — Safety-Class Priority)


Why this module is essential


NI-AI + EchoAuth operate across homes, schools, clinics, community centers, and public spaces.

Different devices have different authority levels — and if the hierarchy is missing, any one device could mistakenly:

• override a parent

• bypass a safety lock

• trigger an intervention during class

• misinterpret a crisis

• or drift into incompatible states


DRHE eliminates all of that by defining the legally correct, safety-aligned, parent-first chain of authority that no device may violate.


This module becomes critical for your federal compliance and your patent protection.


────────────────────────────────────────


407.1 — Technical Field


The DRHE relates to systems for defining and enforcing role-based safety authority among multiple devices interacting with an AI-driven support system for autistic and neurodivergent children.


────────────────────────────────────────


407.2 — Purpose


To create a strict, auditable safety hierarchy that determines:

• who can issue commands

• who can receive alerts

• who can trigger safety escalations

• who can override whom

• which device becomes the “primary” in crisis mode


This prevents role conflicts across guardians, teachers, clinicians, wearables, and child-facing devices.


────────────────────────────────────────


407.3 — Functional Definition


The DRHE operates through five major functions:


(a) Role Assignment Core (RAC)


Defines each device’s role category:

1. Primary Guardian Device (Tier 1) — Absolute Authority

2. Secondary Guardian Device (Tier 2)

3. Clinical / Therapist Device (Tier 3)

4. School / Teacher Device (Tier 4)

5. Child Device (Tier 5)

6. Wearables / Passive Nodes (Tier 6)

7. Autonomous On-Device Safety Module (Tier 7 — lowest)**


(b) Authority Ladder Compiler (ALC)


Compiles the authority chain into a strict, immutable ladder.

No device can “promote itself.”


(c) Cross-Device Permission Resolver (CDPR)


Resolves conflicts by referencing the ladder:

• If a teacher device tries to override a parent → BLOCK

• If a clinician tries to escalate without guardian approval → REQUEST CONSENT

• If a wearable detects crisis → REPORT to Tier 1 but NO self-action


(d) Safety Override Filter (SOF)


Determines when and how an emergency override is allowed.


Tier 1 (parent/guardian) always has:

• final decision

• crisis override

• lock/unlock authority

• data visibility control


(e) Tamper-Proof Audit Ledger (TPAL)


Stores:

• all role assignments

• role changes

• override attempts

• blocked actions

• time-stamped logs


This becomes a legally defensible safety record.

407.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Immutable Hierarchy Map (IHM)


Hard-coded order of authority validated by the NI-AI Doctrine.


(b) Domain-Specific Role Channels (DSRC)


Home, school, and clinic each have specialty permissions without affecting global authority.


(c) Environment Role Switch Guard (ERSG)


Prevents accidental role elevation when transitioning between environments.


(d) Continuous Integrity Checker (CIC)


Prevents:

• spoofed devices

• unauthorized access

• fake guardian signals

• malicious role changes

• compromised networks
