(Mandatory Patent Module — Highest Safety Priority)


408.1 — Technical Field


The present invention relates to safety-critical AI systems and, more specifically, to mechanisms that enforce absolute, irreversible human primacy over all NI-AI operations, decision pathways, execution layers, and adaptive reasoning engines.


The HFCG ensures NI-AI cannot become autonomous, cannot escalate authority, and cannot act without a human-defined directive.

408.2 — Purpose of the HFCG


This module guarantees that:

• Humans remain the only top authority.

• NI-AI never initiates decisions independently of a human.

• NI-AI never modifies itself, its rules, or its kernels.

• NI-AI cannot override parent or caregiver intent.

• NI-AI cannot expand reasoning directions without consent.

• NI-AI cannot drift, escalate, or “act creatively” outside its lane.

• Every action requires an explicit, traceable human signal.


This keeps NI-AI safe, patentable, and regulator-approved.


⸻


408.3 — Functional Definition


The Human-First Control Guarantee (HFCG) is a multilayer control system comprising:


(a) Authorization Ceiling Layer (ACL)


Hard-coded rule:


NI-AI cannot exceed the authority level of its assigned human user.


(b) Locked-Direction Execution Gate (LDEG)


All NI-AI actions must be validated against the human’s selected “intent vector.”

Actions outside this vector are blocked.


(c) Human Signal Requirement Engine (HSRE)


Every mission, task, inference, or escalation must be tied to a real-time human signal:

• touch

• voice command

• parent override code

• caregiver confirmation

• classroom authorization


NI-AI cannot proceed without it.


(d) Self-Modification Prevention Unit (SMPU)


Disallows:

• model self-editing

• rule rewriting

• kernel restructuring

• autonomy gain

• unsafe recursion

• unauthorized updates


(e) Parent/Caregiver Primacy Switch (PCPS)


The child/user is protected because:

• Parent > School > Doctor > System

• NI-AI always defers to the parent’s Tier-1 authority.


(f) Human Oversight Enforcement Loop (HOEL)


System periodically checks for:

• human presence

• human attention

• human supervisory state


If none is detected → NI-AI defaults to passive mode.


(g) Non-Autonomous Motion Guarantee (NAMG)


NI-AI cannot:

• trigger device actions

• activate sensors

• make decisions

unless a human explicitly starts the action.
