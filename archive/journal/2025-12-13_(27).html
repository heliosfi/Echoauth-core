1-PAGE GRANT ABSTRACT


EchoAuth × NI-AI: Safety-First Infrastructure for Non-Verbal Communication


Project Summary


Non-verbal individuals—particularly autistic children and others with communication differences—are routinely placed under systems that require speech, interpretation, or behavioral inference to proceed safely. These requirements introduce pressure, misinterpretation, and risk. Existing digital health and assistive technologies often rely on prediction, optimization, or engagement metrics that are poorly suited to sensitive, non-verbal contexts.


This project proposes the development and validation of a safety-first control infrastructure designed explicitly to support non-verbal interaction by removing the need for speech, explanation, or behavioral interpretation. The system enforces restraint through deterministic state discipline, explicit authorization boundaries, and human oversight, ensuring that non-action, silence, stillness, and withdrawal are treated as valid and successful outcomes.


⸻


Technical Approach


The system integrates three tightly bounded components:

• MCG (Motion / Context Governance):

A deterministic state framework that manages physical presence, time, and transitions without interpreting intent or emotion.

• EchoAuth:

An authorization and boundary-enforcement layer that defaults to deny execution unless explicit conditions and human oversight are met.

• NI-AI (Natural-Intellect AI):

A read-only explanation layer that provides transparency and auditability without prediction, behavioral scoring, or autonomous control.


Optional wearables may be used during pilots solely for passive state reflection and orientation cues. Wearables have no authority, no triggers, and no inference pathways, and the system operates safely without them.


⸻


Innovation


The innovation is architectural rather than predictive. Unlike traditional AI-driven systems that attempt to infer needs or optimize behavior, this system makes restraint enforceable. It ensures that action cannot occur without clear authorization and that not acting is often the correct and safe outcome.


This approach directly addresses long-standing risks in non-verbal contexts: misinterpretation, forced compliance, and escalation driven by uncertainty.


⸻


Pilot & Validation Plan


Funding will support a limited pilot focused on validating:

• Correct enforcement of default-deny execution

• Safe handling of non-verbal interaction (silence, stillness, non-participation)

• Complete, auditable logs of system decisions and refusals

• Finite caregiver onboarding and boundary understanding


No predictive models, behavioral profiling, or outcome optimization will be developed or tested.


⸻


Expected Impact


The project establishes a new standard for safety-first infrastructure in non-verbal environments. By shifting from prediction to enforceable restraint, the system reduces risk, improves trust, and provides institutions, families, and regulators with transparent assurance that safety does not depend on interpretation or pressure.


⸻


Funding Alignment Statement (Use Verbatim)


Funds are used to validate deterministic safety controls, authorization enforcement, auditability, and non-verbal interaction handling. No funds support predictive modeling, behavioral inference, or usage-based optimization.


⸻


One-Line Close


This project proves that systems can listen without requiring speech—and protect by knowing when not to act.
