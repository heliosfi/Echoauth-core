⭐ 64.2 — THE SAFETY SPECTRUM MODEL (SSM-4)


EchoAuth monitors FOUR safety categories:


⭐ 1 — Physical Safety


traffic • heights • sharp items • hot surfaces • water


⭐ 2 — Social Safety


strangers • manipulation • bullying • peer pressure


⭐ 3 — Sensory Safety


overload • panic • sudden noises • visual chaos


⭐ 4 — Emotional Safety


shame • overwhelm • fear • shutdown triggers


This covers the entire holistic ASD safety landscape.

⭐ 64.3 — LOCATION AWARENESS ENGINE (LAE)


EchoAuth helps caregivers by reminding:


✔ “Hold hands here.”

✔ “Busy area — stay close.”

✔ “Noise rising ahead.”

✔ “Exit is to your left.”

✔ “This area is overwhelming — reduce time.”


It builds a spatial safety map.


────────────────────────


⭐ 64.4 — WANDERING PREVENTION LOOP (WPL)


ASD wandering is common and dangerous.


EchoAuth detects wandering signals:

• stepping away

• scanning the room

• walking toward exits

• slowing down near doors

• distraction spikes

• fixation on objects in the distance


Then advises:


✔ “Stay close.”

✔ “Let’s walk together.”

✔ “Safe zone is here.”


────────────────────────


⭐ 64.5 — STRANGER INTERACTION SAFETY (SIS)


EchoAuth identifies risky social cues:

• overly friendly strangers

• confusing questions

• strangers touching objects

• someone offering gifts

• someone asking personal info

• manipulative tone


EchoAuth provides:


✔ safe scripts

✔ exit cues

✔ guidance prompts


Example:


“You can stay with your caregiver.”

“Just wave, you don’t need to talk.”

“Let’s step away.”


────────────────────────


⭐ 64.6 — BULLYING & MICRO-HARM DETECTION (BMHD)


Children often miss bullying cues.


EchoAuth detects:


✔ teasing

✔ mocking

✔ staring

✔ exclusion

✔ sarcastic tones

✔ physical intimidation

✔ imitation of movements or speech


Then:


✔ alerts the caregiver

✔ supports the child

✔ offers immediate scripts

✔ removes the child from danger

⭐ 64.7 — SENSORY-TRIGGER SAFETY INTERVENTION (STSI)


When sensory overload threatens safety:

• loud alarms

• bright lights

• crowded spaces

• echo chambers

• chaotic noise


EchoAuth responds instantly:


✔ grounding cue

✔ exit direction

✔ “cover ears” prompt

✔ “look here” focus point

✔ deep pressure guidance


────────────────────────


⭐ 64.8 — RUNNING & FREEZE RESPONSE ENGINE (RFRE)


Autistic children respond to danger uniquely:


Running Response


EchoAuth guides:

✔ “Slow down.”

✔ “Stop here.”

✔ “Hold hands.”


Freeze Response


EchoAuth guides the caregiver to:

✔ lower demands

✔ kneel down

✔ avoid pulling

✔ reduce sensory load

✔ use grounding touch (if allowed)


This respects neurological safety patterns.
