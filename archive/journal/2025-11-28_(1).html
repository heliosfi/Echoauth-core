EchoAuth™ – Launcher-Referenced Specification (Attorney Ready)


EchoAuth™ provides a caregiver-governed pediatric authentication and safety system for non-verbal autistic children, implemented as an identity-anchored local runtime with auditable inference controls. The included reference implementation, run_local_demo.py, demonstrates the system’s operational sequence in a terminal-only, single-output environment. The launcher script initializes the local database, creates caregiver and child profiles if absent, encodes caregiver-submitted behavioral and sensory cues into immutable identity tokens prior to NI-AI reasoning, and invokes the NI-AI evaluator kernel evaluate_cues(). The evaluator executes internal competing reasoning paths, computes an emotion/stress score, danger behavior probability, conflict score, and confidence estimate, resolves conflicts internally, and releases only one unambiguous caregiver-facing result ("Safe" or "Needs Attention"), ensuring pediatric cognitive clarity and caregiver accountability.


During each check-in simulation, the launcher persists clinical evidence across dual audit touchpoints: (i) structured database records (CheckIn and NIAILog tables via SQLAlchemy/SQLAlchemy-compatible ORM), and (ii) a private, time-stamped JSON audit file (audit_logs.json) that captures scoring payloads, winning inference paths, conflict routing, and human-review flags. The child-facing layer is blocked from receiving multi-candidate outputs or conflicting states; only caregivers and authorized reviewers access flagged outputs and audit records. This architecture establishes the enforceable invariants central to the invention: identity tokenization before inference, internal conflict suppression, measured (not assumed) danger sensing, human-in-the-loop review routing when distress or confidence thresholds are exceeded, and one-output enforcement at runtime.


The launcher serves as the system’s pilot proof-of-use artifact, validating that the invention operates consistently on localized infrastructure without emotional or factual drift in caregiver results and without exposing internal reasoning complexity to pediatric interfaces. All data paths support later export into official patent appendix submissions and grant compliance packages, preserving traceability, audit integrity, and caregiver origin authority while enabling future deployment to therapy centers and caregiver environments at controlled scale.
