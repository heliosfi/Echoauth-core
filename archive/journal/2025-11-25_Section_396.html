SECTION 396 — PUBLIC-SPACE SOCIAL DENSITY FORECAST ENGINE (PSDFE)


(NI-AI Patent Module — Real-Time Crowd Prediction for ASD Safety)

────────────────────────────────────────


396.1 — Technical Field


PSDFE concerns systems and methods for predicting crowd density, movement patterns, and social pressure zones in public environments to reduce sensory overload for neurodivergent individuals.


This includes environments such as:

• parks

• malls

• public transportation hubs

• grocery stores

• school events

• sidewalks

• playgrounds

• indoor venues


This is not a tracking system — it’s environmental forecasting.


────────────────────────────────────────


396.2 — Purpose


ASD children often struggle not because of the moment — but because the crowd collapses around them without warning.


This module:

• Predicts crowd formation before it happens

• Warns the parent or teacher

• Reroutes to safer space (ties into Section 395 MZSRE)

• Softens sensory exposure

• Reduces overwhelm and panic incidents


It creates a sensory safety shield in any public place.


────────────────────────────────────────


396.3 — Functional Definition


PSDFE performs four primary operations:


(a) Social Density Detection (SDD)


Estimates the number of people in the environment based on:

• movement clusters

• sound signatures

• body outline density

• directionality and velocity

• visual motion patterns


(This uses non-identifiable signals — silhouettes only.)


(b) Density Forecast Modeling (DFM)


Predicts where crowds will move in the next:

• 5 seconds

• 15 seconds

• 30 seconds

• 60–90 seconds


Analogous to “weather radar” but for human movement.


(c) Safety Path Overlay (SPO)


Generates safe alternative paths like:

• lower congestion corridor

• standing buffer zone

• quiet corner

• transitional safe pocket


(d) Crowd-Pressure Gradient Engine (CPGE)


Creates a real-time “pressure map” showing:

• where people are entering

• where flow increases

• where loudness spikes

• where children tend to cluster

• where unpredictable motion is rising


────────────────────────────────────────


396.4 — Inputs

• Device microphone

• Device camera silhouettes (ZWSM)

• Motion vectors

• Environmental acoustics

• Public schedules (train, bus, mall events)

• Historical crowd data (optional)


────────────────────────────────────────


396.5 — Outputs

• Crowd Density Score (CDS)

• Pressure Gradient Heatmap (PGH)

• Safe Path Suggestion (SPS)

• Anticipation Alert (AA)

• 90-Second Forecast Card (90SFC)


────────────────────────────────────────


396.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Silhouette Count Engine (SCE)


Counts bodies without identifying them.


(b) Motion-Field Analyzer (MFA)


Examines movement direction and velocity.


(c) Acoustic Burst Detector (ABD)


Detects crowd tension spikes before they escalate.


(d) Crowd-Flow Prediction Model (CFPM)


Uses multi-signal prediction to forecast density changes.


(e) Safe-Zone Recommender (SZR)


Evaluates safe micro-spaces nearby.


(f) Parent/Teacher Alert Tier (PTAT)


Adjusts sensitivity based on the child’s profile.


────────────────────────────────────────
