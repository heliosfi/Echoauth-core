governance/principles.md

EchoAuth Governance Principles

Principle 1: Authority Before Execution

No action may be executed without authorization from the designated authority layer.

Principle 2: Human Authority Preservation

Human authority remains the final source of approval, denial, escalation, and accountability.

Principle 3: Refusal Is Compliant

A refusal to execute is a valid and compliant system state when authorization requirements are not satisfied.

Principle 4: Separation of Powers

Meaning, judgment, authority, and execution shall remain independent functions.

NI-AI interprets.

Governance evaluates.

EchoAuth authorizes.

Execution performs.

Principle 5: Auditability

Every authorization decision must be traceable through logs and decision records.

Principle 6: Escalation Integrity

When confidence, authority, or context is insufficient, the system escalates rather than assumes.

Principle 7: Deterministic Authorization

The same inputs, authorities, and conditions must produce the same authorization outcome.

Principle 8: Accountability Preservation

Multi-party coordination shall never remove responsibility from authorized actors.

Core Statement

Coordination proposes.

Governance permits.

Execution proceeds after authorization.

architecture/system-overview.md

EchoAuth System Overview

Purpose

EchoAuth is a deterministic authorization framework designed to ensure governance always precedes execution.

Architectural Layers

Layer 1: Meaning

Component: NI-AI

Responsibilities:

Interpret information

Analyze context

Generate proposals

Produce recommendations

NI-AI never executes actions.

Layer 2: Judgment

Component: MCG (Multi-Coherence Governance)

Responsibilities:

Evaluate proposals

Assess coherence

Validate safety constraints

Determine governance outcomes

MCG never executes actions.

Layer 3: Authority

Component: EchoAuth

Responsibilities:

Verify authority ownership

Validate permissions

Apply authorization rules

Produce authorization decisions

EchoAuth controls execution access.

Layer 4: Execution

Component: CEG (Controlled Execution Gateway)

Responsibilities:

Execute approved actions

Enforce governance decisions

Record execution outcomes

Execution cannot bypass authorization.

Operational Flow

Information enters NI-AI.

NI-AI generates interpretation.

MCG evaluates interpretation.

EchoAuth validates authority.

Authorization decision is produced.

CEG executes approved actions.

Audit records are generated.

Design Objective

Maintain clear separation between interpretation, governance, authorization, and execution.
