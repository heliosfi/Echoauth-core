⭐ PAGE 17 — PHASE II SCALING & NATIONAL ROLLOUT PLAN


(Commercialization • Clinical Expansion • District Rollout • Research Validation)


EchoAuth’s long-term impact depends on structured, evidence-backed expansion beyond the prototype stage. Phase II enables national deployment across schools, ASD clinics, and community health settings.


The following roadmap outlines how EchoAuth transitions from prototype → validated assistive tool → regional pilot network → national adoption.


⸻


17.1 Phase II Objectives (12–24 Months)


Phase II funding will support:

1. Large-Scale Multi-Site Trials

• 3–7 school districts

• 5–12 ASD clinics or early-intervention centers

• 150–500 autistic users across diverse profiles

• Full clinical metrics (accuracy, stress reduction, usability)

2. Full NI-AI Engine Hardening

• Latency reduction

• Expanded intention templates

• Additional non-verbal mapping modes

• Adaptive learning loops under controlled clinical review

3. Institutional Integration Layer

• FERPA/HIPAA-compliant admin dashboards

• IEP workflow integration

• Clinician data visualization for progress tracking

• Role-based permissions for teachers, aides, guardians

4. Hardware + Device Alignment

• Low-cost tablet kits for school districts

• Rugged in-clinic devices

• Offline-first configurations for low-connectivity settings

5. Commercial Infrastructure & Sales Channels

• Pilot contracts formalized

• Subscription model scaling

• Distribution and support agreements

• Staff training modules and certification


⸻


17.2 Multi-Site Trial Strategy


Each Phase II site receives:

• 20–200 user licenses

• 1–2 trainer sessions

• Institutional onboarding framework

• Guardian consent templates

• Localized data policies (school/care facility)


Data collected:

• Communication success rate

• Identity confirmation accuracy

• Staff effort reduction metrics

• Student stress-level observations

• Clinician-rated usability


Outcomes determine Phase III and statewide expansion.

17.4 Manufacturing & Production Scalability


EchoAuth remains software-first, but Phase II introduces:

• standardized deployment tablets

• protective cases
