320.3 — Functional Definition


MSARE activates when ADEWE (Section 319) detects early dysregulation OR when NI-AI reads an active escalation pattern.


It executes THREE stabilization layers:


Stage 1 – Immediate Grounding Response


Triggered at the FIRST sign of autonomic disruption.


Functions:

• soft guidance prompts

• “short phrase” grounding

• slow-tone safety cue

• sensory dampening suggestions

• space-awareness reminders

• caregiver proximity cue (“move near, but not too close”)


Stage 2 – Sensory Rebalancing


Triggered if Stage 1 fails within 5–15 seconds.


Functions:

• temperature-based grounding suggestion (cold object / warm pressure)

• light-level reduction

• motion reduction

• rhythmic sound cues

• breathing synchronizer suggestion

• recommended “safe posture” based on child’s profile


Stage 3 – Cognitive Return Layer


Activated once autonomic signals stabilize.


Functions:

• gentle re-entry prompts (“it’s okay, you’re safe”)

• presence restoration

• micro-choice re-engagement (“do you want X or Y?”)

• emotional labeling assistance

• visual anchor suggestion (object, color, symbol)
