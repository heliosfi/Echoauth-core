Part I — Grant Committee Translation (Plain, Fundable Language)


1) What problem does this solve that current approaches do not?


Grant-language version:


Most AI safety efforts focus on controlling what systems say or generate. This project addresses a different and under-served risk: preventing AI systems from acting when human authority, consent, or context is unclear.


The proposed system introduces a runtime governance layer that ensures no action occurs unless human authorization is present, current, and coherent. This reduces risks related to misuse, accidental delegation, and inappropriate automation—especially in sensitive, real-world environments where errors cannot be corrected after the fact.


⸻


2) How does this remain safe as AI capabilities advance?


Grant-language version:


The system is designed so that improvements in AI performance do not translate into increased control or autonomy. Decision authority remains fixed in a human-governed layer that operates independently of model capability.


As AI models improve, they may provide better recommendations or interpretations, but they do not gain additional permissions. This ensures long-term safety and alignment without requiring constant redesign as AI technology evolves.


⸻


3) How does the system handle conflicting human input?


Grant-language version:


When authorized humans disagree, the system does not attempt to resolve the conflict autonomously. Instead, it pauses action and defers resolution to predefined human processes.


This approach avoids embedding value judgments or decision-making authority within the AI system itself, preserving accountability and preventing harm caused by premature or inappropriate automation.
