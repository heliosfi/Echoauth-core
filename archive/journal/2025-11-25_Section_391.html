🔒 SECTION 391 — MULTI-ROUTINE BEHAVIORAL CONFLICT ENGINE (MRBCE)


(Option A — Behavior-Only Version — Fully Safe, Fully Compliant)

────────────────────────────────────────


391.1 — Technical Field


The MRBCE relates to systems and methods for detecting, predicting, and mitigating behavioral conflicts arising from overlapping routines, transitions, or environmental changes affecting neurodivergent users.


This module explicitly avoids emotional inference, remaining strictly in federally compliant “behavioral observation” territory.


⸻


391.2 — Purpose


To ensure NI-AI:

• anticipates routine clashes

• prevents conflict-triggered overload

• supports smooth transitions

• assists caregivers, teachers, or therapists

• reduces meltdown risk through early behavior-based warning


No emotional classification, no affect prediction, no psychological inference — strictly behavior-based.


⸻


391.3 — Functional Definition


The MRBCE evaluates:

• scheduled routines

• ongoing activities

• environmental shifts

• new stimuli entering the environment

• timing overlaps

• sensory-incompatible events

• context deviations


If any combination presents a risk for behavioral conflict, the engine triggers a Conflict Anticipation Flag (CAF).


⸻


391.4 — Inputs

• Routine logs (school schedule, home activities, therapy windows)

• Sensory data (noise, light, movement, crowding)

• Device context signals

• EchoAuth environmental state

• Parent/School priority settings

• Behavior-history mapping (non-emotional)


⸻


391.5 — Outputs

• Behavioral Conflict Score (BCS)

• Routine Clash Warning (RCW)

• Transition Preparation Signal (TPS)

• Overload Prevention Recommendation (OPR)

• Safe-Path Adjustment Node (SPAN)

• Parent/Teacher Alert (PTA) (optional)


All outputs remain within permit­ted behavior-observation boundaries.


⸻


391.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Routine Overlap Detector (ROD)


Identifies time-based overlaps between routines that can create conflict (e.g., transitioning from a preferred activity to an unexpected one).


(b) Sensory-Risk Mapper (SRM)


Maps stimuli to behavior and routine expectations (again, no emotion inference).


(c) Behavior Pattern Comparator (BPC)


Compares prior behavioral tendencies to current routine demands.


(d) Conflict Likelihood Calculator (CLC)


Produces the Behavioral Conflict Score (BCS).


(e) Transition Stabilizer (TS)


Suggests or triggers early-transition support steps.


⸻
