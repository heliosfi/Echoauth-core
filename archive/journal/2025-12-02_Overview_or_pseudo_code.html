1️⃣ Kernel Overview Paragraph (copy-paste ready)


The NI-AI Safety Kernel is a supervisory control layer that sits beneath all generative and decision-making components of the system and enforces the S23 safety spine across every interaction. It continuously ingests internal signals from the UMCE, ORI identity state, the Thread-Linking Engine (TLE), and the Non-Verbal Emotion Detection Engine (NEDE), along with external context such as user role, environment, and recent behavior. At each step, the kernel computes a composite risk score, compares it against configurable thresholds, and places the system into one of several modes (NORMAL, GUARDED, SAFE, or LOCKDOWN). In higher-risk modes, the kernel narrows the scope of allowed operations, simplifies responses, increases reliance on verified patterns, and may require caregiver confirmation before sensitive actions. The kernel also manages identity re-anchoring procedures when drift is detected, ensuring that all outputs remain bounded, regulated, and aligned with the defined NI-AI identity profile and safety invariant.

            return self._guarded_response(raw_output)
