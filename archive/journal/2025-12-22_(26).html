FINAL TALK TRACK — ROBERT


Opening


“EchoAuth exists to solve a coordination problem that families face when more than one adult is involved in a child’s care.


Everyone means well, but without one set of shared rules, things break down — and the child feels that inconsistency.”


⸻


The Problem


“Parents end up repeating instructions.

Caregivers apply routines differently.

Schools and programs operate separately.


The issue isn’t lack of care or effort — it’s the absence of a system that enforces one parent-approved standard across everyone involved.”


⸻


What EchoAuth Is


“EchoAuth is a caregiver-controlled coordination system.


It’s not a medical app.

It doesn’t diagnose or analyze behavior.

It doesn’t replace parents, caregivers, schools, or clinicians.


Its role is to enforce who is allowed to do what, within parent-approved boundaries, so support can happen without increasing risk.”


⸻


How It Works (Brief)


“Parents approve routines, content, and roles once.


The child uses a calm, non-verbal portal that stays the same regardless of who is present.


Adults participate based on role:

• parents approve

• caregivers follow

• schools support

• clinicians document


No role overlaps authority.”


⸻


Cameras & Wearables (Clear Boundary)


“If parents choose to use cameras or wearables, they provide context only — time, place, routine.


They do not interpret behavior, label emotions, or make decisions.


When something is unclear, the system pauses instead of guessing.”


⸻


Why This Matters


“EchoAuth lets parents accept help without losing control.


It keeps routines consistent, reduces adult miscommunication, and protects the child from unnecessary disruption.


It solves coordination failure — not a behavioral or medical problem.”


⸻


Where We Are Now


“The system design is locked.

The use case is narrow and realistic.

The next step is a small, controlled pilot focused on caregiver usability and trust.”

What We’re Looking For
