NIH PHASE I COMMERCIALIZATION PLAN


NI-AI × EchoAuth × MCG

(SBIR / STTR Phase I)


⸻


1. Commercial Opportunity


There is a growing national and federal need for trustworthy, explainable, and governable AI systems that can operate safely in sensitive, human-facing environments.


Current AI solutions face adoption barriers due to:

• Unpredictable behavior under uncertainty

• Lack of explainability and auditability

• Regulatory risk in child-, caregiver-, and accessibility-related contexts

• Inability to default safely to non-action


NI-AI addresses this gap by providing a governance-first AI infrastructure that prioritizes restraint, explainability, and human authority over autonomous behavior.


⸻


2. Product Description (Commercial Framing)


Core Product


NI-AI is a decision-support and explanation infrastructure governed by MCG (Motion / Context Governance) and authorized by EchoAuth.


Key characteristics:

• Fail-closed by default (non-action under uncertainty)

• Caregiver- or institution-mediated operation

• Deterministic, rule-based governance

• Explainable and auditable outputs

• No autonomous decision-making


Initial Commercial Offering

• EchoAuth-enabled NI-AI platform for institutions requiring:

• Secure, adaptive authentication

• Stable non-verbal interaction support

• Governable AI decision-support


⸻


3. Target Customers and Early Markets


Primary Early Markets (Phase I → Phase II Transition)

• Research institutions

• Educational technology providers

• Caregiver-support platforms

• Government contractors and pilot programs

• Public-sector agencies evaluating AI safety


Long-Term Markets

• Federal and state agencies

• Schools and special education programs

• Healthcare-adjacent systems (non-clinical)

• Secure enterprise environments requiring adaptive authentication


⸻


4. Value Proposition


NI-AI’s commercial value lies in what it does not do as much as what it does:

• Does not escalate under uncertainty

• Does not infer intent or emotional state as truth

• Does not act autonomously

• Does not require verbal interaction


Instead, it provides:

• Predictable system behavior

• Clear explanations for pause/deny outcomes

• Audit-ready logs

• Governance aligned with federal standards


This significantly reduces regulatory, legal, and reputational risk for adopters.


⸻


5. Competitive Landscape


Most existing AI platforms emphasize:

• Autonomy

• Personalization

• Predictive inference

• Continuous engagement


NI-AI differentiates by emphasizing:

• Governance over autonomy

• Restraint over optimization

• Explainability over prediction

• Human authority over system agency


This positions NI-AI as complementary infrastructure, not a replacement for existing systems.


⸻


6. Intellectual Property Strategy


Helios F.I LLC’s IP strategy includes:

• Proprietary governance logic (MCG)

• EchoAuth adaptive authorization mechanisms

• System architecture and integration methods

• Documentation and implementation frameworks


Phase I focuses on technical validation, with IP protection and filings pursued as appropriate in later phases.


⸻


7. Commercialization Pathway


Phase I (Current)

• Validate governance-first architecture

• Produce empirical evidence of stability and restraint

• Prepare for controlled pilots


Phase II (Future)

• Pilot deployments with institutions

• Integration with existing platforms

• Revenue validation through licensing or subscription models


Phase III (Future)

• Broader market entry

• Federal and enterprise contracts

• Scaled deployment with maintained governance controls


⸻


8. Revenue Model (Conservative)


Potential revenue models include:

• Institutional licensing

• Subscription-based access for organizations

• Integration fees for platform partners

• Government and research contracts


No direct-to-consumer or unsupervised deployment is planned.

10. Commercialization Risks and Mitigation


Risks:

• Conservative system behavior may limit perceived capability

• Slow adoption in markets expecting autonomous AI


Mitigation:

• Position NI-AI as infrastructure, not replacement AI

• Focus on regulated and risk-sensitive markets

• Emphasize governance and auditability as strengths


⸻


11. Commercialization Summary


NI-AI represents a new category of AI infrastructure focused on governability, restraint, and explainability. Phase I funding enables validation of this approach, reducing technical and regulatory risk while laying the foundation for institutional adoption and long-term commercialization.
