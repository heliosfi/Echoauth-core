SECTION 367 — Adaptive Crisis-Avoidance Prediction Engine (ACAPE)


(Real-Time Behavioral De-escalation Predictor for Neurodivergent Support)


367.1 — Technical Field


The ACAPE module relates to predictive analytics, emotional-state forecasting, and proactive intervention support for autistic and neurodivergent individuals.

ACAPE identifies pre-escalation patterns and prevents crises before they occur — without diagnosing, labeling, or categorizing medical conditions.


⸻


367.2 — Purpose


Current systems only respond after a meltdown or sensory overload.

ACAPE changes the entire field by enabling proactive prevention.


What ACAPE does:

• Detects micro-signals that precede overload

• Predicts environmental risk factors

• Anticipates behavioral tension build-up

• Suggests support actions before escalation begins


This creates:

• fewer crises,

• less shame for the child,

• less stress for caregivers/teachers,

• more autonomy for the user.


This is one of the biggest value drivers for both district adoption and insurance reimbursement later on.


⸻


367.3 — Core Functional Model


ACAPE operates as a 5-layer predictive pipeline:


(A) Micro-Signal Input Layer (MSI)


Captures subtle behaviors including:

• breath pattern shifts

• vocal micro-tremors

• micro-expressions

• hand/finger motion deviations

• fidget-pacing changes

• gaze anchor breaks

• wearable data (if present)

• acoustic + camera cues (Zero-Wearable Mode compatible)


These are behavioral signals, not medical.


⸻


(B) Pre-Overload Pattern Detector (POP-D)


Detects early-stage patterns signaling:

• rising overwhelm

• sensory saturation

• emotional tension accumulation

• cognitive disengagement

• environmental mismatch


Patterns stay non-diagnostic and purely behavioral.


⸻


(C) Temporal Escalation Curve Estimator (TECE)


Predicts how fast a situation will escalate if nothing changes.


Outputs:

• escalation probability

• time window until possible overload (e.g., “2–4 minutes”)

• curve shape (linear, exponential, sudden spike, latent)


This allows caregivers to act before a situation becomes stressful.


⸻


(D) Behavioral Diffusion Map (BDM)


Models how external factors influence the emotional state.


Examples:

• sudden noise

• crowd increase

• lighting changes

• unpredictable movement

• social pressure

• transitions


BDM merges internal + external contexts.


⸻


(E) Intervention Forecast Engine (IFE)


Predicts which action will SUCCESSFULLY prevent escalation:

• sensory reduction

• verbal reassurance

• routine reset

• grounding exercises

• wearable stimulation

• sound modulation

• space transition

• parent/teacher cue


The IFE ranks interventions with a “Success Likelihood Score.”


⸻
