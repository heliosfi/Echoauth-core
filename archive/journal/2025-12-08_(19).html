1. PURPOSE OF THE STUDY


Your child is invited to participate in a research study evaluating EchoAuth, a digital communication and identity-support tool designed for autistic and non-verbal individuals.

This study will examine how well the system helps users:

• express needs

• reduce frustration

• confirm identity safely

• improve communication clarity


This is not a medical study and does not involve treatment or diagnosis.


⸻


2. WHAT WILL HAPPEN DURING THE STUDY


Participation involves brief, supervised interactions with the EchoAuth system in a quiet setting.


Your child will:

1. Be shown how to use the device

2. Complete simple communication selections (buttons, icons, needs statements)

3. Participate in short guided sessions (10–15 minutes each)

4. Take breaks as needed

5. Stop immediately if uncomfortable


Total time commitment: 30–60 minutes.


No biometrics, cameras, recording devices, or personal identifiers will be collected.


⸻


3. RISKS


The study is classified as minimal risk.

Possible risks include:

• mild frustration if an option is unclear

• slight sensory discomfort (brightness, sound)

• brief fatigue from digital interaction


All risks are managed by:

• adjustable sensory settings

• immediate caregiver intervention

• the ability to stop at any time

• predictable, low-stimulation interface design


No physical or emotional harm is expected.


⸻


4. BENEFITS


Your child may:

• express needs more clearly

• reduce frustration

• have a structured alternative communication method

• experience more predictable communication


Benefits are not guaranteed.

Participation helps improve future ASD assistive technologies.


⸻


5. CONFIDENTIALITY


No identifiable information will be collected.

We will store only:

• system activity logs

• button selections

• timing data

• caregiver/staff observations


Data is stored securely and will not include name, photos, voice, or location.


You may request deletion of all data at any time.


⸻


6. VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION AND WITHDRAWAL


Participation is voluntary.

You may withdraw your child at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.

The study staff may also stop participation if needed for safety.
