EchoAuth™ is a caregiver-governed authentication and AI output-integrity system designed to deliver a single, non-contradictory, identity-anchored response at inference time, particularly optimized for caregivers of autistic and non-verbal children (ASD) interacting within high-integrity digital environments. Current AI systems resolve contradictions post-output or distribute identity, safety, memory, and logic across fragmented services, increasing the likelihood of conflicting instructions, context loss, and overwhelming sensory load for neurodivergent users. EchoAuth addresses this gap by unifying these functions into a single runtime loop enforced through a Perception Node Routing and Enforcement Layer (PNRE) and a Coherence Gate choke-point that blocks unvalidated or conflicting outputs before user display. The system aggregates parallel authentication or reasoning candidates, verifies coherence to detect logical or factual conflict, resolves execution by ranking candidates according to caregiver constraints, identity rules, and purpose alignment, and exposes only one validated output path to the user interface. Identity anchors ensure inventor persona, caregiver purpose, and operational tone remain persistent without drift or repetition, while pre-output tone enforcement regulates message delivery into a calm, structured, cognitively accessible format. EchoAuth does not compete on generative velocity, model scale, or ungoverned creative variation, but on stability, correctness, auditable decision logging, and identity continuity, excluding religious or doctrinal logic from runtime behavior. The result is a minimal, transparent, caregiver-ruled compliance spine capable of providing consistent authentication, reducing cognitive and sensory overwhelm, preserving user trust, and delivering singular, coherence-verified outputs tailored for neurodivergent child and caregiver interaction. This establishes a novel, auditable, non-contradictory authentication and governed AI output-integrity framework suitable for scaling into safety- and identity-sensitive domains.
