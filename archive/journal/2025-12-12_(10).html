SECTION VII — PROTOTYPE ROADMAP


7.1 Purpose of the Roadmap


This roadmap defines a clear, low-risk execution path for building, testing, and piloting EchoAuth as a caregiver-guided, non-verbal communication support system.


The roadmap prioritizes:

• Safety

• Accountability

• Measurable progress

• Grant-aligned milestones


No speculative research is required to complete this plan.


⸻


7.2 Phase I — Core MVP Build (Months 0–3)


Objectives

• Deliver a functional, secure MVP

• Establish governance and auditability

• Enable caregiver-led observation workflows


Deliverables

• Secure authentication (RBAC enforced)

• Caregiver dashboard (web)

• Observation logging (input-only)

• Audit logging (append-only)

• MCG middleware enforcement

• Basic export functionality (CSV / PDF)


Success Criteria

• Verified caregivers can log in

• Observations can be recorded and reviewed

• All actions are auditable

• Unsafe or unauthorized actions are blocked


⸻


7.3 Phase II — Controlled Pilot Deployment (Months 3–6)


Objectives

• Validate usability in real caregiving environments

• Collect structured feedback

• Confirm safety and governance effectiveness


Pilot Scope

• Small group of families or care environments

• Limited number of caregivers per child

• No autonomous or adaptive system behavior


Activities

• Caregiver onboarding

• Guided usage of dashboard

• Observation review sessions

• Feedback collection (qualitative)


Success Criteria

• Consistent caregiver usage

• Clear audit trails

• No safety violations

• Positive caregiver feedback on clarity and usefulness


⸻


7.4 Phase III — Refinement & Evaluation (Months 6–9)


Objectives

• Refine system based on pilot feedback

• Improve usability and clarity

• Prepare evaluation materials for funders and partners


Activities

• UX improvements (non-functional)

• Performance and reliability tuning

• Reporting and documentation

• Governance review


Deliverables

• Pilot evaluation report

• Updated system documentation

• Refined deployment guidelines


⸻


7.5 Phase IV — Expansion Readiness (Months 9–12)


Objectives

• Prepare for expanded pilots or institutional partnerships

• Maintain strict non-clinical posture

• Demonstrate operational maturity


Activities

• Scalability planning (users, data, access)

• Partner discussions

• Funding and grant reporting

• Optional continuation planning (no scope expansion)


Success Criteria

• System stability under increased use

• Continued governance enforcement

• Readiness for broader deployment under the same constraints


⸻


7.6 Risk Management & Mitigation


Identified Risks

• Misinterpretation of system purpose

• Feature creep into inference or diagnosis

• Over-engineering early stages


Mitigation Strategies

• Strict adherence to Sections IV–VI

• Governance-first development

• Controlled pilot scope

• Regular compliance review checkpoints


⸻


7.7 Funding Alignment


Funding directly supports:

• MVP development

• Pilot deployment

• Compliance and evaluation

• Documentation and reporting


No funding is allocated to:

• Experimental AI

• Clinical research

• Diagnostic model development


This ensures predictable execution and reviewer confidence.


⸻


7.8 Roadmap Summary Statement (Grant-Ready)


EchoAuth follows a phased, safety-first roadmap that delivers a functional MVP, validates caregiver usability through controlled pilots, and prepares for responsible expansion without introducing clinical or diagnostic risk.
