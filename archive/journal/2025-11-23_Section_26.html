⭐ 26.4 Medical Signal Processing Pipeline


The EMCH analyzes signals in FIVE critical steps:


Step 1 — Physiological Pattern Mapping

• breath → rate / rhythm / depth

• movement → weakness / guarding / pain

• temperature → abnormal zones

• posture → stress / shutdown / illness


Step 2 — Behavior-to-Medical Correlation


Integrates BPE, SODE, NEDE signals:

• pacing → GI distress?

• rocking → pain relief?

• freeze → fear or internal pain?

• withdrawal → fever fatigue?


Step 3 — Sensory-Medical Amplification Layer


Certain sensory reactions signal medical issues:

• light sensitivity → fever or migraine

• sound irritability → sickness onset

• touch avoidance → internal pain


Step 4 — NI–AI Medical Fusion


Your parental intelligence modifies medical interpretation:

• “He drinks less when sick.”

• “He freezes before pain.”

• “He hides when fever rises.”

• “His kidney issue increases dehydration risk.”


Step 5 — Medical Summary Generation


The system produces:

• symptom interpretation

• cause probability

• urgent vs non-urgent classification

• triage priority

• suggested tests

• hydration recommendation

• doctor question prompts


────────────────────────


⭐ 26.5 Clinical Decision Assistance


EMCH does NOT diagnose.

It provides decision support, such as:

• “Breathing irregularity detected: evaluate for respiratory distress.”

• “Temperature elevated: fever onset likely.”

• “Weak movement + low grip: dehydration risk.”

• “Abdominal guarding: possible GI discomfort.”

• “Shutdown posture: evaluate for internal pain/fatigue.”


This gives the doctor a starting point, not a conclusion.
