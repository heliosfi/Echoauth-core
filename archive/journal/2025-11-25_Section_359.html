SECTION 359 — LOW-RESOURCE NEUROVISION ENGINE (LRNE)


(Core Engine Enabling Zero-Wearable Sensory Mode)


⸻


359.1 — Technical Field


The LRNE pertains to computer vision and acoustic processing systems designed to operate on low-resource consumer devices, including low-cost Android phones, tablets, and school-issued Chromebooks.

The module enables ASD-related sensory and behavioral pattern detection without specialized hardware or wearables.


⸻


359.2 — Purpose


To provide real-time, low-compute neurobehavioral insights for ASD users using:

• A basic camera

• A basic microphone

• Basic local device sensors


LRNE creates a lightweight, safe, and accessible ASD support engine.


It is the critical module that makes EchoAuth deployable:

• In low-income households

• In schools where wearables are banned

• In countries with limited infrastructure

• On any device parents already own


This instantly expands EchoAuth’s potential reach by 10–50×.


⸻


359.3 — Core Functional Definition


LRNE is a micro-pattern classifier engineered to:

1. Extract very small behavior signals from low-quality video/audio.

2. Classify ASD-relevant patterns such as:

• stimming escalation

• facial tension

• emotional overload indicators

• breath pattern irregularities

• stress micro-movements

• sensory avoidance signals

3. Predict overload risk using low compute (<15% CPU on a budget phone).

4. Operate fully offline using compressed pattern libraries.

5. Feed its results into ZWSM (358) and into the EchoAuth parent app dashboard.


It is NOT a surveillance system.

It is NOT a diagnostic tool.

It is a real-time support engine.


⸻


359.4 — Inputs

• Camera feed (240p minimum)

• Microphone feed (8–16 kHz)

• Ambient light sensor

• Device motion sensor (optional)

• Parent/caregiver selected mode

• ASD profile stored in user’s EchoAuth baseline

359.5 — Outputs

• Micro-Behavioral Pattern Map (MBPM)

• Overload Risk Score (ORS)

• Sensory Spike Prediction

• Real-Time Stimming Index

• Parent Notification Event (PNE)


These remain local to the device unless the parent chooses to sync.

359.6 — Internal Subsystems


(a) Micro-Expression Detection Layer (MEDL)

Detects:

• eyebrow compression

• cheek tension

• jaw-set patterns

• micro-grimace signatures


These correlate to early distress signs.


(b) Low-Bandwidth Audio Spike Engine (L-BASE)

Tracks:

• tonal shifts

• breath irregularities

• vocal strain

• stress-frequency clusters


Works on very low mic quality devices.


(c) Frame-Delta Movement Analyzer (FDMA)

Extracts:

• body rocking rate

• pacing rhythm

• hand-flap intensity

• freeze–unfreeze transitions


Even at low FPS.


(d) Ambient Stressor Detection Layer (ASDL)

Identifies:

• sudden lighting changes

• noise bursts

• crowd-motion patterns


These are common overload triggers.


(e) Predictive Sensory Risk Model (PSRM)

Combines all micro-detections into:

• Overload risk prediction (5–30 sec ahead)

• Escalation risk detection

• De-escalation suggestion triggers
