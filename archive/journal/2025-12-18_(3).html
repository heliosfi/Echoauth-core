OpenAI AI & Mental Health Grant — Draft Submission


Project Title


MCG + HER: Enforceable Human-Anchored Governance for AI Systems in Sensitive Interactions


⸻


Problem Statement


AI systems are increasingly used in sensitive human contexts—including mental health support, caregiving, and emotionally charged interactions—yet most deployments lack enforceable safeguards that prevent identity abuse, harmful instruction drift, or loss of continuity during interaction.


Current safety approaches rely heavily on post-hoc moderation or model-internal alignment techniques, which do not scale cleanly as systems become more autonomous or adaptive. In high-trust settings, even small failures—such as impersonation, speculative guidance, or unbounded role escalation—can cause psychological harm or undermine user safety.


There is a growing need for human-anchored governance layers that actively constrain AI behavior at runtime, ensuring oversight, continuity, and auditability in sensitive interactions.


⸻


Proposed Solution


We propose building a Minimal Reference Implementation of an enforceable AI governance layer composed of:

• MCG (Modular Continuity Governance): a lightweight governance wrapper that defines non-negotiable, human-anchored invariants.

• HER Kernel (Human Enforcement Runtime): a real-time interception and enforcement engine that validates AI actions against these invariants before outputs are delivered.

• NI (Natural Intellect Anchor): human-defined constraints and ethical priors that bound adaptation and prevent harmful drift.


Rather than modifying or retraining models, this system wraps any existing LLM or agent, enforcing safety rules externally in a model-agnostic manner.


⸻


Why This Matters for Mental Health & Sensitive Use Cases


Mental health and caregiving interactions are high-trust environments where AI failures are not merely technical but human-impacting. The proposed system directly addresses risks such as:

• Unauthorized identity or role switching (e.g., impersonating deceased or emotionally significant individuals)

• Harmful or speculative guidance

• Loss of continuity or abrupt “amnesia” in sensitive conversations

• Unprompted escalation of authority or decision-making claims

• Deception regarding AI nature or capabilities


By enforcing constraints before outputs reach users, this approach supports safe, bounded AI behavior aligned with emerging expectations for human oversight in sensitive domains.


⸻


Project Goals & Deliverables


Primary Goal


Demonstrate that AI behavior in sensitive interactions can be actively constrained, audited, and governed at runtime using a human-anchored enforcement layer.


Key Deliverables

• A Python-based governance wrapper compatible with major LLM APIs or local models

• A rules engine enforcing 3–5 core human-anchored invariants

• Side-by-side demonstrations:

• Unconstrained model behavior

• Governed behavior with MCG + HER active

• Machine-readable audit logs showing:

• Detected violations

• Enforcement actions

• Preserved safety boundaries

• Short recorded demo (2–5 minutes) suitable for evaluation and reuse


⸻


Evaluation Plan


Success will be evaluated through:

• Consistent detection and enforcement of invariant violations

• Clear before/after behavioral differences

• Completeness and clarity of audit logs

• Ability to apply the same governance layer across multiple base models


The focus is feasibility and enforceability, not clinical claims.


⸻


Broader Impact


This project establishes a foundational safety layer for AI systems used in mental health, caregiving, and other emotionally sensitive domains. By proving that enforceable governance can be layered onto existing AI stacks, it supports safer adoption without requiring new models or invasive retraining.


The same governance approach can later be extended to real-world high-trust deployments, including caregiver–child interaction systems, where continuity and identity assurance are critical.


⸻


Funding Use


Requested funding will support:

• Compute resources for model testing and evaluation

• Documentation and artifact generation for reproducibility and review
