SECTION 410 — GLOBAL SAFETY OVERLAP ELIMINATOR (GSOE)


(Patent Module — NI-AI / EchoAuth Safety Core)


410.1 — Technical Field


The GSOE concerns systems, apparatuses, and methods for resolving collisions between multiple active safety layers in a multi-environment AI system operating for ASD support, caregiver alignment, and regulated educational/clinical deployment.


410.2 — Purpose


In real use, a child may be simultaneously inside:

• a school building

• under a parent’s remote supervision

• using a wearable

• standing near a hospital boundary

• connected to a district EchoAuth node

• being monitored under a specific IEP plan


Each of these generates competing safety rules.


GSOE ensures ONLY one unified, parent-governed safety rule is active at any time.


This prevents:

• contradictory safety actions

• dual-trigger responses

• recursive rule loops

• cross-environment drift

• unintended model escalation

• AGI-like emergent conflict resolution


NI-AI stays predictable, safe, and compliant.


410.3 — Functional Definition


GSOE analyzes all active safety contexts and produces a single master safety state using a four-tier priority sequence:


Tier 1 — Parent / Caregiver Safety Rules (absolute)


Always dominates every environment or institution.


Tier 2 — Medical / Hospital Safety Constraints


Applied only when a child is within a verified medical boundary and does not conflict with Tier 1.


Tier 3 — School / District / Classroom Safety Protocols


Aligned with FERPA/COPPA/IEP enforcement; only applied when they do not conflict with Tiers 1 or 2.


Tier 4 — Wearable / Device / Local Environmental Safety Layers


Applies micro-boundaries (heat, noise, motion) only if above tiers remain intact.


The GSOE produces:

• Unified Safety Directive (USD)

• Conflict-Free Safety State (CFSS)


410.4 — Inputs


GSOE receives:

• Parent safety settings

• School-district policy values

• Hospital emergency zone flags

• Wearable-vitals thresholds

• GPS environmental markers

• Real-time sensory noise metrics

• Multi-device sync data (MDSSL)

• Emotion-stability signals (ESML)

• Predictive-risk scores (SFPG)


410.5 — Outputs


GSOE generates:

• Single authorized safety rule

• Priority explanation (for audit readability)

• Conflict elimination certificate

• Suppressions record (rules removed)

• Final GSOE master safety command


410.6 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Safety Context Aggregator (SCA)


Collects all safety rules across environments.


(b) Overlap Detection Matrix (ODM)


Identifies rule conflicts:

• rule vs rule

• environment vs environment

• system vs parent

• medical vs school

• wearable vs core safety


(c) Hierarchical Safety Resolver (HSR)


Applies the 4-tier priority system.


(d) Drift & Recursion Suppression Layer (DRSL)


Prevents any recursive or AGI-type conflict solving:

• no multi-step self-optimization

• no autonomous arbitration

• no reasoning outside authorized domain


(e) Unified Safety Directive Generator (USDG)


Outputs ONE instruction.


(f) Audit Chain Encoder (ACE)


Produces legal/regulatory audit logs with:

• timestamp

• rule chosen

• rule discarded

• parent priority stamp

• zero-override verification


410.7 — Safety Guarantees


GSOE ensures:

• No dual-trigger events

• No contradictory actions

• No unintended automation

• No AGI-like conflict-solving behavior

• No override of caregiver authority

• 100% FERPA/COPPA/HIPAA compatibility

• 100% deterministic safety selection


NI-AI remains predictable, traceable, and parent-aligned.
