⭐ PATENT SECTION 25 — ECHOAUTH COMMUNITY SAFETY BEACONS (ECSB)


(Community-level safety, sensory detection, and elopement prevention.)


────────────────────────


⭐ 25.1 Overview


The EchoAuth Community Safety Beacon (ECSB) is a compact, low-cost, low-power sensor unit installed in:

• parks

• playgrounds

• sidewalks

• bus stops

• community centers

• libraries

• stores

• malls

• transit stations

• public bathrooms

• indoor play areas


ECSB creates a protective sensory and safety bubble around children in public environments.


It does NOT use cameras or microphones — making it 100% privacy safe.


Through:

• motion field sensing

• sound exposure detection

• hazard-zone mapping

• transition stress prediction

• wearable integration

• toy module pairing

• emotional/sensory state pairing


ECSB becomes the REAL-WORLD guardian for autistic children.


────────────────────────


⭐ 25.2 Hardware Inputs


ECSB contains environmental sensors that detect:


A. Wide-Angle Motion Radar


Detects:

• wandering toward streets

• fast movement

• directional shifts

• running

• collapse patterns

• pacing near edges


B. Noise Exposure Sensor


(Not a microphone; measures decibel + frequency spikes)

Detects:

• sirens

• traffic noise

• loud crowds

• shopping mall noise volatility

• sudden sensory shocks


C. Visual Intensity Sensor


Detects:

• flashing lights

• visual chaos (movement density)

• bright store lighting

• outdoor sunlight spikes


D. Temperature + Air Sensor


Detects:

• overheating risk

• cold stress

• humidity changes

• park weather effects


E. Proximity + Boundary Sensors


Detects movement toward:

• streets

• parking lots

• water bodies

• playground exits

• open doors

• mall escalators

• crowds


────────────────────────


⭐ 25.3 System Outputs


ECSB outputs:


A. Wearable Alerts (EWSB)

• vibration

• calming patterns

• emergency pulses


B. Caregiver Alerts


Sent to:

• smartphone

• watch

• school hub (if on a trip)


C. Environmental Warnings


Beacon LED signals:

• green = safe

• yellow = mild sensory load

• orange = overload rising

• red = danger / elopement risk detected


D. Public Space Mods (Optional Integrations)

• dimming local lights

• soft tones

• sensory relief zones

• quiet pods activation


────────────────────────


⭐ 25.4 Community Sensory Mapping


ECSB creates a sensory map of each location:

• noise cycles

• bright areas

• high-movement zones

• unpredictable spaces

• calming zones

• danger paths


This allows EchoAuth to learn community patterns.


Example:


Park Zone Map:

• swings = safe

• street side = danger

• playground corner = overload zone


Mall Map:

• food court = high sensory volatility

• toy aisle = safe zone

• escalator entrance = hazard

ECSB detects:

• wandering movement

• shutdown collapse


This directly integrates with:

• BPE (Behavior Prediction Engine)

⭐ 25.6 Emotional + Sensory State Pairing


When the wearable detects:

• rising anxiety

• emotional spike

• sensory overload

• shutdown


ECSB can:

• change local lighting (if integrated)

• activate a soft “safe tone”

• alert caregiver

• predict whether the child will run or freeze

• guide caregiver to supportive zones

⭐ 25.7 Medical Emergency Detection


If the child experiences:

• collapse

• extreme fatigue

• dehydration signal

• overheating

• breath irregularity


ECSB:

• sends caregiver emergency alert

• logs GPS location (only in emergencies)

• communicates with MEWE

• triggers red LED

• signals wearable to vibrate strongly
