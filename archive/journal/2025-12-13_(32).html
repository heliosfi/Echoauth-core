HOSTILE Q&A — PRICING v2


Parents · Institutions · Regulators · Funders


⸻


👪 PARENTS / FAMILIES (DEEPER PUSHBACK)


Q1: “If I stop paying, does my child lose protection?”


Answer:

No. Stopping payment does not remove safety protections or create urgency. The system defaults to observation and non-action.


⸻


Q2: “Does paying more give faster access or better treatment?”


Answer:

No. Payment level never changes access, speed, authority, or system behavior.


⸻


Q3: “Will I be reminded or pressured to keep subscribing?”


Answer:

No. There are no urgency-based reminders tied to safety, outcomes, or usage.


⸻


Q4: “Does subscription mean my child needs to ‘use’ the system?”


Answer:

No. Non-use is a valid and successful outcome.


⸻


Q5: “How do I know charity users aren’t treated differently?”


Answer:

Because funding source is not visible to the system or staff. All users experience the same behavior.


⸻


🏫 SCHOOLS / INSTITUTIONS (BUDGET & LIABILITY)


Q6: “Why shouldn’t we just pay per use?”


Answer:

Per-use pricing incentivizes activation. This system is designed to avoid that incentive entirely.


⸻


Q7: “What if we pay and the system doesn’t ‘do much’?”


Answer:

Then it is working correctly. The value is preventing harm, not generating activity.


⸻


Q8: “Does institutional payment increase our liability?”


Answer:

No. The system enforces boundaries independently of who pays.


⸻


Q9: “Can we restrict access based on funding limits?”


Answer:

No. Restricting access based on funding would reintroduce pressure and risk.


⸻


🏛️ REGULATORS / OVERSIGHT (HARD QUESTIONS)


Q10: “Is this pay-to-access safety?”


Answer:

No. Payment sustains availability, not protection or authority.


⸻


Q11: “Isn’t charging families ethically questionable?”


Answer:

Families are not charged for safety decisions. Multiple coverage paths ensure affordability without changing system behavior.


⸻


Q12: “How do you prevent financial coercion?”


Answer:

By separating payment from outcomes, urgency, and authority.


⸻


Q13: “Is there a conflict of interest in your model?”


Answer:

No. The model removes incentives to increase usage or escalate actions.


⸻


💰 GRANT REVIEWERS / FUNDERS (SUSTAINABILITY)


Q14: “Why not fully grant-fund families?”


Answer:

A blended model ensures long-term sustainability without relying on perpetual grant cycles.


⸻


Q15: “How do donations actually help families?”


Answer:

They expand access pools without altering the user experience or system behavior.


⸻


Q16: “How do you measure financial success ethically?”


Answer:

By stability and access continuity, not usage or activation rates.


⸻


🔒 EDGE CASES (DON’T FLINCH)


Q17: “Isn’t this just paying for peace of mind?”


Answer:

It’s paying for a system that enforces restraint and safety without relying on human judgment.


⸻


Q18: “Could pricing ever change behavior indirectly?”


Answer:

No. Pricing is structurally separated from authority and execution.


⸻


Q19: “What happens if a funder pressures you to change pricing?”


Answer:

We do not change pricing in ways that affect safety or authority.


⸻


Q20: “Why should anyone trust this won’t drift later?”


Answer:

Because pricing changes require architectural review, not business iteration.


⸻


FINAL PRICING CLOSE (USE THIS)


No one pays to make something happen.

Payment exists so nothing unsafe ever has to.
