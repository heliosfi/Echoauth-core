1 ASF Profound Autism Pilot Grant — Proposed Abstract

Title: Identity-Anchored Governance for Safe Inclusion of Non-Verbal Individuals with Profound Autism in Research and Daily Support

Individuals with profound autism—minimally or non-verbal, often with intellectual disability and 24/7 support needs—are systematically underrepresented in research due to challenges in safe, consensual data collection and real-time behavioral support without manipulation or distress.

This pilot introduces EchoAuth, a mobile therapeutic AI companion governed by Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG v3.0), designed from lived experience to process biological signals (heart rate variability, vocal prosody, movement) through a 7-stage pipeline that enforces strict safety invariants before any response.

Unlike conventional AI systems, EchoAuth never diagnoses, labels emotion, or manipulates affect. MCG acts as an independent decision judge (Navigator | Guardian | Architect roles) that halts progression on any ambiguity, instability, or boundary risk—ensuring actions only occur when verifiably safe, coherent, and human-approved.

Specific Aims:

1 Prototype and field-test EchoAuth with 8–12 families of non-verbal children with profound autism to stabilize physiological and behavioral signals during daily transitions and potential meltdown states.

2 Demonstrate improved recruitment and retention feasibility for future biological/behavioral studies by providing caregivers real-time, identity-respecting regulation support without researcher interference.

3 Generate traceable governance logs to establish a replicable safety standard for AI-assisted research involving profoundly autistic participants.

This work directly addresses ASF priorities by enabling safer, more inclusive study of biological mechanisms and behavioral interventions in the profound autism population—currently the most underserved group.

2 Research Strategy Excerpt: Innovation & Approach (Safety Section)

The core innovation lies in governance preceding interpretation.

EchoAuth routes all biological signals through a rigidly governed 7-stage pipeline culminating in dual independent safety layers (MCG v3.0 + Safety Invariant Engine). MCG continuously evaluates one question: “Is it safe, permitted, and coherent to proceed right now?” Using Navigator (direction), Guardian (veto), and Architect (explainable rationale) roles, it enforces non-negotiable invariants including:

• No action during detected emotional or physiological instability

• No guessing on unclear data

• No cross-identity confusion

• Human authority final at all times

This design eliminates risks inherent in predictive AI systems by making safe inaction the default. Every decision produces structured, human-readable justification for audit, creating a certifiable trail absent in current assistive technologies.

For profoundly autistic users, this means support only activates under conditions of clarity and stability—preventing escalation and preserving autonomy.
