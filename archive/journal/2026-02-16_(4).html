📄 1-Page Modular Brief


EchoAuth — Runtime Governance Constraint Layer


Core Mechanism


EchoAuth runtime governance is a deterministic authorization gate that enforces parent-anchored authority before execution in vulnerable care contexts. Identity binding, judgment, and execution are mechanically separated. If authority, coherence, or role alignment is unresolved at runtime, execution halts. Refusal is treated as a valid operational state, not an error condition.


⸻


Structural Separation

• Identity Binding Layer — Establishes role and scope (non-executable)

• Judgment Layer — Validates authority alignment and detects conflict

• Execution Gate (Deterministic) — Opens only upon validated authority; defaults to halt under ambiguity


⸻


Distinction from Existing Systems


Most systems:

• Assign permissions statically (RBAC)

• Monitor behavior

• Flag anomalies

• Correct post-execution


EchoAuth runtime governance:

• Revalidates authority at moment of execution

• Invalidates cached permissions on role conflict

• Collapses agency under disagreement

• Does not arbitrate human disputes

• Does not infer consent


Monitoring observes failure.

This mechanism prevents execution before failure.


⸻


Failure Model (Falsifiable Conditions)


The governance model fails if:

• Execution proceeds under unresolved authority

• Permissions persist after role change or conflict

• Halt states are bypassable

• Consent is inferred without explicit authorization


These are mechanically testable invariants.


⸻


Scope Boundary


This is not:

• A general AI alignment system

• An arbitration engine

• Autonomous decision-making

• A behavioral optimization layer


It is a runtime authorization constraint layer scoped to vulnerable care coordination environments.

🎤 Q&A Rehearsal Sheet (Evaluator Conversation Ready)


Q: What problem does this solve?

It prevents execution under ambiguous authority in vulnerable care contexts by enforcing deterministic runtime authorization constraints.


⸻


Q: How is this different from RBAC?

RBAC assigns permissions statically based on role. This mechanism revalidates authority at runtime before each execution event and defaults to halt under ambiguity.


⸻


Q: What happens when humans disagree?

The system halts. It does not arbitrate. Authority resolution remains external to the system.


⸻


Q: What would failure look like?

Execution under unresolved authority, persistence of permissions after role conflict, or bypassable halt states.


⸻


Q: Is this meant to scale across AI broadly?

No. It is scoped to vulnerable care contexts where authority boundaries must be mechanically enforced.


⸻


Q: Why is refusal central?

Because in high-risk care environments, non-execution under uncertainty is safer than optimization under incomplete authority.


⸻


Q: Why $150k?

Funding validates invariants through adversarial testing, simulation, CI enforcement, and independent review — not product expansion.
