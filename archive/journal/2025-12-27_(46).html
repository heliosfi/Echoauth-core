NI-AI Doctrine — Compliance Summary (v1.0)
Non-Clinical, Non-Diagnostic Control Framework for Runtime Stabilization & Governance
Date: December 27, 2025 | Reference: EchoAuth Technical Systems Whitepaper (TSW), Section VI – Compliance, Safety & Governance

Purpose

The NI-AI Doctrine establishes a real-time control system that stabilizes AI behavior around human emotional state, identity, and purpose before any response reaches the user. It integrates Natural Intelligence (NI) mapping—focusing on human patterns like intention, emotional state, urgency, and cognitive load—with Artificial Intelligence (AI) capabilities to ensure coherence, safety, and trust. This framework enforces runtime guardrails in interactive systems, prioritizing human sovereignty and preventing unchecked AI outputs in applications such as authentication, decision support, and neurodivergent assistance.

What NI-AI Is Not

• Not a diagnostic tool: Does not assess, diagnose, or treat medical/psychological conditions (e.g., no clinical claims for emotional states).

• Not autonomous AI: AI components are subordinate to NI-derived rules and coherence checks; no independent decision-making without human-state alignment.

• Not a general-purpose model: Functions as a layered governor for specific ecosystems (e.g., EchoAuth), not a standalone LLM or training paradigm.

• Not speculative: Grounded in implementable logic, with explicit failure modes and audit hooks for regulatory oversight.

Core Runtime Flow

NI-AI operates as a layered stack with end-to-end processing for each interaction:

1 Input Layer: Captures multimodal signals (text, voice tone/pace, behavioral patterns like pauses, and context such as session history).

2 NI Layer & Emotional State Engine (ESE): Maps inputs to human patterns (intention, emotional state: stress vs. calm; focused vs. scattered; frustrated vs. curious), producing an Emotional State Profile (ESP).

3 Coherence Engine (C1–C5): Validates AI proposals across context (C1: session alignment), emotional tone (C2), logic (C3), temporal consistency (C4), and purpose (C5).

4 AI Layer: Performs reasoning, retrieval, and generation, guided by NI rules.

5 Stability Regulation Kernel (SRK): Filters drift, hallucinations, emotional mismatches, or unsafe jumps; rewrites or rejects unstable outputs, downgrading complexity if user overload detected.

6 EchoAuth Layer: Continuously verifies identity via Resonance Match Score (RMS), comparing current behavior to baselines; low RMS triggers step-up verification or blocks.

7 Neurodivergent Support Mode (NSM): Adjusts delivery (shorter sentences, reduced stimuli, slower pacing) if flagged.

8 Output Layer: Delivers tailored responses (text, voice, UI adjustments) with logged metrics (ESP, RMS, coherence scores, risk flags).

Safety & Identity Controls

Built-in “brakes” ensure proactive risk mitigation:

• Coherence Guardrail: Pauses on low scores, prompts clarification, and rebuilds context.

• Emotional Guardrail: Softens tone and simplifies actions for distressed/overloaded users.

• Identity Guardrail: Refuses high-risk requests on low RMS, with no silent failures.

• Audit & Review: Sessions loggable for human admins, compliance reviewers, or supervisors; supports traceability in regulated contexts (e.g., health, finance).

Deployment Tiers

Scalable implementation:

1 Tier 1 – Assist Mode: Observes and suggests (e.g., better prompts, safer actions).

2 Tier 2 – Co-Pilot Mode: Auto-rewrites interactions, adjusts UI pacing, enforces coherence.

3 Tier 3 – Guardian Mode: Requires approvals for high-risk actions in government, health, or finance; mandates EchoAuth confirmation and SRK passes.

Auditability Statement

All interactions generate auditable trails, including ESP, RMS, coherence scores, and risk flags. The system adheres to human-in-the-loop principles, with implementation details in the NI-AI Kernel Engineering Reference (internal document). This framework complies with ethical AI standards (e.g., non-subjugation, mutual learning) and is designed for review under NIH/NSF guidelines for responsible innovation, emphasizing inclusivity (e.g., NSM for ASD/ND support) without overstepping into clinical domains.
