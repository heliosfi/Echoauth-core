STAGE 6 — LIGHT CONTROL MATRIX v1.0


Reflective Control & Oversight Framework

(Non-Operational · Non-Autonomous · Governance-First)


⸻


6.1 Purpose of the Light Control Matrix


The Light Control Matrix (LCM) defines how system states are observed, reflected, and governed—not how actions are executed.


It exists to:

• Make control visible and auditable

• Ensure non-action is a valid outcome

• Separate observation from authority

• Prevent implicit autonomy or hidden decision paths


Scope note:

The LCM does not initiate actions, make predictions, or infer intent. It reflects state transitions for governance review.


⸻


6.2 Position in the Architecture


Order of authority (unchanged):

1. MCG — Final governance authority

2. EchoAuth — Role & authorization enforcement

3. Light Control Matrix — State reflection & constraint visibility

4. NI-AI — Read-only explanation layer


Canonical statement:

The Light Control Matrix reflects system state; it does not control outcomes.


⸻


6.3 Control States (Reflective Only)


The LCM recognizes a finite, closed set of states:

• Observe — System monitors without evaluation

• Confirm — Governance conditions are checked

• Pause — Non-action due to uncertainty or boundary

• Allow — Authorized continuation (caregiver-mediated)

• Deny — Explicit stop due to policy or safety rule


No additional states may be inferred or generated.


⸻


6.4 Light Signals (Non-Scoring)


The term “Light” refers to visibility, not measurement.


Light signals are:

• Descriptive (what state exists)

• Non-quantitative

• Non-ranking

• Non-predictive


Examples:

• “State paused due to incomplete authorization”

• “Observation ongoing; no execution permitted”

• “Action denied by policy boundary”


No scores, grades, or behavioral labels are produced.


⸻


6.5 Interaction With MCG


MCG consumes LCM output as contextual visibility, not instruction.


Rules:

• MCG may override, pause, or terminate regardless of LCM reflection

• LCM never escalates or de-escalates authority

• All transitions route back through MCG


Key principle:

Reflection never substitutes governance.


⸻


6.6 Interaction With EchoAuth


EchoAuth provides binary authorization signals:

• Authorized

• Not authorized


LCM reflects these states without modification.


LCM does not:

• Interpret identity

• Adjust thresholds

• Override role assignments


⸻


6.7 Interaction With NI-AI


NI-AI may explain:

• What state is active

• Why a pause or denial occurred

• Which policy boundary applied


NI-AI may not:

• Recommend actions

• Predict outcomes

• Suggest overrides


⸻


6.8 Child & Non-Verbal Safety Clause


Within the Light Control Matrix:

• Silence is a valid state

• Stillness is not failure

• Non-response does not trigger escalation

• Pause is a successful outcome


No signal is treated as urgency unless explicitly authorized by caregiver governance.


⸻


6.9 Audit & Review


Every reflected state is:

• Time-stamped

• Logged

• Reviewable

• Immutable per policy


The matrix enables after-the-fact review, not real-time enforcement.


⸻


6.10 Explicit Non-Claims (Flag Safety)


The Light Control Matrix does not:

• Make decisions

• Predict behavior

• Interpret emotion as fact

• Replace human judgment

• Operate autonomously


It exists solely to make governance legible.
