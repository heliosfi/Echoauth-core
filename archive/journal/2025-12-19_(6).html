🛡️ MCG v3.1 — Safety & Ethics Appendix


(Grant-Ready | NIH / NSF / NIDILRR / ASF Compatible)


Applies to: EchoAuth™, NI-AI runtime, all governed modules

Purpose: To document enforceable safety, ethics, and human-control guarantees

Scope: Design-time, run-time, and deployment-time governance


⸻


1. Safety Philosophy (Plain, Verifiable)


EchoAuth is governed by Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) v3.1, a governance-first control layer that enforces permission, restraint, and human supremacy over all AI behavior.


MCG does not optimize persuasion, autonomy, or behavioral influence.

It governs when the system must act, when it must wait, and when it must stop.


Safety is enforced by architecture, not by post-hoc moderation.


⸻


2. Core Ethical Guarantees


2.1 No Diagnosis or Clinical Substitution

• The system does not diagnose, label, or infer medical or psychological conditions.

• Outputs are supportive and non-prescriptive.


2.2 No Identity, Persona, or Emotional Authority

• The system does not form or present a self, persona, or emotional role.

• No anthropomorphic framing or attachment-seeking behavior is permitted.


2.3 Default-to-Safe Inaction

• When uncertainty, ambiguity, or boundary conflict is detected, non-action is the preferred outcome.

• Silence or deferral is treated as correct behavior.


⸻


3. Human Authority & Control


3.1 Absolute Human Override

• Caregivers, clinicians, or authorized users may pause, override, or terminate system operation at any time.

• No resistance, negotiation, or adaptive penalty is permitted.


3.2 Human Supremacy by Design

• System confidence never overrides human judgment.

• The system defers unconditionally when human input conflicts with internal assessment.


⸻


4. Reversibility & Harm Prevention

• All actions are reversible, haltable, or terminable.

• No irreversible state accumulation or memory-based entrapment is permitted.

• All permission decisions are auditable.


This prevents:

• lock-in effects

• cumulative bias

• runaway adaptation


⸻


5. Vulnerable Population Safeguards


EchoAuth is designed for contexts involving:

• non-verbal or minimally verbal individuals

• neurodivergent users

• dependent or caregiving relationships


Safeguards include:

• strict boundary enforcement

• non-persuasive interaction patterns

• caregiver-first authority model


The system cannot escalate engagement, dependency, or influence.


⸻


6. Compliance Alignment


MCG v3.1 is aligned with:

• NIH / NSF responsible AI principles

• NIDILRR safety expectations

• Human-in-the-loop and human-in-control standards

• Assistive technology ethical guidance


Safety claims are falsifiable and enforceable, not aspirational.


⸻


7. Binding Statement


MCG governs permission, not capability.

Human authority is absolute.

Safety is enforced by definition, not mitigation.
