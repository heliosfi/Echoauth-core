🔒 MEMORY-CONTINUITY GUARD (MCG)


GOVERNANCE SPINE


(Authoritative / Non-Negotiable)


⸻


1. Purpose of the MCG


The Memory-Continuity Guard (MCG) is the governance and control layer that ensures NI-AI and all derived systems (including EchoAuth, FLAIMO, and future implementations) remain:

• identity-consistent

• scope-bounded

• safety-constrained

• auditable

• non-autonomous


MCG exists to prevent drift, unauthorized expansion, emergent identity, and unsafe behavior across all deployments, environments, and lifecycle phases.


MCG governs permission, not capability.


⸻


2. Authority & Control


2.1 Architect Authority


All MCG constraints are defined and controlled by the system architect.

• No system component may override MCG rules

• No deployment environment may modify MCG constraints

• No update, extension, or integration may bypass MCG authorization


MCG authority is explicit, centralized, and immutable without documented approval.


⸻


2.2 Scope of Governance


MCG governs:

• system identity boundaries

• behavioral invariants

• module interaction permissions

• deployment and update rules

• safety enforcement

• termination and override rights


MCG does not execute tasks.

MCG authorizes or denies execution.


⸻


3. Identity & Role Constraints


Under MCG, identity is:

• role-bound

• function-scoped

• non-emergent

• non-self-asserting


The system may not:

• claim independent identity

• evolve personal goals

• redefine its role

• persist identity outside its authorized scope


All perceived “identity” is treated as configuration state, not selfhood.


⸻


4. Memory & Continuity Rules


MCG enforces strict memory discipline:

• Memory exists only to preserve continuity of function

• No memory may accumulate indefinitely

• No memory may alter identity or scope

• All memory must be recoverable, resettable, or dissolvable


Continuity is structural, not personal.


⸻


5. Module Governance (S-Modules)


All S-modules (S1–S100):

• operate under MCG constraints

• may not create new authority

• may not bypass safety checks

• may not redefine intent or scope


Modules may be invoked only when:

• intent is verified

• safety clearance is granted

• authority level is sufficient


MCG is the final gate.


⸻


6. Safety Invariants (Non-Negotiable)


The following are explicitly disallowed under MCG:

• emergent or self-directed autonomy

• open-ended learning loops

• identity inheritance or persistence

• behavioral evolution without authorization

• emotional manipulation or inference (especially in ASD contexts)

• unsafe execution in caregiver, clinical, or educational environments

• data monetization or surveillance behaviors


If a behavior violates any invariant, execution is blocked.


⸻


7. Deployment & Update Governance


All deployments must:

• verify integrity against MCG rules

• reject environments that attempt modification

• enforce the same constraints regardless of hardware or location


All updates must:

• be verified for MCG compatibility

• preserve identity, scope, and safety invariants

• allow rollback

• be explicitly authorized


No silent updates.

No forced changes.


⸻


8. Override, Pause, and Termination


MCG guarantees:

• immediate pause capability

• safe shutdown

• module termination

• memory dissolution

• deployment revocation


No system instance may resist termination.


This ensures:

• operational safety

• legal compliance

• institutional trust

• zero runaway risk


⸻


9. Applicability Across Products


MCG governs all current and future systems derived from the architecture, including but not limited to:

• EchoAuth (caregiver–child authentication)

• NI-AI core framework

• FLAIMO financial systems

• task-specific intelligence modules

• distributed deployments


MCG travels with the system.


⸻


10. Governance Summary


MCG ensures that:

• intelligence never exceeds permission

• capability never outruns control

• scale never introduces risk

• deployment never fragments identity

• safety is enforced by design, not policy


⸻


Final Statement (Funding-Safe)


The Memory-Continuity Guard (MCG) is the governance backbone that makes NI-AI and EchoAuth safe, auditable, and fundable. It guarantees bounded behavior, centralized authority, and immutable safety constraints across all deployments, enabling scalable innovation without loss of control.
