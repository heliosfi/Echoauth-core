🔥 Section 421 — Applied Sensory Distress Classification for Non-Verbal ASD Children (ASDC)


421.1 — Technical Field


This invention relates to a device-optional sensory monitoring and classification system that detects environmental stress, pain, dehydration risk, and sensory distress in non-verbal autistic children, translating non-physical signals into caregiver-only alert packets with frozen decision logic to avoid medical or AGI classification.


421.2 — Problem Solved


Children on the ASD spectrum who are non-verbal often cannot explain pain, overheating, dehydration, dizziness, or discomfort, especially during changes in environment, temperature, or motion.

Current AI systems do not classify sensory distress without emotional guessing or medical claims.

NI-AI solves this by classifying distress through calibrated sensory thresholds, not diagnosis.


421.3 — Inputs (Minimal Hardware Set)


NI-AI uses phone sensors or optional wearables to read:

• Ambient temperature shift

• Noise pressure (dB level)

• Sudden motion frequency

• Light intensity change (lux shift)

• Kinetic motion pattern via wristband accelerometer (optional)

• Device-level environmental context frames from EchoAuth™ control pairing


421.4 — Classification Output


NI-AI generates a Sensory Distress Classification Vector (SDCV) with urgency levels 0–5:


Level Meaning Alert Target

0 Normal tolerance No alert

1 Mild discomfort Guardian notice

2 Rising sensory pressure Guardian alert

3 Pain or dehydration likely Guardian priority alert

4 High distress School dashboard + parent alert

5 Immediate safety breach Parent phone emergency


421.5 — Boundary Lock Mechanic

• All classification logic is frozen post-training

• The system cannot adjust by itself

• Every threshold is caregiver-defined

• Parent/IEP overrides remain the highest authority

• No emotional, clinical, or medical inference is generated


421.6 — Sensor → Alert Packet


When a Level 3–5 classification is reached, NI-AI sends only:


“Child Sensory Red-Zone Breach” encrypted command packet containing:

• Environment type

• Stress source (noise/temperature/motion/light)

• Urgency score

• Timestamp

• EchoAuth™ encrypted guardian key

• No video/audio capture

• No PII data retention


421.7 — Expected Outcome


This invention reduces:

• Transition shock

• Classroom meltdown frequency

• Unexplained physical distress escalation

• Cognitive overload

by detecting sensory pain or dehydration risk before the child collapses into shutdown or crisis.
