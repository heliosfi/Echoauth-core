Use of Funds & Repayment Logic


Use of Funds (12–18 Month Horizon)


Requested funds will be allocated across clearly defined, execution-focused categories designed to move EchoAuth from prototype to early revenue while maintaining safety and compliance.


1. Product & Engineering (≈40–45%)

• Completion of EchoAuth prototype build

• Integration of Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG v3.0)

• Sensor integration and non-verbal signal processing

• Internal testing and refinement

• Engineering documentation and handoff materials


2. Safety, Compliance & Validation (≈15–20%)

• Safety logic testing and verification

• Documentation aligned with medical-adjacent and child-safety standards

• Controlled pilot validation

• Data security and privacy safeguards


3. Pilot Deployment & Feedback (≈15–20%)

• Small-scale pilots with families, schools, or community partners

• Caregiver training and onboarding

• Feedback loops to refine usability and safety

• Iterative system improvements based on real-world use


4. Operations & Business Infrastructure (≈10–15%)

• Cloud services and development tooling

• Business operations and administration

• Legal, accounting, and compliance support

• Insurance and basic risk coverage


5. Contingency & Risk Buffer (≈5–10%)

• Reserved to manage unexpected technical or deployment issues

• Ensures continuity without operational disruption


All funds are used for direct project execution.

No funds are allocated to speculative expansion, unrelated ventures, or personal use.


⸻


Repayment Logic


EchoAuth’s repayment strategy is designed to be conservative, diversified, and realistic, consistent with CDFI expectations.


Primary Repayment Sources:


1. Pilot & Institutional Contracts

• Paid pilots with schools, clinics, and community organizations

• Subscription or licensing fees for controlled deployments

• Revenue begins at small scale and grows incrementally


2. Caregiver & Organizational Subscriptions

• Monthly or annual subscriptions for approved environments

• Pricing structured to remain accessible while covering operating costs

• Predictable recurring revenue


3. Grant & Program Blending

• Non-dilutive grants used to reduce operating burden

• Loan repayment prioritized from earned revenue, not grant funds


4. Services & Custom Integrations (Selective)

• Limited custom deployments for institutions

• Engineering and configuration fees for specialized environments


⸻


Cash Flow Philosophy

• No dependence on a single customer or revenue stream

• Gradual scaling aligned with system validation

• Revenue growth tied directly to deployment milestones

• Operating costs remain controlled during early stages


⸻


Risk Mitigation for Lenders

• Phased deployment reduces exposure

• Deterministic governance lowers legal and operational risk

• Safety-first defaults minimize misuse or liability

• Human authority and oversight remain central

• If growth is slower than expected, the system can remain sustainable at smaller scale


⸻


Underwriter Summary (Plain Language)


Loan funds are used to complete a safe, testable prototype and deploy it in small, controlled environments that generate early recurring revenue. Repayment is based on predictable pilot contracts and subscriptions rather than speculative growth.


⸻


One-Line Repayment Close


“EchoAuth repays through steady, contract-based revenue tied to validated deployments—not hype-driven expansion.”
