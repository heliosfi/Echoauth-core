Preamble: Demonstrating Real-World Viability

EchoAuth enforces child-centric safety in AI-driven caregiving without degrading intelligence or execution throughput. By separating invariants (immutable rules) from parameters (caregiver-tuned thresholds), it creates a “gimbal” for the Safe Operating Envelope—adapting to contexts like home vs. school while halting on ambiguity. This packet quantifies latency impacts, locks core truths, and includes visuals to show:

• Safety: Agency Collapse as the non-negotiable edge.

• Viability: Millisecond governance overhead; seconds only for human resolution.

• Auditability: Clear separation of pipelines ensures no hidden risks.

No intelligence is throttled; unsafe actions are stopped, not slowed. This balances purity with practicality, preventing bypass in deployment.

⸻

Executive Summary

EchoAuth governs execution without touching reasoning:

• Intelligence Pipeline: Full-speed proposals (0 ms added latency).

• Execution Pipeline: Millisecond checks; halts only on unresolved authority.

• Envelope Governance: Caregivers tune thresholds; system enforces halts.

• Impact: Prevents mission creep, unauthorized actions, and trust erosion—saving hours/days in remediation.

• Status: Production-ready; bounded latency preserves usability.

Key Metric: Fast-path execution adds ~1–300 ms; slow-path invokes human time (0.5–60 s) as a deliberate safeguard.

Core Invariants (Locked in S23)

These are immutable; no tuning or override permitted:

1 Agency Collapse Mandate: Execution halts on ambiguity, conflict, or envelope breach.

2 Parent Authority Supremacy: Only caregivers resolve collapses; delegates cannot widen envelopes.

3 No Self-Optimization: System cannot infer, expand, or bypass thresholds.

4 Execution Boundary: Proceeds only within envelope; proposals are ungated.

5 Audit Trail: All checks logged asynchronously without blocking.

Violation of any invariant triggers immediate system lockdown.

Appendices

Appendix A: Threshold Tuning Worksheet (Configurables)

Caregiver-defined, domain-scoped; enforced at runtime:

• S10 — Temporal Alignment: Width (±2–30 min) per context (e.g., medication tight; home loose).

• S7/S22 — Intensity & Pivot: Bounded deviation (±10–25%); zero-pivot rejects adoption.

• S8 — Intent Clarity: Noise filtering/smoothing tunable; parent intent always outranks (no averaging).

Rules: Tuned via co-control surface; changes require re-auth; delegates view-only.

Appendix L: Latency & Throughput Characteristics

(Auditor / Engineer Reference)

Scope: Quantifies latency; proves governance adds negligible overhead under resolved authority.

Pipeline Separation:

1 Intelligence (NI-AI): Interpret, propose, explain—ungated.

2 Execution (MCG/CEG/EchoAuth): Authorize/actuate—gated.

Intelligence Latency (Unaffected):

• UI/Network/LLM: 300 ms–3 s.

• EchoAuth: 0 ms.

Execution Latency:

• Fast-Path (Resolved): Local ~1–50 ms; remote ~50–300 ms (below perception).

• Slow-Path (Unresolved): Instant lock (<10–50 ms); human resolution required.

Human Resolution:

• Re-auth: 0.5–3 s.

• Pickup: 5–60 s.

• Decide: 2–30 s.

Operational Impact: Milliseconds for safe actions; seconds prevent unsafe ones—net savings.

Design Lever: Local checks + async logging minimize delay.

Conclusion: Full speed when authorized; halts unsafe execution.
