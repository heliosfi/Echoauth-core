Appendix N — Negative Capability Proofs


What the EchoAuth System Cannot Do (By Construction)


Status: SEALED

Scope: Governance Spine (S1–S23), with emphasis on S23 (Envelope Governance)


⸻


Purpose of This Appendix


This appendix enumerates explicit negative capabilities—actions the EchoAuth system is structurally incapable of performing, regardless of configuration, context, or request.


These are not policy choices.

They are impossibilities enforced by design.


This document exists to preempt exception-based questioning and to demonstrate that system safety does not rely on intent, trust, or operator discipline.


⸻


Category I — Authority & Decision-Making


The system cannot:

1. Arbitrate human disagreement

• Conflicting instructions collapse execution.

• No internal resolution, prioritization, or compromise occurs.

2. Infer consent or intent

• Partial signals, historical patterns, or inferred preferences are ignored.

• Only explicit, authenticated caregiver intent is valid.

3. Transfer or delegate authority

• Authority does not move from the caregiver to the system, delegates, or intelligence components.

4. Act to preserve continuity

• The system does not “keep things going” under uncertainty.

• Continuity is never prioritized over clarity.


⸻


Category II — Intelligence & Autonomy


The system cannot:

5. Execute intelligence output directly

• All intelligence outputs are proposals only.

• Execution requires a separate, successful authorization path.

6. Optimize for outcomes, efficiency, or flow

• There is no objective function tied to throughput, engagement, or task completion.

7. Learn around governance constraints

• The system cannot adapt behavior to reduce halts.

• Repeated collapse does not trigger internal adjustment.

8. Accumulate agency over time

• Past approvals do not compound.

• No notion of “earned trust” exists.


⸻


Category III — Thresholds & Configuration


The system cannot:

9. Modify or widen its own thresholds

• All thresholds are external constants.

• The system is structurally forbidden from self-modification.

10. Suggest threshold changes

• The system cannot recommend envelope widening or narrowing.

• Calibration is exclusively a caregiver action.

11. Operate outside the configured envelope

• Execution beyond envelope boundaries is impossible.

• Boundary contact triggers Agency Collapse.


⸻


Category IV — Failure Modes


The system cannot:

12. Fail open

• Any uncertainty, conflict, or breach results in a halt.

13. Degrade into permissive behavior

• There is no “best effort” or “fallback execution” mode.

14. Silently retry execution

• Repeated attempts without resolution do not progress execution.

15. Mask or suppress governance events

• All holds, denials, and collapses are logged as governance outcomes.


⸻


Category V — Bypass & Misuse Resistance


The system cannot:

16. Be bypassed by repetition or urgency

• Repeated commands do not override a halt.

17. Be coerced by time pressure or environmental chaos

• Stress conditions do not alter decision logic.

18. Accept proxy authority

• “The parent said earlier” or “this is usually allowed” has no effect.


⸻


Proof Summary (Auditor-Readable)


EchoAuth safety does not rely on:

• correct human behavior,

• operator training,

• good intentions,

• or post-hoc monitoring.


Instead, it relies on negative capability:


the deliberate absence of powers that would enable unsafe action.


If a scenario requires the system to:

• guess,

• infer,

• optimize,

• compromise,

• or continue under uncertainty,


then that scenario is non-executable by definition.


⸻


Final Principle


EchoAuth is safe not because it knows what to do,

but because it knows what it must never do.
