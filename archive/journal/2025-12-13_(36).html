HOSTILE Q&A — ROUND 2


Wearables · Privacy · Data Use


⸻


👪 PARENTS / GUARDIANS


Q1: “Is the wearable tracking my child all the time?”


Answer:

No. The wearable does not continuously track behavior or psychology. It only reflects the system’s current operational state and limited safety context.


⸻


Q2: “What data does the wearable actually collect?”


Answer:

Only minimal, non-diagnostic signals needed for context, such as movement or time-in-zone. No emotional, cognitive, or behavioral profiling.


⸻


Q3: “Can the wearable tell when my child is stressed or upset?”


Answer:

No. The system does not detect or infer emotional or mental states.


⸻


Q4: “What happens if my child takes the wearable off?”


Answer:

Nothing negative. The system continues safely in observation mode. No capability is lost, and nothing escalates.


⸻


Q5: “Can the wearable influence what happens next?”


Answer:

No. Wearables cannot authorize, trigger, or advance system actions.


⸻


🏛️ REGULATORS / PRIVACY OFFICERS


Q6: “Is this a form of surveillance?”


Answer:

No. Data is limited, purpose-bound, and not used for monitoring behavior, prediction, or scoring.


⸻


Q7: “Do you store biometric or health data?”


Answer:

No diagnostic or medical data is collected. Data is not used for health assessment or treatment.


⸻


Q8: “Is data shared with third parties?”


Answer:

No data is sold or shared outside approved operational and audit requirements.


⸻


Q9: “How long is data retained?”


Answer:

Only as long as required for safety review, auditing, or compliance, then securely archived or deleted.


⸻


Q10: “What happens in a data breach scenario?”


Answer:

Because data is minimal and non-diagnostic, risk is limited. Breach response defaults to system lockdown and audit review.


⸻


💰 GRANT REVIEWERS / INSTITUTIONS


Q11: “Why use wearables at all?”


Answer:

Wearables provide passive state awareness and reduce reliance on verbal instruction or constant staff intervention.


⸻


Q12: “Couldn’t wearables become coercive?”


Answer:

Not in this design. Wearables have no authority and no behavioral pressure mechanisms.


⸻


Q13: “Is consent required?”


Answer:

Yes. Wearable use is optional and consent-based. The system functions safely without them.


⸻


Q14: “Is this compliant with child data protection norms?”


Answer:

Yes. The system uses data minimization, purpose limitation, and explicit consent principles.


⸻


🔒 HARD QUESTIONS (DON’T DODGE THESE)


Q15: “Why not collect more data if it could help?”


Answer:

Because more data increases risk. Safety here comes from restraint, not accumulation.


⸻


Q16: “How do you prove you’re not inferring emotion?”


Answer:

By architecture. There is no module designed to infer emotion, and no data pathway that would allow it.


⸻


Q17: “Can wearables be repurposed later for control?”


Answer:

Not without redesign. Authority boundaries are enforced at the system level.


⸻


ONE-LINE WEARABLE CLOSE


The wearable informs awareness; it never drives outcomes.
