NIH Cover Letter (Non-Clinical Infrastructure Framing)


To the Review Committee,


We respectfully submit EchoAuth for consideration as a non-clinical, child-first care coordination and access platform designed to support autistic and non-verbal children across home, school, and clinical environments.


EchoAuth addresses a persistent infrastructure gap: children with communication barriers are supported by multiple adults, yet existing systems rely on verbal authentication, fragmented records, or clinical interpretation. EchoAuth does not diagnose, prescribe, or deliver medical treatment. Instead, it provides a safe coordination layer that records observations, stores clinician-provided documents, tracks caregiver-entered schedules, issues factual reminders, and pauses on uncertainty.


The platform enforces parental authority as a system invariant. All professional input is advisory until approved by a parent or guardian, and all actions affecting the child are logged, auditable, and non-interpretive. Children access the system through a calm, non-verbal interface that includes sensory-safe and music-based engagement, without testing, scoring, or labeling.


EchoAuth aligns with NIH priorities related to family-centered care coordination, caregiver burden reduction, accessibility, and ethical deployment of supportive technologies. The system is designed for evaluation and pilot use without regulatory overreach or clinical claims.


Thank you for your consideration.


Respectfully,

EchoAuth Team

NSF Cover Letter (Accessibility & Socio-Technical Systems Framing)


To the Review Committee,


We submit EchoAuth for consideration as an accessibility-focused socio-technical system designed to support autistic and non-verbal children through safe access, role-bounded collaboration, and auditable coordination.


EchoAuth addresses a structural limitation in current digital systems: most assume verbal communication, executive function, and traditional authentication. EchoAuth replaces these assumptions with a child-first access interface, parent-governed authority controls, and a contribution model that enables caregivers, schools, and clinicians to support children without authority confusion.


The system emphasizes pause-on-uncertainty safety behavior, least-privilege access, and non-interpretive logging. It records what happens, who acted, and when—without diagnosis, optimization, or behavioral scoring. These design choices support ethical scaling and reproducibility across public and institutional settings.


EchoAuth demonstrates broader impacts in accessibility, inclusion, and responsible system design, offering infrastructure that can be evaluated, adapted, and deployed without increasing risk to vulnerable users.


Thank you for your review.


Sincerely,

EchoAuth Team

Foundation / Autism-Focused Grant Cover Letter (Family & Impact Framing)


To the Grants Committee,


We are pleased to submit EchoAuth for consideration as a child-first coordination platform designed to support autistic and non-verbal children and the families and professionals who care for them.
