⭐ PATENT SECTION 7 — HIGH-LEVEL SYSTEM OVERVIEW


(Clear, formal, engineering-grade patent language)


The EchoAuth system is a multi-layer neuroadaptive computing platform designed to interpret behavioral, sensory, emotional, environmental, and medical signals produced by nonverbal or neurodivergent individuals. The system integrates hardware sensors, software modules, predictive analytics, and hybrid NI–AI logic to generate real-time assessments and adaptive interventions.


At a high level, the system is composed of the following interconnected subsystems:


⸻


⭐ 7.1 Sensor Acquisition Layer (SAL)


The system collects multi-modal inputs through:

• audio sensors (noise levels, sound spikes)

• optical/motion sensors (movement, pacing, stimming patterns)

• light/brightness sensors (environmental illumination)

• wearable or device-based physiological metrics (optional)

• manual caregiver inputs (behaviors, notes, context)

• environment metadata (home, school, community classification)


These signals form the raw data foundation of the platform.


⸻


⭐ 7.2 Pre-Processing and Signal Categorization Layer (PSCL)


Incoming data is normalized, segmented, or filtered into standardized categories:

• Behavioral Signals (movement, posture, stimming)

• Sensory Signals (noise, light, motion crowding)

• Emotional Indicators (pace changes, breath patterns, facial cues)

• Medical Risk Indicators (fatigue patterns, dehydration patterns)

• Environment Context (location type, risk factors, transitions)


This layer removes noise and prepares data for predictive modeling.


⸻


⭐ 7.3 NI–AI Fusion Kernel (F.I.K) — Core Intelligence Layer


The F.I.K serves as the central decision-making logic of the system. It integrates:

• AI models (predictive classifiers, sequence models, sensory risk mapping)

• NI heuristics (caregiver rules, personalized overrides, child-specific patterns)

• Fusion Weighting Logic (trust distribution between NI and AI)


This kernel outputs the interpreted state of the user and selects appropriate interventions or alerts.


⸻


⭐ 7.4 Predictive Engine Suite (PES)


A set of predictive subsystems operating within the F.I.K:

• Behavior Prediction Engine (BPE)

• Sensory Overload Detection Engine (SODE)

• Nonverbal Expression Decoding Engine (NEDE)

• Emotional Regulation Engine (ERE)

• Medical Early Warning Engine (MEWE)

• Multi-Environment Safety Engine (MESE)


Each engine contributes specialized predictions to form a unified, multi-dimensional understanding of the user’s condition.


⸻


⭐ 7.5 Adaptive Output & Intervention Layer (AOIL)


Based on predictions generated by the F.I.K, the system produces:

• real-time alerts

• adaptive music interventions

• sensory risk warnings

• emotional state summaries

• environment-sensitive recommendations

• medical early warning prompts

• caregiver action guidance

• analytics for clinicians and educators


Outputs adjust according to context, urgency, and NI–AI weighting.


⸻


⭐ 7.6 User Interface & Ecosystem Layer (UI/UXL)


EchoAuth delivers interpreted states and interventions through a suite of interfaces:

• Caregiver Dashboard (mobile app)

• School Safety Portal (classroom dashboard)

• Clinical Insight Module (medical interpretation panel)

• Wearable/Toy Interface (child-facing device)

• Cloud Sync Layer (optional, with strict privacy controls)


Each interface is tailored to the environment’s sensory, operational, and ethical constraints.


⸻


⭐ 7.7 Data Management & Privacy Layer (DMPL)


The system implements:

• local-first data processing

• optional encrypted cloud backup

• NI-controlled privacy permissions

• no facial recognition

• no external biometric sharing

• encrypted device-to-device communication

• caregiver-defined access controls


Designed so the child’s safety and dignity remain central.


⸻


⭐ 7.8 Multi-Environment Transition Framework (METF)


The system maintains state continuity as the user moves between:

• home

• transportation

• school

• community

• medical environments


The transition framework ensures:

• state persistence

• context-aware predictions

• environment-specific overload thresholds

• adaptive tuning of engines during transitions


⸻


⭐ 7.9 System Feedback & Learning Layer (SFLL)


Over time, the system refines its predictions by:

• analyzing historical patterns

• integrating caregiver corrections

• adjusting NI–AI weighting

• recalibrating sensory thresholds

• recognizing new behavioral signatures


This enables personalized, long-term accuracy.


⸻


⭐ 7.10 Summary of the System Architecture


EchoAuth operates as a multi-layer fusion architecture, transforming raw nonverbal signals into meaningful, interpretable guidance for caregivers, educators, and clinicians. Its unique integration of:

• sensor fusion,

• predictive analytics,

• NI–AI hybrid intelligence,

• adaptive interventions, and

• multi-environment continuity


makes it the first neuroadaptive system capable of supporting nonverbal and neurodivergent individuals across all domains of daily life.
