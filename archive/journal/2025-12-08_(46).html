🔥 1. Grant Overview & Positioning

EchoAuth is a secure, NI-AI-powered neuro-support platform for autistic and neurodivergent individuals, addressing communication barriers, identity authentication, and sensory needs. By integrating adaptive AI with child-safe protocols, EchoAuth empowers users to express intentions, confirm safety, and engage in educational/clinical settings — reducing isolation and enhancing independence.

This grant pack targets:

• SBIR/STTR Phase I: Proof-of-concept/prototype refinement (NIH: Autism/mental health; NSF: Assistive tech/AI; DoEd/IES: Ed-tech for disabilities).

• NIH-Specific: Autism Data Science Initiative or R03/R21 for communication tools (up to $275k).

• NSF-Specific: Science of Learning/Augmented Intelligence or Rehabilitation Research programs.

• NIDILRR/ACL: Disability innovation grants for assistive tech (up to $200k Phase I).

• Foundation Grants: ARI (up to $150k research) or Autism Speaks (family/tech support, up to $50k).

Eligibility: HeliosFi LLC qualifies as a small business (<500 employees), U.S.-based, with founder owning >50%. No prior federal grants needed for Phase I.

🔥 2. Grant Purpose & Use of Funds (Tailored Abstract — Copy/Paste for Applications)

SBIR Phase I Abstract (NIH/NSF/DoEd — 200–250 words max):

EchoAuth proposes a Phase I SBIR to develop and validate a novel NI-AI neuro-support system for autistic and non-verbal children, enabling secure identity authentication and structured communication in educational and clinical environments. Current tools lack personalization, sensory adaptation, and intention-reading capabilities, leading to communication breakdowns and safety risks for 1 in 36 U.S. children with ASD (CDC data).

Our innovation: A mobile platform with adaptive NI-AI layers for real-time need expression, emotional state confirmation, and caregiver/teacher integration — compliant with FERPA, HIPAA, and COPPA. Phase I will refine the prototype (75% complete), conduct usability testing with 20 ASD users/clinicians, and prepare for school pilots.

Funds ($275,000 requested):

• Prototype optimization + NI-AI core ($100,000)

• Sensory/accessibility testing ($50,000)

• Compliance + patent advancement ($40,000)

• Pilot recruitment + data collection ($50,000)

• Project management/evaluation ($35,000)

Outcomes: Validated MVP with efficacy metrics (e.g., 30% improved communication accuracy). This positions EchoAuth for Phase II scaling, federal pilots, and commercialization — advancing NIH/NSF goals for neurodivergent equity and AI-assisted independence.

Adaptations:

• For NIH Autism Focus: Emphasize genomic/clinical integration for personalized AI.

• For NSF: Highlight AI learning algorithms for neurodiversity.

• For NIDILRR: Stress disability empowerment and tech accessibility.
