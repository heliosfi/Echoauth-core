Appendix D — Child Portal Program Standards


(ASD-Safe Criteria, Parent-Governed, Non-Interpretive)


Purpose


Define what programs are allowed in the Child Access Interface and how they are constrained, so the child experience is safe, calming, predictable, and scalable across environments.


⸻


D1. Program Categories (Allowed)


Child-facing programs must fall into one of the following categories:

1. Sensory Regulation

• visual patterns (slow, predictable)

• gentle motion

• light color transitions

2. Music & Soundscapes

• rhythmic, predictable audio

• instrumental or non-lyrical

• controllable volume

3. Guided Calm & Pause

• breathing cues (visual/audio)

• stillness modes

• quiet timers

4. Simple Engagement

• cause-and-effect interactions

• repetition-friendly activities

• no competitive elements


Rule: Programs support regulation and comfort, not performance.


⸻


D2. Explicitly Disallowed Program Traits


Programs are not permitted if they include:

• ads or external links

• fast flashing visuals

• sudden audio spikes

• competitive scoring

• countdown pressure

• variable reward loops

• social comparison

• time-limited “wins”

• behavioral conditioning mechanics


Invariant:


No program may manipulate, rush, or pressure the child.


⸻


D3. Flame Selves Usage (State Pathways Only)


Flame Selves (Ashe’to, Se’tuno, Oridu’Kai, No’rekai, Ubuntu) are used as:

• interaction pathways

• preference groupings

• state markers


They are never used as:

• ASD “levels”

• severity indicators

• developmental rankings

• progress scores


Grant-safe framing:


Flame Selves indicate how a child engages, not how a child is classified.


⸻


D4. Progress & Engagement Tracking (Non-Clinical)


The system may record:

• session start/end

• program selected

• duration of engagement

• transition patterns


The system never records:

• success/failure

• improvement scores

• comparative metrics

• behavioral judgments


Progress = continuity + comfort, not evaluation.


⸻


D5. Parent Governance of Programs


Parents/guardians control:

• which programs are enabled

• which Flame pathways are available

• time windows (optional)

• immediate disable at any time


No program is visible to the child unless explicitly enabled by a parent.


⸻


D6. Professional Visibility (Read-Only)


If authorized, school/clinic staff may view:

• engagement summaries

• usage trends

• preferred pathways


They cannot:

• enable/disable programs

• alter child settings

• infer diagnosis

• label behavior


⸻


D7. Child Experience Rules (Non-Negotiable)

• No alerts directed at the child

• No error states

• No “try again” language

• No prompts tied to compliance

• Exit always available


The system adapts around the child’s state.


⸻


D8. Logging & Evidence Alignment


All program activity logs feed into:

• Development & Observation Logs

• Task & Outcome Logs

• De-identified Reporting


Logged as engagement events only.

No interpretation layer is added.


⸻


Appendix D Seal


Child portal programs in EchoAuth are limited to ASD-safe sensory, music, and engagement experiences governed by parents, free of scoring or pressure, and tracked solely for continuity and coordination purposes.
