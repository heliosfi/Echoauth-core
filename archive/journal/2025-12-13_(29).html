BOARD / INVESTOR Q&A


Non-Verbal First · Safety Architecture · Sustainable Model


⸻


🎯 STRATEGY & DIFFERENTIATION


Q1: “What exactly are we building?”


Answer:

A safety-first control infrastructure that enables non-verbal interaction by removing the need to speak, explain, or perform—while preserving human oversight and auditability.


⸻


Q2: “How is this different from AAC or behavioral tools?”


Answer:

AAC translates speech; behavioral tools optimize behavior. We remove pressure entirely. Silence, stillness, and non-participation are valid signals and outcomes.


⸻


Q3: “Why is non-verbal the core, not a feature?”


Answer:

Because forcing verbalization introduces risk, bias, and harm. Our architecture listens without requiring speech.


⸻


🧠 TECHNOLOGY & SAFETY


Q4: “Where is the AI value if you don’t predict?”


Answer:

AI provides explanation and transparency—not control. The value is deterministic restraint that prevents unsafe action.


⸻


Q5: “Isn’t this too conservative to be innovative?”


Answer:

Innovation here is architectural: making non-action enforceable and auditable in sensitive environments.


⸻


Q6: “What prevents misuse or drift over time?”


Answer:

Authority separation, default-deny execution, and audit logs. Any failure collapses capability back to observation.


⸻


💰 BUSINESS MODEL


Q7: “Who pays?”


Answer:

Caregivers pay a one-time onboarding fee. Families access via subscription, with institutional coverage and invisible charity subsidies to ensure equity.


⸻


Q8: “Why not usage-based pricing?”


Answer:

Usage-based pricing incentivizes pressure. Our model avoids incentives to activate or escalate.


⸻


Q9: “Is the revenue model scalable?”


Answer:

Yes. It scales with institutions and access pools—not with increased pressure on families.


⸻


🏫 MARKET & ADOPTION


Q10: “Who buys first?”


Answer:

Schools, districts, clinics, and agencies serving non-verbal populations—often via pilots funded by grants or institutions.


⸻


Q11: “What’s the pilot success metric?”


Answer:

Correct refusal to act under uncertainty, clean logs, and calm handling of non-verbal interaction.


⸻


Q12: “What if competitors add prediction and move faster?”


Answer:

Speed without safety isn’t our benchmark. Regulators and institutions reward restraint in sensitive contexts.


⸻


⚖️ RISK & GOVERNANCE


Q13: “What’s the liability profile?”


Answer:

Lower than predictive systems. We avoid behavioral inference and autonomous escalation; responsibility is traceable via logs.


⸻


Q14: “How do we defend this in diligence?”


Answer:

With architecture: default-deny, bounded authorization, human stop at all times, and explicit non-verbal safeguards.


⸻


🧩 ROADMAP (CONTROLLED)


Q15: “What’s next after pilots?”


Answer:

Institutional contracts, expanded access pools, and broader deployments—without changing authority or adding prediction.
