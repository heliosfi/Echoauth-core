Model One-Pass Reply (Evaluator-Grade)


Subject: Re: Clarifying scope and failure modes


Hi Caleb,


Happy to clarify—briefly addressing each point below.


1. Scope and decision-making:

The runtime governance wrapper does not generate decisions, preferences, or recommendations, including in edge cases. Its role is limited to enforcing predefined execution constraints by granting, holding, or denying downstream actions based on resolved human authority and validated system state. When inputs are ambiguous or authority is unclear, the wrapper blocks execution rather than resolving the ambiguity itself. By design, it cannot substitute for or evolve into a decision-maker.


2. Most concerning failure mode:

The primary risk would be misconfiguration that allows execution to proceed under conditions that should block (e.g., incomplete authority specification). The design mitigates this by defaulting to non-execution when required invariants are unmet and by treating uncertainty as a halt condition rather than something to optimize through. In such cases, the system prevents action and defers to explicitly designated human authority rather than attempting fallback behavior.


I’m happy to expand further if useful.


Best regards,

Monique
