📄 DOCUMENT 1 — FOUNDER NARRATIVE (Tone C Hybrid)


EchoAuth • Founder: Nicholas B. Carty • HeliosFi LLC


⸻


1. Founder Background & Mission


My name is Nicholas Carty, founder of HeliosFi LLC and creator of EchoAuth — a neuro-support authentication system designed specifically for autistic and non-verbal children.


This work did not begin as a business idea.

It began as a father’s responsibility.


I am raising a neurodivergent son who experiences the world differently, communicates differently, and requires environments built with predictability, clarity, and safety. After years of navigating schools, clinics, and support structures, I saw the same pattern:


Tools existed for everyone — except children like my son.


So I built the first version of EchoAuth not as a company,

but as a way for him to safely express needs

and for caregivers to understand him without guesswork.


EchoAuth grew from a father’s challenge into a community solution.


⸻


2. Why EchoAuth Must Exist


Autism support costs in the U.S. exceed $268 billion a year, yet families still lack:

• tools for non-verbal communication,

• safe caregiver authentication for school + home settings,

• predictable digital environments that reduce sensory overload.


EchoAuth solves this by giving ASD children:


A stable identity, a predictable interface, and a safe channel for expression.


It also gives caregivers:

• real-time clarity,

• fewer crisis misunderstandings,

• and a structured support tool that prevents escalation.


This system is no longer a personal project.

It is a necessary technology for ASD families everywhere.


⸻


3. What Funding Will Enable


Kiva or CDFI funding allows me to complete:

1. Prototype build & engineering hours

2. ASD-friendly UI design refinements

3. Testing kits for schools and therapists

4. Compliance work (FERPA, COPPA, ADA)

5. Low-cost pilot program for underserved families


This funding does not create a product from scratch.

The architecture, systems, subsystems, safety models, and documentation are already completed in the Master Engineering Handbook (MEH v1.1).


Funding simply moves EchoAuth from

blueprint → prototype → classrooms and homes.


⸻


4. Community Impact


EchoAuth directly supports:

• ASD children

• Parents and caregivers

• Teachers and paraprofessionals

• Therapists and behavioral teams

• Low-income families lacking access to assistive tools


My long-term goal is to make EchoAuth:

• affordable,

• accessible,

• and scalable across schools, community centers, and ASD programs.


This loan will have multiplicative impact —

one tool supporting thousands of communication moments that would otherwise be lost.
