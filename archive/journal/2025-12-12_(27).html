DOCUMENT 4


MCG 3.0 — “NO SURPRISES” LOCK SHEET


Scope Lock | Safety Lock | Authority Lock


⸻


Purpose of This Document


This document defines explicit boundaries for the Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) system.


Its purpose is to ensure:

• no scope creep

• no misinterpretation

• no unsafe expansion

• no future contradiction


If a feature or behavior conflicts with anything below, it is out of scope.


⸻


1. What the System Will NEVER Do


MCG will never:

• diagnose medical, psychological, or neurological conditions

• infer emotions, intentions, or mental states

• label behavior or assign meaning to behavior

• operate autonomously

• self-modify its rules or governance logic

• override human caregivers, clinicians, or supervisors

• act without explicit permission

• replace human judgment

• learn from users without explicit authorization

• create persistent psychological profiles

• attempt to simulate consciousness or awareness


⸻


2. What the System Is NOT


For clarity and compliance, MCG is not:

• a chatbot

• a therapeutic device

• a decision-maker

• an autonomous AI agent

• a diagnostic tool

• a consciousness model

• a belief system

• a behavioral classifier


Any interpretation of MCG as the above is incorrect.


⸻


3. Authority Hierarchy (LOCKED)


At all times, authority flows in this order:


Human → Governance Rules → System → Output


The system has no authority to:

• initiate irreversible actions

• override humans

• expand its mandate


If authority is unclear, the system must pause and escalate.


⸻


4. Default Safety Behaviors


Under any of the following conditions:

• ambiguity

• signal conflict

• overload

• sensor failure

• internal fault

• unclear permissions


The system will:

1. Deny or pause action

2. Notify a human

3. Log the event

4. Await instruction


There are no exceptions.


⸻


5. Learning & Adaptation Constraints


MCG may only adapt if:

• adaptation is explicitly enabled

• scope is narrowly defined

• safety boundaries are unchanged

• human oversight is present


Otherwise:

• learning is disabled

• memory is session-bound

• rules remain static


⸻


6. Expansion Policy


Any future expansion must:

• preserve the 3-layer architecture

• respect Identity Kernel immutability

• maintain Governance Halo veto power

• pass safety and ethical review

• be documented separately


Expansion without review is prohibited.


⸻


7. Audit & Accountability


All system actions must be:

• logged

• reviewable

• explainable

• attributable


No silent behavior is permitted.


⸻


8. Final Lock Statement


This document represents a hard boundary.


If a feature, request, or implementation proposal violates any section above, it is rejected by design.
