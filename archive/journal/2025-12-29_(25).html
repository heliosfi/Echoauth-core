EchoAuth — Caregiver Portal


Canonical Explanation (Execution-Focused)


The EchoAuth Caregiver Portal exists to support execution, not decision-making.


It enables approved caregivers to assist a child within parent-defined boundaries, without altering rules, interpreting behavior, or introducing inconsistency. The portal ensures help is delivered consistently, calmly, and accountably.


⸻


Purpose of the Caregiver Portal


The Caregiver Portal is designed to solve a coordination problem:


When multiple adults support a child, inconsistency creates stress and risk. EchoAuth prevents this by ensuring all caregivers operate from the same approved rules, regardless of who is present.


The portal does not assess, diagnose, or guide behavior.

It exists solely to carry out approved support actions.


⸻


What Caregivers Can Do


Caregivers may:

• Assist the child in using the EchoAuth Child Portal

• Support access to parent-approved content or activities

• Follow established routines and permissions

• Help the child complete simple confirmation actions when needed

• Observe outcomes without interpretation or modification


All actions are bounded by parent-defined policy.


⸻


What Caregivers Cannot Do


Caregivers cannot:

• Change access rules or permissions

• Override parent or guardian policies

• Introduce new routines, content, or modes

• Analyze or label behavior

• Access data beyond their assigned role


The system does not allow hidden overrides or silent changes.


⸻


Role and Authority Model

• Parents / Legal Guardians define all rules, permissions, and boundaries

• Caregivers operate strictly within assigned roles

• EchoAuth enforces policy automatically and consistently


Authority does not shift based on who is present.

Execution remains stable.


⸻


Safety, Consistency, and Accountability

• If a request falls outside approved rules, access pauses automatically

• No caregiver is required to “decide” in ambiguous situations

• Every access attempt is logged with:

• time

• role

• action

• outcome


This protects the child, the caregiver, and the family.


⸻


Important Boundaries


The Caregiver Portal is:

• Not clinical

• Not diagnostic

• Not therapeutic

• Not behavioral analysis


It is an execution interface, governed by parental authority.


⸻


Core Principle (Lock This)


Caregivers support the child.

Parents define the rules.

EchoAuth enforces consistency.
