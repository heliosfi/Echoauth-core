⭐ PAGE 21 — IRB & ETHICS PROTOCOL (Human Subjects Protection Plan)


EchoAuth involves structured interaction with autistic and non-verbal individuals in educational, clinical, and home environments. Because the project includes the potential for human subjects research during prototype validation, an IRB-compliant ethics protocol is required. The following framework aligns with NIH, NSF, NIDILRR, and academic IRB standards.


⸻


21.1 Study Purpose


The purpose of this study is to evaluate the usability, communication accuracy, safety, and stress-reduction potential of the EchoAuth neuro-support system for autistic and non-verbal children and young adults.


The study does NOT aim to alter clinical diagnosis, modify therapy outcomes, or replace professional judgment.

It only assesses:

• communication clarity

• user comfort

• identity confirmation reliability

• system predictability

• caregiver/clinician satisfaction


This is strictly an assistive technology evaluation, not a medical trial.


⸻


21.2 Study Population


Participants (Expected N = 20–30)


Participants will include:

• Autistic children (ages 6–17)

• Autistic young adults (ages 18–25)

• Non-verbal or low-verbal individuals

• Individuals with sensory processing challenges


Participation is voluntary and requires informed consent from parents/guardians and assent from the participant when appropriate.


Exclusion Criteria

• Users with acute medical instability

• Users currently in crisis situations

• Any individual whose clinician determines participation is unsuitable


This ensures ethical protection and appropriate participant selection.


⸻


21.3 Ethical Safeguards


Informed Consent & Assent


All participants (or guardians) will receive:

• A plain-language study description

• Details about potential risks and benefits

• A clear explanation that participation is optional

• Permission to withdraw at ANY time without consequence


Consent forms will follow IRB templates and will be reviewed prior to study launch.


Participant Safety


EchoAuth’s safety logic includes:

• Fail-closed behavior (system pauses when uncertain)

• Low-sensory mode to minimize overstimulation

• Guardian/clinician override for all functions

• Zero-risk data pathways (no external sharing, no adaptive learning on user identity)


Risk Assessment


Risk Level: Minimal Risk

Comparable to using a structured AAC communication device or educational learning app.


Privacy Protection

• No biometric data collected

• All data anonymized before analysis

• Data stored in encrypted environments

• No cross-user data training


These protections meet IRB, HIPAA, FERPA, and COPPA guidelines.


⸻


21.4 Study Design & Procedures


Session Structure


Each participant will complete 2–4 short sessions (10–20 minutes each) in a familiar environment:

• Home

• Clinic

• School classroom

• Therapy room


Procedures Include:

1. Participant uses EchoAuth to express needs or identify objects/emotions

2. Caregiver or clinician verifies prompt accuracy

3. System logs communication attempts and responses

4. Observers rate clarity, stress level, and ease of use

5. Optional sensory-mode adjustments tested


Measured Outcomes (Non-Clinical Metrics)

• Communication accuracy rate

• Time-to-expression (latency reduction)

• User stress or discomfort indicators

• Caregiver confidence ratings

• Identity confirmation consistency


These outcomes serve assistive research purposes ONLY.


⸻


21.5 Data Collection & Management


Data Types Collected

• Anonymous interaction logs

• Communication choices (non-identifiable)

• Session timestamps

• Caregiver/clinician feedback

• Optional short surveys


Data NOT Collected

• Biometrics

• Facial recognition

• GPS/location

• Audio/video recordings (unless explicitly approved by guardian and IRB)


Data Storage

• Encrypted local or secure cloud environment

• Access limited to research personnel

• Destroyed after the study period per IRB rules


⸻


21.6 Confidentiality


All data will be coded with participant IDs and stored separately from consent forms. No identifiable information will appear in any report, publication, or presentation.


EchoAuth never shares data with third parties.


Reports will only present aggregated results.


⸻


21.7 Potential Benefits


Participants may experience:

• Improved ability to express needs

• Reduced frustration due to communication difficulties

• Increased independence

• Enhanced caregiver understanding


Broader societal benefits include:

• Improved ASD communication methodologies

• Insights into non-verbal intention mapping

• Strengthening the ecosystem of assistive technologies


⸻


21.8 Withdrawal Rights


Participants may withdraw at any time with no penalty.


If withdrawal occurs:

• All related data will be immediately deleted

• No additional follow-up required


This aligns with ethical standards for vulnerable populations.


⸻


21.9 IRB Oversight & Reporting


This study will be submitted to:

• A partnering academic IRB

or

• An accredited independent IRB specializing in autism and pediatric research


Before data collection:

• Protocol approval

• Consent/assent form approval

• Safety procedures approval


During the study:

• Periodic monitoring

• Reportable event protocol

• Final study summary submission


EchoAuth complies fully with IRB expectations for assistive technology involving minors.
