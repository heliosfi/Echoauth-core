Child Portal Enhancement — Sensory, Music & Program Layer


(ASD-Safe, Non-Clinical, Parent-Governed)


What this layer is


A regulated sensory and engagement environment inside the Child Access Interface, designed to support:

• regulation

• comfort

• engagement

• developmental continuity


It is not therapy, not diagnosis, not scoring.


⸻


How it integrates (no structural change)


This enhancement sits inside Stage 3 + Stage 9, and logs through Stages 11–18.


Nothing new replaces anything.

This is an extension, not a rewrite.


⸻


Component A — Sensory & Music Modules (Child-Facing)


The child portal may include pre-approved, ASD-safe modules, such as:

• music (calming, rhythmic, predictable)

• soundscapes

• visual patterns

• sensor-based interactions (touch, timing, motion — device dependent)

• guided calm modes


Rules:

• No ads

• No variable reward hooks

• No overstimulation

• No sudden transitions


All modules are:

• selected or enabled by parents

• delivered passively to the child

• optional and interruptible


⸻


Component B — Flame Selves as State Markers, Not Labels


Flame Selves (Ashe’to, Se’tuno, Oridu’Kai, No’rekai, Ubuntu) are used as:

• state indicators

• preference groupings

• engagement pathways


They are not:

• ASD severity labels

• diagnostic categories

• progress scores


Important framing (grant-safe):


Flame Selves represent interaction modes, not clinical classifications.


⸻


Component C — Progress Tracking (Non-Clinical)


The system may track engagement patterns, such as:

• which programs the child chooses

• duration of engagement

• transitions between modes

• consistency over time


What is never tracked:

• “improvement scores”

• ASD “levels”

• comparisons to other children

• deficit framing


Progress = participation + continuity, not judgment.


⸻


Component D — Parent & Professional Visibility (Read-Only)


Parents, caregivers, and authorized professionals may see:

• engagement summaries

• usage trends

• preferred sensory modes


They cannot:

• infer diagnosis

• see rankings

• alter the child experience without approval


⸻


Component E — Logging & Evidence Alignment


All activity feeds into existing logging stages:

• Stage 13: Development & Observation Logs

• Stage 15: Task & Outcome Logs

• Stage 18: De-Identified Reporting


Logged as:

• “engagement events”

• “sensory interaction sessions”

• “program participation”


No interpretation layer added.


⸻


Safety & Compliance Lock (Non-Negotiable)

• Child portal remains pressure-free

• No alerts directed at the child

• No gamified dependency loops

• No escalation based on behavior

• Parents can disable any module instantly


⸻


One Sentence (Insert Anywhere)


The child access interface may include parent-approved sensory and music-based programs designed for autistic children, with engagement tracked for continuity and coordination purposes only, without diagnosis, scoring, or clinical interpretation.
