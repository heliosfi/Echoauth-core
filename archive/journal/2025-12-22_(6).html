1️⃣ One-Paragraph Executive Version (Grant Reviewers)


Executive Safety & Protection Summary


EchoAuth is a parent-governed coordination and safety infrastructure designed to reduce risk in shared caregiving environments without introducing autonomous decision-making or clinical interpretation. The system enforces parent-approved rules, role-based access control, strong encryption, and immutable audit logging to ensure that caregivers act consistently and within defined boundaries. All processing occurs inside a secured, isolated environment with data minimization, least-privilege access, and continuous integrity monitoring. EchoAuth does not diagnose, predict, or replace human judgment; it functions solely as a stability and coordination layer that protects children, caregivers, and institutions by preventing unauthorized access, misuse, and inconsistency in care delivery.

2️⃣ Parent-Facing Plain-Language Version


(For family materials, school meetings, or community partners)


How EchoAuth Helps Keep Children Safe


EchoAuth is designed to help adults work together correctly when caring for a child. It does not watch, judge, or diagnose children. Instead, parents set the rules, and EchoAuth helps make sure everyone who is helping follows those rules the same way. Only approved caregivers can access the system, and they only see what they are allowed to see. All information is protected with strong security and encryption, and every important action is safely recorded so parents and organizations can review what happened if needed. EchoAuth does not replace parents or caregivers—it simply helps everyone stay aligned, consistent, and safe.

3️⃣ NIST / SSP Control-Mapped Version


(For SSP cross-reference, auditors, and security reviewers)


Protection Mechanisms Aligned to Federal Controls


EchoAuth protects children and caregivers through layered technical and governance controls aligned with NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5. Boundary protection (SC-7) and cryptographic safeguards (SC-12, SC-13) prevent unauthorized access and data exposure. Role-based access control and least privilege (AC-2, AC-3, AC-6) ensure caregivers operate strictly within parent-approved permissions. System integrity monitoring (SI-4) and stability enforcement prevent drift or unsafe system behavior. Data minimization and encrypted storage protect privacy (PM-17), while immutable audit logging (AU-2, AU-6, AU-12) ensures accountability without continuous surveillance. External integrations remain outside the authorization boundary (per NIST SP 800-37), with secure, purpose-limited data exchange. The system performs no diagnosis, prediction, or autonomous decision-making, preserving human authority and regulatory compliance.
