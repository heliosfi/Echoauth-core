PAGE 11 — FEDERAL SECURITY & COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS


NI-AI and EchoAuth can be adopted across federal agencies only if they meet the required security, privacy, audit, and compliance standards.

This page outlines the exact requirements NI-AI satisfies and the ones it is designed to meet during deployment.


⸻


1. Zero-Trust Architecture (ZTA) Compatibility


NI-AI is engineered to operate within Zero-Trust environments:

• Continuous authentication

• Behavioral + emotional + contextual identity checks

• Device and session verification

• Adaptive escalation responses

• No persistent trust at any layer


This aligns with:

• Executive Order 14028 (Cybersecurity Improvement)

• DoD Zero Trust Strategy (2022–2030)


⸻


2. Federal Information Security Modernization Act (FISMA) Controls


NI-AI can map into the following FISMA families:

• AC – Access Control

• IA – Identification & Authentication

• AU – Audit & Accountability

• RA – Risk Assessment

• SC – System & Communications Protection

• CM – Configuration Management


EchoAuth becomes the primary identity and access control enhancement.


⸻


3. NIST Compliance Alignment


NI-AI aligns with these NIST frameworks:

• NIST 800-53 Rev5

(security controls for federal systems)

• NIST 800-63-3 Digital Identity Guidelines

(for adaptive authentication)

• NIST 800-207 Zero Trust Framework

• NIST AI RMF 1.0

(AI risk management and safe model behavior)


NI-AI’s multi-layer coherence engine directly satisfies the “Harm Avoidance” and “Explainability” sections.


⸻


4. FedRAMP Cloud Integration Path


For federal cloud adoption, NI-AI can be deployed on:

• FedRAMP-Moderate cloud

• FedRAMP-High cloud

• On-premise DoD IL4/IL5 compliant servers


NI-AI components needed for FedRAMP integration:

• Audit Logging Module (ALM)

• Access Control Enforcement Module (ACEM)

• Encryption Layer (AES-256 / FIPS 140-3 validated)


⸻


5. Privacy Act & Data Minimization Compatibility


NI-AI’s architecture does not require biometric storage for functionality.


All emotional, behavioral, or resonance data can be:

• non-persistent

• anonymized

• transformed into temporary vectors

• stored only for the session


This satisfies federal privacy and minimization standards:

• 5 U.S.C. §552a (Privacy Act)

• E-Government Act §208

• Minimization principle of NIST AI RMF


⸻


6. Section 508 Accessibility Alignment


EchoAuth + NI-AI support:

• sensory-friendly interaction

• neurodivergent-friendly timing

• reduced cognitive load

• simplified language modes

• adaptive interface pacing


This satisfies:

• Section 508 (Accessibility)

• WCAG 2.1 AA


⸻


7. Audit & Traceability Requirements


The system includes:

• Immutable session logs

• Identity trace maps (non-biometric)

• Coherence score tracking

• Drift probability reports

• Real-time model correction reports


These support federal audits and internal investigations.


⸻


8. Supply Chain Security


NI-AI supports:

• SBOM (Software Bill of Materials)

• Integrity verification

• Model versioning

• Secure deployment pipelines

• CISA supply-chain recommendations
