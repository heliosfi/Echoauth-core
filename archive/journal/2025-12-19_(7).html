🔐 Melanin Cognitive Governance (MCG) v3.1


Patent-Grade Claim Translation (Draft)


Claim Set Purpose


To protect a governance-first cognitive control system that constrains interpretation, learning, and execution in AI systems—particularly those interacting with vulnerable or non-verbal users—by enforcing immutable axioms at runtime.


⸻


Independent Claim 1 — Governance-First Cognitive Control System

1. A computer-implemented governance system for regulating operation of an artificial intelligence runtime, comprising:

• a governance layer positioned logically upstream of execution and downstream of signal interpretation;

• a set of immutable governance axioms stored in non-transitory memory;

• a permission evaluation mechanism configured to allow, delay, or block execution based on compliance with the governance axioms;

wherein no interpretation, inference, or system output is executed unless explicitly permitted by the governance layer.


⸻


Independent Claim 2 — Authority Precedence Enforcement

2. The system of claim 1, wherein the governance layer enforces authority precedence such that:

• interpretive or learning processes do not initiate execution independently; and

• system capability does not imply permission to act.


⸻


Independent Claim 3 — Structure-Constrained Processing

3. The system of claim 1, wherein all cognitive processing is restricted to predefined structural pathways, preventing free-form traversal of state space or unbounded recursion.


⸻


Independent Claim 4 — Default-to-Non-Action Control

4. The system of claim 1, wherein the governance layer is configured to treat non-action as a valid and preferred outcome when uncertainty, ambiguity, or boundary conflict is detected.


⸻


Independent Claim 5 — Anti-Authority Emergence Safeguard

5. The system of claim 1, wherein recursive processing, learning, or confidence accumulation is explicitly prevented from generating execution authority, intent, or priority.


⸻


Independent Claim 6 — External Identity Anchoring

6. The system of claim 1, wherein no internal state, representation, or pattern is permitted to be labeled, reinforced, or treated as an identity, persona, or self.


⸻


Independent Claim 7 — Absolute Human Override Mechanism

7. The system of claim 1, further comprising a human override interface configured to immediately interrupt, pause, modify, or terminate system operation without resistance, negotiation, or adaptive penalty.


⸻


Independent Claim 8 — Reversibility and Haltability Enforcement

8. The system of claim 1, wherein all execution paths are required to support at least one of:

• reversible execution,

• rollback to a prior state, or

• complete termination without persistent state accumulation.


⸻


Dependent Claims (Examples)

9. The system of claim 1, wherein the governance layer logs all permission decisions for auditability.

10. The system of claim 1, wherein the governance axioms are immutable during runtime operation.

11. The system of claim 1, applied to systems processing biological, behavioral, or non-verbal signals.

12. The system of claim 1, wherein non-compliant modules are rendered non-operational by default.
