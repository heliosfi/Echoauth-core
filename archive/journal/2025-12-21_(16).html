1️⃣ Field Intelligence Kernel (FIK) — What It Is


Field Intelligence Kernel (FIK) is the system layer responsible for passive, real-world signals coming from:

• approved wearables

• approved cameras

• environmental sensors (optional)


Key rule:


FIK observes and reports. It does not decide, diagnose, or command.


This keeps EchoAuth non-clinical and safe.


⸻


2️⃣ Wearables — Core Feature (Child Safety & Continuity)


What wearables can provide (examples)

• movement patterns

• sleep/wake cycles

• heart rate variability (optional, device-dependent)

• location safety zones (optional)

• routine consistency signals


What the system does with wearable data

• logs patterns over time

• flags changes (not conclusions)

• supports reminders and check-ins

• feeds the development timeline


What it NEVER does

• no health diagnosis

• no treatment suggestions

• no scoring the child

• no automatic alerts to the child


Authority

• Parent enables or disables wearables

• Parent controls who can see summaries

• Raw data never turns into decisions

3️⃣ Cameras — Core Feature (Environmental Context)


Camera use cases (explicitly scoped)

• safety monitoring in approved spaces

• context for incidents or disruptions

• environmental understanding (noise, crowding, transitions)


Camera rules (non-negotiable)

• parent-controlled activation

• location-specific permissions

• no facial recognition

• no identity inference

• no emotion diagnosis

• no continuous surveillance by default


What cameras provide

• context, not judgment

• time-stamped environmental records

• optional clips tied to logged events
