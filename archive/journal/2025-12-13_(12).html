🚀 HANDOFF PACK — SECTION 8


ECHOAUTH INTEGRATION ROADMAP


From Core Architecture to Deployed Product


Audience: Product leaders, engineers, partners, funders

Goal: Define a clear, safe, staged path to deploy EchoAuth using the NI-AI + MCG stack


⸻


8.1 Purpose of the Roadmap


This roadmap defines how EchoAuth is deployed incrementally while preserving:

• MCG governance

• safety invariants

• role-based identity

• ASD-centered design

• auditability and compliance


Deployment is deliberate, staged, and reversible.


⸻


8.2 Phase I — Core Integration (Foundation)


Objective: Bind EchoAuth to the NI-AI core safely.


Actions:

• Integrate EchoAuth authentication logic as a domain module

• Bind caregiver–child roles to MCG-governed identity scopes

• Enforce S1–S7 (identity, intent, stability) on all interactions

• Disable all non-essential features


Outputs:

• Role-verified caregiver–child pairing

• Stable non-verbal interaction baseline

• MCG-enforced execution gates


⸻


8.3 Phase II — Pilot Deployment (Controlled Environments)


Objective: Validate EchoAuth in real settings without scale risk.


Targets:

• Single school

• Single clinic

• Small caregiver organization


Controls:

• Limited user count

• Manual update approval

• Real-time observability

• Immediate termination capability


Outputs:

• Safety validation

• Caregiver usability feedback

• Institutional readiness signals


⸻


8.4 Phase III — Institutional Subscription Rollout


Objective: Expand into schools and clinics under contract.


Actions:

• Deploy under institutional subscription agreements

• Maintain caregiver free access

• Enable audit and reporting features

• Enforce identical MCG constraints across sites


Outputs:

• Predictable revenue

• Stable institutional adoption

• Compliance-ready deployments


⸻


8.5 Phase IV — Parent Access & Subsidy Enablement


Objective: Extend access without exclusion.


Actions:

• Enable optional parent subscriptions

• Integrate government subsidy pathways

• Support charitable sponsorship models

• Enforce privacy and non-monetization rules


Outputs:

• Equitable access

• Family continuity

• Public-sector alignment


⸻


8.6 Phase V — Scale & Replication


Objective: Replicate EchoAuth safely across regions and hardware.


Actions:

• Deploy across cloud, edge, and device environments

• Enforce MCG on all instances

• Use integrity-verified updates only

• Reject non-compliant environments


Outputs:

• Multi-site consistency

• Zero drift across deployments

• Scalable yet controlled growth


⸻


8.7 Phase VI — Cross-System Expansion (Optional)


Objective: Extend architecture without risk.


Actions:

• Integrate with other NI-AI–derived systems (e.g., FLAIMO)

• Share governance and safety infrastructure

• Prevent cross-contamination of scope or data


Outputs:

• Ecosystem growth

• Shared governance

• Maintained product integrity


⸻


8.8 Risk Management & Safeguards


At every phase:

• MCG remains the final authority

• No autonomous behavior is enabled

• No identity expansion occurs

• All memory is bounded and revocable

• All deployments remain auditable


⸻


8.9 Roadmap Summary (External-Facing)


EchoAuth is deployed through a phased, governance-first roadmap that prioritizes safety, auditability, and equitable access. Each stage enforces MCG constraints, ensuring scalable adoption without loss of control or drift.
