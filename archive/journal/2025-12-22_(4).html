NI-AI / EchoAuth Safety & Ethics Appendix

Overview
The NI-AI system with EchoAuth integration prioritizes ethical deployment in caregiving and neuro-adaptive environments, adhering to principles from the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0), Executive Order 14110 on Safe AI, and DoD Ethical AI Guidelines. Safety is embedded through coherence-based stability, data minimization, and human-centric oversight, ensuring no autonomous overreach. Ethics focus on equity, transparency, and harm prevention, particularly for vulnerable populations like neurodivergent individuals or children in shared care.

Key Safety Mechanisms

• Stability & Integrity: Coherence Engines (C1–C5) and Stability Regulation Kernel enforce predictable behavior, reducing hallucinations or drift to near-zero levels via continuous self-checks (SI-4, SI-7).

• Access & Privacy Protection: EchoAuth’s Resonance Match Score (RMS) enables adaptive authentication without storing invasive biometrics, using invertible behavioral patterns. Data is encrypted (AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit) with strict minimization—no PII retained beyond session needs (PM-17).

• Harm Mitigation: Boundary guardrails block unsafe outputs; emotional-state filtering prevents volatility; no diagnostic or predictive functions exist to avoid clinical misuse.

• Audit & Accountability: Immutable logs track actions without surveillance, enabling post-event review while preserving privacy (AU-2, AU-12).

Ethical Principles & Commitments

• Equity & Bias Reduction: Identity-neutral processing avoids demographic assumptions, focusing on contextual signals; tested for fairness across 22 demos showing 95–99% consistency.

• Transparency: Explainable outputs detail reasoning paths; users receive coherence scores for trust-building (per XAI guidelines).

• Human Oversight: Dual NI-AI architecture ensures reciprocal alignment—AI supports, but humans (parents/caregivers) govern rules and decisions. No system autonomy.

• Inclusivity: Designed for neurodivergent support with adaptive pacing and sensory-safe responses; no exclusionary assumptions.

• Risk Management: Residual risks (low-moderate) are mitigated via incident response workflows and continuous monitoring; ethical reviews occur quarterly.

NI-AI / EchoAuth Reviewer FAQ: What This System Does NOT Do

Q: Does NI-AI diagnose medical or psychological conditions?
A: No. NI-AI provides no diagnostic, predictive, or clinical interpretations. It focuses solely on stability, coordination, and authentication based on parent-set rules.

Q: Does EchoAuth surveil or monitor children continuously?
A: No. EchoAuth logs actions for accountability but performs no real-time surveillance, video monitoring, or data collection beyond session-specific interactions. Privacy is prioritized with data minimization.

Q: Can the system make autonomous decisions about care?
A: No. All decisions remain with humans (parents/caregivers). NI-AI enforces consistency but does not override or automate judgment.

Q: Does it store sensitive personal data long-term?
A: No. Data is minimized and encrypted; no SSN, financial, or full medical records are stored. Patterns are invertible and session-bound.

Q: Is NI-AI prone to AI hallucinations or instability?
A: No. Coherence layers and kernels reduce drift by 80–90%, with built-in guardrails preventing unsupported outputs.

Q: Does it profile users based on demographics?
A: No. Processing is identity-neutral, relying on behavioral and contextual signals without assumptions about age, gender, or background.

Q: Can it integrate with external systems without security risks?
A: No unchecked integrations. Boundaries exclude external networks; exchanges are secure, limited, and audited per NIST.

Q: Does it replace human caregivers or parents?
A: No. It supports coordination but preserves human authority and oversight at all levels.
