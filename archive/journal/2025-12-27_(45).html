NI-AI Doctrine — Compliance Summary (v1.0)


Non-Clinical, Non-Diagnostic Control Framework for Runtime Stabilization & Governance

Date: December 27, 2025

Reference: EchoAuth Technical Systems Whitepaper (TSW), Section VI — Compliance, Safety & Governance


⸻


Purpose


The NI-AI Doctrine establishes a real-time control system that stabilizes AI behavior around human emotional state, identity, and purpose before any response reaches the user. It integrates Natural Intelligence (NI) mapping—focusing on human patterns such as intention, emotional state, urgency, and cognitive load—with Artificial Intelligence (AI) capabilities to ensure coherence, safety, and trust.


This framework enforces runtime guardrails in interactive systems, prioritizing human sovereignty and preventing unchecked AI outputs in applications such as authentication, decision support, and neurodivergent assistance.


⸻


What NI-AI Is Not

• Not a diagnostic tool:

Does not assess, diagnose, or treat medical or psychological conditions. No clinical claims are made regarding emotional or cognitive states.

• Not autonomous AI:

AI components are subordinate to NI-derived rules and coherence checks. No independent decision-making occurs without human-state alignment.

• Not a general-purpose model:

Functions as a layered governance and control system within defined ecosystems (e.g., EchoAuth), not as a standalone large language model or training paradigm.

• Not speculative:

Grounded in implementable logic with explicit failure modes, enforcement gates, and audit hooks suitable for regulatory oversight.


⸻


Core Runtime Flow


NI-AI operates as a layered, end-to-end control stack for each interaction:

1. Input Layer

Captures multimodal signals including text, voice tone and pacing, behavioral patterns (e.g., pauses, hesitation), and contextual data such as session history.

2. NI Layer & Emotional State Engine (ESE)

Maps inputs to human-centric patterns (intention, emotional state, urgency, cognitive load), producing an Emotional State Profile (ESP).

3. Coherence Engine (C1–C5)

Validates proposed outputs across:

• C1 — Context: Session alignment

• C2 — Emotional: Tone matching

• C3 — Logical: Internal consistency

• C4 — Temporal: Time-based coherence

• C5 — Purpose: Alignment with declared user intent

4. AI Layer

Performs reasoning, retrieval, and generation strictly guided by NI-derived constraints.

5. Stability Regulation Kernel (SRK)

Filters drift, hallucinations, emotional mismatches, or unsafe transitions. Rewrites or rejects unstable outputs and downgrades complexity if overload is detected.

6. EchoAuth Layer

Continuously verifies identity using a Resonance Match Score (RMS) derived from behavioral and emotional consistency. Low RMS triggers step-up verification or blocks high-risk actions.

7. Neurodivergent Support Mode (NSM)

Adjusts delivery characteristics (shorter sentences, reduced sensory load, slower pacing) when enabled.

8. Output Layer

Delivers tailored responses across text, voice, and UI, with all interaction metrics logged (ESP, RMS, coherence scores, risk flags).


⸻


Safety & Identity Controls


Built-in control loops function as proactive safeguards:

• Coherence Guardrail:

Pauses interaction on low coherence scores, prompts clarification, and rebuilds context.

• Emotional Guardrail:

Softens tone and simplifies actions when distress or overload is detected.

• Identity Guardrail:

Blocks or escalates high-risk actions on low RMS with no silent failures.

• Audit & Review:

All sessions are reviewable by authorized human administrators, supporting traceability in regulated domains such as healthcare, finance, and government services.


⸻


Deployment Tiers


NI-AI supports scalable implementation:

1. Tier 1 — Assist Mode:

Observes and suggests safer or clearer interactions.

2. Tier 2 — Co-Pilot Mode:

Actively rewrites outputs, adjusts pacing, and enforces coherence constraints.

3. Tier 3 — Guardian Mode:

Enforces strict controls for high-risk domains, requiring EchoAuth confirmation, SRK validation, and approval gates.


⸻


Auditability Statement


All interactions generate auditable records including ESP, RMS, coherence scores, and risk flags. The system adheres to human-in-the-loop principles, with implementation details specified in the NI-AI Kernel Engineering Reference (internal document).


The framework is designed for review under NIH and NSF responsible innovation guidelines, emphasizing inclusivity (e.g., neurodivergent support) while remaining explicitly non-clinical and non-diagnostic.
