STEP 3 — FUNDING-READY NARRATIVE (ONE PAGE)


EchoAuth


The Problem (Funders Understand This)


Care systems for autistic and non-verbal children break down at scale due to coordination failure, not lack of care.

As demand outpaces the availability of trained professionals, families rely on mixed support networks that lack enforced consistency—introducing risk, stress, and inefficiency.


The Solution


EchoAuth is authority-gated coordination infrastructure that enforces parent-approved rules across all adults involved in a child’s care.


The system ensures:

• consistent routines across environments

• clear role boundaries

• prevention of unauthorized or inconsistent actions

• safety through pause-on-uncertainty rather than automated inference


What EchoAuth Is Not

• Not a medical device

• Not diagnostic or therapeutic

• Not behavioral or emotional AI


This positioning reduces regulatory burden while increasing adoption potential.


Why This Is Timely

• ASD support capacity is constrained nationwide

• Care coordination remains manual and error-prone

• Existing tools observe or message; they do not enforce


EchoAuth addresses a structural gap in the care ecosystem.


Technical Differentiation


EchoAuth uniquely combines:

• authority-gated execution

• role-based permissions

• context sensing without interpretation

• pause-on-uncertainty logic


This produces measurable risk reduction without automated decision-making.


Use of Funds (Early Phase)


Funding supports:

• controlled pilot deployment

• caregiver and parent usability validation

• system hardening and compliance readiness


Outcome Metrics


Success is measured by:

• reduced routine inconsistency

• increased parent acceptance of help

• fewer coordination-related disruptions


Closing Line (Funding-Safe)


EchoAuth scales support capacity without lowering safety standards by enforcing coordination instead of interpretation.
