Borrower: Helios F.I LLC

Founder: Nicholas B. Carty

Product: EchoAuth

Location: Mount Vernon, NY


What EchoAuth Is


EchoAuth is a safety-first, caregiver-mediated system designed to support non-verbal communication by removing the need for speech, interpretation, or behavioral explanation—while preserving human oversight, accountability, and auditability.


The system listens without requiring speech.


EchoAuth is not diagnostic, predictive, or autonomous. It does not interpret intent, emotion, or behavior as truth. Silence, stillness, withdrawal, and non-participation are treated as valid states and successful outcomes.


⸻


Problem


In schools, clinics, and home environments, non-verbal and neurodivergent children are often placed under pressure to:

• speak,

• explain,

• confirm understanding,

• or justify disengagement.


This pressure—often unintentional—can escalate distress, increase risk, and undermine caregiver trust. Existing tools tend to prioritize interpretation or prompting rather than restraint, pause, and safety.


⸻


Solution


EchoAuth provides protective infrastructure, not intervention.


The system:

• verifies who is authorized to engage (caregiver, staff, parent),

• enforces governance rules that default to non-action under uncertainty,

• supports non-verbal interaction by allowing time, presence, and stillness to stand on their own,

• explicitly prohibits staff from forcing verbalization or explanation,

• logs decisions for accountability without surveillance.


Wearables, when used, provide predictable orientation cues only—they do not interpret emotion or intent.


⸻


Who It Serves

• Non-verbal and neurodivergent children

• Caregivers and families

• Schools, clinics, and community care organizations


Caregivers remain the sole decision authority at all times.


⸻

• cover early operating and infrastructure costs during rollout.


Funds are used strictly for business operations and pilot readiness.

Community Impact


EchoAuth directly supports:

• safer care environments,

• reduced escalation and distress,

• caregiver authority and trust,

• equitable access for families in underserved communities.


The platform prioritizes restraint over action and care over automation.
