EchoAuth — Grant & Funding Readiness Pack


(Role- & Feature-Aligned | Child-First | Non-Clinical)


1. Executive Summary (1 Page)


Use this verbatim if needed


EchoAuth is a child-first care coordination and access platform designed for autistic and non-verbal children. It enables parents, caregivers, schools, and clinicians to support a child through shared visibility, sensory-safe engagement, and time-based care reminders—while preserving parental authority and enforcing strict safety controls.


The system does not diagnose, prescribe, or make medical decisions. Instead, it records observations, stores clinician-provided documents, tracks caregiver-entered schedules, issues factual reminders, and pauses on uncertainty. All actions affecting a child require parental approval and are fully logged for accountability.


EchoAuth is built to reduce caregiver burden, improve coordination across environments, and increase safety and continuity for children who cannot reliably authenticate, communicate, or self-advocate—while remaining scalable, auditable, and institution-ready.

2. Problem Statement (Grant-Safe)


What funders recognize immediately

• Autistic and non-verbal children are supported by multiple adults across home, school, and clinical settings

• Care information is fragmented, repeated, or lost

• Existing tools assume verbal ability, executive function, or self-authentication

• Caregiver burnout and missed care actions are common

• Schools and clinics lack a shared, safe coordination layer


Impact:

Children experience inconsistency, adults experience overload, and safety risks increase.
