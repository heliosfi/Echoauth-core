389.1 — Technical Field


PSPSM concerns systems and methods for:

• real-time public-environment risk prediction,

• sensory overload forecasting,

• crowd-pattern modeling,

• stranger-interaction prediction,

• movement-trajectory analysis,

• and safe-path computation


…for autistic individuals and anyone with sensory or behavioral vulnerabilities.


It protects individuals in all uncontrolled environments.


⸻


389.2 — Purpose


Public spaces are unpredictable:

• noise spikes

• crowds shifting

• flashing screens

• unexpected touches

• strangers approaching

• narrow exits

• police presence

• escalators / elevators

• train horns

• airport announcements

• street crossings


NI-AI’s PSPSM protects the user by predicting threats before they happen.


⸻


389.3 — Core Functions


(a) Geo-Environmental Signature Mapping (GESM)


NI-AI learns the “energy profile” of each public space:

• a mall’s noise rhythm

• a park’s movement pattern

• a bus station’s traffic cycle

• an airport’s boarding flow

• a grocery store’s aisle density


It builds dynamic maps for each location.


⸻


(b) Multi-Trajectory Safety Predictor (MTSP)


Forecasts:

• safe pathways

• high-pressure zones

• unpredictable clusters

• potential triggers

• fast-moving objects

• elevated sensory hotspots


Outputs a “safe movement route” in real time.


⸻


(c) Stranger-Approach Threat Estimator (SATE)


Predicts:

• unwanted interactions

• sudden approach

• unsafe proximity

• dangerous emotional signatures

• aggressive body language patterns


Works using anonymized pattern detection—never facial identity.


⸻


(d) Sensory Spike Forecast Engine (SSFE)


Predicts:

• loudspeaker announcements

• train arrivals

• crowd surges

• street noise bursts

• flashing light sequences


Gives the user a pre-signal warning 3–8 seconds before the spike.


⸻


(e) Pathway Safe-Exit Routing (PSER)


Computes:

• safest exits

• least crowded paths

• quiet corners

• nearby comfort zones


Uses micro-GPS + motion prediction + crowd density mapping.


⸻


(f) “Guardian Mode” for Parents


A special mode that shows the parent:

• where the child is heading

• upcoming risks

• safest intervention points

• overload trajectories

• potential elopement vectors (running off)


This solves one of the biggest ASD dangers.


⸻
