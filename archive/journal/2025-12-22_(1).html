NI-AI / EchoAuth 3-Month Institutional Safety & Coordination Pilot Proposal
Version 1.1 – Institutional Focus
Prepared by: Nicholas B. Carty, Helios F.I LLC
Date: December 22, 2025
Target Agency: [e.g., NSF / State Agencies / Institutional Partners]
Pilot Focus: Demonstrate reliable coordination infrastructure for shared caregiving or operational environments, enforcing parent-approved policies with consistent access controls and system stability in compliance with federal guidelines.

1. Executive Summary

Helios F.I LLC proposes a 3-month pilot of the NI-AI system with EchoAuth integration to validate its role as a safety and coordination layer in institutional settings (e.g., schools, disability services, or agency operations). The pilot will deploy a sandboxed instance in a [agency-hosted or hybrid] environment, involving 10–20 participants, to ensure consistent policy enforcement and access reliability. Budget: $150,000 (scalable). Outcomes include a validated coordination framework, integrity metrics, and implementation roadmap, aligning with NIST AI RMF, EO 14110, and agency priorities. Emphasizes non-autonomous support; no diagnostic or independent decision-making.

2. Pilot Objectives

• Primary: Validate NI-AI’s internal mechanisms for enforcing parent-approved policies consistently under real-world conditions, maintaining system integrity across sessions.

• Secondary: Test EchoAuth’s adaptive, non-biometric authentication for reliable access decisions, ensuring consistency in caregiver or operator verification.

• Tertiary: Gather feedback on usability (e.g., parent dashboards, multi-device sync) and safety compliance, confirming no unauthorized actions or privacy issues.

• Alignment: Supports [agency goals, e.g., NSF’s innovation in public-interest tech, state agencies’ disability coordination]. Measurable KPIs: Policy enforcement consistency >95%, access reliability >90%, user satisfaction >85%.

3. Scope and Methodology

• Deployment Environment: FedRAMP-equivalent cloud (e.g., AWS GovCloud) or agency on-prem, with SSP-defined boundaries. Zero-trust overlay enforced.

• Participants: 10–20 volunteers (e.g., caregivers, institutional staff, or coordinators). Recruitment via [agency partners or ethics-approved channels]; informed consent required.

• Key Activities:

◦ Week 1–2: Setup and baseline testing (configure policies, integrate with mock systems).

◦ Week 3–8: Core piloting (daily coordination tasks, access verifications, integrity monitoring).

◦ Week 9–12: Evaluation and iteration (analyze logs, user surveys, minor refinements).

• Tools/Tech: NI-AI Core (policy enforcement engine, thread-linking for consistency), EchoAuth (adaptive access layer), with immutable auditing. No data export outside boundaries.

• Ethics & Safety: Per Safety & Ethics Appendix—human oversight mandatory; escalation protocols for anomalies; data minimization.

4. Timeline

• Month 1: Preparation (Weeks 1–4)

◦ W1: Kickoff meeting, system provisioning, participant onboarding.

◦ W2: Baseline configurations, initial training sessions.

◦ W3–4: Dry-run tests; verify SSP controls (e.g., encryption, logging).

• Month 2: Execution (Weeks 5–8)

◦ W5–6: Active piloting—daily use cases (e.g., policy-aligned coordination sessions).

◦ W7: Mid-pilot review; adjust thresholds based on metrics.

◦ W8: Stress testing (e.g., multi-thread coordination scenarios).

• Month 3: Evaluation & Closeout (Weeks 9–12)

◦ W9–10: Data analysis, user feedback collection.

◦ W11: Final report drafting; demo to agency stakeholders.

◦ W12: Decommissioning, lessons learned, and transition plan.

5. Budget Estimate

• Personnel: $60,000 (System Architect: 50% time; Developer: 75% time; Ethics Reviewer: 25% time).

• Infrastructure: $30,000 (Cloud hosting, security tools; assumes agency credits).

• Testing & Tools: $20,000 (Participant stipends, survey software, audit services).

• Overhead/Misc: $40,000 (Travel for demos, contingencies, reporting).

• Total: $150,000. Funding sources: [e.g., NSF SBIR Phase I, state innovation grants]. Scalable down to $100K for smaller scope.

6. Evaluation Metrics & Success Criteria

• Quantitative: Policy enforcement consistency (>95%), prevention of unintended system actions (>90% effectiveness), access reliability (>90%), system uptime (99%). Tracked via built-in monitoring.

• Qualitative: User surveys (NPS >70), feedback on usability/safety. Third-party audit for compliance.

• Reporting: Weekly status updates; final report with dashboards, metrics, and recommendations. Success: Greenlight for broader institutional rollout or Phase II funding.

7. Risks & Mitigations

• Risk 1: System Inconsistency (Low Probability): Mitigated by internal stability mechanisms and continuous monitoring (SI-4).

• Risk 2: Privacy Concerns (Medium): Addressed via data minimization, encryption (SC-13), and consent models.

• Risk 3: Participant Dropout (Low): Backup recruits; flexible scheduling.

• Overall Residual Risk: Low, per SSP assessment. Contingency: Early termination protocol.
