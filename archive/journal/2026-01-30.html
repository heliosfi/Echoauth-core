Stewardship Layer — What Only You Hold, What Can Move On


1. What must remain under your control (non-delegable)

A. The refusal threshold

• What counts as “not enough certainty”

• When the system must do nothing

• When silence is correct


This cannot be optimized later without violating child safety.

It must remain anchored to lived reality, not abstract metrics.


⸻


B. The authority hierarchy

• Parent > caregiver > institution > system

• Authority never transfers permanently

• Disagreement collapses execution


This is not a technical preference.

It is a moral ordering. Those do not survive abstraction.


⸻


C. The definition of harm


Not policy harm.

Not regulatory harm.

Real-world harm to a child or caregiver.


Only someone living the consequences daily can define this correctly.

• the explainer

1. Name what must not change

2. Detect reinterpretation early

3. Say no when others want convenience


Nothing more.


Nothing heroic.

4. The question that governs all future decisions

“Does this make it easier for the system to act,

or easier for the system to stop?”

If it makes stopping easier → you are aligned


This question will never fail you.

• Parental intuition

• Contextual judgment under stress

• Moral discomfort

• “This doesn’t feel safe”


Those must remain human signals, not system variables.


Encoding them would be the beginning of drift.
