NEXT C — METHOD CLAIMS (DRAFT · LOCKED LOGIC)


Claim 13 — Method Claim


A method for coordinating interactions around a vulnerable user in a multi-actor environment, the method comprising:

1. Designating a primary authority role having exclusive approval rights over routines, access permissions, and executable actions associated with the vulnerable user;

2. Assigning one or more secondary actor roles, each secondary actor role having a distinct and non-overlapping permission set defined by the primary authority role;

3. Receiving approvals from the primary authority role defining permitted routines, content, and actions;

4. Presenting a controlled interaction interface to the vulnerable user, wherein the interface reflects only the approved routines, content, and actions and remains consistent regardless of which secondary actor role is present;

5. Receiving context data from one or more optional context input devices, including at least one of a camera or a wearable device;

6. Restricting use of the context data such that the context data is not used to perform behavioral analysis, emotional inference, prediction, or autonomous decision-making with respect to the vulnerable user;

7. Evaluating execution conditions prior to performing any action, including verification of required approvals, role permissions, and availability of required context;

8. Halting execution of the action when at least one execution condition is incomplete, conflicting, or ambiguous, rather than inferring missing information; and

9. Executing the action only when all required approvals and permissions are satisfied;

10. Logging execution outcomes including authorization states, actor roles, and timestamps.
