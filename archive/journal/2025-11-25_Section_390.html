390.3 — Functional Definition


MRPAS assembles and compares five simultaneous representations of reality, then converges them into a unified perception map:


1. Human-Observed Reality (HOR)


What the parent/child actually sees and experiences.


2. Device-Observed Reality (DOR)


Sensors, camera, audio, wearables, phone mic, micro-patterns.


3. Social-Context Reality (SCR)


Cues from crowd behavior, teacher reactions, environmental norms.


4. Emotional-State Reality (ESR)


Internal emotional signals detected via NI-AI emotional engine.


5. Baseline-Expectation Reality (BER)


What SHOULD be expected for this child (their baseline).


These five are fused and cross-verified, ensuring that:

• No single source dominates

• No hallucination is allowed

• No false inference slips through

• All perception is aligned with the REAL event

(a) Cross-Reality Comparator Layer (CRCL)

(f) Contextual Reality Alignment Layer (CRAL)


Ensures NI-AI’s understanding matches the true environment.
