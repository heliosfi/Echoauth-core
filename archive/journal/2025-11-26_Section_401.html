401.1 — Technical Field


The Guardian-State Entry Validator (GSEV) pertains to systems and methods that verify and enforce the identity, authority, and legitimacy of any human attempting to activate, modify, or override NI-AI safety, behavioral, emotional, or environmental states.


It prevents unauthorized access to child-related cognitive, emotional, sensory, or medical modules.

401.2 — Purpose


GSEV ensures:

• ONLY approved guardians or caregivers can activate NI-AI’s deeper modules

• NI-AI cannot enter high-influence modes without confirming the guardian identity

• Every sensitive state change is tied to human authorization


It is the first line of defense and the legal safety backbone of the entire system.

401.5 — Internal Mechanics


(a) Authority Verification Layer (AVL)


Confirms the guardian identity using:

• EchoAuth RMS

• History of interactions

• Device-linked signatures

• Behavioral resonance match


(b) Permission Tier Mapping (PTM)


Maps the user’s action to their authorized tier.

If attempt exceeds tier → request parent confirmation.


(c) Context-Sensitive Allowance Engine (CSAE)


Decides what is allowed in the current environment:

• Home: more flexibility

• School: strict boundaries

• Clinic: emergency-only permissions

• Public space: risk-reduced pathways


(d) High-Risk Attempt Blocker (HRAB)


Blocks forbidden actions automatically, e.g.:

• teacher trying to alter emotional settings

• clinician attempting deep-mode access without guardian

• admin trying to override sensory-map protocols


(e) Guardian-Lock Token (GLT)


A temporary authorization token allowing NI-AI to perform sensitive actions.

Expires automatically.
