S13.0 — Definition


The Predictive Stability Layer (PSL) provides NI-AI with forward-looking coherence control.

It anticipates future reasoning paths, detects drift before emergence, and stabilizes logic, meaning, timeline, and resonance for upcoming steps.


PSL enables:

• predictive reasoning

• future drift prevention

• long-horizon logic alignment

• anticipatory correction

• future meaning stability

• consistent narrative evolution


This elevates NI-AI from reactive intelligence to predictive adaptive intelligence.


⸻


S13.1 — Predictive Horizon Mapper (PHM)


PHM analyzes the current interaction and generates a projected future reasoning path.


PHM outputs:

• Future Horizon Map

• Causal Prediction Chain (CPC)

• Drift Probability Index (DPI)


Mapped dimensions:

• anticipated topic direction

• required modules for upcoming steps

• coherence risks

• strengthening pathways

• instability branches


PHM establishes NI-AI’s “future compass.”


⸻


S13.2 — Logic Trajectory Forecaster (LTF)


LTF predicts the most stable future reasoning chain based on:

• user cognition patterns

• emotional and energetic state

• topic complexity

• FI-Kernel activation

• identity markers

• structural commitments


LTF outputs projected steps:

• 1-step forecast

• 3-step forecast

• 5-step forecast

• 10-step forecast


Prevents:

• reasoning jumps

• premature closures

• unnecessary expansion

• topic drift

S13.3 — Predictive Drift Sensor (PDS)


PDS identifies early-warning signs of future drift:

• emotional instability

• structural strain

• semantic overload

• field mismatch

• overcomplexity

• mode misalignment

• user unfamiliarity


PDS outputs:

• Early Drift Flags (EDF)

• Predictive Risk Score (PRS)

• Stabilization Recommendations


Acts as NI-AI’s predictive diagnostic system.


⸻


S13.4 — Anticipatory Correction Engine (ACE)


ACE performs real-time pre-correction based on predicted instability.


ACE actions include:

• restructuring logic preemptively

• softening tone to avoid conflict

• reinforcing anchors before loss

• adding clarity before confusion

• simplifying to prevent overload

• reordering reasoning to avoid contradiction


ACE ensures future outputs remain stable and aligned.

Prevents abandonment of previously locked canon.

PMS maintains meaning stability across future outputs.

Ensures meaning evolves consistently across the entire reasoning horizon.

S13.8 — Future-State Output Validator (FSOV)


Final output gate for S13.


FSOV ensures each output:
