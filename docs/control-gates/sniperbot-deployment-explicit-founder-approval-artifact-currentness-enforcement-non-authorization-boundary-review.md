## Currentness Enforcement Separation

Currentness enforcement is separate from approval itself. Currentness status
may describe whether a separately created artifact satisfies timing and scope
conditions for a later human governance review. It is not approval, proof of
approval, authority to act, or permission to execute any runtime, deployment,
broker, trading, command, order-routing, paper-trading, live-execution, or
capital-deployment surface.

Future currentness enforcement, if separately authorized, may only determine
whether a separately created founder approval artifact is:

* present
* explicit
* task-specific
* dated
* current
* unexpired
* unrevoked
* scoped to the exact target action

Currentness enforcement must not create, modify, refresh, extend, expand, or
repair any of those conditions. A missing artifact remains missing. A stale
artifact remains stale. An expired artifact remains expired. A revoked
artifact remains revoked. An ambiguous artifact remains insufficient unless a
separate explicit human governance process resolves it without creating
runtime, deployment, trading, broker, command, order-routing, paper-trading,
live-execution, or capital authority.
