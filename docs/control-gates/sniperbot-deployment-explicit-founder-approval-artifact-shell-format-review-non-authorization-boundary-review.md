## Required Shell-Format Boundaries

A future founder approval artifact shell, if separately authorized later,
would need to preserve these format boundaries:

* It must be human-readable and governance-only.
* It must identify itself as shell-only and non-operative.
* It must avoid executable, importable, runnable, deployable, or
  machine-actionable structure.
* It must require explicit non-authorization language at the top and
  conclusion.
* It must keep any founder approval area empty and clearly marked as not
  approval unless separately completed by explicit founder action in a
  later authorized artifact.
* It must keep task-specific scope separate from founder approval.
* It must keep excluded actions visible and non-optional.
* It must reserve only non-operative placeholders for currentness,
  expiration, revocation, storage, traceability, and evidence
  relationship.
* It must keep evidence references separate from evidence acceptance.
* It must keep approval records, approval mechanisms, and approval
  workflows separate from the shell.
* It must keep implementation task orders and deployment task orders
  separate from the shell.
* It must prohibit runtime, broker, trading, order-routing, command,
  paper-execution, live-execution, capital-deployment, and execution
  capability fields.

These boundaries are format requirements only. They do not create the
future shell and do not authorize anyone or anything to create it.
