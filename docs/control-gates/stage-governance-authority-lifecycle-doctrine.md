## Foundational Authority Rules

The lifecycle is governed by these repository-established rules:

1. **Founder authority is the governing authority source.** A repository,
   system, executor, agent, document, capability, gate result, or stage cannot
   create its own authority.
2. **Authority must be explicit.** Silence, chronology, readiness,
   capability, cleanliness, publication, indexing, evidence, prior work, or an
   obvious next step is not permission.
3. **Authority is bounded.** It applies only to the named subject,
   repository, branch, checkpoint, paths, actions, executor, conditions, and
   completion boundary.
4. **Authority is current or unusable.** Stale, not-yet-effective, expired,
   consumed, suspended, revoked, superseded, conflicting, over-scoped,
   improperly transferred, or unverifiable authority requires refusal.
5. **Delegation does not transfer final authority.** Delegation and any
   redelegation must be exact and explicit. A delegate cannot enlarge,
   renew, transfer, or self-assign authority.
6. **Evidence is not authority.** Validation, `PASS`, acceptance,
   traceability, synchronization, and clean repository state cannot create a
   new lane.
7. **Entry is not operation.** Entering a stage changes stage status only.
   It does not authorize performance of the stage subject.
8. **Completion is not acceptance.** The performer cannot convert completion
   or validation evidence into independent acceptance.
9. **Acceptance is not follow-on authority.** Acceptance resolves only its
   named acceptance question.
10. **Closure is not next-stage entry.** Exiting one stage does not enter,
    authorize, or operate within another.
11. **Non-action is a valid protected result.** `FAIL`, `BLOCKED`, refusal,
    halt, or a determination that no subject is eligible can validly complete
    a bounded lane without creating repair or follow-on authority.
12. **Every single-use lane ends.** Consumption and exhaustion prevent
    replay, continuation, adjacent work, or authority inheritance.

## Canonical Lifecycle

The canonical stage-governance lifecycle is:

1. **ENTER STAGE**
2. **OPERATE WITHIN STAGE**
3. **IMPORTANT GOVERNANCE ACT**
4. **ACCEPTANCE**
5. **EXIT STAGE**
6. **AUTHORITY EXHAUSTION**

These are governance lifecycle functions, not runtime modes. A stage may
contain multiple separately authorized operations and important governance
acts, each with its own acceptance and exhaustion boundary. The sequence does
not create standing authority and does not require every ordinary repository
step to become a separate stage.

## 1. ENTER STAGE

### Purpose

Change one exact stage from an unentered posture to an entered posture only
after the predecessor state, lineage, governing contracts, evidence,
readiness, repository state, and founder authority have been independently
verified.

### Governing Effect

Entry changes only the durable governance status of the named stage for the
named stage subject. A valid entry record establishes that the stage exists as
the current governed review habitat.

Entry does not perform the stage subject and does not create standing
authority to operate within the stage.

### Authority Boundary

Stage entry requires:

- defined entry prerequisites and predecessor state;
- exact repository, branch, checkpoint, stage, and subject identity;
- current, accepted, traceable evidence;
- explicit founder entry authority;
- an exact entry-validation order when the controlling contract requires one;
- independent entry validation;
- a durable entry determination;
- synchronization and cleanliness verification; and
- explicit non-authorization of stage operation and later stages.

Readiness is not entry. Founder entry authority is not entry. An entry task
order is not entry. Validator `PASS` is not entry. Entry occurs only through
the separately authorized durable entry record after all required prior
functions validly complete.

### Completion Condition

ENTER STAGE completes when:

- the permitted entry result is validly determined;
- any required durable entry record is published;
- local, tracked-remote, and live-remote state is verified as required;
- the entry authority and entry-validation order reach their defined
  consumption and exhaustion events; and
- work stops before stage operation or another governance act.

If the result is `FAIL` or `BLOCKED`, the entry lane completes without entry
and without repair or retry authority.

## 2. OPERATE WITHIN STAGE

### Purpose

Perform one exact, bounded act that belongs to the entered stage's defined
subject and preserves the stage's governing habitat.

### Governing Effect

An operation may produce only the exact artifact, review, evaluation, or
bounded result named by its current authority. It advances no other subject
and changes no other stage.

An entered stage is a governed container, not a blanket permission. Every
separately bounded lane within the stage requires its own applicable
authority.

### Authority Boundary

Authority to operate within a stage must identify, as applicable:

- the exact stage and subject;
- authority and order identity;
- repository, branch, checkpoint, and ancestry rule;
- executor and task/session identity;
- permitted and prohibited paths, actions, and outputs;
- governing prerequisites;
- validation, evidence, and acceptance boundaries;
- independence requirements;
- refusal and halt conditions;
- consumption and exhaustion events; and
- the explicit non-authorization effect of completion.

Stage entry, prior acceptance, repository presence, capability, or incomplete
work does not supply operating authority. Discovery of an adjacent need
requires halt and report, not scope expansion.

### Completion Condition

OPERATE WITHIN STAGE completes when the one authorized act reaches its defined
`PASS`, `FAIL`, `BLOCKED`, publication, reporting, or other exact completion
boundary; required verification is complete; evidence is preserved; and the
lane stops.

Operation completion does not constitute acceptance unless the governing
contract explicitly combines them and validly preserves required
independence. It never authorizes the next operation.

## 3. IMPORTANT GOVERNANCE ACT

### Purpose

Create, change, resolve, or durably record a fact that controls authority,
stage status, governing precedence, eligibility, task scope, acceptance,
closure, or future permitted movement.

### Governing Effect

An important governance act can change the architecture's governing state
only for its exact question. Examples include:

- issuing, narrowing, suspending, revoking, or explicitly superseding
  authority;
- defining or changing a governing contract;
- issuing a task order;
- selecting a stage, subject, repository boundary, or governed disposition;
- recording stage entry or exit;
- accepting or rejecting governing evidence;
- resolving a governance conflict or ambiguity;
- authorizing a transfer or implementation lane;
- changing a canonical status determination; or
- closing a governed phase.

An important governance act does not inherit adjacent operative effect from
the importance of its subject.

### Authority Boundary

An important governance act requires a formal authority block before the act
or its repository mutation begins.

The formal authority block must identify:

- founder and authority identity;
- exact governing purpose and question;
- repository, branch, checkpoint, and allowed output;
- effective and currentness conditions;
- exact allowed and prohibited actions;
- executor, delegation, and independence rules;
- governing prerequisites and conflict precedence;
- validation and completion conditions;
- consumption, exhaustion, expiry, suspension, revocation, and supersession
  treatment;
- refusal and halt conditions; and
- explicit non-authorization of adjacent or later movement.

A separate task order is required when an applicable governing contract
requires separation between founder authority and execution. This doctrine
does not convert every governance document into a task order and does not
waive a task-order requirement that already applies.

### Completion Condition

IMPORTANT GOVERNANCE ACT completes when the exact authorized governing record
or result is published and verified, its stated effect is durably
identifiable, and the authority stops at its named boundary.

The act is not independently accepted merely because it was published,
validated, committed, pushed, indexed, or synchronized.

## 4. ACCEPTANCE

### Purpose

Independently determine whether one completed artifact, evidence package,
governance act, implementation result, transfer result, or stage result
conforms to its exact governing contract.

### Governing Effect

Acceptance changes only the acceptance status of the named subject. It may
record `ACCEPTED`, `REJECTED`, `FAIL`, `BLOCKED`, or another closed result
defined by the controlling contract.

Acceptance does not create the accepted artifact, rewrite its history, cure a
defect, perform repair, authorize operation, or permit follow-on movement.

### Authority Boundary

Acceptance requires:

- separate explicit acceptance authority;
- an exact acceptance question and evidence identity;
- a current governing contract and acceptance criteria;
- an independent reviewer or context when independence is required;
- reproducible validation and provenance;
- closed result vocabulary;
- refusal and halt conditions; and
- a non-authorization statement.

The creator, performer, publisher, or validator must not accept its own work
when the applicable contract requires independent acceptance. Validation
success is evidence only. `PASS` is not permission.

### Completion Condition

ACCEPTANCE completes when the authorized reviewer determines one permitted
result, publishes or reports it as required, verifies synchronization and
evidence preservation, and stops. Acceptance authority is then consumed and
exhausted under its exact lifecycle rule.

An accepted result permits only a later founder decision when a governing
sequence expressly says so. It does not supply that decision.

## 5. EXIT STAGE

### Purpose

Determine and durably record whether the exact entered stage has completed its
governed subject and may be formally closed.

### Governing Effect

Exit changes only the named stage's status from entered or open to closed.
Closure preserves the complete stage record as historical and governing
lineage.

Exit does not erase prior `FAIL`, `BLOCKED`, refusal, repair, acceptance, or
authority records. It does not create repository-transfer, implementation,
runtime, operational, deployment, or next-stage authority.

### Authority Boundary

Stage exit requires:

- exact completion and closure prerequisites;
- current accepted evidence and completion status;
- explicit founder closure authority;
- a separate closure evaluation or task order when required;
- independent verification of the complete stage chain;
- a durable closure determination;
- preservation of unresolved or historical findings;
- synchronization and cleanliness verification; and
- an explicit statement that closure is not next-stage entry.

Completion, acceptance, indexing, readiness, or an empty remaining-work list
cannot be inferred as closure.

### Completion Condition

EXIT STAGE completes when the permitted closure result is validly determined,
the durable closure record is published if authorized, the repository is
verified, the closure authority and any closure order are exhausted, and work
stops in a closed posture.

If closure returns `FAIL` or `BLOCKED`, the stage remains in its prior posture.
No repair, reopening, retry, or next-stage movement is authorized by the
result.

## 6. AUTHORITY EXHAUSTION

### Purpose

Terminate the usable scope of a bounded authority and prevent replay,
continuation, inheritance, or conversion into adjacent authority.

### Governing Effect

Exhaustion leaves the completed record and its evidence intact while reducing
remaining executable authority under that lane to **NONE**.

Consumption and exhaustion are distinct:

- **consumption** is the exact event that invokes or spends the single-use
  authority or task order; and
- **exhaustion** is the terminal state after the authorized lane and required
  verification have ended, regardless of result.

A consumed authority cannot be replayed. An exhausted authority cannot
authorize cleanup, repair, retry, acceptance, synchronization outside its
scope, follow-on work, or a later stage.

### Authority Boundary

Every formal authority block must define:

- its consumption event;
- its exhaustion event;
- whether a task order has a separate consumption event;
- the result of `PASS`, `FAIL`, or `BLOCKED` on remaining authority;
- expiry, suspension, revocation, and supersession effects;
- evidence-preservation requirements; and
- the required final stop posture.

Unless a narrower earlier event controls, single-use authority is exhausted
after the one authorized act and required verification, regardless of
success, failure, or blockage.

Suspension makes authority unusable while suspended. Revocation, expiry,
invalidation, consumption, or explicit supersession prevents further use.
None creates replacement authority. Chronology alone does not supersede or
restore authority.

### Completion Condition

AUTHORITY EXHAUSTION completes when:

- the defined consumption or terminal event has occurred;
- required evidence and reporting are preserved;
- publication and synchronization are verified when applicable;
- no authorized continuation remains;
- repository posture is clean; and
- the lane returns to `WAIT`, `CLOSED`, `REFUSED`, `FAIL`, `BLOCKED`, or
  another exact non-operative posture defined by its controlling contract.

Further movement requires a new explicit founder authority.

## Authority Object State

Every authority record or task order must make its own exact state
determinable. The canonical state logic is:

| Authority state | Meaning | Permitted effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `ABSENT` | No exact applicable authority exists. | No governed movement. |
| `ISSUED_NOT_EFFECTIVE` | A record exists but its effective event has not occurred. | No execution. |
| `EFFECTIVE_UNCONSUMED` | The exact authority is current, applicable, conflict-free, and available for its one bounded use. | Only the named act may begin. |
| `IN_EXECUTION_OR_CONSUMED` | The defined consumption event has occurred. | Only completion, evidence preservation, and verification already authorized by the same lane may continue. |
| `EXHAUSTED` | The lane and required verification ended. | No replay or follow-on movement. |
| `SUSPENDED` | Use is temporarily prohibited by an explicit applicable condition or record. | Halt; no movement until separately and validly restored under the controlling rule. |
| `REVOKED`, `EXPIRED`, `INVALID`, or `SUPERSEDED` | The authority is unusable for the named act. | Refuse or halt; no replacement authority is implied. |
| `CONFLICTING_OR_UNVERIFIABLE` | Applicability, precedence, identity, or currentness cannot be conclusively established. | Fail closed with the controlling `BLOCKED`, refusal, or escalation result. |

These states classify authority availability. They do not create a runtime
state machine or implementation requirement.

## Ordinary Repository Activity and Governance-Changing Events

Repository activity is classified by effect, not by how easy, familiar, or
mechanical it appears.

| Activity | Classification | Authority treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Reading files, inspecting history, checking status, comparing blobs, or examining a live remote without mutation | Ordinary read-only activity | May occur only within the current review lane and creates no authority or governance effect. |
| Running an exact validation or preserving evidence already named by an active order | Ordinary execution inside a bounded lane | Covered only by that lane; it cannot expand scope or become acceptance. |
| Staging, committing, and publishing the one exact artifact named by an active order | Ordinary publication execution inside a bounded lane | Requires the existing explicit mutation authority; no second authority block is required solely because the authorized publication is mechanical. |
| Mechanical index or synchronization update that changes no substantive decision | Traceability activity | Requires explicit bounded mutation authority, but does not itself change stage status or create acceptance or follow-on authority. |
| Creating or changing authority, a governing contract, task order, acceptance, eligibility disposition, stage entry, stage exit, conflict resolution, transfer decision, or implementation decision | Important governance act | Requires a formal authority block and all applicable separation and independence controls. |
| Any discovered action, file, decision, repair, retry, or next step not named by the active authority | Outside the lane | Stop and report. A new founder order is required. |

Repository cleanliness, a fast-forward push, a one-line edit, an index entry,
or an apparently clerical change does not remove the requirement for explicit
mutation authority.

## When a Formal Authority Block Is Required

A formal authority block is required before:

- any governance-changing event;
- any repository mutation not already exactly authorized by the active
  bounded lane;
- stage entry or exit;
- creation or change of founder authority;
- task-order issuance;
- independent acceptance;
- conflict, ambiguity, revocation, suspension, or supersession resolution;
- selection or authorization of implementation or transfer;
- any action that changes what may happen next; or
- any action for which the applicable governing contract expressly requires
  separate founder authority.

A new formal authority block is not required for each mechanical substep
already exact, necessary, and explicitly included in one active authority.
Mechanical substeps do not acquire independent scope and must stop with their
parent lane.

If it is uncertain whether an activity changes governance, authority,
acceptance, stage status, or future movement, treat it as governance-changing
and fail closed pending explicit founder authority.

## Cross-Repository and Lineage Rule

Authority follows the canonical governance lineage; it does not arise from
repository location.

- Echoauth-core remains the canonical cross-stage historical and governance
  authority unless a later explicit governing decision changes that
  relationship.
- A dedicated repository may contain current stage records and bounded
  domain artifacts.
- Repository creation, file presence, branch status, source availability, or
  ownership does not transfer governing authority.
- A cross-repository act must identify both repositories, branches,
  checkpoints, paths, blobs, lineage effect, and exact governing authority.
- Transfer is not implementation. Implementation is not transfer. Neither is
  authorized by stage entry, this doctrine, or repository lineage.

## Conflict and Precedence

This doctrine consolidates common lifecycle rules. It does not silently
supersede an existing record.

When records differ:

1. identify the exact applicable subject, stage, authority, repository,
   checkpoint, and time;
2. apply an explicit repository-grounded precedence or supersession rule;
3. preserve narrower valid boundaries;
4. do not use chronology alone to resolve the conflict; and
5. return the controlling `BLOCKED`, refusal, or escalation result if
   precedence cannot be conclusively established.

A later record controls only when its applicability, precedence, and effect
are explicit. Ambiguity is non-permission.
