## Why Evidence Requirements Are Not Approval

Evidence requirements are prerequisites to a later review. They are not the
review result.

Evidence requirements may describe categories that a later governance lane
would need to inspect, but they do not prove that any evidence exists. They
may describe sufficiency boundaries, but they do not determine sufficiency.
They may describe freshness or provenance expectations, but they do not
validate freshness or provenance. They may describe traceability
expectations, but they do not create audit records, approval records,
traceability runtime, or approval mechanisms.

Evidence requirements are not deployment approval because deployment
approval would require later separate review of actual evidence, approval
authority, approval scope-limits, founder approval separation, and
implementation non-authorization gates. This review does not perform those
future reviews.

Evidence requirements are not founder approval because founder approval is
a separate governance act. Mentioning founder-facing evidence,
founder-selected gates, founder review, founder approval, or founder
authority does not create approval, authority, readiness, deployment
permission, runtime permission, command permission, or execution
