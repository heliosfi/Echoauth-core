## 3. Architectural Relationship

EchoAuth is the governing architecture and deterministic authority system.
SniperBot is a governed domain system. Repository separation changes a storage
and ownership boundary only; it does not make SniperBot an independent source
of authority and does not transfer, duplicate, supersede, or weaken EchoAuth
governance.

`heliosfi/Echoauth-core` remains the canonical historical and governance
authority for the complete SniperBot Stage 2 record. Its published history,
governance records, source, schemas, tests, evidence, acceptance,
certification, closure, Advancement Gate lineage, and canonical indexes remain
permanent source lineage.

A future dedicated repository must cite and remain subordinate to the
applicable EchoAuth authority and non-authorization contracts. It must not
claim independent origin, inherited Stage 3 authority, implementation
authority, runtime authority, deployment authority, broker authority, trading
authority, funding authority, autonomous authority, or command-execution
authority.

The separate Governed Agentic Engineering methodology repository remains
separate and parked unless independently activated through its own explicit
authority. It is not part of this separation plan and must not be copied,
linked as operative authority, or collapsed into EchoAuth or SniperBot.

  domain;

1. stop Order 2 immediately;
2. do not execute Order 3 or any later order;
3. preserve the exact repository, remote, commit, file, command, and error
   evidence required for audit and disposition;
4. do not silently repair, rename, reconfigure, push, force-push, import,
   delete, recreate, or otherwise exceed Order 2 authority;
5. record no successful completion or inherited authority;
6. obtain separate explicit founder authority for any repair, quarantine,
   archival, visibility correction, ownership correction, or disposition; and
7. delete only when deletion is explicitly authorized, the exact target is
   verified, required evidence is preserved, and deletion would not destroy
   canonical or required audit evidence.

No cleanup convenience, apparent reversibility, repository emptiness, or
administrative access creates repair or deletion authority.

If Order 3 finds any discrepancy, the sequence stops. Order 3 may report the
discrepancy but may not repair it. A separate founder decision must determine
repair, preservation, quarantine, or disposition.
