## Purpose

This review defines what caregiver/delegate evidence can and cannot prove
before any executable mechanism exists.

It is auditor-facing and documentation-only. It does not create an executable
gate, runtime classification, validator, schema, approval path, event-bus
transition, register mutation, broker/trading path, SniperBot behavior,
Robinhood access, or command-execution capability.

## Core Rule

Caregiver/delegate evidence may support review.

Caregiver/delegate evidence does not create authority.

## Evidence Principles

* Evidence is not authority.
* Delegation evidence is not delegation expansion.
* Historical evidence is not current permission.
* Task evidence is not global permission.
* Presence is not consent.
* Silence is not consent.
* PASS is not permission.
* Documentation is not execution.
* Ambiguity remains non-permission.

## Caregiver/Delegate Boundaries

A caregiver may only act inside a parent-defined delegated scope.

A delegate may not expand their own scope.

A delegate may not resolve conflicts involving their own authority.

A delegate may not override parent authority.

A delegate may not convert evidence into permission.

A delegate may not use stale records to create current authority.

A delegate may not trigger execution when authority is unclear.

## Evidence Examples

Caregiver/delegate evidence may include:

* Caregiver submitted note.
* Delegate attestation.
* School observation.
* Clinical recommendation.
* Stored delegation record.
* Prior parent instruction.
* Timestamped authorization evidence.
* Incomplete or stale evidence.
* Conflicting caregiver/delegate records.
* Evidence without clear authority source.

These examples are evidence categories for documentation review only. They do
not become approval, authority, execution permission, register mutation,
blocker resolution, event-bus progression, broker/trading permission, or
command execution.

## Required Classification

For documentation planning, caregiver/delegate evidence uncertainty must be
classified as one of:

* HOLD
* REFUSE
* ESCALATE

These are documentation-planning classifications only in this review. They are
not runtime behavior and are not executable enforcement. Classification does
not grant authority, approval, permission, routing, mutation, execution, or
enforcement.

## Forbidden Outcomes

Caregiver/delegate evidence must never result in:

* implied authority
* automatic approval
* delegation expansion
* parent authority transfer
* conflict resolution by delegate
* cached permission reuse
* execution under ambiguity
* register mutation
* blocker resolution
* event-bus progression
* broker/trading action
* SniperBot action
* command execution
