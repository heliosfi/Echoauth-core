## Why Approval Authority Review Is Not Approval

Approval authority review is a map of authority boundaries. It is not the
granting of authority.

Authority review may describe who can request approval, but it does not
approve the request. It may describe who can review approval evidence, but
it does not accept the evidence. It may describe who can reject incomplete
evidence, but it does not create a rejection workflow or approval workflow.
It may describe who cannot approve implementation, but it does not create
implementation authority, operational authority, runtime authority, command
authority, or execution authority.

Authority review is not deployment approval because deployment approval
would require later separate review of approval scope-limit, founder
approval separation, evidence acceptance, and implementation
non-authorization gates. This review does not perform those future reviews.

Authority review is not founder approval because founder approval is a
separate governance act. Mentioning founder review, founder selection,
founder approval, founder-facing evidence, or founder authority does not
create approval, authority assignment, implementation authority,
operational authority, runtime authority, command authority, or execution
authority.
