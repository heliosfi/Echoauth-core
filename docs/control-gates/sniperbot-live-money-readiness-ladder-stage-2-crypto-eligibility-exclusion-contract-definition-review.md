## Eligibility, Exclusion, Restriction, and Deferral / No-Action

The four concepts remain distinct subjects or postures:

* **Eligibility** is descriptive and non-authorizing. At most, a future
  founder-defined eligible posture may mean continued governed Stage 2
  consideration. It is never approval, selection, safety, suitability,
  tradeability, routability, transaction authority, or execution authority.
* **Exclusion** is descriptive governance posture only. It is not a runtime
  filter, blocklist, denylist, selector, scorer, router, pre-trade gate, order
  rejection rule, enforcement mechanism, or execution authority. Absence of
  exclusion never implies eligibility or permission.
* **Restriction** is a potentially separate descriptive posture whose exact
  existence, meaning, representation, mappings, and precedence remain founder
  decisions. It is not a runtime limit or executable control.
* **Deferral / No-Action** remains the already accepted, separate Crypto lane.
  It classifies whether supplied facts require a pause or no action. This
  review neither reopens nor changes that lane, and it does not assume that a
  Deferral / No-Action result is an eligibility result.

Whether eligibility, exclusion, and restriction become outcomes, supplied
facts, reason categories, or another exact typed representation remains
unresolved. This section preserves semantic separation without resolving the
future contract.
