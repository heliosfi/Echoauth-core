## What Founder Approval Artifact Rejection and Revocation Checklist Means

A founder approval artifact rejection and revocation checklist would mean a
possible future non-operative review aid that helps a human reviewer ask
whether a separately created and separately authorized future founder
approval artifact may have rejection or revocation concerns.

The phrase does not mean the checklist exists now. It does not mean the
checklist can reject or revoke anything. It does not mean rejection or
revocation authority exists. It does not mean founder approval exists. It
does not mean a founder approval artifact, artifact shell, approval record,
approval mechanism, approval workflow, authority registry mutation,
implementation task order, deployment task order, runtime path, broker
path, trading path, command path, or execution path exists.

A future checklist may only describe review questions or verification
points for identifying possible rejection or revocation conditions. It may
not define operative rejection criteria, operative revocation criteria,
automatic pass/fail outcomes, approval-state mutation, authority registry
mutation, artifact creation, runtime behavior, or execution behavior.
