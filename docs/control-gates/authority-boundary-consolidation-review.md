This auditor-facing review consolidates the authority-boundary rules locked by
the Authority Conflict Boundary Review, the Caregiver Delegate Evidence
Boundary Review, and the Parent Authority Preservation Review.

It exists after Parent Authority Preservation Review to keep the authority
spine coherent before any separate future request could discuss mechanical

runtime, enforcement, event-bus, register, blocker, broker/trading, Robinhood,

## Consolidated Locked Rules

Parent/final authority is not transferable by evidence.

Parent/final authority cannot be overridden by delegated, observational,
clinical, stale, silent, or system-held records.

Authority conflict is not a decision state. Authority conflict is a halt
condition.

Caregiver/delegate evidence may support review, but does not create authority.

Evidence is not authority.

Delegation does not transfer final authority.

Silence is not consent.

Presence is not consent.

PASS is not permission.

Documentation is not execution.

Ambiguity remains non-permission.

Trading/Robinhood remains a separate domain with no execution alignment.

These rules are governance boundaries only. They do not grant approval,
permission, routing, mutation, execution, enforcement, broker/trading access,
Robinhood access, SniperBot behavior, macro/hotkey automation, or command
execution.

## Boundary Relationships

Parent/final authority:
Parent/final authority remains the preserved authority anchor. It may not be
transferred, diluted, or overridden by evidence, delegated support,
observational records, clinical recommendations, stale records, silence,
presence, system-held records, PASS classifications, planning documents, or
implementation artifacts.

Delegated support:
Delegated support may assist only inside explicit, current, parent-defined
scope. Delegation is task-scoped support. It is not final authority, does not
expand itself, and does not convert a delegate, caregiver, school actor,
clinical actor, system actor, record, or stored artifact into an authority
source.

Evidence:
Evidence may support review, traceability, and auditor-facing reasoning.
Evidence does not create authority, expand delegation, resolve conflicts,
refresh stale permission, imply consent, mutate registers, resolve blockers,
trigger event-bus progress, authorize runtime behavior, or permit execution.

Conflict:
Conflict means the governance record must treat the matter as halted rather
than decided by implication. Conflicting authority claims, conflicting records,
stale records, unclear authority sources, delegated-scope disputes,
caregiver/delegate overreach, school observations, clinical recommendations,
and system-held records remain non-permission. This review does not define a
runtime halt, an enforcement mechanism, an unblock path, or a conflict
resolution process.

Ambiguity:
Ambiguity remains non-permission. Missing, incomplete, stale, silent, disputed,
or unclear authority evidence cannot be converted into approval. Ambiguity may
support a documentation-level posture of HOLD, REFUSE, or ESCALATE, but those
postures are not runtime behavior, enforcement behavior, approval authority,
routing authority, register mutation, blocker resolution, event-bus behavior,
or execution permission.

System-held records:
System-held records may preserve information for review only. A system record
is not an authority source. Stored state, cached records, audit material,
monitoring observations, documentation classifications, or prior artifacts
must not override current parent/final authority or convert evidence into
permission.

Halt/refusal posture:
Halt and refusal posture protects parent/final authority by preserving
non-permission under conflict or ambiguity. In this review, halt, refusal, and
escalation language is governance-only and non-executable. It does not create
executable enforcement, runtime routing, service behavior, agent behavior,
autonomous action, broker/trading access, Robinhood alignment, SniperBot
behavior, macro/hotkey automation, or command execution.

## Consolidation Finding

The current governance spine remains coherent only if parent/final authority
remains non-transferable and evidence remains non-authoritative.

Authority conflict, caregiver/delegate evidence, delegated support, ambiguity,
system-held records, observation, clinical recommendation, stale records,
silence, presence, PASS classifications, and documentation artifacts must all
remain unable to create approval or execution permission.

This finding is not schema readiness, validator readiness, test readiness, CI
readiness, runtime readiness, enforcement readiness, event-bus readiness,
register readiness, blocker readiness, broker/trading readiness, Robinhood
readiness, SniperBot readiness, macro/hotkey readiness, or execution
readiness.

Any separate future request involving mechanical work would have to preserve
this relationship. It must not reinterpret evidence as authority, conflict as
a decision state, ambiguity as permission, delegation as final authority, or
system records as override power.
